The Monetary Authority of Singapore today announced a new risk-based Liquidity Supervision Framework for banks in Singapore. The Liquidity Supervision Framework is a move away from the existing 'one-size-fits-all' 18% statutory Minimum Liquid Assets (MLA) requirement to one that is based on a bank's own liquidity risk profile and liquidity risk management capabilities. This will enable banks with stronger liquidity risk management to have lower MLA requirements.
