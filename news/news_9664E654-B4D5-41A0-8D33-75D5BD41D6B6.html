High Court Makes Civil Penalty Order Against Fund Manager and its Chief Executive Officer for Market Rigging
9664E654-B4D5-41A0-8D33-75D5BD41D6B6
The Singapore High Court today found that a Malaysian fund manager, Pheim Asset Management Sdn Bhd ("Pheim Malaysia"), and its founder and CEO, Dr Tan Chong Koay had contravened the market rigging provisions under section 197(1)(b) of the Securities and Futures Act (SFA) by trading with the intention of creating a false or misleading appearance in the price of United Envirotech Ltd ("UET") shares. The High Court ordered Pheim Malaysia and Dr Tan to each pay a civil penalty of $250,000 and legal costs to the Monetary Authority of Singapore.
