Senior Principal Manufacturing Equipment Engineer
R10095635
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman's Manufacturing Operations Engineering organization is seeking a Senior Principal Manufacturing Equipment Engineer for the Advanced Interconnects and Multilayer Solutions (AIMS) manufacturing cell.

AIMS is committed to the rapid development, manufacture and delivery of leading-edge discriminating products, utilizing advances in organic materials, technology and processing to meet or exceed customer expectations.

 

The Manufacturing Equipment Engineer will provide manufacturing technical expertise and direction to facilitate the fabrication of printed wiring boards, manifolds, and backplanes for next generation RF hardware.  This position will be responsible for leading, systematizing and sustaining equipment maintenance efforts within AIMS.

 

Responsibilities include, but are not limited to, the tasks outlined below:

  • Daily interaction, support and collaboration with skilled machine electricians and mechanics providing them with systematic troubleshooting guidance on complex state of the art manufacturing equipment.

  • Successfully apply complex knowledge of fundamental concepts, practices, and procedures to expedite troubleshooting and repair of complex industrial equipment with integrated electronic, hydraulic, robotics, and pneumatics systems.

  • Communicate maintenance and repair efforts frequently to management and cross functional groups in a customer facing capacity.

  • Provide project management level support and leadership for maintenance and repair events and ownership over outcome.

  • Gain a solid understanding of complex machine system functions, controls and kinematics and communicate issues to stakeholders.

  • Develop work instructions for recurring repairs and processes.

  • Support capital equipment projects as needed.

  • Work with Manufacturing Engineering SME’s to develop Predictive and Preventive Maintenance Program for a variety of production assets to ensure correct reliability checks are being made.

  • Ensure all applicable documents, drawings and instructions are available for machine evaluation, troubleshoot and repair.

  • Develop and maintain metric performance, i.e., machine uptime, predictive, and OEE.

 

The chosen candidate must have excellent verbal and written communication skills; be able to multi-task in a fast-paced, dynamic, and high visibility environment; and work well as part of a team. He/she should also have excellent prioritization, planning and documentation skills.

 

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor of Science degree or higher in Engineering or related STEM area and 9 years of relevant professional experience (or 7 years with a Master's). Candidates with a high school diploma and 13 years of relevant experience or an Associate's degree and 11 years of relevant experience will be considered.

  • Extensive experience in a manufacturing environment working with industrial manufacturing machinery, production and test related equipment

  • Able to work independently or in small teams using schematics, diagrams, written and verbal descriptions

  • Fluency in MS Office software applications

  • Ability to obtain and maintain a US Secret Security Clearance (US Citizenship is required.)

 

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Knowledge of manufacturing processes used in the production of PCBs in the aerospace market

  • Troubleshooting experience PLC Logic (Siemens, Allen-Bradley, GE)

  • Thorough knowledge of electrical schematics and technical drawings

  • Prior experience as a maintenance or service technician on heavy industrial, automated and/ or manufacturing equipment

  • Electronics experience in a manufacturing, assembly and/ or troubleshooting capacity

  • Experience with developing and conducting classroom and/or virtual training
