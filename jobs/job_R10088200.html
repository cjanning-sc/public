DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

Under direction of the Security Manager, this position primarily serves as the Industrial Security Analysis for the DoD collateral programs. Will support the management and execution of program security as it applies to program protection and industrial security oversight for our DoD Industrial Security Programs.  

Must be well organized, be willing to work in a team environment, be able to prioritize tasking, perform in a multi-tasked and dynamic environment

Under minimum supervision, administers routine aspects of the DOD or other industrial security program to ensure compliance with government and company security policies and procedures

Adhering to policies, practices, and procedures of the National Industrial Security Program (NISP) and implementing the National Industrial Security Program (NISPOM) 32_CFR, the company policies, practices, and procedures.

Physical Security – Understanding the principles of physical security to safeguard and protect DoD assets.  Execute the physical security program, planning and implementation. Demonstrates the countermeasures and concepts of physical security.

Shall conduct new hire orientation, Security Indoctrination briefings and maintains security education programs.

Responsible for Document Control; accountability, reviewing classified documents, receiving, and dispatching classified information.

Knowledgeable with the use of Company and Government Data Bases; SIMS, DISS and NISS.

Requires the ability to make sound decisions, manage time, take independent action, and provide focused solutions.

Demonstrated ability to effectively communicate information to various audiences and all levels of the organization both verbally and through written communications.

Must be dependable, responsive, customer focused and possess the qualities of diplomacy, tact, excellent judgment, discretion, and initiative.

Information Security; accountability, transmission, marking, reproduction, and disposition of classified information.

Monitors security procedures; ensures compliance with all aspects of the program including personnel, physical and administrative security procedures.

Perform other duties as assigned or required.          

Basic Qualifications:

Level II -

- High school Diploma with 7+ years of relatable experience, Bachelor’s degree in Business Administration or relatable subject + 3 years of relatable experience or Masters degree with 1 years experience.

- Minimum of DoD SECRET clearance, with the ability to obtain a Top-Secret security clearance with a current Single Scope Background Investigation (SSBI).

Level III -

- High school Diploma with 10+ years of relatable experience, Bachelor’s degree in Business Administration or relatable subject + 6 years of relatable experience or Masters degree with 4 years experience.

- Minimum of DoD SECRET clearance, with the ability to obtain a Top-Secret security clearance with a current Single Scope Background Investigation (SSBI).

Preferred:

- Center for Development of Security Excellence (CDSE) Industrial Security Certifications, Physical Security and FSO certification a plus.
