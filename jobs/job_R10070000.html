Senior Principal System Test Engineer
R10070000
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems has an opening for a Senior Principal System Test Engineer to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals within our South Bay Aircraft Design Center organization. This role is located in Redondo Beach, California.

As a System Test Engineer, you will be responsible for defining and executing ground and flight tests campaigns for air launched and air-breathing weapons.  

Essential Functions:

  • Plan and execute system level I-III, ground, and flight test programs for air-launched/air-breathing weapons.
  • Develop Verification Cross Reference Matrices (VCRM).
  • Writing test plans, procedures, and reports.
  • Responsible for Platform/Store electrical and physical integration and testing.
  • Perform trade studies, and support design, analysis and operation of miniaturized airborne instrumentation and telemetry systems.
  • Lead and support Flight Termination System (FTS) design and analysis. Understand, derive, and maintain requirements from Range Commanders Council (RCC) documentation and RCC-319 tailoring process.
  • Lead flight certification (Airworthiness) process(es) for airborne stores (Seek Eagle, DARPA, FAA) on DoD and commercial aircraft.
  • Lead/support system safety, mishap risk identification and mitigation processes.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree in a STEM discipline (Science, Technology, Engineering or Math) with 9 years of related engineering experience; OR a Master’s degree in STEM with 7 years of related engineering experience, or a PhD in STEM with 4 years of related engineering experience.
  • Must have an active DoD Secret or higher clearance.
  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain Special Access Program (SAP) clearance.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in a STEM discipline, or Aeronautical Engineering.
  • Current Special Access Program (SAP).
  • Experience with Company and DoD explosives/munitions handling, transportation, and storage logistics.
  • Knowledge of Stores Management Systems (SMS)/ Suspension and Release Equipment (S&RE), Universal Armament Interface (UAI), MIL-STD-1760, MIL-STD-8591, MIL-HDBK-1763, etc.
  • Familiarity with IADS software and IRIG-106 standards.
  • Experience with Airworthiness processes: MIL-HDBK-516C (section 17) and AFI63-104.
  • Experience with MIL-STD-882E and Non-Nuclear Munitions Safety Board (NNMSB).
  • Excellent oral, written, and interpersonal communication skills.

We offer flexible work arrangements, 9/80 work schedule with every other Friday off, phenomenal learning opportunities, exposure to a wide variety of projects and customers, and a very friendly team environment. We are looking for self-motivated, proactive, and goal-oriented people to help us grow our services and become even better at what we do.
