Northrop Grumman has an exciting opening for a  Manager Navigation and Control Engineering 3 to join our team in Plymouth, MN.

Responsibilities of this role:

Lead and develop a team of Guidance, Navigation & Control (GN&C) engineers within the System Engineering, Integration and Test (SEIT) department.  The GN&C Team supports all Northrop Grumman Armament System programs in the product areas of medium and large caliber ammunition, precision and guided projectiles, air defense systems, loitering and miniature strike munitions.

Drive Team technical excellence in GN&C architecture, design and modeling simulation and analysis (MS&A).  Balance group workload and staffing requirements to meet current and future program needs.   Be responsible for and employ the tools and process needed to generate guidance algorithms, control laws, estimation filters, system simulation models and system performance simulations. Support Provide both GN&C Team technical oversight and direct, hands-on project technical review, and project work, as required. Collaborate and coordinate with GN&C Team stakeholders including peer Engineering managers on the Systems, Aerodynamics and Design teams, technical directors/project engineers, program managers and integrated product teams. 

Staff all programs. Coach direct reports in the areas of career development, performance and teamwork.    Provide training and development opportunities.  Provide continual feedback and hold mid-year and end-of-year performance communications.

Serve as the external AS GN&C interface with national universities, government labs, and GN&C managers at other Northrop Grumman sites. Manage department overhead budget and continuous improvement projects. Support continuous improvement, including Digital Transformation and Model-based Engineering initiatives.

Minimum Qualifications:

Bachelor of Science in Aerospace, Mechanical or Electrical Engineering

14 years of Engineering experience

Technical depth in projectile, rocket, missile and/or munition GN&C, M&SA and flight dynamics.

DoD Secret Security Clearance, or ability to obtain one

Team management and/or Integrated Product Team (IPT) leadership experience.

Preferred Qualifications:

MSAE, MSEE

Department team leadership, supervisory, or people management experience.
