Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems has an opening for a Structural Testing and Engineer to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Clearfield, UT.

Our team works with experienced engineers, supporting sophisticated structural analysis and testing of both composite and metallic materials of aerospace structures. We engineer the latest structures for military/commercial aircraft and launch structures, and are leaders in next-generation structure development. Would you like to work on the cutting edge of aerospace? Come join our team!

Responsibilities:

  • Conduct structural mechanical testing from small scale coupons to large scale sub-components
  • Perform structural analysis including static, dynamics, buckling and fatigue analysis.
  • Build Finite Element Models (FEMs) using CAD geometry, meshing, and running solutions using commercial software (Femap, Abaqus, Nastran)
  • Compute margins of safety to size parts using structural analysis methods
  • Conduct structural and material tests on composite and metallic specimens/components to evaluate if parts meet acceptance test criteria
  • Develop, maintain, and execute test plans for products ranging from components to sub-assemblies to vehicle-level systems.
  • Operate and/or support complex test facility systems (e.g MTS and Instron machines) and help direct a team of technicians to execute test processes
  • Analyze and review data to ensure that hardware is operating as expected utilizing numerical data analysis tools to interpret test results
  • Write test reports that include test results and conclusions based on the data collected 
  • Directly support testing, such as material preparation and testing, testing using an Instron and other material testing equipment, metallurgical lab sample cutting, mounting, polishing and evaluation.

Our team enjoys a significantly diverse range of assignments, and collaborates with many Northrop Grumman teams to provide innovative solutions to complex and exciting problems. We work a 9/80 work week, which gives us every other Friday off to pursue passions outside the office.
