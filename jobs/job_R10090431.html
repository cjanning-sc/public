Northrop Grumman Aerospace Systems has an opening for a System Engineering Functional Homeroom Section Manager to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Melbourne, Florida.


The selected candidate will manage a section of 10-12 Modeling & Simulation Engineers and report to the System Architecture, Modeling and Simulation Department Manager on a 15% allocation. The selected candidate will be responsible for the direct supervision/management of the staff, providing objective-oriented direction to subordinates utilizing management guidelines & general policies, and maintain a technical statement of work for the remaining 85% of the work week. This is a full-time, dual-role position. The technical statement of work will be part of the System Analysis & Simulation (SA&S) team that provides Operational Analysis and Virtual Simulation support to divisions across Aeronautics Systems sector.

Management responsibilities include: planning and program performance addressing cost, schedule, technical performance, and quality of a system/mission thread work packages, subsystems, or related group of work packages or across multiple interrelated programs with full responsibility for all aspects of technical performance on large system development-type contracts, large delivery orders or mid-size technical services-type contracts.
Essential Functions:
•    Conducts weekly flow down meetings with assigned staff
•    Attends weekly flow down meetings with the Department Manager
•    Supports staffing needs as assigned
•    Conducts goal planning, performance evaluations merit budgeting
•    Provides technical guidance related to process and training to staff 
The selected candidate should thrive in a fast-paced work environment with high expectations, significantly diverse assignments, collaborative/team settings across all levels.  
To learn more about our hiring process for manager positions, please view our Selecting the Best Qualified Managers” video: www.northropgrumman.com/SQMVideo”


Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering or Mathematics (STEM) discipline and 9 years engineering experience OR a Master’s Degree in a STEM discipline and 7 years of engineering experience
  • Recent demonstrated knowledge of Systems Engineering practices
  • Recent demonstrated knowledge of modeling & simulation tools such as MATLAB and AVSIM to perform operational analysis and/or virtual simulation.
  • Candidate must have an active U.S. Government security clearance at the DoD Secret or Top Secret level.
  • Candidate must be able to obtain and maintain special program access.
  • Proficiency in the use of the Microsoft Office Suite and Microsoft Project.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Active, In-scope DoD Top Secret or SCI clearance
  • Advanced STEM Degree
  • Experience modeling large, complex systems with multiple developers.
  • Previous people leadership experience.
  • Experience in the military aerospace development environment.
  • Excellent communication, interpersonal skills, and the ability to interface with all levels of employees and management.
