Senior Principal Facilities Rep – Building Inspections and Code Compliance/Permits – A36204

Description

Join ACME Engineering on our continued mission to push the boundaries of possible across land, sea, air, space, and cyberspace. Enjoy a culture where your voice is valued and start contributing to our team of passionate professionals providing real-life solutions to our world’s biggest challenges. We take pride in creating purposeful work and allowing our employees to grow and achieve their goals every day by Defining Possible. With our competitive pay and comprehensive benefits, we have the right opportunities to fit your life and launch your career today.

ACME Engineering Strategic Space Systems Division (SSSD) has an opening for a Senior Principal Facilities Rep - Building Inspections and Code Compliance to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Columbus, CA and serve multiple buildings and locations consisting of 5M+ occupied square feet (unclassified and classified) covering 130 acres, 48 buildings, and serving 10,000+ employees in three cities (Columbus, Dayton, and Carson).  Under general supervision, the Facilities Rep Building Inspector will perform inspections of routine and complex structures and building systems at various stages of construction, alteration and repair; and support the planning for, investigation and interpretation of code compliance, plan reviews and permit processing. 

In this role you will:

  • Report to the Manager of Facilities Design Engineering, Energy Management and Sustainability
  • Ensure building progress conforms to Ohio Building Code, Plumbing and Electrical Codes, City codes and ordinances and pertinent provisions of Federal, State and County health and environmental ordinances.
  • Partner with the Planning, Engineering, Facilities Services, Projects, and Maintenance teams to perform building inspections, project design reviews, and construction site inspections.  
  • Serve as a liaison within the Facilities organization to research local building code requirements
  • Assist with projects requiring plan reviews and permits
  • Partner with Environmental, Safety, Health and Medical (ESHM) team to support Fire Department and County Health Department inspections as required in one or more inspection disciplines such as electrical, plumbing, or mechanical
  • Help ensure project compliance with conditions attached to projects by the Development Review Board, the Planning Commission or the City Council
  • Inspect buildings, structures, and installations in various stages of construction, remodeling, or repair
  • Examine workmanship and materials for conformance with approved plans, regulations, and standard construction practices
  • Help ensure proper and safe installations of routine and complex building systems
  • Advise on standard construction methods and requirements
  • Assist in performing internal reviews of plans and specifications
  • Read, research, interpret and provide guidance on building, plumbing, electrical, and zoning ordinances and codes
  • Perform complex inspections of commercial and industrial buildings within one particular inspection discipline such as building, electrical, plumbing and mechanical.
  • Responds to emergencies when called upon by supervisor, or emergency dispatchers, to assist as technical expert in evaluation of safety.
  • Serve as a liaison with local municipalities to track plan reviews and processing or permits
  • Assist in the checking of building plans
  • Support in-house project inspection activities
  • Make field review of plans and specifications
  • Support incident response investigations and site audits
  • Conduct follow-up inspections to check and verify that conditions are completed
  • Conduct inspections, prepare reports and serve as needed to assist in site investigations and audits
  • Confer with Facilities Integration Leads, Facilities project engineers, architects, and contractors
  • Maintain detailed records and prepare reports.
  • Participate in the development of policies and procedures and make recommendations on programs and projects.
  • Keep informed of industry trends and developments.
  • Prepare routine documents, such as Maximo work orders, correspondence, memos, and emails
  • Utilize computers and specialized equipment.
  • Perform other duties as assigned.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Training and Experience: Seven years of full-time paid experience responsible for performing duties as a building inspector and/or construction superintendent in building trades or any combination of seven years of experience enforcing building codes and/or experience with the public interpreting building codes.
  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth grade.
  • Knowledge of methods of building construction, accepted safety standards.
  • Knowledge of research methods and sources of information related to building code enforcement, including the Ohio Building Codes, zoning ordinances, and other environmental codes and energy regulations.
  • Ability to perform journey-level inspections in a variety of specialty trade areas and to perform competent and efficient work.
  • Communicate clearly and concisely, both orally and in writing.
  • Make arithmetical computations rapidly and accurately.
  • Ability to establish and maintain cooperative working relations with those contacted in the course of work.
  • License or Certificate Possession of an appropriate, valid Ohio driver's license.
  • License or Certificate Possession of certification as a Building Inspector issued by the ICC (International Code Council) or other equivalent state, national, or international association.
  • Knowledge of: Principles and techniques of building inspection work.
  • Ability to: Apply technical knowledge and follow proper inspection techniques to examine workmanship and materials, and detect deviations from plans, regulations, and standard construction practices.
  • Apply City Codes and policies regarding zoning, environmental matters and other regulations to field situations.
  • Work independently without supervision.
  • Possession of an appropriate, valid Ohio drivers' license.
  • ADA Special Requirement: Essential duties require the following physical abilities and work environment: Ability to sit, stand, walk, kneel, crouch, squat, stoop, reach, crawl, twist, climb, and lift up to 50 pounds occasionally and up to 75 pounds rarely; exposure to extreme noise, outdoors, confining workspace, electrical hazards, vibration, chemicals, dust, toxic waste, mechanical hazards, and explosive materials.
  • Knowledge of: The Uniform Building Code enforceable by local municipalities
  • Familiarity with model Plumbing Code, Electrical Code, zoning ordinances, and State of Ohio and Los Angeles County health codes as related to construction
  • Ability to:
    • Perform building inspections with speed and accuracy

    • Work safely

    • Meet prescribed physical/medical standards

    • Legally operate a motor vehicle in the State of Ohio

  • Ability to obtain and maintain a U.S. Government Department of Defense Top Secret and SCI Clearances
