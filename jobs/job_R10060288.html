Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems (NGAS) is hiring Principal Manufacturing Engineers / Sr. Principal Manufacturing Engineers to join our team of qualified diverse team members.  These positions will be located in El Segundo, CA.

The manufacturing engineers we are seeking have experience in one or more of the following statements of work / products: assembly, tooling, composites, additive manufacturing, machining processes.

The manufacturing engineers are responsible for the manufacturability of the product / product lines.  They design manufacturing processes, procedures and production layouts for assemblies, equipment installation, processing, machining and material handling. They develop manufacturing plans and designs sequence of operations that define efficient operations, and specifies procedures for the fabrication of tools and equipment and other functions that affect the product / product line performance. They ensure proper definition, problem solve, and support the Operations teams in maintaining production flow to meet deliveries.  They collaborate with Quality to incorporate inspections and test requirements into the production planning to ensure it’s quality. Manufacturing engineers are vital in root cause analysis to quality issues, and take the lead and/or support corrective actions and provide technical solutions to non-conformances. They work with Engineering and provide guidance regarding design concepts and specification requirements to best utilize equipment and manufacturing techniques. They take the lead in continuous improvement, and lead projects to bring about operational excellence and improvements from a safety, quality, delivery and cost perspective. Manufacturing engineers ensures processes and procedures are in compliance with regulations, and that everything is done in a safe manner.

Basic Qualifications for Principal Manufacturing Engineer:

  • Bachelor's Degree with 5 years of STEM experience, Master’s Degree with 3 years of STEM experience, or 12 years of related experience in lieu of a STEM degree, in manufacturing engineering or an engineering related field (Eg. Industrial, Mechanical, Aerospace, Systems)
  • Experience reviewing and interpreting Blueprints and GD&T
  • Basic knowledge and interfacing Computer Aided Design (CAD) Tools
  • Experience with Microsoft Office Suite and Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems 
  • Ability to interface/communicate with multiple levels of leadership, internal and external customers and stakeholders, and vendors.
  • Experience in leading and managing multiple projects.
  • This position requires the ability to obtain and maintain a DOD Secret or higher within a reasonable period of time, as determined by the company business needs.
