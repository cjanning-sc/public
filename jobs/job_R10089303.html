ACME Engineering is seeking an Associate Supply Chain Procurement Specialist to join our team.

Roles and Responsibilities may include but are not limited to :

  • Expedites contracts for materials or services by following up on due dates, ensuring material receiving documentation is correct, and updating the ERP system to reflect the status of procurements.
  • Sources and procures goods and services of a category nature from many suppliers.
    Initiates requests for quotations and proposals and receives same, analyzing proposed pricing from potential suppliers.
  • Establishes firm fixed price agreements with suppliers.  
    In conjunction with quality organization, evaluates and monitors supplier quality and reliability, as well as supplier ability to support required delivery schedules.
  • Works with internal customers to ensure specifications are appropriate for the services or products required.
  • Identifies opportunities to reduce cost, improve efficiency and manage risk through targeted negotiations.
  • Issues purchase orders.
  • Manages performance of purchase orders, ensures compliance with all public law requirements as well as company (and business) principals and operating procedures including import/export regulations.
  • Employs sound business practices and assesses supplier performance as part of follow-up activities.
  • Ensures that appropriate documentation is provided to logistics/transportation and warehouse organization so that proper receipt is recorded.
  • Reviews and approves supplier payment as needed.

    We are seeking a candidate who works positively in a high-energy and fast-paced environment, is self-motivated and highly organized, and delivers quality work on time
    This position requires travel up to 25% of the time

     

Basic Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s degree from an accredited institution in supply chain or similar field of study
    Associate's degree from an accredited institution and 2 years of supply chain experience
    4 years of work related experience in supply chain in lieu of degree

    Preferred Qualifications
  • Experience procuring raw materials such as electronics, adhesives, tapes, or chemicals and hazardous materials
  • Ability to read technical drawings and documents
  • Knowledge of government contracting requirements and regulations (e.g., FAR, DFARS, ITAR, etc.)
