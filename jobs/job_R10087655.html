Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems is seeking a successful candidate to fill a Test and Evaluation (T&E) Engineer Lead role for the B-2 program within the Lab Support IPT. Responsible for working with an integrated product team (IPT) structure to perform technical planning, design, execution, system integration, verification and validation, cost and risk, and supportability and effectiveness analyses for the Labs within the Weapon System Support Center (WSSC) within Tinker AFB, OK.  


Reports directly to System Test IPT Lead.  The position is located in Oklahoma City, OK

The roles and responsibilities include (but not limited to) the following:

  • Technical responsibility over Obsolescence, Maintenance, and management of the labs. 

  • Technical responsibility for design, management and plan for the modernization of the labs. 

  • Control Account Management (CAM) responsibilities for these efforts.  

  • Oversight of Engineering Task Assessments (ETAs) related to the labs.  

  • Work with multiple efforts and efficiently merge them into supporting all efforts and their milestones

  • Must be familiar with managing suppliers and working with supplier management teams and Global Supply Chain partners.

  • Must be comfortable briefing Government and Northrop Grumman leadership on technical progress, risks, issues, and opportunities.

Basic Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s Degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering or Mathematics) discipline from an accredited university with 9 years of experience; Master’s Degree in STEM with 7 years of experience or PhD with 4 years of experience.

  • Successful experience in planning, risk management, and/or project performance addressing cost, schedule, and technical quality.

  • Understanding of the application and development of functional and physical architectures, interfaces, and requirements in large systems.

  • Experience building, supporting, or running aerospace labs

  • Current in-scope active final DoD Secret Security Clearance with ability to obtain a DoD Top Secret Security Clearance.

  • Ability to obtain and maintain Special Program Access (PAR)

 
Preferred Qualifications

  • Master’s Degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering or Mathematics) discipline from an accredited university with 10 years of experience or PhD with 7 years of experience.

  • Successful experience in modernization or sustainment planning, risk management, and project performance addressing cost, schedule, and technical quality

  • Proven experience in managing labs, setting & operating to objectives & providing guidance to subordinates based on goals, objectives & company policy. 

  • Logistics/Sustainment experience in a military environment

  • Experience in developing and managing Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) elements on a large system development-type contract or full responsibility for all aspects of program performance on a large technical services-type contract.

  • Preference in knowledge / experience of B-2 operational CONOPS 

  • Successful experience in major lab upgrade or lab modification programs

  • Current DoD Top Secret/SCI Security Clearance

  •  Active B-2 technical experience and program clearances
