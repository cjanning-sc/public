1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Global Operations Security is seeking a Cyber Information Assurance Manager 2 to support the GBSD Cyber Information Systems Security team in the Roy, UT area.  A classified information systems security/information assurance background is required, including experience with Special Access Programs (JSIG) and/or DoD collateral (NISPOM and DAAPM) processes.

This position is part of a growing Cybersecurity management staff and will oversee various A&A activities across the GBSD footprint. This role is supported by a team of direct ISSMs/ISSOs with matrixed support from the Cybersecurity Engineering and Cyber Operations organizations. The A&A Manager will act as the Deputy Cybersecurity Program Lead with direct interface to program senior and executive level leadership.   Responsibilities of this position will include but not be limited to the following:

  • The oversight and support of classified systems, team processes, analysis, coordination, security documentation, as well as investigations, software research, inventory management, and periodic audits. 

  • Effective team management to include fostering individual development, performance management, open communication, and innovative ideas to enable business objectives.

  • Ensure strict program control processes to ensure mitigation of risks and support obtaining formal Assessment & Authorization of systems.

  • Maintain accurate metrics in order to make business intelligence decisions.

  • Assist in the implementation of the required government policy; this may include project management and supporting the Northrop Grumman processes for the JSIG or DAAPM.

  • Regular and routine communication with key stakeholders including GBSD Cybersecurity Management, program management teams, the ES Program Management Office, the Information Technology Organization, Industrial Security, and internal and external customers.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree with  7 years of experience, or a Master’s degree with  5 years of experience.

  • Current DoD Secret security clearance with an original adjudication or reinvestigation date within the last 6 years, and the ability to maintain the required clearance as a condition of continued employment.

  • Must have the ability to obtain, and maintain, a Special Access Programs (SAP) clearance within a reasonable period of time as determined by the company and also by customer and/or program requirements, as a condition of continued employment. 

  • Must have Information Systems Security Officer/Manager experience in at least one of the following 3 areas: National Industrial Security Program (NISP), Department of Defense Special Access Programs (DoD SAP), or Sensitive Compartmented Information (SCI) programs.

  • Must have a current DoD 8570 IAM Level III security certification (Examples: CISM, CISSP, or GSLC).

  • Demonstrated knowledge of cybersecurity practices, network technologies, system development lifecycles, and understanding of information technology infrastructure management/monitoring and applications.

  • Understanding of network architectures, Active Directory, and Group Policy management.

  • Experience developing guidelines, monitoring policies, and enforcing standards for cybersecurity frameworks and industry best practices supporting National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) Special Publication (SP) 800-53, CNSSI 1253, and DoD Risk Management Framework (RMF).

Preferred Qualifications: 

 

  • The ideal candidate will have a Master's degree in Cyber Security, Information Security, or similar STEM related discipline, along with 4 years of ISSO/ISSM experience.

  • Prior experience in a leadership role.

  • Diverse classified information systems security/information assurance background.

  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills.

  • Prior experience communicating with customer and program leadership.

  • Active Top Secret security clearance with SAP/SAR access.
