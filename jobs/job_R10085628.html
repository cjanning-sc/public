ACME Engineering is looking for an Accounts Payable Director. This position will be located in Irving, TX and offer partial telework.
 

The Accounts Payable Director will lead the Financial Shared Services teams supporting the ACME Engineering payables and cash management processes.  Accounts Payables teams located in Bloomington, MN and Irving, TX recently were aligned within a single organization and will be managed by the Accounts Payables Director.  Primary responsibilities include timely and accurate processing and payment of invoices, compliance with government rules and regulations, continuous improvement to maintain reliable, efficient, and cost-effective processes, and collaboration/coordination with businesses and functions.  This role requires significant experience and competency with successful system conversions and change management to support ACME Engineering’s current transition from SAP ERP to S/4 HANA.


The Accounts Payable Director will report to the Financial Shared Services Director, will be highly visible to executive leadership, and will be a key player in establishing a best-in-class Financial Shared Services team at ACME Engineering. 
 

Core Responsibilities:

  • Provide operational management and leadership for the ACME Engineering Accounts Payable teams with approximately 85 staff and managers based in Bloomington, MN and Irving, TX.
  • Forge strong partnerships with business and functional leaders to understand key quality, regulatory, and service requirements.  Use these relationships to develop pathways for regular, timely, and effective communication with business partners.
  • Proactively champion and lead change management activities to ensure comprehensive adoption and acceptance of improvements, including engaging stakeholders and leaders, managing training and communications, governing implementation/stabilization, and performing post-implementation analysis.
  • Develop, maintain, and document actionable metrics and KPIs. Leverage these to understand opportunities for improvement and lead remediation efforts.
  • Own, monitor, and certify to the design, implementation, and operating effectiveness of payable and cash management-related SOX controls.
  • Support monthly financial close activities.
  • Guide employee development and education to ensure all in Accounts Payables remain current on relevant processes and procedures.
  • Empower and encourage employees to develop in their roles and their skill sets.
  • Ensure all payable and cash management-related policies and manuals, organizational operating instructions, and desktop procedures are maintained and appropriately followed.
  • Work with external financial auditors to support the annual audit and quarterly reviews.
  • Work with internal audit and SOX compliance teams to ensure effective controls, compliance and governance process and monitor progress, risks/issues, and barriers and create applicable mitigation strategies.
  • Take an active role in evolving the organization’s processes, methodologies and capabilities; drive continuous standardization of process, with the objective of improving payables and cash management and related accounting quality.
  • Lead by example and set the standard for professional behavior and excellent work habits that support a positive, inclusive, and progressive working environment.
  • Focus on talent development and building a strong team.
  • Provide regular feedback, including documented performance reviews.
     

Required Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Business, Accounting, or related field of study with 15+ years of relevant experience successfully leading Accounts Payables and Cash Management and related processes in large, complex, and rapidly changing organizations OR Master’s degree in Business, Accounting, or related field of study with 10+ years of relevant experience successfully leading Accounts Payables and Cash Management and related processes in large, complex, and rapidly changing organizations
  • Significant experience with ERPs including processes, upgrades, troubleshooting, conversions, and implementations.
  • Significant experience working with auditors and SOX compliance professionals.
  • Demonstrated strategic and critical thought leadership.
  • Excellent communications, writing, and presentation skills.
  • Working knowledge of MS Office Suite (including Excel, pivot tables, and analysis techniques)
  • Demonstrated experience in developing, leading, and executing comprehensive change management plans.
  • Record of collaborating across organizational boundaries to understand best practices, with demonstrated success in influencing without direct authority.
  • Ability to:
    • Support multiple priorities
    • Take the big picture and distill down to executable actions 
    • Partner and align with leaders cross-functionally on strategy
    • Communicate effectively at all levels of the organization.
    • Travel approximately 10% of the time.
       

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Graduate degree in Business, Accounting, or related field of study.
  • Certified Public Accountant or Certified Management Accountant
  • High level technical understanding and professional work experience in a government contracting organization.
  • Operational experience with SAP, S/4, and Costpoint ERPs.
  • Vendor master file management.
