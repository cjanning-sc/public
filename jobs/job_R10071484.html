Northrop Grumman Defense Systems (NGDS) is seeking a Staff Contract Administrator to join its team of qualified and diverse individuals. The Staff Contract Administrator will be the on-site lead for NGDS’ Aircraft Manufacturing and Fabrication Center's (AMFC) E-6B contract portfolio. Northrop Grumman has secured multiple E-6B maintenance and modification contracts and is focused on securing additional work in this area. The successful candidate will be responsible for managing all contractual documentation and communication between NG and a very collaborative customer. Must be located or willing to relocate to be onsite at AMFC in Lake Charles, LA. Relocation assistance, if needed, will be provided to the successful candidate.

The Staff Contract Administrator will produce high quality Contract Management related products supporting the NGDS Sector and AMFC OU. This position requires a strong background in DoD contracting, excellent communication skills, and strong interpersonal and organizational skills. The ideal candidate will be a team player but also a strong independent contributor, will be proactive, will present leadership skills, will possess good analytical skills, will demonstrate problem-solving skills, and will be detail oriented.

The Staff Contract Administrator will be required to deal effectively with competing priorities and deadlines and handle multiple tasks. The Staff Contract Administrator must possess the ability to work well with others in high-stress high-volume situations, effectively interface with internal functional and program organizations, effectively deal with external customers, and meet the goals/objectives of the ATAS business unit.

Basic Qualifications for Staff Principal Contract Administrator:

  • This position emphasizes skills and experience.  Must have a High School Diploma or equivalent (GED) with a minimum of 19 years of applied experience.

  • Will also consider:

  • Bachelor’s Degree with a minimum of 15 years applied experience in a related field

  • Master’s Degree with a minimum of 13 year applied experience in a related field

  • Strong DoD technical contracts background to include proposal development and negotiation skills

  • Excellent leadership and interpersonal skills

  • Experience building and developing high-performing teams that will achieve success through a strong culture of early engagement, internal collaboration and critical thinking

  • Ability to lead meetings with government regulatory organizations

  • Excellent oral and written communication skills

  • Ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Secret Clearance

  • Working knowledge of FAR and DFARS as well as experience with different contract types

  • Experience leading teams, including cross-functional/cross-organization teams.

  • Strong problem solving and analytical skills; ability to make recommendations and provide guidance to effectively bring projects to completion.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience with Contractor Logistics Services (CLS) contracts

  • Experience with Aircraft Programmed Depot Maintenance (PDM) or Modification contracts

  • NCMA Certification (CPCM, CFCM and/or CCCM)

  • MS/MA/MBA/JD
