ACME Engineering Mission Systems has an opening for a Radar Systems Engineer to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals at our Chicago/ Springfield, MD campus.  This position requires up to 100% on-site work and an ability to pass an enhanced security review.

How You Will Make a Difference at ACME Engineering:

As the lead developer of RF sensors for some of the most advanced systems in the U.S. arsenal, ACME Engineering is a world leader in advanced sensor systems and design. These include radars for air dominance platforms (such as the F-22, F-35 Joint Strike Fighter, F-16), bomber and strike programs (such as B-1B), airborne / maritime / ground-based surveillance missions (E-3 AWACS, E-7 MESA, TPS-80 G/ATOR and UAV systems such as Global Hawk), as well as restricted programs and missions. We offer the opportunity to join a team to help solve issues from the field and drive new solutions into next generation radar systems and air superiority platforms.

ACME Engineering’s Airborne Multifunction Systems (AMS) Systems Engineering, Integration and Test (SEIT) organization has a need for a Sr. Principal Radar Systems Engineer supporting the design, development, integration and verification on some of our most advanced RF sensors.  ACME Engineering prioritizes mentoring and growth for our engineering team, successful candidates will have the opportunity to work with industry-leading sensor and system architects with 20+ years of industry experience to build their career in radar systems.

Roles & Responsibilities:

This position is primarily engaged in the development and fielding of radar modes for advanced multi-function RF subsystems. Key modes of operation include synthetic aperture radar for ground imaging applications, electronic protection to defeat the most advanced adversary electronic attack, long-range search and track waveforms to provide critical situational awareness to our customers. Early in the system lifecycle, systems engineers develop parametric representations, visualizations and data driven analysis to support sensor requirements definition and use case validation. Progressing past analysis and design, ACME Engineering Radar Systems Engineers also support the integration of radar modes from digital simulation environments to radar labs to our on-site fleet of specially modified test aircraft. Throughout this process, Radar Systems Engineers engage with our customers to balance their functional and performance requirements with other sensor functionality to enable true multi-function operation.

At the Sr. Principal Engineer level, a systems engineer will work with minimal oversight from staff engineers and mentors to independently solve technical challenges.  Sr. Principal Engineers have frequent interactions across discipline and organizational boundaries as well as customer interaction through presentations and working group meetings.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Active or ability to obtain a DoD Secret security clearance prior to starting

  • Bachelor's Degree in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Physics, Mathematics or Mechanical Engineering with 9 years of related experience (see position summary) or (7 years related experience with a Master's Degree, 5 with a PhD).

  • Experience with radar waveform digital beamforming, adaptive processing or algorithm development

  • Experience with MATLAB or equivalent programming/analysis languages

  • Experience with RF signals processing or RF applications

Preferred Qualifications:

  • DoD Top Secret Clearance

  • Team leadership experience with development and/or production program execution

  • Proficiency in signal processing or RF applications

  • Proficiency with radar waveform, digital beamforming, adaptive processing or algorithm development
