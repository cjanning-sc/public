Sr. Principal Military Trainer - Exercise Designer / Planner
R10081731
1st Shift

ACME Engineering Defense Systems sector (NGDS) is seeking a Sr. Principal Military Trainer - Exercise Designer / Planner to join our growing team of diverse professionals supporting our III Corps program at Fort Riley in Kansas.  The Exercise Designer / Planner provides subject matter expert support in all phases of exercise planning and the development and implementation of an effective, reliable, exercise program that will be systematically and periodically updated.

Responsibilities:

  • Plans exercises that meet all Joint Exercise Life Cycle (JELC) requirements and IAW CJCS Guide 3501, the Joint training System; a Primer for Senior Leaders

  • Quickly establishes credibility and interfacing with Senior Military Leaders in order to determine exercise objective and convey associated Exercise Support Manning Document (ESMD) requirements

  • Facilitates In-Progress-Reviews (IPRs) with unit leadership in order to determine and address information and capability gaps

  • Determines and communicates the Exercise Simulation Control Plan (SCP) to unit leadership

  • Leads/Facilitates IPRs for all simulation exercises with unit and key team members

  • Designs and plans exercise scenarios in a collaborative effort with training audience representatives, training officer and event planner

  • Develops and delivers scenario products such as historical reviews, Road-To-Crisis (RTC), Operational Environment (OE) overview, and other scenario products as each exercise evolves incrementally of the JELC

  • Provides instruction in Master Scenario Event List (MSEL) operations to members of the Exercise Control Group

  • Coordinates and adjusts suspense dates based on the needs to develop products

Basic Qualifications:

  • One of the following:

    • Bachelor’s degree with a minimum of 10 years of professional and/or military leadership experience

    • Master’s degree with a minimum of 8 years of professional and/or military leadership experience

  • Experience at the battalion, brigade, and division level

  • Thorough understanding of MCIS and their inter-dependencies with other WfF systems

  • Must have an active U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) Secret Security Clearance with the ability to obtain a Top Secret security clearance

  • Must be capable of conducting IPRs to schedule and tailor unit training and forecast requirements for upcoming progressive events

  • Experience in operations and training requirements at brigade or higher level

  • Experience with training management

  • Thorough understanding of U.S. Army Intelligence operations to include both G2 /S2 operations and ACE/BISE operations

  • Must have Military Education Level 4 (MEL-4) or equivalent Intermediate Level Education (ILE)

  • Must be able to work on-site at Fort Riley in Kansas

  • Must be able to travel as needed (up to 25% of the time)

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Master’s degree

  • Current TS/SCI clearance

  • CTC Observer Controller and/or a Small Group Instructor

  • Former service as a primary or deputy corps or division staff officer FA 57 with exercise experience

  • Collection management experience

  • Outstanding communication skills with the ability to correspond and communicate with senior military leaders

  • OIF and OEF experience, working knowledge of MSEL development and providing an uncooperative, challenging OPFOR during command post exercises
