This position is for the Structural Analysis Engineer with emphasis in Multi-Body Dynamics (MBD), reporting to MBD/Analysis manager. The successful candidate will be a part of the growing team of MBD engineers who are responsible for motion simulations of various on-orbit servicing and robotics operations, as well as detailed kinematics simulations of underlying mechanical/mechatronics systems.

Essential Functions:

As a MBD engineer, he/she will use expert knowledge of MBD, as well as structural dynamics, to provide design guidance to the programs and systems/subsystems engineering teams.

  • The assignment might also involve testing of prototypes and flight hardware, and simulation model correlations.
  • The job will require strong understanding of computational methods and ability to process and produce reliable interpretations of complex simulation/test data.
  • The assignments will also include Finite Element Analysis tasks (stress/strength, vibration, and loads analyses).
  • As an expert of Computer Aided Engineering (CAE), the candidate's tasks, up to 10% of the total work hours, might involve maintenance/deployment of engineering applications and computational resources, including specialized computational desktops and servers.
  • The physical job location is Dulles, Virginia.

This requisition may be filled at a higher grade based on qualifications listed below.

Basic Qualifications:

This is a dual requisition and can be filled at the Structural Engineer or Principal Structural Engineer level. Qualifications for each level listed below.

Basic Qualifications for Structural Engineer 2:

  • ABET accredited Graduate Degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, or Math) discipline along with 2 years' experience OR an ABET Masters Degree in a STEM discipline.
  • Demonstrated excellence in a space vehicle dynamics/kinematics field such as structural design, stress analysis, materials, and/or vehicle dynamics.
  • You must also be able to obtain and maintain all required security clearances and program specific accesses.

Basic Qualifications for Principal Structural Engineer 3:

  • ABET accredited Graduate Degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, or Math) discipline along with 5+ Years' experience OR an ABET Masters Degree with 3 Years of experience OR a PhD Degree in a STEM discipline..
  • Demonstrated excellence in a space vehicle dynamics/kinematics field such as structural design, stress analysis, materials, and/or vehicle dynamics.
  • You must also be able to obtain and maintain all required security clearances and program specific accesses.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • ABET accredited PhD Degree in Engineering.
  • Ability to obtain DoD Top Secret Clearance. - Expert knowledge of NGC MBD Robotics engineering processes.
  • 3 years of space system simulation engineering experience.
  • Demonstrated success in leadership/management roles.
  • Experience with Simcenter 3D Motion simulation tool.
  • Experience in controller modeling using Matlab/Simulink
