NRE Operator

Rotating Shifts

This full-time position is an entry level production position at New River Energetics (NRE) in Mason City, VA, located at the Mason City Army Ammunition Plant. Once an employee has completed new hire training, they will take direction from leaders and supervisors to safely produce quality products following work procedures and operating a variety of machinery.

Essential Functions
• Follow all safety, environmental, and production rules and procedures.
• Stop operations and notify Supervision of any abnormal conditions, including any potential safety hazards or unsafe conditions.
• Perform job so that product quality is assured.
• Inspect operating equipment on a daily basis.
• Call maintenance for equipment repairs, or report to leader or supervisor.
• Detect and inform leader or supervisor of any problems with equipment or materials.
• Support NRE’s Performance Enterprise System (PES) initiatives.
• Perform all activities outlined in the operating procedures for assigned work area.
• Set up and operate various types of production machinery.
• Maintain work area and equipment in a clean and orderly condition.
• Maintain production and operation records, including online record keeping and product identification tools.

Physical requirements to perform essential functions
• Lift up to 50 pounds repetitively
• Push carts weighing up to 1,000 pounds
• Bend and stoop repetitively
• Stand, walk, and climb steps repetitively
• Ability to safely operate a motor vehicle

Work environment
• Must be able to work in all weather conditions
• Must be able to work overtime when needed, per the guidelines outlined in the Collective Bargaining Agreement

Key abilities required to perform essential functions
• Read and understand written and verbal procedures and work instructions
• Effective verbal communication skills
• Basic math skills
• Ability to function in a team environment
• Ability to carry out job assignment without direct supervision
• Ability to clearly write and record information
• Ability to access and accurately record time worked in electronic timekeeping system
• Ability to use a computer for production record keeping
• Ability to drive various vehicles including, but not limited to, powder van, passenger van, sedan, and pickup, as well as forklift as required by work assignment
• Commitment to operating safely, with environmental awareness, and with a focus on producing a quality product

Pay Information

  • $21.88/hr., then after 120 days, pay increases to $23.51/hr.
  • Yearly pay increases, as outlined in the Collective Bargaining Agreement

Schedule

  • 7 day swing shift
