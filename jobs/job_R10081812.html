The Power Electronics Group consists of incredible people with backgrounds in Mechanical, Electrical, and Systems Engineering with focus on Control Systems and Electromagnetic Design.  The Power Electronics Group is one facet of the Hardware Engineering community here at ACME Engineering Beavercreek which also includes Mechanical Design, Thermal and Structural Analysis, and Materials Analysis to name a few.  Our engineers find professional fulfillment in leading teams, performing cutting-edge research, and being in the forefront of the design process through our partnership with the program office business leadership.

The Power Electronics group is seeking an Electrical/Power Electronics Design Engineer with knowledge of and experience with circuit design/analysis and power electronics design to military, space, or aerospace standards.  In addition, the candidate should be familiar with testing and troubleshooting of these designs from a validation perspective.  These components are integrated into larger electric machine systems and in that vein, it is desired for the prospective candidate to have knowledge/experience of electrical testing to characterize machine performance, design for EMI/EMC test experience, and control systems experience.

This position may be filled at either as an Electrical Engineer or a Principal Electrical Engineer.

Basic Qualifications for an Electrical Engineer: 

  • Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering with 2 years related experience; Master’s Electrical Engineering with no related experience

  • Analog and Digital Circuit Design and Analysis ability and experience

  • Demonstrated ability to troubleshoot electronic circuits (e.g. analog or high-voltage circuitry)

  • Demonstrated ability to integrate and model electronics and power electronics in high level modeling environments

  • Power Electronics design or analysis capability and experience, primarily with Pulse Width Modulated Power Amplifiers

  • Familiarity with non-contacting sensors such as hall effect or inductive position sensors and integration into larger systems

  • Must be a US citizen with the ability to obtain a DOD secret security clearance

Basic Qualifications for a Principal Electrical Engineer: 

  • Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering with 5 years related experience; Master’s in Electrical Engineering with 3 years related experience; PhD in Electrical Engineering with no related experience

  • Analog and Digital Circuit Design and Analysis ability and experience

  • Demonstrated ability to troubleshoot electronic circuits (e.g. analog or high-voltage circuitry)

  • Demonstrated ability to integrate and model electronics and power electronics in high level modeling environments

  • Power Electronics design or analysis capability and experience, primarily with Pulse Width Modulated Power Amplifiers

  • Familiarity with non-contacting sensors such as hall effect or inductive position sensors and integration into larger systems

  • Must be a US citizen with the ability to obtain a DOD secret security clearance

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Two (2) or more years of work experiences with DOD related products

  • MATLAB/Simulink proficiency

  • Demonstrated experience modeling and performing analysis of integrated systems encompassing electronics, power electronics, control systems, sensors, and mechanical systems

  • Demonstrated ability in production issue resolution

  • Experience working with Large Scale Rotating Electric Machinery (e.g. magnetic bearings generator, magnetic actuator), Control system design, modeling, and tuning

  • PSpice proficiency

  • Zuken proficiency

  • Current DOD secret security clearance

Technical Skills:

Strong candidates for this position will possess:

  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills

  • Strong organization skills in managing time, projects and resources

  • Leadership ability

  • Technical attention to detail

  • Drive and motivation to be proactive in problem solving and continuous improvement.
