The Naval & Oceanic Systems business unit headquartered in Rockford, IL is seeking dependable individuals willing to work in a collaborative environment and that are interested in expanding their horizons. The Mechanical Engineering Department is seeking a Principal Mechanical Systems Engineer / Senior Principal Mechanical Systems Engineer with a hydrodynamics and/or fluid dynamics/CFD background, preferably in underwater vehicles and/or manned submersibles, to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals.

Essential Functions:

Perform hydrodynamics analyses including Computational Fluid Dynamics, modeling and simulation, flight controls and dynamics, drag and powering, propulsor design, seakeeping, towing, 6DOF modeling, and model testing of underwater vehicles.  Translate the results of analyses into practical system level requirements and concept designs.  Support system integration and test.  The successful candidate will be expected to provide technical leadership to their programs in all areas of hydrodynamics.

The candidate must be able to collaborate in a team environment, prioritize and adjust tasks to accomplish the project result, prioritize multiple responsibilities and/or accomplishing them simultaneously, and comprehend and analyze complex problems and develop solutions. Excellent verbal and written communication skills are required.

Basic Qualifications for a Principle Mechanical Systems Engineer:

  • BS degree in Mechanical Engineering, Naval Architecture, Ocean Engineering, Aerospace Engineering, or STEM equivalent and a minimum of 5 years of experience in hydrodynamic analysis or MS degree with 3 years of experience
  • Proficiency in hydrodynamic or fluid dynamics analysis and developing system concepts and requirements from those analyses
  • Proficiency in using common engineering design, production, analysis, and office tools
  • US citizen and possess an active Secret clearance to start with the ability and willingness to obtain a TS/SCI and pass a polygraph.

Basic Qualifications for a Senior Principal Mechanical Systems Engineer:

  • BS degree in Mechanical Engineering, Naval Architecture, Ocean Engineering, Aerospace Engineering, or STEM equivalent and a minimum of 9 years of experience in hydrodynamic analysis or MS degree with 7 years of experience
  • Proficiency in hydrodynamic or fluid dynamics analysis and developing system concepts and requirements from those analyses
  • Proficiency in using common engineering design, production, analysis, and office tools
  • US citizen and possess an active Secret clearance to start with the ability and willingness to obtain a TS/SCI and pass a polygraph.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Exceptionally skilled in hydrodynamic analysis of underwater vehicles and developing system concepts and requirements from those analyses
  • Skilled in the use of Star-CCM, Ansys-Fluent, OrcaFlex, LAMP, MatLab, and NX
  • Active TS/SCI clearance
