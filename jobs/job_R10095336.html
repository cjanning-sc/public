​Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems has several openings available for Structural Analysis Engineers and Principal Structural Analysis Engineers (levels 2 and 3) to join our Vehicle Engineering team in Melbourne, Florida. CURRENT CLEARANCE NOT REQUIRED.

Essential functions:

  • Performs static, dynamic, and DaDT structural analyses of metallic and composite airframe structure, mechanical systems, and equipment, by hand and by using the automated methods and procedures approved for the program

  • Ensures that designs meet program requirements and are the lightest, lowest cost, and highest quality products consistent with schedule and budget objectives

  • The analyst may also be expected to derive non-flight applied loads, create, extract and distribute internal loads, and perform weight and stiffness optimization.

  • Tools utilized may include FEMAP, NX, Teamcenter and other software applications

  • Collaborate in a team environment, prioritize and adjust tasks to accomplish project results, prioritize multiple responsibilities and/or accomplish them simultaneously

  • Comprehend and analyze complex problems and develop solutions

This requisition may be filled as either a Structural Engineer (level 2) or Principal Structural Engineer (level 3) based on the qualifications below.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Structural Engineer: BS in a Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics (STEM) discipline and 2+ years' relevant experience to include a background in aerospace structural analysis; OR MS or Ph.D. in a STEM discipline and 0+ years of experience to include a background in aerospace structural analysis

  • Principal Structural Engineer: BS in a Science, Technology, Engineering or Mathematics (STEM) discipline with 5+ years' relevant experience to include a background in aerospace structural analysis; OR MS in a STEM discipline and 3+ years’ relevant experience to include a background in aerospace structural analysis; OR Ph.D. in a STEM discipline and 1+ years of experience to include a background in aerospace structural analysis

  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Secret clearance and Special Access Program (SAP) clearance prior to start

Preferred Qualifications: 

  • Degree in Mechanical, Structural, or Aerospace Engineering

  • Airframe experience with Advanced Composite Materials

  • Current in-scope DoD clearance

  • Ability to collaborate in a team environment

  • Able to prioritize and adjust tasks to accomplish the project result while prioritizing multiple responsibilities and/or accomplishing them simultaneously

  • Able to comprehend and analyze complex problems and develop solutions
