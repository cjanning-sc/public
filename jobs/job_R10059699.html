Essential Functions:

The successful candidate will be the primary interface between our technical engineering team and program test organizations in support of program critical test events and milestones.

•     Define communication architectures for System simulation efforts

•     Perform modeling and analysis of communication networks using Cameo, HFSS, EMIT, and STK in support of antenna placement and cosite analysis.

•     Analyze network architectures and topologies for distributed communication applications

•     Conduct trade studies for radio and ICS subsystem architectures.

•     Interaction with Comms suppliers for integration of Radio and ICS systems into the lab.

•     Authoring of Supplier Statements of Work, Source Control Documentation, and Interface Control Documents.

•     Provide lab integration and test.

Basic Qualifications:

•     Bachelor's Degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics) discipline from an accredited university with 5 years of related experience OR a Master's Degree in a STEM discipline with 3 years of related experience

•     Active DoD Top Secret security clearance

Preferred Qualifications:

•     Experience in communications system integration and test

•     Familiarity with common industry standards related to communications systems, such as MIL-STD-1553, RS-422, RS-232, IPv4/IPv6

•     Experience with Networking infrastructure at both the Layer 2 and Layer 3 IP levels for VOIP implementations

•     Experience with RF based test equipment to include spectrum analyzers, network analyzers, and power meters
