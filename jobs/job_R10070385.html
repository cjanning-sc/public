ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems has an opening for a Materials and Processes Engineer / Sr. Materials and Processes Engineer to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Green Bay, Wisconsin, and requires onsite presence due to the nature of the role. 

RESPONSIBILITIES

The selected candidate’s responsibilities may include, but not limited to:

  • Execute and document research on high temperature materials for functional RF structures (~75%)
  • Support active programs with materials selection and establishing processing routes for ceramics, polymers, metals, and composites (~20%)
  • Support various business critical functions (~5%)
  • Develop, support, and/or lead R&D or direct programs, test plans and reports, finance tracking, proposals, and presentations.  At any one time, the individual will serve in roles ranging from supporting engineer to team lead. 
  • Apply knowledge and expertise in materials science, chemistry, physics, and related fields to provide technical information, guidance, and solutions to engineers and scientists in support of R&D and production programs. 
  • Author, assist, and/or maintain material specifications and process standards, work instructions, and other engineering‑related documentation.
  • Collaborate cross‑functionally across functional Engineering Teams, Operations, Supply Chain, Finance, to support business needs, including active communication with suppliers.
  • Support hands‑on composites fabrication, mechanical testing of composites, thermal analysis and materials characterization, chemical analysis, qualification testing, and more. 

We offer flexible work arrangements, 9/80 work schedule with every other Friday off, phenomenal learning opportunities, exposure to a wide variety of projects and customers, and a very friendly team environment. We are looking for self-motivated, proactive, and goal-oriented people to help us grow our services and become even better at what we do.

BASIC QUALIFICATIONS:

This requisition may be filled at either a Materials and Processes Engineer level or a Sr. Materials and Processes Engineer level based on the qualifications below.

Materials and Processes Engineer

  • Bachelor's Degree in STEM with 2 Years of related engineering experience ; Masters Degree in STEM
  • Familiarity with composites and related materials (coatings, resins, epoxy or polyimide adhesives, prepregs, fabrics, etc.)
  • Familiarity with polymeric materials or composite test method and process development, and qualification
  • Ability to prioritize and divide time between office, laboratory, and production floor wherever demand is required to achieve outcomes
  • Ability to obtain and maintain a DOD Secret level clearance, including Special Access Program within a reasonable period, as determined by the company to meet its business needs

Principal Materials and Processes Engineer

  • Bachelor's Degree in STEM with 5 Years of related engineering experience ; Masters Degree in STEM with 3 Years of related engineering experience ; PhD
  • Familiarity with composites and related materials (coatings, resins, epoxy or polyimide adhesives, prepregs, fabrics, etc.)
  • Familiarity with polymeric materials or composite test method and process development, and qualification
  • Ability to prioritize and divide time between office, laboratory, and production floor wherever demand is required to achieve outcomes
  • Ability to obtain and maintain a DOD Secret level clearance, including Special Access Program within a reasonable period, as determined by the company to meet its business needs
