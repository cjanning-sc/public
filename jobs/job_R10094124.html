Northrop Grumman Mission Systems Sector (NGMS), Systems Engineering Group, Bethpage, NY is seeking a Radar Systems Engineer to fill roles on multiple radar system programs. As the lead developer of radars for some of the most advanced systems in the U.S. arsenal, NGMS is a world leader in advanced radar systems and design. These include radars for the F-22, Joint Strike Fighter, F-16, B-1B, AWACS, MESA, G/ATOR and UAV systems such as Global Hawk.
 

Position Summary / Responsibilities

Radar Systems Engineers are responsible for the development of sensor system modes and advanced signal- and data-processing algorithms, from mathematical/analytical derivation through testing and verification using simulated and real sensor data. Included are topics such as sensor resource management, mode design, machine learning, multiple hypothesis tracking and statistical analysis of system performance.

  • Develop system specifications derived from customer requirements
  • Design documentation and review, reporting on progress, and presentations before management, colleagues, and customers
  • Some travel will be required for meetings and program support, as well as test and evaluation of system performance during deployment
  • May be involved with proposal writing
     

Basic Qualifications

The position may be filled at either the Principal or Senior Principal level. Degree should be in Electrical Engineering, Physics, Mathematics, or a related discipline

  • Level T03 / Principal Engineer Systems qualifications:  5 Years with Bachelor’s in science; 3 Years with Masters; 0 Years with PhD.
  • Level T04 / Sr. Principal Engineer Systems qualifications:  9 Years with Bachelor’s in science; 7 Years with Masters; 4 Years with PhD.
  • Proficiency with engineering programming languages (e.g., C/C++, MATLAB, Python)
  • Microsoft Office suite (i.e., Word, PowerPoint, Excel).
  • Demonstrated analytic problem-solving skills
  • The ability to proficiently use computer tools to code algorithms and process data
  • Excellent communication skills - both written and verbal
  • Current active DoD Secret clearance, or the ability to obtain a Secret Clearance before hire
     

Preferred Qualifications

  • Background in digital-signal processing, spectral estimation, and adaptive signal processing
  • Experience with radar systems, particularly: mode design and development and/or signal and data processing
  • Experience with techniques associated multiple target tracking (e.g., Kalman filtering, multiple hypothesis logic) and multi-sensor data association and fusion
