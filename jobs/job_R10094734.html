Northrop Grumman Program Integration Team (PIT) is seeking a Staff Info Systems Project Manager to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals and supplier partners.  This position is in Roy, UT and will report to the GBSD PIT IDE Infrastructure & Integrations Program Manager.

As a key member of the PIT Integrated Digital Environment (IDE), the MES/ERP Project Manager:

  • Oversees and manages the operational aspects of ongoing projects and serves as liaison between program management and planning, project teams, and line management.

  • Represents the GBSD program requirements to solution architects and support solutions architecting process.

  • Ensures projects are completed on schedule, within budget and meets technical requirements of the assignment.

  • Assesses project issues and develops resolutions to meet productivity, quality, and client-satisfaction goals and objectives.

  • Prepares project execution plan and identifies appropriate technical and business resources needed for the team.

  • Will have knowledge or ability to gain the technical understanding of software/hardware applications and their integration and implementation.

  • Develops mechanisms for monitoring project progress and for intervention and problem solving with project managers, line managers, and clients.

  • Regularly briefing senior leadership on project progress.

  • Oversees and manages the technical and IT related needs of the program and serves as a liaison between the IDE organization and the program teams.

  • Leads and directs one or more project teams engaged in the design, implementation and modification of IT projects that span across functional organizations.

  • Demonstrates knowledge and best practices for IT related technologies and processes used throughout Northrop Grumman and GBSD. Communicate IT related technology detail in a non-technical manner to program IPT leaders and management.

  • Ability to successfully work on and deliver multiple projects simultaneously either independently or within a small team.

Summary of Roles and Responsibilities: 

  • The Project Manager will have knowledge or ability to gain the technical understanding of software/hardware applications and their integration and implementation.

  • The Project Manager is a member of the Agile Team responsible for streamlining the execution of program priorities while maintaining the technical and conceptual integrity of the program infrastructure.

  • Excellent communication skills and a proactive approach to coordinating and standing up the program.

  • Experience leading large classified program application and infrastructure projects.

  • Experience with ERP and MES solutions architecting process and implementation.

  • Familiarization with Database administrator, systems administration and application administrator roles and responsibilities.

  • Problem Solving.

  • Develop technical solutions to complex problems which require the regular use of ingenuity and creativity.

  • Discretion/Latitude.

  • Work is performed under direction as appropriate. Exercises latitude in determining technical objectives of assignment. Completed work is reviewed from a relatively near to short term perspective (e.g., Program Increment or 2 week sprint perspective), for desired results.

  • Guides the successful completion of product development and execution teams and may function in a project leadership role, at times. 

This position’s standard work schedule is a 9/80. The 9/80 schedule allows employees who work a nine-hour day Monday through Thursday to take every other Friday off.
