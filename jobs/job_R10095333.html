Sr Staff Engineer Supplier Quality (Team Lead)
R10095333
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Space System Sector is seeking a Senior Staff Engineer Supplier Quality to serve as the Payload and Ground System Division Supplier Quality Team Lead.

The PGS Lead will collaborate closely with the division Mission Assurance, GSC leadership and Supplier Quality across Space Sector to provide strategic thought leadership and preventive solutions to ensure the quality of procured material and services. Qualified candidates will have demonstrated success leading broad organization changes within cost and schedule while achieving internal customer expectations and focusing on organizational and team success.

The PGS Lead will acts as advisor to management, co-workers and customers on advanced technical research studies and applications across the PGS Business Units; and provide technical leadership, direction and mentoring to other employees.

The position requires a solution-based orientation, unwavering ethics and values, and the demonstrated ability to be a trusted partner.

This role can be filled out of one of five Northrop Grumman locations:

1.       Linthicum, MD

2.       Aurora, CO

3.       Redondo Beach, CA

4.       Azusa, CA

5.       Colorado Springs, CO

Roles and responsibilities include:

Oversight of all programs and proposals within the PGS Division. This may be through a lead directly working a program, or oversite of a junior engineer assigned to directly work the program (in full or in part), and assignment of ad hoc tasks in support.

  • Assuring first time supplier quality, contractual and regulatory compliance.  
    • Develop, monitor, and control of BU Approved Supplier List supply base.
    • Identifying procurement and supplier risk and planning for risk-commensurate mitigation activities such as readiness reviews, product inspections and SDRL outputs
    • Creation and flowdown of appropriate and achievable technical, contractual, and quality requirements (drawing, SOWs, q-notes),
    • Verification of product outcomes requirements through FAI and data reviews
    • Assessment of risks for ATSNMs
    • Ensuring compliance of supplier activities to PrOP
    • Training or awareness of other job functions in supplier quality activities required by PrOP or contractual obligations.
    • Lead Sentinel/GIDEP usage and impact analyses for named suppliers.
    • Ensuring resourcing for program support (resource assignment, hours estimations)
       
  • Mitigating the impact of supplier poor quality. 
    • Determination of compliance status versus requirements (product and documentation)
    • Evaluation of technical impacts of defects to product integrity and program schedule (material review board)
    • Working with supplier and program to implement containment, correction, and work around actions to support program schedule while maintain product integrity.
       
  • Eliminating the recurrence of poor supplier quality through rigorous corrective action process. 
    • Continual engagement with higher risk/problematic suppliers, the Supplier Quality field team, program office and global supply chain
    • Ensuring capture and validity of metrics, ensuring feedback is provided (QAF, SAP scorecards, SAMS)
    • Leading RCCA (Supplier Corrective Action Requests) analysis of supplier quality issues and improvement supplier quality processes
    • Leading overall monthly supplier quality and risk mitigation reporting for BU PCAB
    • Providing input to audit planning based on performance.
    • Adjusting procurement and supplier risk mitigation activity based on performance.

  • Develop and maintain best practices and operational efficiency for PGS
    • Serve as a spokesperson for PGS at PCAB
    • Develop and deliver trainings for the division supplier quality team.
    • Assist in establishing strategy to improve supplier performance and ensure supplier quality, regularly communicating program status.
    • Use data-driven processes to measure supplier quality performance, mitigation of risk and to lead continuous improvement processes.

Basic Qualifications

  • Bachelor's + 20 years of exp; Master's + 18 years of exp: PhD + 15 years of exp.
  • Solid written and verbal interpersonal, communication skills and confidence to brief senior management.
  • Demonstrated ability to formally present technical solutions to senior leader.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Degree in Mechanical, Electrical, or related engineering
  • Demonstrated ability to work without oversight and in a multi-discipline team environment, providing insight and solutions to complicated issues both technical and procedural.
  • Extensive experience with leading process improvement projects
  • Active DoD Secret or Top-Secret Clearance
