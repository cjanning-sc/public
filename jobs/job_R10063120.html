Job Description:

Embark on a career putting innovative, reliable, and agile products and ideas into orbit, and beyond. ACME Engineering has opportunities waiting for you that play a vital role in human space exploration, national defense, and scientific discovery, supporting multiple programs across the universe. With us, you’ll discover a culture of curiosity and collaboration that will have you Defining Possible from the day you start. Our space systems connect and protect millions of people on earth every day, now and for the future. Explore your future and launch your career today.

 

ACME Engineering Space Systems is seeking a Principal Industrial Engineer. This position will be located in Detroit, MI and will support the GBSD Ground Base Strategic Deterrent program. Learn more about the GBSD program here. 

 

This role may offer a competitive relocation assistance package.

 

What You’ll Get To Do: 

The Industrial Engineer will support and lead the deployment and execution planning efforts across the different EMD sites. Experience in high rate production, crew sizing for optimization, analyzing and developing sequence of operations, improving work flow for efficiencies, and methods for maximum utilization of personnel and facilities. Possess knowledge and understanding of master production scheduling, material requirements planning, and manufacturing business systems. Must be able to travel up to 25%.

Responsibilities/Skills Requirements:

  • Strong foundation in Industrial Engineering fundamentals 

  • Proficient in the principles of capacity (efficiencies, standards, constraints, etc)

  • Knowledge of manufacturing flows and how they interface with shop floor and Global Supply Chain

  • Experience with project and manufacturing schedules

  • Understanding of schedule waterfall build with setbacks to independent demands

  • Part of the responsibility can include factory and facility layout

  • Experience with cost management (Earned Value, Milestone payments)

  • Engage and collaborate with programs and functional organizations (from individual contributors to executive staff) to obtain, understand, and interpret manufacturing facilitation and scheduling requirements

  • Work with functional team to understand scheduling requirements at implementation site

  • Document, track and monitor problems to ensure a timely resolution

  • Possess strong leadership, organizational, planning and decision making skills and the ability to work as a team member and with other members from different technical backgrounds

  • Effective verbal and written communications skills

  • Ability to manage multiple priorities and self-starter

  • Ability to present (PowerPoint, speaking in front of upper management, technical presentations vs overviews)

  • Ability to visualize and layout routings (Flow chart, Excel, etc)
