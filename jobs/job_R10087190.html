Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems sector is currently hiring a Structural Analysis Engineering Manager 2 (Section Manager) to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This leadership role is located in Redondo Beach, CA.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Manage the functional engineering activities of approximately 15 Structural Analysis Engineers.
  • In addition to functional Section Manager duties, the candidate will perform IPT engineering tasks, which will comprise most of the statement of work. Note: Internal candidates aligned to Vehicle Engineering will typically continue to perform their existing IPT engineering tasks.
  • Product integrity, process adherence, staffing, training, and tools.
  • Process oversight, participation in the performance review process, management of personnel records and participation in corrective or disciplinary actions.
  • Staffing responsibilities may include hiring, recruiting of personnel, and working to ensure that engineers with the appropriate skills and experience are deployed.
  • Manage training opportunities and budget allocations, as well as participation in tool, process, and metric initiatives.
  • Team engagement is a key focus.
  • Mentor, provide career guidance and coaching, and leading others.
  • Travel to other Northrop Grumman and/or customer sites as required.
  • Use excellent verbal and written communication skills.

As a leader, you always act with integrity, and the highest ethical standards characterize everything you do. You enable your team to achieve predictable and balanced results, while satisfying the needs of internal and external partners. You treat all people with respect, and you model personal growth and continuous development. You know how to build an inclusive environment that attracts, retains, and inspires a diverse and engaged team. You develop trusted and valued customer relationships and communicate effectively, so that people throughout the organization feel engaged and connected to their work. You deliver excellence, strive for continuous improvement, and respond vigorously to change.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor of Science degree in a STEM discipline AND 9 years of professional/military engineering experience, OR a Master’s degree in a STEM discipline AND 7 years of professional/military engineering experience.
  • Minimum 7 years of applicable Stress/Structural Analysis experience working on military or government aircraft projects.
  • Must be able to obtain and maintain a DoD Secret Clearance. This clearance must be obtained within a reasonable amount of time as determined by the company to meet its business needs.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Demonstrated technical leadership and management experience.
  • Nine (9) years of applicable Stress/Structural Analysis experience working on military or government aircraft projects.
  • Excellent communication skills and ability to effectively interact with the customer and program management.
  • Proficiency with MS Office software (Excel, Word, PowerPoint).
  • Experience with CAD tools, such as CATIA.
  • Active Secret clearance
