The F-18 E/F Aircraft Mechanic will be responsible for Organizational Level (“O” level) support of the aircraft’s Engine (F414-GE-400) and General Aircraft Mechanic duties.  To include:

  • Understanding pre / post flight activities and inspections
  • Ability to service aircraft with engine oil and hydraulics
  • Able to perform aircraft jacking evolutions
  • Understand engine fuel systems
  • erforming maintenance operations on F-18 aircraft, aircraft equipment, engines, components, and systems in accordance all applicable directives.
  • Ability to read and interpret schematics, assembly drawings, process specifications, technical manuals, written instructions, test procedures, etc.
  • Able to setup and use pneumatic tools such as screw guns, etc.
  • Able to setup and use a torque wrench and use common hand tools such as: wrenches, safety wire pliers, etc.
  • Ability to remove and replace panels, floorboards, insulation and sealing compounds, by means of all type of mechanical fasteners.
  • Able to remove, replace, check out aircraft components and assemblies
  • Understand hydraulics; to include primary, secondary, and emergency systems.

The successful candidate will demonstrate comprehensive knowledge of all Aircraft engine and mechanical (e.g., landing gear, wheels, brakes, etc.) systems aboard the aircraft and will be tasked to resolve technical problems encountered.  You may also be called upon to also provide technical advice, training, and troubleshooting assistance to “O” Level technicians.

You should be familiar with IETM’s, as well as the NAMP 4790 and applicable NAVAIR technical publications.  As required, you may be required to generate written reports for management in support of any contractual obligations.

Basic Qualifications:

Should have at least 3 years of F/A-18 E/F experience and familiarity with F/A-18 inspections, servicing, troubleshooting, removals, and repairs.

Must have and maintain an active secret DOD clearance

Must have comprehensive written and verbal English communication skills.

Must have the ability to work flexible schedules to accommodate customer maintenance requirements.

Must be able to clearly document maintenance and repair actions, equipment status tags, logs, parts requests, etc. 

Must be able to perform assigned duties while wearing required safety equipment.

Will be required to comply with all rules and regulations concerning health and safety, tool control, FOD control, and all training and certification requirements.

Preferred Qualifications:

Strong preference to candidates who have supported U.S. Navy F-18 squadrons on deployment.

A & P License
