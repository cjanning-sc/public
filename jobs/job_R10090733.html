Northrop Grumman Mission Systems is seeking a Manager, General Manufacturing 2 for 1st shift to join as the Cell Manager for the Microelectronics manufacturing cell within the Shop Operations Organization. This position is located in Rolling Meadows, Illinois.

What You'll get to Do:

The General Manufacturing Manager 2 is responsible for the direct

supervision of employees and processes in a manufacturing environment. The Manager provides direction to employees utilizing management guidelines and general policies.

Overall responsibilities include:

  • Driving employee efficiency.
  • Meeting product cost and schedule targets.
  • Managing the area to produce quality products.
  • Engaging employees to achieve their highest potential.

This Manager will be responsible for the direct supervision of approximately 3 Exempt Managers, and the indirect supervision of approximately 60 Non-Exempt touch labor employees.

The Manager is expected to work independently across organizations; manage the use of consumables to achieve budget targets; assign and manage employees to maximize efficiency and achieve target productive yields; implement process improvements to improve affordability, quality, and schedule; and perform required SAP/MES transactions to manage Operators/Finishers.

Other Responsibilities include:

  • Provide direction for employees utilizing management guidelines and general policies for with direct supervision of employees in a manufacturing environment across multiple shifts
  • Issues all job assignments and creates reports of any hardware discrepancies to various support group personnel in a timely manner, meeting quality and cost goals
  • Work independently across organizations
  • Manage the use of consumables to achieve budget target
  • Support calibration process for equipment (POC)
  • Assign and manage staff to maximize efficiency and achieve target productive yields
  • Responsible for driving the team to achieve monthly delivery objectives for the cell, meeting scrap rates, reporting on training requirements
  • Support ISO, LEAN, World class manufacturing practices
  • Ability to document and standardize tasks and efforts
  • Implement process improvements to improve affordability, quality, and schedule
  • Perform required SAP/MES transactions to manage personnel
  • Support time input and Labor relations regarding policies and procedures
  • validate work instruction and machine process documentation are up to date and followed
  • Ensure that equipment and work area remain in a clean and orderly condition.
  • Position is onsite only

  • Bachelor's degree plus 5+ years of relevant experience; Master's degree with 3+ years of experience; Relevant management experience will be considered
  • At least 3+ years of experience in management or leadership of people and/or processes
  • At least 3+ years of manufacturing or production or engineering experience.
  • Understanding of manufacturing and production concepts
  • Strong communication, presentation, and analytical skills
  • Must be able to obtain and maintain a Secret security clearance (US citizenship required)

Preferred Qualifications:
