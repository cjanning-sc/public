Mission Assurance Director 1 - Space Components and Strategic Businesses
R10094855
Days (United States of America)

An organization within Northrop Grumman Space Systems' Payload and Ground Systems (PGS) division, the Space Components and Strategic Businesses (SC&SB) Business Unit (BU) seeks a Mission Assurance Director 1 to join our diverse team of professionals.

The Director of Mission Assurance will lead a team responsible for ensuring the proper implementation of mission assurance, EHS and Training principles and processes for the SC&SB BU. This position may be located at any SC&SB BU facility in California (San Diego, Commerce, Goleta, or Oxnard). Functional responsibilities include Quality Engineering and Inspection, Supplier Quality Engineering, EHS, and Training.

Primary responsibilities include:

  • Establish the overall strategies, objectives, and long-term goals of the Mission Assurance functions within the Business Unit.
  • Provide leadership that fosters an inclusive, motivated and engaged work culture. Driving the continuous improvement of people (to include training & development), products, processes, systems and services through the application of innovative technologies, lean principles, six-sigma projects, common best practices and other appropriate tools and techniques.
  • Meet or exceed the Safety, Quality, Employee, Delivery and Cost expectations for internal and external customers. Staffing the function in alignment with customer needs.
  • Develop, maintain, and ensure compliance with mission assurance and EHS programs, policies, processes, procedures and that the performance and quality of products/processes conform to established standards, specifications and agency guidelines.
  • Maintain and continuous improvement of the Quality Management System(s) including AS9100 and NADCAP certifications across the Business Unit.  This also includes leadership and execution of our Corrective Action and Material Review Boards and internal audit processes
  • Lead Supplier Quality Management and team of Supplier Quality Engineers partnering with Global Supply Chain to improve supplier performance through the Business Unit Procurement Quality Board, PQB at each Operating Unit and creating a support request system, turnback tracker, and observation logs in accordance with NGC’s Aggregated Approved Supplier List (AASL)
  • Support programs by assuring engineering and manufacturing products and processes conform to internal and customer requirements.
  • Enhance Training function capability and support to the business and create consistent monitoring and maintenance of multiple systems
  • Lead teams of product quality specialists servicing customers and EHS professionals ensuring the safety of our employees and our environment.  Including the case management of COVID-19 impacts and reporting requirements
  • Maintains strong customer relationships and acts as a liaison with customers to understand and improve business unit quality performance partnerships.
  • Develops, implements, and continuously improves upon safety and mission assurance processes and standards within the business unit and in support of the Payload and Ground Systems Division.
  • Ensures the flightworthiness of products relative to physical and functional requirements.  Ensures review of product and process data to identify weaknesses in processes and appropriate actions taken to mitigate weaknesses.
  • Leads root cause and corrective action process (RCCA) and analysis on failure investigations for high impact issues and lead personnel in use of RCCA for low impact level issues. Analysis of data to assist teams with focus areas for solutions to problems/opportunities

As a leader, you always act with integrity, and the highest ethical standards characterize everything you do. You enable your team to achieve predictable and balanced results, while satisfying the needs of internal and external partners. You treat all people with respect, and model personal growth and continuous development. You know how to build an inclusive environment that attracts, retains, and inspires a diverse and engaged team. You create trusted and valued customer relationships and communicate effectively, so that people throughout the organization feel engaged and connected to their work. You deliver excellence, strive for continuous improvement and respond vigorously to change. You ensure that your team has the information and tools needed to perform at their best. You demonstrate our committed pursuit of applying innovation to meet the requirements of our customer. You create and execute strategies that result in sustainable value creation that delivers measurable results.

Basic Qualifications:

 

  • 10 years of relevant experience with a Bachelor’s degree
  • Aerospace and Defense industry experience
  • Management/leadership experience (direct-reports or via projects/teams)
  • Quality or Mission Assurance experience working all phases of a product life cycle
  • Demonstrated ability to effectively lead across multiple organizations and disciplines
  • Demonstrated skill interpreting and presenting data to influence business outcomes

Preferred Qualifications:

 

  • Extensive leadership experience, preferably in Quality or Mission Assurance within the Aerospace and Defense industry
  • Demonstrated ability to translate strategy into business action plans and successfully execute
  • Demonstrated ability to manage multiple functions across geographically dispersed sites
  • Demonstrated ability to develop managers
  • Demonstrated superior customer relationship skills
  • Proficiency in an ERP, CAD/PLM systems
  • Experience with ISO 9001/AS9100 based Quality Management Systems and Certifications  
  • Lean/Six Sigma experience
  • Experience conducting or managing internal audits
  • Master’s degree in STEM discipline or Business Administration
