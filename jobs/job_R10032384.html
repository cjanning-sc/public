Senior Principal Microelectronics Product Engineer

1st Shift (United States of America)

As a Senior Principal Microelectronics Product Engineer at Northrop Grumman you will have a challenging and rewarding opportunity to be a part of our Enterprise-wide digital transformation. Through the use of Model-based Engineering, DevSecOps and Agile practices we continue to evolve how we deliver critical national defense products and capabilities for the warfighter. Our success is grounded in our ability to embrace change, move quickly and continuously drive innovation. The successful candidate will be collaborative, open, transparent, and team-oriented with a focus on team empowerment & shared responsibility, flexibility, continuous learning, and a culture of automation.

Northrop Grumman Mission Systems in Baltimore, Maryland is seeking a Staff Microelectronics Product Engineer for our Advanced Technology Laboratory (ATL). The qualified candidate will have a general knowledge of circuit design, fabrication, test, and qualification of microelectronics. Primary duties involve ensuring timely delivery of products, performing electrical data analysis and yield monitoring to support the development and production of internally fabricated semiconducting integrated circuits. Responsibilities also include supporting test and assembly process flows, maintaining and refining specifications, and communicating status to program management. Candidate will join a product engineering team working closely with multiple ATL programs and area managers to achieve on-time delivery of products.

As a Product Engineer you will interface with both peers and representatives from design, assembly, test, inspection, product integration, and program management. You will work to summarize program status, drive root cause analysis, identify areas for improvement, and coordinate efforts to drive them to completion. Candidates must have an aptitude for data manipulation and analysis via tools such as JMP and/or custom programming via languages such as Python. He/she must also have the ability to communicate with employees at all levels in the organization, lead engineering teams, and be able to demonstrate excellent problem solving and critical thinking skills. #NGATL

Basic Qualifications:

- Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering, Physics, Materials Engineering or other closely related technical field with 9 years of relevant experience (7 years with an MS; 4 years with a PhD)

- Experience in the design, fabrication, and test of semiconductor devices

- Experience with data analysis using tools such as JMP, SAS, R, Tableau, Python, or other data analysis software

Preferred Qualifications:

- Device qualification & reliability testing for Space applications

- Deep understanding of analog, digital and RF device test and characterization techniques

- Understanding of statistics and process control

- Yield enhancement or manufacturing experience including six sigma disciplines
