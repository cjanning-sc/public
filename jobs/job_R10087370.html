Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems sector is seeking a Quality Analyst to join our team of qualified and diverse individuals at our El Segundo, California facility where technology and teamwork come together. The qualified applicant will become part of Northrop Grumman's Mission Assurance (MA) organization.

The successful candidate will:

  • Develop and implement program quality plans, processes, and procedures using statistical quality control statistics, lean manufacturing concepts, and six-sigma tools and analyses.
  • Ensure that performance and quality products conform to established company and regulatory standards
  • Review, analyze and report on quality discrepancies related to assembly, process, mechanical, electrical and electro-mechanical systems
  • Collaborate with engineering and manufacturing functions to ensure quality standards are in place
  • Participation in Risk Management process and support to team in identification of risks
  • Corrective action review and reporting to include monitoring status of corrective and preventive actions and tracking through closure, reviewing/analyzing defect data, monitoring test problem report data and closure status, monitoring causal analysis and resolution
  • Supports hardware control to Bill of Materials (BOM), First Article Inspections, close-out and delivery inspections and control of end item data packages
  • Interface with manufacturing, engineering, customer, supplier and subcontractor representatives to ensure compliance with requirements.
  • Maintain liaison with manufacturing, engineering, material management, supplier, and customer personnel to resolve quality problems.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Must have a Bachelor’s degree 3 years of related professional/military experience that includes a background in aircraft, aerospace, or manufacturing industries OR a Master’s degree AND 0 years of related professional/military experience that includes a background in aircraft, aerospace, or manufacturing industries or 6 years of related professional/military experience that includes a background in aircraft, aerospace, or manufacturing industries in Lieu of a Degree
  • Ability to obtain a DoD Secret clearance.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Active Secret DoD clearance
  • Experience in utilizing Agile methodology
  • Strong orientation to change in a dynamic and logistically challenging environment
  • Advanced level of understanding of complex quality policies, procedures, and governmental regulations
  • Ability to analyze alternative courses of action where decisions have risk/reward implications for program or area of influence
  • Proficiency in root cause and corrective action processes
  • A demonstrated ability to communicate effectively, globally, and to all levels of any organization
