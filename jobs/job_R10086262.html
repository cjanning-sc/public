We are looking for you to join our team as a Machinery Design Engineering Manager 2. In this role at Northrop Grumman, you will have a challenging and rewarding opportunity to be a part of our Enterprise-wide digital transformation.

Through the use of Model-based Engineering, DevSecOps and Agile practices we continue to evolve how we deliver critical national defense products and capabilities for the warfighter.  Our success is grounded in our ability to embrace change, move quickly and continuously drive innovation.  The successful candidate will be collaborative, open, transparent, and team-oriented with a focus on team empowerment & shared responsibility, flexibility, continuous learning, and a culture of automation.

What you’ll get to do:

The Machinery Design Engineering Manager 2 is responsible for providing functional leadership and technical direction to Machinery Research and Development Design team, a mechanical design group which consists of a staff of approximately 10 to 12 Mechanical engineers. Responsibilities include leading a diverse team of mechanical engineers in the areas of Mechanical design and development of the hardware systems for the U.S. Navy. The position responsibilities also include management of time keeping, performance reviews, staffing and resource planning, training, and team engagement. The ideal candidate will have demonstrated aptitude in areas of design of fabricated and machined components, rotating components such as gears, bearings and coupling, system integration, load path and stress analysis, and deflection analysis.

Responsibilities include but are not limited to:

  • Provide project and technical oversight of research and development design activities performed by the group
  • Prepare cost estimates, provide proposal inputs, and review proposal content for both internal and external customers
  • Increasing machinery design engineering competencies through employee development and strategic hiring
  • Balancing employee needs with business goals
  • Provide ownership of and direction to the design processes used in the group
  • Basic understanding of the Design, Manufacturing and Test Integration process
  • Support department initiatives, forecasting and recruiting, technology roadmap creation, and champion continuous process improvements

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor Degree in Mechanical Engineering or a related field with 9 years of engineering experience; OR a Master Degree in Mechanical Engineering or a related field with 7 years of engineering experience; OR a PhD in Mechanical Engineering or a related field with 5 years of engineering experience
  • US Citizenship, with the ability to receive DoD Secret clearance (or higher)
  • Strong fundamentals in Design Engineering
  • Experience in functional/people management
  • Production issues resolution
  • Strong leadership qualities, including excellent communication and collaboration, team engagement, and organizational skills.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Master Degree in Mechanical Engineering and 7 years of engineering or technical management experience
  • Experience as a Technical Lead, Project Manager, or other demonstrated leadership role related to program execution
  • Understanding of launcher system principals of operation and design
  • Model Based Systems Engineering (MBSE), requirements development, or technical project management experience preferred
  • Understanding and knowledge of Agile (SCRUM) techniques and activities.
  • Knowledge of Design Engineering processes, procedures and work instructions required to support both management and engineering tasks
  • Experience with proposals and Earned Value
