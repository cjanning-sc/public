Staff Power Subsystems Engineer - Space / Satellite Systems - Dulles, VA
R10090281
Days (United States of America)

We have an opening for a Power Subsystem Engineer to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals in the Tactical Space organization. This position will be located in Dulles, VA.

This position is an engineer that will support power subsystem applications on space vehicles. In this role you will research, develop, design, and test electrical components, equipment, systems, and networks. You will design electrical equipment, facilities, components, products, and systems for commercial, industrial, and domestic purposes.

Duties & Responsibilities:

  • Analyze and interpret requirements and apply them to the design and development of a power architecture
  • Complete power subsystem trade studies for best application from a technical, schedule and cost
  • Complete power analyses
  • Cross-Collaborate with multiple development projects and team members to reach common solutions
  • Oversee the design and development of power avionics components
  • Support Integration and Test of power avionics at a spacecraft level
  • Support Mission Operations during launch of spacecraft

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s in a STEM degree (ideally Engineering) with 14 years of experience; OR a Master’s with 12 years; OR a PhD with 9 years
  • Experience in design, analyses, and/or application of power management and/or distribution electronic assemblies
  • Experience analyzing system requirements for subsystem application
  • Experience conducting power trades and analyses
  • Extensive knowledge of power architectures
  • US Citizenship with the ability to obtain a Top Secret Clearance

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Active Top Secret clearance
  • Application of power subsystems for spaceflight application
  • Experience designing analog hardware
  • Experience integrating analog hardware at a system level
  • Experience with high voltage power systems

The Northrop Grumman Tactical Space Division is a strategic partner specializing in commercial and classified partnerships with the design, delivery, operation and sustainment of satellites and human spacecraft.  We support science and space exploration through our various partnerships, including NASA’s Artemis program with the goal to return humans to the Moon in 2024 and the TESS (Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite) program that has discovered more than twenty confirmed planets.  Recognized as an industry leader, we also develop highly specialized space and satellite components.
