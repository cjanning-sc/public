Northrop Grumman Space Systems – Launch and Missile Defense Systems has an exciting career opportunity for a Principal Network Engineer - Automation to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located out of Schriever Space Force Base, Colorado Springs, CO.

Position Overview:

The candidate will be a primary Automation Engineer supporting the program responsible for providing technical support and expertise in automation driven engineering. Candidate must have proven experience in developing Cyber-secure Network device configurations (Firewalls, switches, routers). The candidate will also lead automation efforts within the Network development team. Some travel may be required depending on the work to be performed.

Basic Qualifications:

If applicable, please note your updated security clearance and IAT/relevant certifications on your resume.

  • An active Secret clearance is required.
  • 5 Years with Bachelor’s degree in a related field; 9 years’ experience in lieu of a degree.
  • Must possess a DoD 8570 certification at IAT Level II or higher (Security+, GSEC, SCNP, SSCP, CISSP, CISA, GSE, SCNA)
  • Hands on Product Integration experience with complex DoD software systems.
  • Experience installing, configuring and optimizing virtual machines
  • Experience creating automated software deployments using Ansible
  • Experience integrating complex applications on Windows, and Unix/Linux
  • Candidate must have hands on experience with DoD integration methodologies and associated automation tools.
  • Candidate must have basic System Administration experience on Windows and/or RHEL systems.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Industry standard certification in Juniper/Cisco
  • Domain expertise in the user interface, Warfighter interaction, and C2BMC concept of operations
  • Experience with software containers (Docker, Kubrenetes, OpenShift)
  • Basic understanding of network architecture, engineering, design, implementation, testing, network communication protocols, network routing protocols (BGP, OSPF, and EIGRP) and IP network troubleshooting.
  • Basic knowledge of TCP/IP behavior, network communications
