Cable and Harness Technician 1
R10095495
1st Shift

Manufacture cable / harness products. Reads and interprets Work Instructions, schematics, wirelists, and drawings. Ensures quality of the hardware is maintained and company procedures are followed. Ensures work is done with completeness, accuracy, adherence to operation sheets, ISO procedures, schedules, and assign tasks. Provides regular communication to shop leads and Work Center management on status and technical issues.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Minimum of a High School diploma and a minimum of 2 years of related experience.
  • 2 years of related experience Cable harness fabrication and assembly experience, preferably with Militarized style components.
  • Knowledge of tools and equipment to perform required functions.
  • Knowledge of ESD product handling, standard safety procedures and PPE requirements.
  • Ability to read, interpret, understand, apply, and enforce Wire-Lists, Drawings, Work Instructions, schematics, Engineering directives.
  • Basic math skills.
  • Basic use of general hand tools as well as crimp tools.
  • Eager desire to learn on the job.
  • Strong organizational, planning and analytical skills.
  • Must be able to organize to meet deadlines.
  • Reliable transportation, professional appearance and attitude.
  • Must demonstrate effective verbal, written and communication skills.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Previous IPC A-620C training or knowledge.
  • Experience working with program requirements, equipment performance specs, wire connection lists, schematics, wire gauge sizing, connector selection and related cabling and harnessing documentation.
  • Ability to collaborate with Harness Engineering regarding technical issues and design resolutions.
  • Must be computer literate and able to navigate engineering related software.
  • Strong problem-solving skills.
  • Ability to work as a team member.
  • Active Secret Clearance
