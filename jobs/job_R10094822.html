ACME Engineering Test Organization is looking for a well-qualified Sr Principal or Staff Assistant Flight Operations Administrator to join our diverse team. This position will be located in PAX River, MD.

Position Overview:

Assists The Triton Flight Test Operations Manager with administration of: operational procedures (Ground and flight), safety official duties, monthly crew qualification tracking and approval and other duties as required.  Pilots unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) during developmental test and operational flights. Remotely pilots vehicle to determine compliance with specification and operational suitability during engineering tests and flight test/demonstration support through specifically prescribed tests and maneuvers. Defines, plans, organizes and executes complex flight programs and flight test events. Conducts briefings and debriefings, and coordinates with maintenance personnel to analyze and correct vehicle discrepancies. May conduct demonstration flights with customers, government officials or other personnel to exhibit vehicle performance, flying qualities and system capabilities. Typically FAA rated.

This requisition may be filled at the higher grade based on requirements listed below.


Basic Qualifications:

  • For Sr Principal (Level 4 ) consideration: 9 Years with Bachelors in Science; 7 Years with Masters; 4 Years with PhD.
  • For Staff (Level 5 ) consideration: 14 Years with Bachelors in Science; 12 Years with Masters; 9 Years with PhD.
  • Graduate of a recognized Test Pilot School: U.S. Naval Test Pilot School (USNTPS), U.S. Air Force Test Pilot School (USAFTPS), Empire Test Pilot School (ETPS) or National Test Pilot School Long Course (NTPS).
  • Must have a FAA Commercial Pilot Certificate with Instrument Rating and a minimum of 500 flight hours as pilot-in-command of a manned aircraft (fixed or rotary). Previous experience as a military Aircraft Commander with at least 500hrs and eligible for a commercial pilot certificate will be acceptable as commercial certificate for hiring purposes.
  • Current Class II or higher FAA medical certificate. 
  • Must have an active DOD secret clearance and able to upgrade to Top Secret Clearance.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Active Top Secret DoD Clearance.
  • P-3, P-8 or previous experience with ISR platform. Qualified Global Hawk or UAS Pilot.
  • Previous experience as mission planer highly desired.
  • Previous experience as flight safety officer or mishap investigator desired.
  • Experience with JMPS, AFMS desired.
