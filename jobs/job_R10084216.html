We have an opening for an Electronics Test Engineer to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals in the Strategic Space Systems Division (SSSD) organization supporting the Electronics Assembly and Test Department (EA&T). This position will be located in Dayton, CA.

The Test Engineering group in the Electronics Assembly and Test Department (EA&T) is looking for a level 2 or 3 Test Engineer with experience:

  • Working with a cross-disciplinary team (including technicians, design engineers, system engineers, quality engineers, and program management)

  • Writing test procedures

  • Supporting board, slice, and unit level electronic testing of Digital, RF, Optical, and Power Converter products

In this position you will research, design, develop, and test a variety of electronic equipment and systems, including radar and radiation detection equipment in a fast paced manufacturing environment. You will be expected to be a self-starter and work with minimal supervision.

Test Engineers are responsible for:

  • Working with the design team to maximize product testability

  • Providing test flows

  • Reviewing work plans

  • Reviewing test equipment and test set requirements to ensure compatibility with available facilities

  • Identify additional facilities that may be required

  • Identify general support type test equipment and test fixtures that will be needed during production testing

  • Write, review, and approve all test procedures

  • Interface with the ground support department during test set design

During the test phase of production, test engineers are EA&T’s test liaison with systems engineering, design engineering, software engineering, test set support, security, process planning, environmental test (such as vibration and thermal vacuum testing), quality, and program management. Test engineers are responsible for providing technical support for generating procedure change orders, dispositioning test discrepancy reports, and providing on-floor technical support during production.

This position can be filled at level 2 or 3.

Basic Qualifications:

Engineer Electronics

  • Bachelor’s degree in a STEM field and 2 years of experience, OR A Master’s degree, OR a PhD

Principal Engineer Electronics

  • Bachelor’s degree in a STEM field and 5 years of experience, OR A Master’s degree with 3 years of experience, or a PhD

Preferred Qualification:

  • Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering

  • Working with electronic test equipment such as oscilloscopes, network analyzers, and signal generators, etc.

  • Working in a hardware manufacturing environment

  • Supporting flight hardware troubleshooting, and reviewing test data

  • Familiar with different product types (i.e. Digital, RF, Power, Optics, etc.)
