MMIC RFIC Design Engineer
United States of America

The MMIC RFIC Design Engineer position serves as an expert for Northrop Grumman’s MMIC and RFIC product development. You will be joining an industry leading, technically broad, foundry independent design team generating products for cutting edge systems and applications.

Position Responsibilities:

The selected candidate will support design engineering on RF/microwave highly integrated circuit design and architecture including requirements derivation, schematic capture, schematic simulation, circuit layout, electro-magnetic simulation, verification (DRC and LVS), and device test and characterization.

Typical designs include:

- Low noise amplifiers (LNA)

- Power amplifiers (PA)

- Mixers

- Switches

- Phase shifters

- Attenuators

- Filters


Qualifications:

This position may be filled at the T03 Principal Engineer, or T04 Sr. Principal Engineer level.

T03 Level requires 5 Years with Bachelor's in science; 3 Years with Masters; 0 Years with PhD.

T04 Level requires 9 Years with Bachelor's in science; 7 Years with Masters; 4 Years with PhD.

  • Proficient with microwave design tools (ADS/Momentum, AWR Microwave Office, Cadence Virtuoso)

  • Knowledge and experience with design, packaging, test, and troubleshooting of RF/microwave circuits

  • Experience working in a team environment and have the ability to lead a diverse and talented team of engineers

  • Experience with technologies will include GaAs, GaN, InP, and SiGe.

    Ability to perform RF front-end architecture development, system requirement derivation and decomposition to IC level is a plus

  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain a Secret clearance per business requirements. U.S. Citizenship is a prerequisite

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Expertise including multiple patents/disclosures/relevant technical awards or external publications/presentations in the field of RF/microwave circuit design
  • Proficiency with design CAD Tools such as Cadence Virtuoso and Spectre RF, ADS Suite, Mentor Calibre, and Verification Tools
  • Experience designing in SiGe technologies is a plus.
  • Active Secret Clearance
