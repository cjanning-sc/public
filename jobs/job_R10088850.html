Sr Principal Systems Safety Engineer
R10088850
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Mission Systems Sector is seeking a Systems Safety Engineer 4 to join our team in Huntsville, AL. The qualified applicant will become part of Northrop Grumman’s System Safety Engineering Team. This position is responsible for reviewing, establishing, and implementing safety requirements associated with support equipment, facilities, operations, and products. The position requires experience in the development, implementation and maintenance of a System Safety Program. This includes the identification and evaluation of system safety and health hazards, definition of risk levels, and management of the probability and severity of all hazards associated with installation/integration, use, and disposal of hardware. This position will develop and maintain all necessary documentation necessary to implement and maintain a System Safety Program.

The successful candidate will be able to independently develop Safety documents, such as System Safety Program Safety Assessments Reports (SAR); perform audits; and report the effectiveness of the system safety program. The individual will work closely with the Huntsville Health and Safety Sector manager to ensure all requirements align with Northrop Grumman corporate policies. Metric reports will be provided as necessary. The candidate will participate in accident investigations involving damage to products or related facilities, and harm to personnel. The individual will be responsible for assisting in the identification of the causes and the corrective actions necessary to prevent re-occurrence. The candidate will develop System Safety Program Safety Assessments Reports (SAR) as required. The individual will possess the skills to develop implement and maintain a Hazardous Material Management Program.

Basic Qualifications:

To be considered for this position, the candidate must minimally meet the knowledge, skills, and abilities listed below.

  • DOD security clearance
  • Systems Safety Engineer 4: requires 9 years with Bachelor of Science; 7 Years with Masters; 4 Years with PhD. Degree in engineering, computer science, mathematics, or physics.
  • Demonstrated experience in one or more systems safety engineering job function as described above.
  • Develop analysis products in support of the Safety Assessment Report (SAR) including a Functional Hazard Analysis (FHA).
  • Analyze requirements at all levels within the system to identify or create safety critical requirements and assign appropriate criticality index and level of rigor to HW/SW requirements.
  • Ability to generate MIL-STD-882 type analyses
  • Experience documenting hazard analysis and test results in a Hazard Tracking System
  • Experience developing test cases to satisfy safety requirements
  • Experience reviewing hardware engineering drawings for safety
  • Experience documenting hazardous materials as part of a hazmat program
  • Strong communication and interpersonal skills

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Missile Defense experience
  • Experience reviewing code for application of safety best coding practices
  • Experience performing functions such as installing, repairing, inspecting, overhauling, adjusting, aligning, rigging, and calibrating military structures, systems, and components preferred.
  • Experience with the following: Mil-Std 882 System Safety; Mil-Std 1432 Human Factors; Mil Hdbk 454 General Guideline for Electronic Equipment; OSHA Health Standard; MIL STD 1808; AMCOM Policy 385-17.
  • 3+ years using model-based approaches and tools with experience in UML and DoDAF.
