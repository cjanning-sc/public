Northrop Grumman Mission Systems is seeking a Manufacturing Engineer to join its Lifecycle Sustainment Operations (LCSO) team within the Manufacturing Organization.  This team member will support our Industrial Engineering platform as well.  This position is located in Elkridge, Maryland.

The LCSO team supports electronic testing and repair operations for multiple programs for both DoD and foreign military customers. The LCSO executes low volume production; typically, production of spare LRUs, prototype manufacturing; developing one-off components, systems, or test sets to support engineering development, and full systems integration for products.

Descriptions

  • Designs manufacturing processes, procedures and production layouts for assemblies, equipment installation, processing, machining, and material handling.
  • Develop process documentation for new and existing systems’ Bonding, Mechanical Fabrication, Coating, and Additive Manufacturing
  • Work with the chief engineer to develop repair procedures
  • Evaluate incoming production for feasibility and process definition
  • Define fabrication process based on component features, materials, and dimensions
  • Create and maintain work instructions
  • Designs sequence of operations and specifies procedures for the fabrication of tools and equipment and other functions that affect product performance.
  • Develop equipment maintenance and provide an interface to BWI for equipment maintenance
  • Assist with the maintenance of the environmental test equipment
  • Work with the Industrial Engineering group to support routing development and other IE activities
  • Support ongoing expansion of LCSO; multiple moves and build outs, provide an interface to facilities
  • Designs arrangement of machines within plant facilities to ensure most efficient and productive layout.
  • Providing supplier support, as needed, to resolve issues within supply base
  • Providing Request for Proposal (RFP) input specifically as it relates to Non-Recurring Engineering (NRE) requirement such as tooling, capital equipment and/or new process development

The chosen candidate must be able to multi-task in a fast-paced, dynamic, and high visibility environment; work well in a team environment; and have excellent written and verbal communication skills.  This team member will have a lead Engineer to support training and development. This position may be filled as a Manufacturing Engineer based on the qualifications listed below:

Basic Qualifications for a Manufacturing Engineer: 

  • Bachelor's degree or higher in Engineering or related STEM area. At least 1 year Manufacturing experience is preferred
  • Must have fluency in MS Office software applications
  • Drafting/modeling experience using a CAD software
  • Candidate must be able to obtain a Secret security clearance (U.S. citizenship is a prerequisite)

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Familiarity with J-STD and MIL-STD requirements
  • 3D modeling experience in Unigraphics/NX
  • Machining
  • Experience with the Teamcenter Manufacturing tool suite and Manufacturing Process Planning
  • Familiarity with Statistical Process Control (SPC), and 6 sigma concepts for process development and control
  • Knowledge of Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T)
