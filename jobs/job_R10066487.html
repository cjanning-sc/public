Sr. / Principal Engineer: RF Microwave Design - Space / Satellite Systems (Gilbert, AZ)
R10066487
Any (United States of America)

We have an opening for an RF Microwave Design Engineer to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals in the Tactical Space organization. This position will be located in Gilbert, AZ.

In this role, you will work in a Technical Lead capacity on National Security Systems (NSS) programs. You will focus on the development of space communications systems and concepts through the production of flight programs. As a radio frequency (RF) subsystem lead engineer, you will work in a team environment to define and describe robust RF solutions to challenging applications while working within system constraints on mass, power, and volume. To be successful, you should have strong technical skills, ability to organize and direct the efforts of a multi-disciplined engineering team, and demonstrated motivation to investigate new concepts.

Job Responsibilities:

In particular, you will be involved in:

  • Design and analysis of communications systems for satellite applications. You must be familiar with RF analyses such as link budgets, digital modulation/coding, gain/loss budgets, noise figure, etc.
  • Requirements flow-down from top-level customer documents through subsystem-level definition to component-level specification. You will be expected to produce clear, concisely written documents that will be used as the technical basis for high dollar value procurements of RF equipment such as converters, filters and high power amplifiers
  • Prepare and present design reviews for internal and external customers. You will have regular interaction with the internal engineering and program management team as well as external customers
  • Subcontractor management, oversight of RF component design and development (travel required,) and manufacturing interface definition including performance specification, interface control drawing, and statement of work generation.
  • You will work with potential subcontractors to ensure any technical solution meets the top-level customer's needs while satisfying system and program constraints
  • After transition from concept to production program, you may also support ongoing technical interface with the subcontractors during testing, component manufacturing, as well as integration and test of the communications system including unit-level functional and environmental testing, spacecraft level testing, and on-orbit checkout
  • You should have familiarity with the operation of standard RF test equipment (i.e., spectrum analyzers, power meters, network analyzers)
  • Possess experience in the areas of satellite communications subsystem design, operation, and/or repair and maintenance
  • In this role, you will lead a subsystem design, build/integration, test, and delivery. As the RF lead, you will also be working with the other discipline leads and systems engineering to develop and deliver the spacecraft on time and under budget.

This position can be filled at the Principal (level 3) or Sr Principal (level 4)

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree in a STEM field (ideally Electrical Engineering) with some combination of the following course work: antennas, microwave theory, satellite communications, radio-wave propagation, electromagnetics.
  • Level 3-Minimum of 5 years of related experience (Or 3 years with a Masters; or a PhD)
  • Level 4-Minimum of 9 years of related experience (Or 7 years with a Masters; or 4 years with a PhD)
  • U.S. Citizenship
  • Willingness to obtain Top Secret level clearance

Preferred Qualifications:

  • MSEE a strong plus
  • Ability to work with others from engineering and non-engineering disciplines in a fast-paced team environment
  • Good oral presentation skills and comfort with customer interaction. Ability to write technical documents clearly and concisely
  • Strong skills in Microsoft Office (particularly Excel)
  • Experience with digital modulation/demodulation schemes for high data rates
  • Experience with microwave component/system measurement
  • Familiar with engineering analysis tools such as MATLAB/Simulink and STK
  • Experience leading engineering teams in development or production of RF systems
  • Familiar with cost account management concepts, including cost and labor estimation, task scheduling and earned value tracking

The Northrop Grumman Tactical Space Division is a strategic partner specializing in commercial and classified partnerships with the design, delivery, operation and sustainment of satellites and human spacecraft.  We support science and space exploration through our various partnerships, including NASA’s Artemis program with the goal to return humans to the Moon in 2024 and the TESS (Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite) program that has discovered more than twenty confirmed plants.  Recognized as an industry leader, we also develop highly specialized space and satellite components.
