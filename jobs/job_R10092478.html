Northrop Grumman Space Systems is seeking to add Sr Principal Systems Safety Engineer to its Safety Engineering team in support of the Ground Based Strategic Deterrent (GBSD) program.  Learn more about the GBSD program here.  Northrop Grumman supports the Air Force’s sustainment, development, production and deployment of hardware and system modifications for Intercontinental Ballistic Missile (ICBM) Ground and Airborne Launch Control Systems, Launch Facilities and associated infrastructure.  This position will be located on campus in Roy, UT or Huntsville Alabama and may require on-site support at product manufacturing and integration sites.

What You’ll Get To Do: 

Specific duties to include, but are not limited to the following:

  • The System Safety Engineer is to influence design for meeting safety expectations and requirements for our Northrop Grumman Products.
  • Independently analyze and recommend design features that meet safety criteria in system design to control or eliminate hazards.
  • Active participation in the design of products/systems, associated support equipment and facilities.
  • Creation of functional specifications and establishes safety requirements for assigned systems.
  • Developing safety documents, as required, to support customer and test/operational range requirements and performing audits of operational areas and reporting on the effectiveness of system safety program.
  • Evaluating the interrelationship of system safety requirements and other aspects of concept design and development requirements to ensure the most cost-effective program.
  • Extensive expertise in the application of MIL-STD-882E, software development, design development, or similar systems engineering experience.
  • Generating or review of design artifacts to support System Safety analyses utilized in maturing weapons system design for safety and support flight test and flight worthiness of complex systems.
