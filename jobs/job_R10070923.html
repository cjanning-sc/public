Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems has an opening for a Sr. Staff Pilot Test to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals within our Test and Evaluation organization. This role is located in Melbourne, FL.

The Chief Test Pilot (CTP) is a key contributor to the program and sits on the program leadership team.  The CTP will support the design, development, and certification of the aircraft and air system. The CTP will work closely with other Integrated Product Teams, the Chief Engineer, as well as systems engineers including Safety, Airworthiness, and Human Factors teams to validate and verify aircraft design and performance. The CTP is a key participant in the flight test risk management process. 

Key Responsibilities:

  • Building and sustaining a cadre of skilled test pilots and production pilots to safely execute the flight test program
  • Ensure early and effective contributions to air vehicle design, cockpit/crew station design and Pilot Vehicle Interface areas
  • Support the Chief Engineer on various design trades and analysis
  • Chair the pilot working group
  • Provide flight test leadership in critical program phases and reviews, such as SRR, PDR, CDR, FRR, and TRR
  • Provides direction to engineers on design definition, simulator flights, test planning, test card writing, flight test techniques, test conduct test reporting, and proposal preparation
  • Ensure effective test pilot training, FTE training, MCR training and ensuring proficiency of staff
  • Perform initial flights on prototypes and test articles for new clean sheet air vehicles
  • Plan, develop, and perform flight tests to verify air worthiness, performance capabilities, and mission effectiveness using various simulators, surrogate aircraft, test-bed aircraft, and the program air vehicle. 
  • Partner closely with the Director of Test and Safety to ensure that the test team is conducting safe and effective ground and flight test operations
  • Collaborate with Service test pilots as well as Integrated Test Team personnel
  • Responsible for developing standards and protocols, and ensures lessons learned are communicated with appropriate teams
  • Responsible for process associated with developing and managing the test vehicle pilot handbook and eventual NATOPS manual
  • Other duties as assigned

We offer flexible work arrangements, 9/80 work schedule with every other Friday off, phenomenal learning opportunities, exposure to a wide variety of projects and customers, and a very friendly team environment. We are looking for self-motivated, proactive, and goal-oriented people to help us grow our services and become even better at what we do.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Must have a Bachelor's Degree in Science AND 13 Years of experience OR Master's Degree in Science AND 11 Years of experience OR PhD AND 8 years of experience
  • Must have an active DoD Secret or higher clearance (with a background investigation completed within the last 6 years or currently enrolled into Continuous Evaluation)
  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Top Secret clearance and SCI
  • Must have the ability to obtain and hold an FAA Second Class medical certificate
  • Must have a minimum 1,500 hours tactical aircraft pilot time with 500 hours as Pilot in Command (PIC)
  • Must have previous experience as a test pilot in a developmental test program
  • Must have aircraft Carrier pilot experience (>150 arrested landings)
  • Must have strong leadership skills

Preferred Qualifications:  

  • Weapons School graduate 
  • Master’s degree in a related STEM field
  • Strong communication skills with demonstrated flight test leadership experience
  • Multi-Engine Land and Single Engine Land FAA Commercial pilot license
  • Military Test Pilot School graduate of USNTPS, USAFTPS, Empire Test Pilots School, or EPNER
  • Knowledge and experience with risk management and mitigation
  • Previous experience leading a test organization
