Manager Programs 3 "Deputy Chief" Glide Phase Interceptor
R10095216
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman’s Space Systems sector is looking for a Deputy Chief Engineer for Glide Phase Interceptor (GPI). This leadership role is located in Chandler, Arizona. The Deputy provides leadership to ensure successful development and integration of a multi-stage booster propulsion system with the interceptor, development of avionics, All Up Round testing, lead Flight Test activities, as well as integration of the key interceptor guidance & control, software, and sensor technologies into the overall system, the associated kill-chain, and the models and simulations used to inform design and predict performance.

 

 Responsibilities:

·         Driving technical execution in support of major program events

·         Collaborating within the Office of the Chief Engineer to deliver exceptional customer value

·         Focusing horizontally on resolving high-level Systems challenges

·         Driving technical execution with an emphasis on GPI development and integration

·         Ensuring execution of overall technical plan within the Integrated Master Schedule

·         Driving technical rigor into technical interchange meetings with GPI partners

·         Advising program technical leadership on program, schedule, and technical matters


Basic Qualifications:

·         Bachelor of Science degree in an engineering discipline or related technical field and 9 years of systems engineering experience OR a Master’s degree in an engineering discipline or related technical field and 7 years of systems engineering experience.

·         Technical leadership experience including total lifecycle, integrated product-team, and Systems Engineering Integration and Test (SEIT) leadership.

·         Experience in developing avionics, rocket motor and missile airframes

·         Experience developing planning, training, and technical briefings for wide ranging audiences including technician, engineer, sub-contractor, client, executive leadership.

·         U.S. Citizenship is required

·         Interim or Active Secret Security Clearance is required

·         Ability to travel is required.

·         Ability to work on location.


Preferred Qualifications:

Bachelor of Science Engineering degree plus 14 years of experience is preferred; Master degree plus 12 years of experience is preferred

·         Broad systems development experience to include first-article concept development and operational deployment of new systems.

·         Robust understanding of both the development, operations, and sustainment of missile systems and their interactions with the Aegis Weapons System.

·         Prior experience in leadership as the prime contractor directing and coordinating execution of multiple sub-contractors toward an integrated solution.

·         Experience designing and executing cross-functional trade studies using requirements-based criteria assessments.
