SENIOR I&T PROGRAM LEAD

Northrop Grumman Space Division designs, builds and delivers space, defense and aviation-related systems to customers around the world. Northrop Grumman Tactical Space Systems Division (TSSD) designs, manufactures and operates small and medium-class spacecraft for communications, imaging, scientific, exploration and national security space applications. The Group is a leading supplier of space components that power and enable satellites of all classes, provides technical engineering services to government agencies and laboratories, and is developing exciting new satellite servicing and advanced space flight technologies.


Northrop Grumman Tactical Space Systems Division is seeking to fill multiple I&T Lead Positions in support of the Integration & Test (I&T) Department located in Gilbert, Arizona.

Successful candidates will clearly demonstrate strong leadership skills to achieve a sustainable top performance. She/He will have a track-record as an effective communicator and problem solver who is able to develop and maintain good working relationships with internal and external stakeholders and customers. This candidate will demonstrate a commitment to ethics, integrity, and the capability to innovate while challenging traditional ways of doing business.

Roles & Responsibilities for the Senior I&T Program Lead Role:

  • Responsible for the complete integration and test effort for a spacecraft or product line, plans and sequences all assembly, integration and testing activities to include integration with the launch vehicle
  • Coordination of mechanical, electrical, radio frequency, propulsion, harness and quality assurance resources and activities on a given vehicle/program
  • Coordinates shared facility utilization for Test Planning
  • Develops and maintains the Test and Integration Master Plan
  • Performs short and long term planning and schedule maintenance
  • Serves as the Cost Account Manager for I&T activities on the program
  • Interfaces with our customers, program offices and launch vehicle providers
  • Reviews and approves engineering products to ensure manufacturability
  • Supports all review boards, tiger teams, RCCA investigations, deep dives, and pre-milestone reviews.
  • Serve on and participate in program MRR, PDR, CDR and TRR as required
  • Provides Problem Solving Expertise and capability to mentor the team in problem solving skills
  • Support Metrics for Quality, Schedule, Cost and Productivity for the Test Area. Continuous Process Improvement
  • Mentors and trains Deputy I&T Program leads

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor of Science Degree with 14+ year’s experience.  4 additional years of experience will be considered in lieu of the degree.
  • Minimum of 5-8 years of proven demonstrated leadership skills and the ability to work effectively in a team environment.
  • Experience working within multi-disciplinary organizations
  • Excellent written and oral communications skills and technical presentation experience
  • Familiarity/experience with CAM roles & Responsibilities

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Active Top Secret/SCI clearance
  • Extensive experience within Integration & Test organizations
  • Experience leading cross-functional organizations
  • Demonstrated experience in ERP, MES, Lean Manufacturing and/or 5S type programs
