Northrop Grumman Space Systems is seeking a Embedded Software Engineer. This position will be located in Colorado Springs, Colorado and will support the Ground Based Strategic Deterrent (GBSD) program.  This position may be filled at multiple levels depending on candidate experience and education.

What You’ll Get To Do: 

Northrop Grumman supports the Air Force’s sustainment, development, production and deployment of hardware and system modifications for Intercontinental Ballistic Missile (ICBM) Ground and Airborne Launch Control Systems, Launch Facilities and associated infrastructure.

 The Software Engineer is responsible for developing software for the Command & Launch (C&L) Software Team.  Using DevOps and Agile methodologies you will be constantly optimizing systems and services for security, automation, and performance/availability, while ensuring solutions developed adhere and align to the baseline systems designs, configuration management, and architecture standards. You will work closely in a DevOps environment with the program personnel to ensure smooth execution of continuous integration and test.

This position’s standard work schedule is a 9/80. The 9/80 schedule allows employees who work a nine-hour day Monday through Thursday to take every other Friday off.  

Job Qualifications: 

You’ll Bring These Qualifications:

  • Level 2 Requirements: 2 Years of experience with Bachelors in Science; 0 Years with Masters

  • Level 3 Requirements: 5 Years of experience with Bachelors in Science; 3 Years with Masters; 0 Years with PhD

  • Active DoD Secret clearance with a reinvestigation date occurring in the last 5 years.

  • US Citizenship and ability to obtain Special Program Access (SAP)

  • Direct experience implementing embedded software in real-time systems

  • Experience with low level Operating System drivers, boot-up, and hardware integration

These Qualifications Would be Nice to Have:

  • Candidates will work on software systems primarily in C/C++ development, with a focus on  Real-Time Operating System (RTOS) software design for Single Board Computers (SBC) (e.g., Wind River, Green Hills Software)

  • Experience with Software Development Lifecycle (Requirements, Design, Implementation, Integration, and Test)

  • Experience with C/C++

  • Experience with DO-178C standards and levels

  • Embedded/multi-threaded application programming experience

  • RTOS Board Support Package (BSP) experience

  • Experience with Atlassian tools (Jira, Confluence, etc) for Agile development and continuous integration

  • Experience with Linux (Redhat) (Servers and Workstations)

  • Excellent communications and teamwork skills

  • Implementation experience with command and control systems

  • Automation experience via scripting (e.g. Perl, Python, etc)

  • Experience with SonarQube

  • Experience operating in test lab systems

  • Experience documenting configuration of systems, including troubleshooting approach/methodology

  • Experience with command and control, missile, launch, and/or warning systems

  • Experience with hardware and working closely with hardware engineers

  • Active Top Secret security clearance with adjudication in the last 6 years
