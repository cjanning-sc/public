Northrop Grumman, Mission Systems is seeking a well-qualified Windows / Linux System Administrator for the Engineering and Sciences Organization. The position is located at the Advanced Technology Lab (ATL) in Linthicum, MD where we design, manufacture, and test semiconductor products for internal production and emerging programs. The ideal candidate will have solid experience in Red Hat System administration and Windows system administration, and the ability/desire to automate any admin tasks using Puppet or Nagios tools. Must be comfortable supporting users in a manufacturing environment.

#NGATL


Roles and responsibilities include, but are not limited to:

  • Perform as a Windows/Linux System Administrator for the ATL Foundry infrastructure.
  • Manage and maintain security patches for RHEL 7.x and Windows VMs.
  • Develop and document technical procedures.
  • Read, modify, and create shell script and scripts in various other languages.
  • Learn new technologies on own and work effectively independently, while still coordinating actions as required.
  • Use Nagios XI to monitor application performance and uptime.
  • Implement Puppet modules.
  • Able to partition disks, create logical volumes, create file systems, and mount file systems in Linux environments.
  • Produce system performance metrics for ATL infrastructure.
  • Take ownership of all ATL software applications (install, start-up, stop, upgrade, etc.).
  • Perform monthly security patches on Windows and RHEL operating systems.
  • Be on site at ATL Foundry as needed in support of troubleshooting, testing, and/or deployment activities of local IT assets.
  • Lead actions with peer systems administrators, network engineer, security teams, and software and hardware engineers.
  • Install and configure COTS software (FactoryWorks, APF, SPACE, etc.)
  • Collaborate as needed with software vendors to remedy.
  • Recommend and implement system enhancements that will improve the performance and reliability of the system including installing, upgrading/patching, monitoring, problem resolution, and configuration management.
  • Provide backup and recovery services; manage file systems and disk space; manage virus protection on a routine basis.
  • Create and maintain user and computer accounts and modify file permissions and security access lists.
  • Test new releases of products to ensure compatibility and minimize user impact.
  • Develop and document technical processes and procedures as needed.
  • Make recommendations to purchase hardware, software, system components.
  • Interact, meet, discuss, and troubleshoot issues with vendors; evaluate vendor products, services, and suggestions.
  • Adhere to strict Information Systems security guidelines in all cases.
  • Will be expected to document all tasks in JIRA.
  • Work after hours and weekends as needed.

This requisition may be filled as a Principal Systems Administrator or Senior Systems Administrator

Basic Qualifications for Level 3:

  • Bachelor’s degree and 6 years of experience OR Master's degree and 4 years of experience; 9 years of related experience may be considered in lieu of completed degree.
  • Experience with RedHat Enterprise Linux OS
  •  Experience supporting application in a production environment.

Basic Qualifications for Level 4:

  • Bachelor’s degree and 9 years of experience OR Master's degree and 7 years of experience, OR PhD and 4 years' experience, or 13 years of related experience may be considered in lieu of completed degree.
  • Experience with administration RedHat Enterprise Linux OS 7.x.
  • Experience with administration of all versions of Windows.
  • Experience with RedHat Enterprise Linux OS
  • Experience supporting application in a production environment.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Advanced scripting skills.
  • Red Hat 7.x and/or Windows System Administration certification(s).
  • Solid understanding of system back up and restoration.
  • Experience with Puppet or Ansible.
  • Experience with Nagios.
  • Advanced understanding of Linux/Windows configuration and commands.
  • Understanding of Agile framework.
