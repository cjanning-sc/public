Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems has an opening for an Operations & Sustainment (O&S) Maintenance Modification Manager to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Palmdale, CA with frequent travel.

The Maintenance Modification Manager will lead a small group of employees and will direct all aspects of maintenance modifications and logistics. Must be proficient in reading and following technical manuals, or repair manuals. Must be able to communicate effectively with design engineers and work closely with other aerospace specialties when learning and testing the functionality of new repair procedures or processes.  The Maintenance Modification Manager is expected to lead a team that develops installation procedures, steps, and redlines drawings for newly designed parts or components. Once the newly designed part or component is ready to be installed, the selected individual will be expected to accompany the retrofit and modification team to the field where they will execute the install or repair and provide training or instructions to available technicians for follow on maintenance when required. The candidate must have a deep and diverse mechanical background in aviation systems and sub-systems.

In this role, the selected candidate will be responsible for:

  • Modification management and leadership at various sites on a rotating basis
  • Possible direct customer interface at all operating sites
  • Quality and Mission Assurance
  • Detailed scheduling and execution of maintenance
  • Standardized training of personnel
  • Communication with Program Management and IPT leadership
  • Complex and dynamic problem resolution
  • Strategic maintenance modification plans development

The selected candidate will establish milestones and monitors adherence to master plans and schedules, identifies program problems, and yields solutions. The selected candidate will direct the work of employees assigned to the program from technical, manufacturing, and administrative areas.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor of Science degree in Science, Technology, Engineering, or Math (STEM) and at least 9 years of experience in aviation maintenance and training.
  • Active DOD Secret clearance, within scope.
  • Ability to obtain and maintain Top Secret and Special Program Access (SAP).
  • Must be willing to work evenings, weekends and holidays as needed and up to 30% of travel.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Master of Science degree in the field of aviation.
  • At least 10 years of experience in aviation maintenance, operational planning, execution, and training.
  • Experience managing highly technical projects which have delivered critical/complex hardware and/or capability to customers.
  • Military experience leading tactical and operational teams with increasing levels of responsibility.
  • Experience living and working in OCONUS locations.
  • Willing to work evenings, weekends and holidays as needed and up to 75% of travel.
  • Ability to understand new technologies and help expand new business opportunities.
