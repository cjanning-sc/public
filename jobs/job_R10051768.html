Northrop Grumman Space Systems is seeking a Sr Principal GN&C Product Support Equipment Software Engineer. This position will be located in Roy, Utah and will support the Sentinel (GBSD) program. 

What You’ll Get To Do: 

Specific duties to include, but are not limited to the following:

Support Equipment engineering utilizes a Systems Engineering approach to establish Support Equipment functional and performance requirements.  Support Equipment engineering is involved in all phases of research and product lifecycle development starting with requirements, initial concepts, trade studies, early air vehicle design influence, design and development of the Support Equipment item, hardware-software integration, fabrication/procurement, engineering evaluation testing, verification, delivery of hardware, and support of delivered hardware.  The candidate will use technical principles, theories and concepts and apply them to develop recommended solutions to technical problems.  The ideal candidate will have demonstrated the skill and ability to perform professional tasks in one or more aspects of a product lifecycle, to include coordination between other teammates, supervisors, project leaders and other professionals. Must possess the ability to handle multiple tasks in a dynamic environment, including critical thinking and technical problem-solving skills.

The Support Equipment Software Engineer designs, develops, documents, tests and debugs software for Support Equipment containing logical and mathematical solutions. The engineer conducts multidisciplinary research and collaborates with equipment designers and hardware engineers in the planning, design, development, and utilization of embedded systems for safety and mission critical software.  The engineer ensures program mandated software standards and compliance criteria are met. Understands and can develop in a continuous integration tool chain meeting technical and compliance requirements

This position’s standard work schedule is a 9/80. The 9/80 schedule allows employees who work a nine-hour day Monday through Thursday to take every other Friday off.
