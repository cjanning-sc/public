As one of the largest global security companies in the world, ACME Engineering is proud to help our nation’s military personnel make the transition to civilian careers. Approximately 1/4th of ACME Engineering’s 90,000 employees self-identifies as veterans, and more than 1,600 are reservists.

The ACME Engineering Military Internship Program (ACME-MIP) is an approved SkillBridge Program under Dept. of Defense Instruction 1322.29. ACME-MIP is an opportunity for transitioning service members to gain valuable civilian work experience through an individual internship during their last 6 months of service, for up to 180 days. The ACME Engineering Military Internship Program is open to all ranks and experience levels. SkillBridge participants are not eligible for compensation from ACME Engineering, as they continue to receive military compensation and benefits as active-duty service members.

Responsibilities for this internship position are:

ACME Engineering Corporation (ACME) has developed the ACME Engineering – Military Internship Program (DoD SKillbridge) utilizing the DoDI guidance for Skillbridge. During this program the service member will be on-site at his or her host company performing an individual internship in an entry to mid-level career type role. The service member will be on the job training supporting a work schedule equivalent to 40hrs per week. Outlined below are the Goals, Objectives, and Outcomes for the program.

Goals – Provide transitioning service members fellowship-style job skills training during the last portion(s) of their military commitment. This program is specifically designed to offer internships that result in the potential to transition to a full-time opportunity as the conclusion of the training. Interns will serve as a pipeline for high-speed, motivated military candidates into ACME.

Objectives - Service Members who complete the Intern program will be highly trained, capable, future employees that align to the specific needs of the organization and are prepared to meet the ACME mission “Defining Possible” on Day 1. This program provides a comprehensive internship experience including professional development, networking with leadership, and training specifically focused on ACME leadership principles, company history, customer/stakeholder engagement, product and service overview, and core job responsibilities.

Outcome – Offer transitioning service member a rewarding opportunity to join the ACME Engineering team.

DoD SkillBridge Eligibility:

· Has served at least 180 days on active duty

· Is within 12 months of separation or retirement

· Will receive an honorable discharge

· Has taken any service TAPS/TGPS

· Has attended or participated in an ethics brief within the last 12 months

· Received Unit Commander (first O-4/Field Grade commander in chain of command) written authorization and approval to participate in DoD SkillBridge Program prior to start of internship.

This position is for Mission Management Engineer (MME) as a member of the Contractor Logistics Support (CLS) team at the Mission Control Station at Buckley Space Force Base.

This role provides real-time, operational support to Air Force crews and contractor test teams for the operation and maintenance of Space Based Infrared System (SBIRS) Mission Management system software and equipment (identifying system problems, trouble shooting, and correcting both software and hardware failures). Utilizes strong analytical and troubleshooting approach / skills as required.

The Mission Management Engineer (MME) will provide technical support to the Mission Management (MM) areas of the Space Based Infrared System (SBIRS).  Support includes:

  • Assist in sustaining, updating, and advancing ongoing MM projects, studies, analysis, collection strategy development, and documentation.

  • Provide recommendations on the operational integration, configuration, and updates to support SBIRS constellation. Attend meetings, working groups, and conferences as a SBIRS MM Technical Advisor (TA) to the customer, other government representatives and stakeholders.

  • Provide after Action Reports and tracking of resultant actions as well as lessons learned, and resolution actions related to MM in support of Satellite Anomaly Working Group (SAWG).

  • Support briefing preparation for all SBIRS MM related meetings and working groups when MME personnel are presenting.

  • Provide subject matter expertise to support the development of SBIRS Constellation Management methodologies and integration efforts to maximize SBIRS sensors across the full spectrum of capabilities and mission areas.

  • Recommend and/or develop training scenarios and lesson plans required to provide MM training to the military operators and Contractor Logistics Support (CLS) team members.

  • Recommend upgrades/enhancements to systems, and/or revise Tactics, Techniques & Procedures (TTP) to improve and clarify SBIRS MM operations.

  •  Develop and maintain system expertise to include: SBIRS automated commanding, payload systems, satellite bus fundamentals, and OPIR collections and tasking.

  • Bachelor’s degree with 2 years of experience, 0 years of experience with master’s degree. In lieu of a degree, 6 years of experience will be considered.

  • Active Top Secret Clearance and SCI eligible

  • Experience with Command and Control of Satellite Systems

  • Understanding of SBIRS program and Ground assets

  • Ability to support shift work and perform on-call duties

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Familiar with UNIX SOLARIS, Red Hat, IRIX, Windows operating systems

  • Ability to use Satellite Tool Kit platform

  • Experienced in Microsoft Office (Word, PowerPoint, Excel)
