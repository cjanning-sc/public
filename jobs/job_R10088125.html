Integrated National Systems (INS), a Business Unit of Northrop Grumman Space Systems Payload and Ground Division (PGSD), is seeking a Deputy Program Manager (DPM) to join our multidisciplinary team of dedicated, diverse professionals supporting the COAST II program located in Redondo Beach, CA.

As the selected COAST II Deputy PM, you will partner with the PM Director to provide the programmatic, technical and people leadership necessary to support complex ground command and control capabilities at globally distributed operation sites. The COAST II program provides factory-based Echelon 2 operations, maintenance, and sustainment activities across multiple baselines and deployed locations for a USG customer. This includes operations engineering, software, hardware, system engineering, and infrastructure support to systems deployed to CONUS and OCONUS locations. COAST II is a seamless integrated team comprised of professionals from NG Space’s Strategic Space Systems Division (SSSD) and the Payload and Ground Systems Division (PGSD). As DPM, you’ll report programmatically to the SSSD COAST II Program Director in leading overall program execution. You will also serve as the program manager and leader for ~100 personnel who execute the program’s PGSD scope and will report functionally to the PGSD Ground Enterprise Systems Operating Unit Director. Since the specific scope of the program evolves and changes with time depending on customer requirements, you’ll have the opportunity to demonstrate flexible and dynamic leadership.

Key responsibilities include:

  • Partner with the PM in building and sustaining an effective and engaged cross-divisional team in a complex and dynamic environment.
  • Leverage NG’s mission, architecture, and software development expertise to provide forward-thinking solutions that conform to customer constraints (funding, capabilities of related systems, etc.)
  • Drive innovation to help the team establish and achieve challenging objectives for modernization, resilience, automation, and affordability.
  • Support the PM in developing/conducting program review sessions with customer and/or NG leadership
  • Support the smooth transition to the sole source follow-on COAST-Next contract in 2QCY23
  • Ensure program priorities align with customer expectations, are understood by the team, and reflected in applied resources
  • Manage the cost, schedule and technical performance of complex projects and dynamic priorities across multiple baselines and operational locations
  • Advance program and NG strategic objectives by building and maintaining trusted relationships with customers and other stakeholders and by engaging in internal and customer forums related to the program’s roles in the broader end-to-end Enterprise
  • Act for the COAST II PM when they are unavailable or as designated via their Delegation of Authority
  • Ensure effective identification and management of issues, risks, and opportunities
  • Ensure team readiness to respond to system issues and provide support to occasional planned and unplanned 24/7 events
  • Direct large and small subcontractors and suppliers
  • Support the preparation and approval of proposals, ROMs, ECPs, specifications, and operating budgets. Lead coordination, development, and approval for PGSD and provide support to same for SSSD
  • Effectively coordinate and collaborate with functional and P&L leadership across NGC to ensure required staffing, processes and infrastructure are available to efficiently execute the contract.
  • As a senior leader, support color teams or other independent reviews when requested (consistent with program commitments)
  • Provide leadership support to program and OU engagement and DE&I initiatives
  • Ability to conduct occasional domestic and international travel

Basic Qualifications:

  • TS/SCI clearance w/SSBI
  • Bachelor’s Degree in a STEM discipline with 6 years of experience in program/project management with prior experience in software development, integration, and test, and/or system deployments supporting U.S. Government contracts and customers or 10 years of exp in lieu of degree.
  • Demonstrated ability to create trusted customer relationships
  • Ability to lead complex and geographically disperse program teams comprised of engineering, business, contracts, law, supply chain, and mission/quality assurance
  • Familiarity with and willingness to work in a secure environment
  • Leadership experience building effective and engaged multi-discipline teams

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in a STEM discipline from an accredited university
  • Five years of experience leading programs and/or teams on contracts of similar scope, type, and complexity
  • Command and control experience in defense industry
  • Demonstrated depth of experience in System Engineering and/or Software Development
  • Cross-sector/division program experience
  • Knowledge and familiarity with the COAST II customer and business area
  • Excellent conflict management and negotiation skills
  • Effective dealing with personnel issues
  • Experience with CONUS and OCONUS deployments
  • Proven ability to effectively work across multiple organizations to form teams, gain internal support, act with speed, and make tough decisions
