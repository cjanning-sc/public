Staff Cyber Systems Engineer – Cyber Mission Lead (23-002)
R10094685
Days (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Space Systems – Launch and Missile Defense Systems has an exciting career opportunity for a Staff Cyber Systems Engineer – Cyber Mission Lead to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located out of Schriever Space Force Base, Colorado Springs, CO.

Position Overview:

  • Operates under the general technical direction/oversight of the Cybersecurity Chief Engineer to implement Cyber capabilities intended to reduce open vulnerabilities across Dev Labs and Ops systems.
  • This position increases the program's ability to meet customer expectations and also allows for greater emphasis on end product compliance in the OPS environment â
  • Works closely with C2BMC NT Agile Release Trains (ARTs) to implement system hardening, monitoring, incident response, disaster recovery, and capabilities to counter emerging cybersecurity threats.
  • Implements a variety of cyber platforms including security information and event management (SEIM), data loss prevention (DLP), intrusion prevention systems (IPS), ACAS security scans, End-Point-Security and other cyber tools & platforms in order to accelerate remediation actions for high/critical vulnerabilities.
  • Partners with MDA and C2 NT stakeholders to drive Closure and Extension PACs on eMASS for active POA&Ms and Cyber Task Orders (CTOs)
     

Basic Qualifications:

If applicable, please note your updated security clearance and IAT/relevant certifications on your resume.

  • An active Secret clearance is required.
  • 14 Years with Bachelor’s degree in a related field; 18 years’ experience in lieu of a degree.
  • Must possess a DoD 8570 certification at IASAE Level II or higher (CISSP or equivalent)
  • Strong Cybersecurity analysis background to include knowledge of the following: Identity and Access Management methodologies, vulnerability management, compliance management, database and enterprise information security infrastructure.
  • Proficiency in the implementation of Cybersecurity principles and requirements relevant to confidentiality, integrity, availability, authentication, and non-repudiation.
  • Experience in cybersecurity requirements evaluation and validation. Knowledge of Cybersecurity systems engineering principles.
  • Familiarity with network protocols (e.g., Transmission Control Protocol and Internet Protocol [TCP/IP], Dynamic Host Configuration Protocol [DHCP]) and directory services (e.g., Domain Name System [DNS]). Knowledge of STIG application and hardening operating systems.
  • Knowledge of Cyber technologies (e.g., HBSS, ArcSight, ACAS, etc.) and how they impact a mission system.
  • Experience in Cyber threat assessment and risk management of a mission system. Knowledge of cyber operations and operational impacts.
  • Experience in Continuous Monitoring and Authorization activities.
  • The position requires a strong working knowledge of IA concepts such as patch management, two-factor authentication, host-based security, intrusion detection, security event management and defense-in-depth.
  • Must have experience in IA requirement generation, IA architecture design, IA audit tools and IA compliance for mission systems.
  • Excellent interpersonal skills with a demonstrated ability to support complex organizational relationships, technical document preparation and verbal communication skills are required for presentation of technical IA issues and reports to senior government and military officials.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Desired experience hardening Windows and Linux servers and workstations in accordance with GPOs, IAVMs and STIGs.
  • Recent experience and familiarity with creating/updating A&A packages.
  • Network design and software engineering backgrounds are a plus.
