We are looking for you to join our team as a Field Technician 4 based out of McClellan, CA (Sacramento area).

What You’ll get to Do:

Be part of a technical team that installs, operates, maintains, repairs and modifies equipment. You will support and participate in the test, modification, fabrication, assembly, integration, and verification of electrical and mechanical systems. Be responsible for supporting field evolutions up to 60 days in duration with extremely limited contact with the home office.

Responsibilities include: Installation operations, maintenance, repair, configuration management, inventory control, and generation of support documentation.

Additional responsibilities include:

  • Mobilization/demobilization and operational activities related to field support equipment

  • Maintaining and updating schematics, manuals and procedures

  • Generation and tracking of deficiency reports, operational logs, and official daily logs

  • Generation of trip reports and presentations, as required

  • Conducts technical analysis of product implementations, modifications and enhancements.

  • Troubleshoots technical problems, determines technical solutions, and implements and/or supervises repairs without communications reach back or guidance

  • Develops and conducts technical training and product briefings with customers, vendors and company representatives.

  • Supports prototype and production builds, to include: assembly, integration, manufacturing, testing and verification of systems/subsystems

  • Develops and executes test plans and equipment procedures to ensure products are operated and maintained within customer specifications

  • Conducts inventory audits to identify field equipment shortages

  • Provides recommendations for hardware enhancements and operational efficiencies

  • Acts as company representative and maintains effective customer relations via daily direct customer interface

Field Technicians are responsible for maintaining equipment in an operational state including but not limited to:

  • Hydraulic power and control equipment

  • High pressure air production and management

  • Optical communications equipment

  • Industrial electric power distribution (high voltage/high current)

  • Telecommunication networks

  • Diesel generators

  • Video Management Systems

Basic Qualifications:

  • High School diploma or equivalent and 6 years additional education and/or related experience

  • US Citizen with Active Top Secret/SCI and current CI Poly

  • Experience trouble-shooting and repairing electronic/electrical components using a variety of test equipment such as, amp meters, multi-meters, oscilloscopes, and test lamps, or similar.

  • Knowledge interpreting engineering drawings, diagrams, and schematics.

  • Must be willing and able to travel (up to 25%)

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience in planning and execution of end-to-end field event

  • Experience in administering network based systems, to include LAN, WAN and Wi-Fi networks

  • Associates degree, advanced technical school or equivalent and 7 years of verifiable, relevant, real world, experience in planning and execution of end-to-end field events

  • Experience with PTC Windchill PLM Knowledge of IPC-WHMA-A-620 Knowledge of IPC-A-610

  • Working knowledge of NFPA 70: National Electric Code

  • Experience with requirements development, developing design materials, supporting implementation and helping develop training materials, operational procedures, and user guides

  • Experience troubleshooting and maintaining HVAC systems

  • Experience soldering

  • Experience with power distribution systems

  • Experience installing instrumentation on sensitive equipment and performing subsequent monitoring and recording

  • Experience in a communications related field such as: Inmarsat, Ku SATCOM, UHF SATCOM, SOTOM,   TCDL, Link Analysis, Link 16, AEHF
