Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems is seeking a Cable and Harness Technician to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position is located in New Town, North Dakota.
 

What we offer:

  • Comprehensive benefits including competitive medical and dental options
  • Retirement Options
  • College Education Assistance (employees may have the ability to apply to an apprenticeship program and further education with various colleges and universities)
  • Annual Bonuses
  • No experience required, on the job training/certification
  • Opportunities for career advancement across North America

Job duties include but are not limited to:

  • Under the direction of supervision or higher classified Aircraft Electrician, assembles, mounts, braid and connects electrical cables, harnesses, sub-assemblies and component parts.
  • Work from drawings, blueprints, manufacturing specifications and procedures, quality procedures and instructions, operations work sheets and company, military and customer specifications procedures.
  • Adhere to all applicable environmental, safety and health, and operations procedures and directives.
  • Notify supervision of any/all malfunctions, defects and damages of equipment, material and product.
  • Check work for completeness and accuracy.
  • Perform Production Accountability duties associates with the processing, verification, and quality auditing and inspection of assemblies, parts and tooling for which the individual has been delegated production accountability.
  • Keep work area clean and orderly.
  • May be required to work in other fields incidental to, and in connection with, completion of assignment.
  • May be required to perform other related duties as assigned.

Candidates applying to the position must be available to work one or more of the following shifts:
First Shift: 6am to 4:30pm 4/10 M-TH
Weekend Shift: 6am to 6:30pm 3/12 F-Sunday

Basic Qualifications:

  • High School Diploma/GED
  • Must be a U.S. Citizen and provide the proper documentation
  • Ability to read, solve basic math and complete measurements
  • Ability to lift, carry, push and pull up to 25 lbs.
  • Ability to work First, Second or Weekend Shift
  • Ability to complete an initial paid 3-4 week training class (Monday-Thursday, 6AM-4:30PM or Friday-Sunday, 6AM-6:30PM, as assigned) is required to start, regardless of final shift position.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience in assembly operations, and familiarity with shop routines and use of hand and power tools.
  • Experience in interpreting shop math, blueprints, electronic data, specifications, manuals, operations work sheets and verbal instructions.
