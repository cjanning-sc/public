Northrop Grumman Space Systems Sector designs, builds and delivers space and defense systems to customers around the world. Our main products include launch vehicles and related propulsion systems; missile products, subsystems and defense electronics; precision weapons, armament systems and ammunition; satellites and associated space components and services; and advanced aerospace structures.

The Tactical Space Systems Division is seeking a Mechanical Analysis &Test Manager level 2 reporting to the GEOStar Analysis Senior Manager to assist an experienced team of structural engineers to perform mechanical simulation and test tasks on one or more spacecraft systems or subsystems. Operations Involvement / Direct Work Involvement Functions as an advisor to unit regarding tasks, projects, and operations. Becomes actively involved in daily operations only when required to meet schedules or to resolve complex problems. Impact Ensures that projects are completed on schedule and within budget. Erroneous decisions or recommendations or failure to complete assignments would normally result in serious delays to assigned projects resulting in considerable expenditure of additional time, human resources and funds.

The ME Mechanical Analysis Manager will work with other engineering managers and program managers across the multiple business areas to develop and enhance critical technical capabilities that drive our businesses. The successful candidate will work closely with their direct reports and business area leaders to develop engineering talent, tools, and processes, while also working closely with peer departments in manufacturing, design, quality, and program management. The ME Mechanical Analysis Manager will be responsible for the communication and engagement to their direct reports and assisting the employee’s career growth.

This position provides a broad, exciting leadership opportunity in high-growth, strategically important businesses for NG TSSD. We are looking for a successful leader with exceptional people skills, one who is valued by employees and customers alike. Our ideal candidate will develop teams, can influence and motivate them to perform at higher levels, has excellent writing and verbal skills; must be highly influential in conversation and have a record of collaborative approach with peers/peer organizations.

The successful candidate will be responsible for performing, leading, and mentoring all tasks for Structural Analysis associated with Space Vehicles. Responsibilities pertain to understanding simulation tools, computational resources, coordinating a team of support analysts and may include performing relevant analyses and liaison activities to the program and customers. Candidate should have some working knowledge of sine, random, acoustic, shock environments, Craig-Bampton model reduction, fracture and fatigue, hardware testing for satellite space systems. The manager will be responsible for establishing, maintaining, and facilitating communications, tasking, scheduling, and technical reviews with each individual contributor’s associated functional task. For functional home room support, approximately up to 10% of the manager’s time may be focused on recruiting, staffing, retaining talent, and holding performance reviews.

Position Responsibilities:

  • Performs as a working manager by being both an Program subsystem leadand functional leader of a team (80-90% direct labor, 10-20% indirect labor) of 8-12 engineers.
  • Is responsible for the technical performance and filling staffing demands of the Mechanical Analysis Group
  • Will be an Program Subsystem Lead within the Mechanical Analysis group providing mechanical support on TSSD programs
  • Support improvement initiatives, including employee engagement
  • Work with the Senior Leadership to establish, maintain, encourage, and enforce common design practices, standards, and policies in the Mechanical Analysis discipline
  • Assist in hiring staff to establish and maintain a broad range of capabilities across the business area that meets the area’s overall needs and objectives
  • Identify training for staff to support a broad range of capabilities across the company as well as employee growth
  • Work collaboratively with Program Managers across NG facilities within the overall framework of the organization
  • Be an active and engaged manager; know what programs employees are working on and the tasks they are performing on those programs
  • Ensures that projects are completed on schedule and within budget.
  • Provide general management, mentorship, motivation, guidance, and support to assigned staff
  • Support the Capital procurement process by recommending tools, equipment, and facilities, needed to support the organization
  • Support Mechanical IRAD initiatives
  • Performing static and dynamic structure analysis.
  • Reviewing requirements for materials, structure systems & subsystems.
  • Selecting appropriate technical information and data from applicable products.
  • Determining the technical principles involved, identifying the design path forward and computing the engineering characteristics to ensure consistency with the specifications design needs and requirements of space applications.
  • Releasing results as “technical documents” after review by the senior members of the team.
  • Developing innovative solutions and elaborating on design concepts.
  • Evaluating completed designs with supervisor and other technical groups, and making modifications necessary to achieve the most ideal design.
  • Developing, coordinating, and conducting test programs required to evaluate design work.
  • Other duties as assigned

  • Work on-site at the Dulles campus a minimum of 2 days per week.
