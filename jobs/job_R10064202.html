Seeking a creative and energetic electronics technician with a hands-on knowledge and experience with the use of a variety of electrical, RF, and/or computer equipment.  The ideal candidate will have the ability and initiative to tackle and understand new technical and engineering designs especially as it relates to spaceflight hardware and software.  The ability to read schematics, block diagrams, and troubleshoot electronics and RF circuits is required.   The candidate will work as an electrical/RF test technician on all aspects of integration and test for satellite production.  Electrical subsystem and environmental testing (including thermal vacuum and vibration testing), and launch campaigns will be required throughout the test campaigns for the satellites under test.  The candidate will operate and maintain automated test equipment, special test equipment and test cabling used in all aspects of satellite testing.  When required, shift rotations and weekend support will be required for several test phases of the satellite development.  Will be asked to move and/or push Test equipment around the facility to complete various test phases.  Also, will have to be able to lift or move equipment up to 30-50lbs.  Performs a variety of duties including, but not limited to, fastening, assembly, assisting engineers in development of engineering designs, test, fabrications, modifications of assemblies and components. Diagnose and correct equipment and system malfunctions which fail to respond to standard corrective measures.

PRIMARY DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES: Under close supervision, performs routine technical functions related to the support of development engineering activities such as design, test, checkout, fabrication, modification, and assembly of electronics and Electro-mechanical systems, experimental design circuitry, prototype models or specialized test equipment. Applications may include analog, digital and/or video type circuits and logic systems. Guidance is readily available from superiors. Works from schematics, diagrams, written and verbal descriptions, layouts or defined plans to perform routine testing, checkout, troubleshooting, prototype building, environmental testing, and simple developmental engineering tasks. Performs routine operational tests and fault isolation on development systems and equipment to ensure conformance with design specifications. Diagnoses and isolates malfunctions down to component level. Conducts defined engineering tests and collects data as assigned. Utilizes routine development or diagnostic equipment including oscilloscope, VOM, DVM, signal generators and similar test apparatus. Normally assigned in area such as development, engineering, test equipment design, or the like. Excludes those assigned to production test activities.

Basic Qualifications - External

  • Minimum of a High School diploma and 6+ years of related experience or additional education
  • Applicant must be able to read and understand basic wiring schematics. 
  • Basic soldering skill are required for this position as well as familiarization with aircraft type wire cutting and mechanical splicing. 
  • Basic hand tool knowledge as well as familiarization with some power tools is required.  
  • Applicant must be flexible in types of work assignments. Applicant must be able to react to changing priorities as directed.

​Preferred Qualifications:

  • Familiar with MS Office software,
  • NASA/Military specification Solder training course completion.
  • CreoView or other 3D modeling experience is also preferred. 
  • experience with break out boxes, Scopes, Dual Volt Meters and other types of electronic test equipment is preferred.
