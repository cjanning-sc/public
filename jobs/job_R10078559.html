ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems (NGAS) has an opening for a Production Test Technician 3 or 4-Radio frequency testing of Composite Structures, Randomes, and Antenna Systems for military applications to join our team of qualified and diverse individuals. This position will be supporting our Green Bay, and Racine, OH. facilities, and requires onsite presence due to the nature of the role.

Production Testing has various responsibilities including radio frequency and electronic testing with duties as assigned in our Green Bay and Racine locations in support of plant wide operations.  Work in this position includes test and evaluation of manufactured products and in process testing using state of the art electronic test equipment, anechoic chamber test facilities, and in the less cooperative factory environment as well. 

Our team advances the Aerospace Structures Business Unit through the application of multidisciplinary engineering approaches and problem solving.  We pride ourselves on technical excellence and our ability to drive positive change.

Essential Functions / Responsibilities:

  • RF circuit testing, tuning, and assembly
  • Radar cross section measurements using the linear FM homodyne radar test set in an uncooperative environment
  • Transmission and reflection arch measurements using a vector network analyzer
  • Soldering and assembly.  (J-STD certification a plus)
  • Electronic and supporting mechanical system repair, calibration, and maintenance
  • In process measurements in support of low-rate manufacturing operations
  • Radar cross section measurements within the compact range including execution of predetermined test matrices and directed R&D efforts
  • Antenna pattern and gain measurements within the Anechoic Chamber
  • Antenna and RF network VSWR measurements

The ideal candidate is comfortable working independently and excels in a team environment.  They should possess a good math and science aptitude, should possess good verbal and written communication skills, be able to work with limited supervision, be able to lift, push, and pull up to 50 pounds and climb a ladder on a continuous basis, has an aptitude and desire to learn the practical application of the RF test environment and the theory behind the work you do.  Has experience in operation of various electronic test instruments such as oscilloscopes, spectrum analyzers, signal generators, vector network analyzers, multi-meters, time domain reflectometers and power meters.  Has some mechanical and electrical aptitude with a demonstrated ability to learn new skills and follow written procedures. Identifies opportunities for continuous improvement with a focus on customer service excellence.

*This is primarily a first shift position, but should have the ability to work flexible hours, overtime, and different shifts occasionally based on demanding manufacturing schedules.
