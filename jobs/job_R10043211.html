Role

The primary purpose of this job is to deliver Mission Assurance support across assigned bids and projects/programmes, with a focus on software development, deployment and support services. This role has the authority to prevent the release of deliverables until accepted standards are achieved.  

Key Responsibilities

  • Conduct MA/QA activities during business acquisition and programme execution in accordance with company processes and procedures
  • Provide independent oversight on assigned bids/programme to ensure compliance with all required external standards, internal command media and contractual requirements
  • Provide MA/QA input into proposals including MA/QA Plans and associated cost forecasts for MA activities
  • Co-ordinate and participate in bid and programme Gate Reviews to assist the teams in achieving mission success
  • Work with project teams, Supply Chain staff and the ES Compliance Team to assure Supplier Quality, including supplier on-boarding activities and ongoing supplier performance management, and ensuring projects complete quarterly assessments of critical suppliers
  • Establish relationships with supplier Quality personnel
  • Assist with the flow down of MA/QA requirements to suppliers
  • Assess adequacy of and support Risk and Opportunity Management activities on bids and programmes
  • Support sub-contract management activities during programme execution
  • Develop and document any programme-specific processes required
  • Provide MA/QA reports as required to support NSS internal activities, escalating significant issues or risks to the Programme Manager and the NSS Mission Assurance Manager
  • Ensure customer deliverables are of acceptable quality and meet all required standards and contractual requirements
  • Liaise with customers and other third parties including suppliers regarding all aspects of Mission and Quality Assurance for assigned projects
  • Collect, interpret and present MA/QA metrics to the Programme Manager, NSS Mission Assurance Manager and the Technical Director to support the development of process improvement initiatives
  • Follow up audits to validate the resolution of non-conformities and findings raised
  • Undertake any other reasonable tasks as requested

Person Specification

Essential Qualifications/Experience

  • Quality Assurance experience, ideally in a software engineering environment
  • Experience across the bid and project execution life cycle
  • Experience in developing and implementing ISO compliant processes
  • Track record of delivering to identified Quality requirements
  • Experience of leading continuous improvement activities
  • Practical experience of bid, programme and supply chain management
  • Demonstrable experience of Risk Management

Preferred Qualifications/Experience/Knowledge

  • UK Government working practices
  • Experience of conducting internal ISO 9001 audits

Competency/Skill requirements

  • A good understanding of software development lifecycles (waterfall/agile)
  • Knowledge of ISO 9001:2015, 27001:2013 and 20000-1:2011
  • Sound working knowledge of Defence engineering standards and working practices including AQAP, DefStan
  • Self-motivated and able to work both autonomously and as part of a wider team
  • Excellent interpersonal skills, able to engage effectively with a wide range of stakeholders and customers
  • Fluent in written and spoken English
  • Strong analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Proficient IT skills, specifically in MS Office tools
  • Flexible and responsive attitude
  • Proficient at multi-tasking, working under pressure and to tight deadlines
  • Very good customer orientation
  • Excellent attention to detail

Other requirements

  • Travel requirements: Some UK travel is required to attend team, preferred supplier or customer meetings, and to attend training events. A valid UK driving license is required.
  • Clearance requirements: The post-holder must hold and maintain UK Government clearance at DV level.
  • Other: Must be a UK national
