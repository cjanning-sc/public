Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems has an opening for an Aircraft Assembler – Fuel Cells to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in El Segundo, CA.

In this role, the Aircraft Assembler – Fuel Cells will be expected to:

  • Assembly, installation, rework, adjustment, and testing of Environmental Control System assemblies, and portions of systems of pre-production, developmental, or production aircraft.

  • Determine locations, identify, and relationship of parts from production blueprints and adequate information to assemble, install, rework and adjust airplane Fuel assemblies and portions of systems.

  • Work with jigs, fixtures, and tooling or without them when work operations do not require their use.

  • Improvise simple production aids such as holding clamps to assist in accomplishing assembly or installation operations.

  • Routine pressure testing for malfunction or leaks previous to or after installations, including filling Fuel lines and checking for leaks.

  • Perform such work in other fields as is necessary to complete assignments.

  • Use blueprints and production illustrations to obtain easily identified reference information and process and material callouts.

  • May work with the assistance of lower classified employees; may assist higher classified workers on occasion.

  • May direct and train other employees as assigned.

  • Perform pickup, repair and rework as necessary where the type and kind of work is within the level of difficult required to perform the regular assignments of the classification.

  • Use simple blueprints and schematics.

  • Use arithmetic involving decimals and fractions.

  • May monitor and verify quality in accordance with statistical process or other control procedures.

  • May be required to work in confined space.

  • Maintained required certifications and qualifications on area of specialization.

  • Must be able to utilize computer-based systems to perform duties.

  • Must be willing/able to work any shift or schedule.

  • Ability to obtain and maintain a DOD Secret or Top-Secret Clearance as well as Program Special access within a reasonable period of time, as determined by the company to meet its business needs.

Basic Qualifications: 

  • High School Diploma or Equivalent. Must have two years related fuel experience.

Preferred Qualifications: 

  • High School Diploma or Equivalent. Must have two years related systems (Fuel, Hydraulic, ECS, Electrical) experience.
