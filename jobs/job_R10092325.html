Northrop Grumman is seeking a Principal Database Architect to join our team supporting the NKW program.  The position will be onsite and based at a facility in Aurora, Colorado.

The NKW program is transforming a mission critical application to work in tomorrow’s environments through increased DevOps implementation and needs a DBA to support the team’s growth and continued success. 

This is an opportunity to help a customer and team take advantage of leading-edge development practices. If you have found yourself saying on more than one occasion “Hey folks, maybe we should really try <insert modern best practice>!” Then this might be the job for you!

Basic Qualifications

  • 5 years of related experience with a Bachelor’s degree; 3 years of related experience with a Master’s degree; 0 years of experience with a Ph.D.; 9 or more years of experience may be considered in lieu of a degree
  • Ability to obtain a DoD Top Secret (TS) security clearance (clearance not required to start) – US Citizenship is required**
  • Ability to obtain a Counterintelligence Polygraph (CI-Poly) (not required to start- candidate will be submitted for CI Poly upon hire)
  • Experience with Agile software development using Java, Python and/or C++ software development in a Red Hat Enterprise Linux environment
  • Experience with database administration with either Oracle or Postgres databases
  • Experience with tuning databases performance
  • Ability to troubleshoot database issues
  • Ability to design and implement database systems and schemas

Preferred Qualifications

  • Current/Active Top Secret/SCI security clearance
  • Current Counterintelligence Polygraph (CI-Poly) completed within the last 5 years
  • Experience with database administration with both Oracle and Postgres databases
  • Ability to tune and maintain high-performing databases
  • Ability to troubleshoot complex database issues
  • Ability to support development, test, and deployment teams through DB administration

This position’s standard work schedule is a 9/80. The 9/80 schedule allows employees who work a nine-hour day Monday through Thursday to take every other Friday off.  This schedule is flexible to accommodate those that prefer the traditional 40-hour week/8 hour day work weeks as well.
