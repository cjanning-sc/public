Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems sector is seeking an Associate Manufacturing Analyst/ Manufacturing Analyst to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals.  This position will be located in Clearfield, UT where technology and teamwork come together.  

 

 

Specific Responsibilities include, but are not limited to: 

  • Supports production, business, and other processes in a team-based manufacturing environment. 

  • Performs activities in a variety of cross-functional areas including production planning and control, total quality management, systems, manufacturing, equipment and facilities engineering, material management, and process reengineering. 

  • Experienced in one or more areas of manufacturing operations.  

  • Create and improve area tools and systems designed to support manufacturing stability and efficiency. 

  • Understand demand vs. capacity for the Assembly business, create and execute plans to support future needs. 

  • Analyze Operational Availability data and develop plans with support groups for readiness to execute to future demands. 

  • Identify opportunities for mistake proofing and create innovative solutions for Quality Improvement. 

  •  Develop innovative solutions to improve and simplify work. 

  • Develop and implement process improvement initiatives. Provide coaching and support for improvement activities throughout the work center. 

  • Communicate effectively, initiate and drive necessary actions to completion that ensure organizational goals are accomplished.  

  • Resolve functional or process related problems/issues and makes sound business decisions. 

 

Basic Qualifications: 

Associate Manufacturing Analyst 

  • Bachelor's degree or 4 years of work experience in lieu of a degree 

  • Ability to work projects both independently and in a team-based environment. 

  • Demonstrated proficiency in the Microsoft Office Suite, specifically Outlook, Excel, PowerPoint, Word 

  • Must be able to work any shift 

  • Must be able to demonstrate the ability to solve real world complex Assembly problems 

  • Must understand how to operate measuring equipment and tools 

  • This position requires U.S. Citizenship 

  • This position requires a current DoD (Department of Defense) U.S. Government security 

  • Ability to obtain Special Program Access 

 

 

Manufacturing Analyst  

  • Bachelor's degree and 2 years of work experience or 6 years' work experience

  • Must be able to work any shift 

  • Must be able to demonstrate the ability to solve real world complex assembly problems 

  • Must understand how to operate measuring equipment and tools 

  • This position requires U.S. Citizenship

Preferred Qualifications: 

  • Strong knowledge of Manufacturing processes and procedures 

  • Possess strong leadership and communication skills, ability to counsel, coach, develop, and motivate a diverse group of professionals and personnel. 

  • Demonstrated ability to effectively present information on complex projects to key stakeholders. 

  • Experience in subsystem and structure fabrication. 

  • Experience in a manufacturing environment.
