1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems has an opening for a Systems Engineering – Configuration Analyst to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position is located in San Diego, CA.

The selected candidate will: be assigned to the Systems Engineering Integration Team (SEIT) Configuration Data Management organization. In this position, the individual will be responsible for supporting the Data Management team. They will be expected to adhere to Company and Program procedures and policies and reflect excellent quality and attention to detail; Administer Data Management on Subcontractor Data Requirements List (SDRL) using our Product Data Management tools such as SharePoint, Data Management Delivery System (DMDS) and Teamcenter Enterprise. Schedule, track and process Supplier Data Requirement Lists (SDRLs) and ensure data integrity through audit processes.

Additional requirements include receive, verify and process government furnished items; Participate in Supplier Statement of Work (SSOW) review and assist in the creation of Supplier Requirements Document (SRD) to ensure that Subcontractor Data Requirements List (SDRL) items are flowed down the suppliers. Responsible for authoring the Contractor Data Requirement List (CDRL) Data Accession List (DAL) to capture internally generated data and supplier delivered data per DD Form 1423. Support and work closely with the program, Supplier Management Team (SMT), engineering, technical disciplines, and suppliers to ensure that data management processes comply with the program, company and customer requirements and policies. Provide inputs into program level metrics to benchmark effectiveness of Data Management performance. Support data management activities of Contract Data Requirement Lists (CDRLs) as needed.

Required to work on-site as required.

This position may be filled as an Assoc Configuration Analyst (A01) or a Configuration Analyst (A02).

Basic Qualifications:

  • A01: 0 years with BA, A02: 3 Years with Bachelors or 1 Year with Masters.
  • Experience working in a fast-pace environment
  • Proven written/communication and analytical skills and proven ability to handle detail-oriented work processes
  • Must be computer literate and proficient with Microsoft Office Suite
  • Must have excellent communication skills with both internal and external customers.
  • Must be detail oriented and have the ability to follow processes.
  • U.S. citizen and ability to obtain and maintain a US Government DoD secret clearance

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Knowledge and experience of Data Management and SDRL/CDRL processing on defense programs.
  • Proficiency with product lifecycle management (PLM) system.
  • Demonstrated understanding of general configuration management concepts, practices, and processes.
  • DoD Active SECRET clearance.
