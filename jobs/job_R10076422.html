​ACME Engineering’s Space Systems, Payload and Ground Systems Division is looking for a Manufacturing Operations Manager to support the Space Components organization/business unit in the Propulsion operating unit in Commerce, CA. We design and manufacture high performance pressure vessels for use in space applications.  The ACME Engineering Commerce facility is the leading supplier of pressure vessels for space missions in the world.  At the facility we produce a wide variety of pressure vessels supporting a wide variety of missions, both foreign and domestic, commercial, defense, and civil space. 

The ideal candidate is a dynamic individual who will be a role model for the practice of ACME Engineering values and the leadership characteristics of competency, connection, character and trust.

Overview:

Managing supervisors who manage the shop floor personnel, the Manufacturing Operations Manager will direct and lead the activities of a cross functional product team responsible for continuous improvements to the cost, quality and schedule of its product line.

In this roll you will have complete responsibility to ensure performance goals are met on a daily basis. Expectations for success will be to develop and maintain open lines of communication to ensure high levels of customer satisfaction, promote the development of self-directed work groups and a work environment which encourages training and opportunities for its work force, ensure compliance to all state, corporate and sector policies related to health, safety, environmental and equal opportunity regulations. 

 Responsibilities:

  • Provide direction and coaching to technicians and front line leads in the Space Components Propulsion Team 

  • Ensure team members receive the required training and prepares the daily work plans in support of overall program objectives. 

  • Encourage and provide direction for career development of employees, including educational options for highly motivated employees. 

  • Provide leadership/direction to ensure all affordability initiatives and opportunities for process improvements are systematically identified, pursued, and supported.    

  • The selected candidate should thrive in a fast-paced work environment with high expectations, significantly diverse assignments, collaborative/team settings across all levels.  

  • Manage resolution of complex technical issues within responsible production area and drives quality improvement plans. 

  • Report and manage daily output while working any constraints that prevent the team from meeting targets.   

  • Establish targets for the production team and promotes cross training

  • Coordinate all work in process and workflow between production areas

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's Degree with 5 years of experience in an aerospace, automotive, military, manufacturing and/or maintenance environment 

  • 2 years leadership experience

  • US Citizenship required

Preferred Qualifications:

  • 3+ years of related leadership experience 

  • Composite wrap manufacturing experience

  • Degree in engineering (mechanical, aerospace, industrial or manufacturing) 

  • Demonstrated ability to effectively communicate and present presentations with various levels of leadership
