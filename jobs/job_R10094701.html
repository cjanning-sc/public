Principal Manufacturing Analyst
R10094701
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Aerospace Systems has an opening for a Principal Manufacturing Analyst to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Melbourne, FL, where technology and teamwork come together.

The selected candidate will become part of Northrop Grumman's Manufacturing Engineering Team at the Melbourne Design Center working in a collaborative environment while promoting a positive, proactive and team environment.

Essential Functions:

  • Facilitate self-directed work teams, provide training in core skill areas to teams and peers, and interact with internal and external customers with respect to product planning, problem resolution, and process improvement.
  • Maintains configuration of the aircraft to Engineering requirements
    • Provides guidance to Engineering regarding design concepts and specification requirements to best utilize existing equipment and manufacturing techniques.
    • Translates Engineering requirements into a product build package.
    • Collaborates with tool design on manufacturing requirements.
  • Controls the flow of parts
    • Coordinates make vs. buy part decisions.
    • Structures Manufacturing Bill of Material (MBOM)
  • Defines sequence of operations and specifies procedures for product build.
    • Ensures manufacturing processes and procedures are in compliance with customer requirements.
    • Follows manufacturing processes that are applicable to statistical process control and supporting data collection.
    • Uses established manufacturing engineering procedures for the fabrication of tools and equipment used to fabricate products.
  • Supports the build and test of product.
    • Clears constraints affecting production of aircraft.
    • Investigates and initiates corrective actions and improvements in product quality and efficiency.
    • Collaborates with tool design to ensure product build is repeatable.
    • Oversees the integration of aircraft assembly technologies and processes.
  • Provides technical solutions to a wide range of complex and difficult problems.
    • Experience integrating products from engineering design to manufacturing floor.
    • Provides detailed written instructions that help facilitate product fabrication.
    • Independently determines and develops approach to solutions.

The selected candidate should thrive in a fast-paced work environment with high expectations, significantly diverse assignments, and must have the ability to interface/communicate with multiple levels of leadership, Engineering, and Production. 

Basic Qualifications:

  • High School Diploma or equivalent and 10 years of relevant experience in Project Management, Program Integration, or Business Management in an aerospace or military aviation role, or Associates Degree and 8 years, Bachelor’s Degree and 6 years or Master's degree and 4 years' experience
  • Ability to obtain and maintain a DOD Secret Security Clearance and Special Program Access within a reasonable amount of time as required by the business
  • Experience with Microsoft Office Suite

Preferred Qualifications

  • Degree Business Management, Mechanical Engineering, Aerospace Engineering, Systems Engineering, Industrial Engineering, Electrical Engineering, or Engineering Technology with 5 years of experience in an aerospace or military aviation role
  • Experience creating work instructions, visual aids, and with tooling maintenance
  • Experience working with Manufacturing Execution System (MES)
  • Experience working with Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems (Bill of Materials)
  • Active in-scope DOD Secret security clearance that has been granted / renewed within the past 5 1/2 years
