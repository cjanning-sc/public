ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems sector has an opening for an Air Vehicle Engineer specializing in Mechanical Subsystems to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This role will be located in Philadelphia, PA and can be hired at a higher grade based on experience.

In this role you will be responsible for developing, modifying, maintaining the Mechanical (Mechanisms, Landing Gear, Braking, Steering) Systems components that support the overall architecture using engineering practices and standards.

Key Responsibilities:

  • The candidate will have responsibility for procurement of aircraft mechanical systems components (flight controls systems, up-lock and down-lock systems, weapons carriage systems, electromechanical and hydraulic rotary and/or linear drive actuators and etc.) 
  • The candidate will be responsible for integration activities within the mechanical systems team and with other disciplines (Design team, Supplier Management team, Software, VMS, etc)
  • The candidate will provide support to lab testing and verification activities, as well as problem solving solutions and test for validation and understanding of system dynamics.
  • The candidate will be required to attend internal program, customer and/or supplier technical exchange meetings and/or formal program milestone reviews to provide and/or obtain progress status updates. 
  • The candidate will support qualification testing of Landing Gear System components (Landing Gear, NWS, Brakes including qualification plans and certification efforts.

The selected candidate should thrive in a fast-paced work environment with high expectations, significantly diverse assignments, and collaborative/team settings across all levels. The candidate must be a self-starter capable of flourishing in an environment with minimal direction.  The candidate will need to have excellent communication and writing skills with experience in Microsoft Word and Excel. 

We offer flexible work arrangements, 9/80 work schedule with every other Friday off, phenomenal learning opportunities, exposure to a wide variety of projects and customers, and a very friendly team environment. We are looking for self-motivated, proactive, and goal-oriented people to help us grow our services and become even better at what we do

Basic Qualifications:

  • Basic Qualifications for an Associate Mechanical Engineer is a Bachelor’s degree in a STEM discipline. 

  • Basic Qualifications for the Mechanical Engineer are a Bachelor of Science degree in a STEM discipline and 2 years of related professional/military experience in mechanical/aerospace engineering OR Master’s degree in a STEM discipline

  • Must have an active DoD Secret clearance or higher (that has a background investigation completed within the last 6 years or is under continuous evaluation).
  • Must be able to obtain and maintain Special Access Programs (SAP) prior to the commencement of employment.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Master’s Degree in Mechanical or Aerospace Engineering or related discipline.
  • Familiarity with NX CAD software and Teamcenter PLM.
  • Managerial / Team Lead experience.
  • Aircraft mechanical systems design experience.
