U103 WELDER B
R10078503
2nd Shift (United States of America)

PRIMARY FUNCTION:

Set up and weld various types, sizes and shapes of materials and standard fabrications

utilizing the full range of welding processes in all positions, requiring extreme care

to produce a quality joint where methods, sequences and tooling have been established.

TOOLS AND EQUIPMENT:

GMAW (Gas Metal Arc), GTAW (Gas Tungsten Arc), SMAW (Shielded Metal Arc Welding), SAW

(Submerged Arc Welding), EW (Electroslag Welding), burning equipment, regulators, induc‑

tion heaters, welding positioners, fixtures, slag gun, grinder, crane, ladder, scaffold; hand truck, forklift, safety glasses, face shield, gloves, hammer, punch, square, chalkline.

MATERIAL:

Ferrous and nonferrous metals and their alloys, plate, structural shapes, casings and weldments, Oxygen, acetylene and inert gases, flux, welding wire and rods, etc.

DIRECTION OF OTHERS:

Direct Welder Trainee assigned to help in the type, size and location of weld required

And direct Filer Chippers and Chippers assigned to help.

WORKING PROCEDURE:

THE FOLLOWING ARE THE USUAL MAJOR JOB DUTIES BUT THIS JOB DESCRIPTION DOES NOT PRECLUDE THE PERFORMANCE OF OTHER DUTIES BY THE INCUMBENT, NOR THE QUESTION BEING RAISED ON THE PROPER RATE OF PAY FOR THE ASSIGNMENT.

1.   Receive work assignment from supervision.

2.   Read and follow safety regulations and procedures and MI package including drawings, change notices, operation sheets, specifications and work authorizations.

3.   Lay out and mark or scribe reference lines on parts, strongback and positioner and assemble standard fabrications where sequences have been established and locate parts in positioning fixtures for welding. Trim parts to fit using burning equipment. Brace weldment to retain proper size and shape during welding.

4.   Set up equipment by setting amperage, voltage, pressures, feeds and speeds within limits specified and obtaining specified electrode.

5.   Preheat and maintain temperature of work as specified.

6.   Operate welding equipment to deposit single and multiple layers of weld in all positions where extreme care must be exercised to produce a quality joint with uniform penetration that will withstand nondestructive testing arid machining to exacting tolerances. Clean weld between passes.

7.   Repair weld all visual defects, voids and incomplete welds, Remove runoff tabs and clean up ends of weld joints. .

8.   Identify weld areas with personal identification stamp.

9.   Maintain tools, equipment and work area in a clean and orderly condition and maintain equipment by replacing wire reels, straighteners, conduits, liners, guide tubes, tips, flow meters, nozzles, gas, etc.

10.  Sign off operations completed and obtain first piece and final inspection signoff on operation sheets as required.

11.  Check work for conformance to specifications.

12.  Make proper time charge entries and record data as required.

13.  Identify parts in accordance with method specified.

Maintain in effect certification for welding ferrous and nonferrous metals and alloys and take and acceptably pass periodic working tests. Perform other work of equal or lower classification as required.
