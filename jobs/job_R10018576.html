Avionics Engineer (Design Engineer)
R10018576

Northrop Grumman Australia is truly committed to being the most trusted provider of systems and technologies that ensure the security and freedom of our nation and its allies. We provide meaningful and purposeful employment opportunities across a diverse range of functions delivering solutions and capabilities to our customers every day.

Northrop Grumman Australia partners with the Royal Australian Air Force to provide through-life support for its fleet of C-27J Spartan Airlifter aircraft. The C-27J complements the Australian Defence Force’s existing Air Mobility fleet and provides airlift of people, equipment and supplies in Australia and our region.

The C-27J platform has been recognised for its unique capabilities and performance and a steady pipeline of Avionics/Electrical upgrades, updates and modifications are forecast well into the future.

As a member of the C-27J Capability Enhancement Team, you will be responsible to support the development of avionics/electrical modifications to the aircraft through design, certification and other technical support processes. Activities undertaken will include those under DASR 21J Regulatory Design Organisation and DASR M Continuing Airworthiness Management Services (CAM Services) as required.

Key duties and responsibilities:

  • Development, Review and Approval of various aircraft Avionics / Electrical and/or Electronic System Modifications
  • Holding and exercising Engineering Authority (EA) as a Design Engineer (DE) under NGA’s Military Design Organisation Exposition.
  • Applying Systems Engineering processes across the product lifecycle
  • Coordinating and supporting the project management of tasks through the design development and installation phases.
  • Providing advice to internal/external stakeholders and representing the enterprise on specific projects, technical issues and in various intra-/inter-organisational meetings as required
  • Providing oversight and reviewing work of other engineers for technical accuracy, adequacy and quality.
  • Investigating, reporting and communicating engineering compliance issues
