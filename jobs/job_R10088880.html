The team at Northrop Grumman Mission Systems (NGMS) is looking for you to join our team as an Acoustics Engineering Manager.

What You’ll get to Do:

With Northrop Grumman, you will play a critical role in the development of products for the United States Navy. You will lead your fellow engineers to solve the toughest challenges in the industry and deliver world-class performance.  Your duties may include:

  • Analyzing product designs to determine predicted vibration and noise levels
  • Maintaining and advancing state-of-the-art analytical software, modeling and simulation capabilities, tools, and high-performance computational SW and HW
  • Providing technical, cost and schedule performance oversight of multiple projects, including cost account management (CAM)
  • Reviewing and approving proposal estimates for new work
  • Developing and implementing employee career development, knowledge sharing and training plans
  • Maintaining proper levels of staffing through forecasting and recruiting
  • Supporting strategic planning
  • Championing and executing continuous process improvements

Basic Qualifications:

  • An engineering degree with 8 years work experience in a related field, or a Master’s degree in Engineering with 6 years work experience in a related field, or a PHD with 4 years work experience in a related field.
  • A government secret clearance will be required
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills
  • Ability to comprehend and analyze complex problems and develop solutions
  • Ability to collaborate in a team environment
  • Ability to supervise and prioritize multiple projects on schedule and within cost
  • Ability to analyze data and make decisions that impact the organization’s credibility, operations and services
  • Ability to develop work products such as detailed reports, work‐related manuals, publications, etc.
  • Ability to present material to those outside the immediate work function, to all levels of the organization, and to a variety of external customers

Preferred Qualifications:

  • An engineering degree with 14 years work experience in a related field, or a Master’s degree in Engineering with 12 years work experience in a related field, or a PHD with 10 years work experience in a related field.
  • Demonstrated leadership experience
  • A strong background with vibration and noise predictive tools
  • A strong background with vibration and noise test methods and tools
  • A familiarity with US Navy vessels or machinery
