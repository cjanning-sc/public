Northrop Grumman is currently seeking a new Corporate Lead Executive (CLE) to oversee government relations with Pacific Theater operational forces, with a clear focus on Hawaii and further west. Reporting to the Vice President, Navy and Marine Corps Programs, the CLE is responsible for establishing and maintaining effective US customer relationships across INDOPACOM and the service components to foster an in-depth understanding of multi-service military requirements of the Pacific Theater. This individual will orchestrate and coordinate high level customer interface with all military services and other customer organizations designated by the Vice President. This engagement will be accomplished in collaboration with other corporate and sector teams through internal cross-sector and external strategy development and outreach.

Responsibilities will include, but are not limited to:

  • Coordinate, integrate and synchronize corporate and sector customer engagements and campaign strategies, and facilitate the development of new business opportunities within customer markets
  • Support corporate and sector teams in the design, construction and execution of customer analysis plans, strategic customer approach plans, and customer contact plans
  • Provide inputs for the development of company priority programs
  • Maintain awareness of significant policy, program, budget and legislative initiatives effecting corporate goals
  • Travel will be required - this will primarily be domestic, including occasional visits to Corporate Headquarters in Falls Church, VA.

Basic Qualifications - to be considered for this position, you must minimally meet the knowledge, skills and abilities listed below:

  • This role will require the chosen candidate to work out of Hawaii
  • Bachelor's degree (or equivalent related experience) with twenty or more year’s DoD service and associated operational experience, including senior level leadership responsibilities and management experience
  • Top Secret security clearance with SCI eligibility
  • The ability to analyze a broad range of issues and exercise strategic thinking to best influence senior DoD civilian and military decision-makers
  • A high level of confidence, integrity and hands on approach to problem solving as well as a professional executive presence for interacting with and influencing DoD decision makers, senior management and executives internal and external to the company
  • Experience with DoD budget and requirements process
  • Strong judgment, decision-making skills, flexibility, agility, and political acumen
  • Unswerving commitment and the ability to work cooperatively in a team environment
  • Excellent organizational, verbal and written communications skills, as well as project management, analytical, planning, presentation and human relations’ skills
  • Ability to travel - this will primarily be domestic, including occasional visits to Corporate Headquarters in Falls Church, VA.
     

Preferred Qualifications - candidates with these desired skills will be given preferential consideration:

  • Retired Flag/General Officer or Senior Executive
