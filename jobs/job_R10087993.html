ACME Engineering Mission Systems is seeking a Sr. Principal Quality Engineer to join our Final Assembly Factory Quality Engineering Team at our BWI campus. This specific position will serve as the Lead Test Quality Engineer for Final Assembly.

Primary Responsibilities

  • Applies extensive technical knowledge in industry standard quality engineering methods, interpretation of drawing requirements and applicable specifications & standards, and mechanical measurement & analysis
  • Proactively establishes and maintains high technical competency in products, manufacturing and business processes, and related technologies
  • Confidently facilitates efforts to resolve complex quality problems using sound problem-solving methodology – including through application of established root cause analysis and corrective/preventive action methods
  • Leads efforts to identify and reduce safety, quality, and business risks through application of formal and informal methods
  • Leads efforts to reduce waste and improve processes through application of established continual improvement methods
  • Engages directly in shop floor activities across Final Assembly manufacturing cells – including through monitoring of manufacturing processes, leading quality assessments and audits, and controlling and review of non-conforming material
  • Facilitates Preventive & Corrective Action Board (PCAB) process – including leading and participating in data gathering, analysis, and presentation activities
  • Promotes and implements World Class Manufacturing (WCM) framework
  • Serves as a “strong voice for quality” and a role model for ACME Values
  • Trains, mentors, and develops junior-level personnel, and participates in the design and implementation of training materials
  • Serves as a Factory Quality Engineering liaison for adjacent Quality functions – including Supplier Performance Management, Compliance, Program Quality, and Inspection

Job Characteristics

  • Knowledge, Skills, and Ability: Applies extensive technical expertise, and has full knowledge of other related disciplines. Able to communicate effectively and clearly present technical approaches and findings.
  • Problem Solving: Develops technical solutions to complex problems which require the regular use of ingenuity and creativity.
  • Discretion/Latitude: Work is performed without appreciable direction. Exercises considerable latitude in determining technical objectives of assignment. Completed work is reviewed from a relatively long-term perspective, for desired results.
  • Impact: Guides the successful completion of major programs and may function in a project leadership role. Erroneous decisions or recommendations would typically result in failure to achieve major organizational objectives.
  • Liaison: Represents the organization as the prime technical contact on contracts and projects. Interacts with senior external personnel on significant technical matters often requiring coordination between organizations.

Basic Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s Degree in Engineering or related STEM field
  • Minimum of nine (9) years of relevant work experience; or seven (7) years with Master’s Degree; or four (4) years with PhD
  • Ability to obtain and maintain a SECRET clearance. US Citizenship Required.
  • Strong interpersonal skills – including ability to establish and maintain positive working relationship with stakeholders and customers at all levels, ranging from conversations on the shop floor through briefing senior management
  • Manufacturing or Quality Assurance Experience
  • Understanding of Root Cause Corrective Action process
  • Experience working with electrical or mechanical design requirements and specifications.
  • Experience with SAP or other similar logistical software
  • Working knowledge of Microsoft Office applications

Preferred Qualifications

  • J-STD-001, IPC-A-610, and IPC/WHMA-A-620 certifications
  • Experience with World Class Manufacturing (WCM)
  • Experience with Quality Management Systems (e.g. AS9100, ISO 9001, etc…)
  • Experience working with DCMA or other defense or government customers
  • Strong background in electronic and RF testing requirements and troubleshooting failures
  • Defense industry experience, including interface with government customers
  • Experience conducting, leading and supporting audits
  • Experience working or leading Root Cause and Corrective Action activities
  • Active DoD Secret Clearance
