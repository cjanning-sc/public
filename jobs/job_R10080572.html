The Lab Operations Department is seeking a Section Manager to lead a team of Lab Cognizant Engineers supporting the Strike Division in Philadelphia, FL.

Training will occur on first shift; however, the successful candidate will be placed on weekend shift and be paid additional compensation for working the non-standard shift  on the weekend 3/10 shift (Friday, Saturday, and Sunday 10 hours per day).  This is a dual role Section Manager (Functional Role) and Engineering Manager (Program Role).  

Functional Role and Responsibilities include:
The successful candidate will help their team understand the SIT&E organization's vision and principles, build a culture that empowers and motivates individual excellence, and establish functional homeroom shared accountability for program/project performance.  Ensure projects are completed on schedule and within budget.  Perform technical planning and cost and risk analyses for total systems.  Section Manager responsibilities include working closely with the Department Manager and fellow section managers to implement department initiatives, ensuring successful planning and execution of program test engineering activities, collaborating with programs to satisfy staffing needs and provide operations support (incident investigations, metrics collection and analysis, etc.), setting goals and providing regular performance feedback to employees, supporting the annual salary review and promotion process, mentoring and developing the next generation of technical and project leadership, and improving and enforcing test engineering processes and procedures.

Program Role and Responsibilities include:
This position will entail maintaining smooth operation of multi-user System Integration Lab (SIL) environments and Software Engineering Lab (SEL). Efficient communication and shared understanding among stakeholders regarding relationships between system requirements and the system being developed, through precise engineering artifacts and traceability of designs to requirements.  Frequent contact with individuals from other departments, and/or outside organizations.  Candidate will be responsible for planning, executing and status programs to include technical performance.  Responsible for looking across the organization for efficiencies including reduction of current plans while still meeting program needs. 

Basic Qualifications:

  • Minimum of 9 years with Bachelor’s Degree in STEM; 7 years with a Masters
  • DOD Active Secret Required to start work
  • 3+ years Leadership experience
  • This position requires the ability to obtain and maintain a DOD Secret or Top Secret Clearance as well as Program Special access within a reasonable period of time, as determined by the company to meet its business needs
  • Experience using electrical test equipment, operating and maintaining electrical systems, and operating and maintaining mechanical aircraft systems

Preferred Qualifications:  

  • Current DoD Top Secret clearance
  • Current Program Access
  • In depth knowledge of system integration and test
  • Experience troubleshooting electrical systems associated with aircraft and ground power
  • Knowledge of Systems Test in a lab and/or on aircraft environment
