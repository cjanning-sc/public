Northrop Grumman is currently seeking a RF Systems Modeling Simulation Engineer/ Principal RF Systems Modeling Simulation Engineer located at our Northridge CA. location.

Northrop Grumman Defense Sector – Advanced Weapons Business Unit, Advanced Programs (AP) is designing, developing, integrating, and testing new capability for future  advanced missile systems for the current and future battlespace.

This position is to provide technical evaluation, and aid in development, integration, and testing of various Active RF sensors from different providers into these missile systems under the Active Seeker Technical Lead.

  • The successful candidate is the primary AP evaluator of active seeker suppliers.

  • They will work with supplier Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) and be responsible for providing technical evaluations to the Active seeker technical lead, the Chief Engineer, the Guidance IPT Lead, and the Customer's System Design and Seeker SMEs.

  • Candidates should have experience creating RF performance and operation models of

    • Radar and signal processing modeling & simulation

    • RF propagation from X through W bands

    • signal processing

    • Multipath effects

    • Ground clutter

    • Radar receiver architecture impacts

    • Traditional and modern signal processing techniques

    • Complex and adverse electromagnetic environments, including Jamming and spoofing

    • RF integration with the platform

    • Active radar integration with other RF sensors

  • This position will require up to 25% travel.

Basic Qualifications for the RF Systems Modeling Simulation Engineer level 2:

  • Must have a Bachelor of Science degree in a STEM discipline AND 2 years of related professional/military experience in Electronics Engineering to include a background in Radar/RF, OR a Master’s degree AND 0 years of related professional/military experience in Electronics Engineering to include a background in Radar/RF 

  • Must have an active DoD Secret or higher clearance (with a background investigation completed within the last 6 years or currently enrolled into Continuous Evaluation).

  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain Special Access Program (SAP) clearance. 

  • Must be on-site in Northridge, CA

  • Background in one or more of the following:

    • Radar design

    • Radar developmental testing

    • Radar integration and test

    • Traditional and modern Radar modes

    • Radar system modeling (IQ through signal processing and tracking)

Basic Qualifications for the Principal RF Systems Modeling Simulation Engineer level 3:

  • Must have a Bachelor of Science degree in a STEM discipline AND 5 years of related professional/military experience in Electronics Engineering to include a background in Radar/RF, OR a Master’s degree AND 3 years of related professional/military experience in Electronics Engineering to include a background in Radar/RF 

  • Must have an active DoD Secret or higher clearance (with a background investigation completed within the last 6 years or currently enrolled into Continuous Evaluation).

  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain Special Access Program (SAP) clearance. 

  • Must be on-site in Northridge, CA

  • Background in one or more of the following:

    • Radar design

    • Radar developmental testing

    • Radar integration and test

    • Traditional and modern Radar modes

    • Radar system modeling (IQ through signal processing and tracking)

Preferred Qualifications

  • Active DoD Top Secret clearance

  • Current/Active Special Program Access (SAP)

  • Background in the following:

    • Radar Integration, Assembly, Test and Checkout (IAT&C)
