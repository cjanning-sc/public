Principal Military Trainer - Technical Support Technician (TST) Lead
R10093086
1st Shift (United States of America)

Put your skills to the test by pushing the boundaries of what’s possible.  From global defense to sustainment and modernization to mission readiness, your experience and ability will make it a reality.  Our programs are built on equal parts of curiosity and collaboration.  Our combined effort means our customers can connect and defend millions of people around the world.  With Northrop Grumman, you’ll have the opportunity to be an essential part of projects that will define your career, now and in the future.

Northrop Grumman Defense Systems sector (NGDS) is seeking a Principal Military Trainer – Technical Support Technician (TST) Lead to join our growing team of diverse professionals supporting our III Corps Mission Command Training program at Fort Carson in Colorado Springs, Colorado. The TST Lead provides training units with the simulation environment and mission command stimulation that fulfills exercise planning documents provided by training units.

Responsibilities:

  • Interpret exercise planning documents to best install and configure hardware, software, and networks on physical or virtual systems running Microsoft Windows (Pro and/or Server) and RedHat Linux operating systems

    • If exercise planning documents do not provide sufficient details; the employee will research and use best judgement to fill in needed information for system configuration and operation

  • When system errors occur, the employee will evaluate error message(s), system / program logs, observed system operation deviations, etc. to best evaluate the problem(s) and attempt corrective action(s). If employee cannot find a corrective action, they will inform team manager of the issue, if issue(s) require outside support the employee shall author problem ticket(s) with sufficient descriptive detail to allow help desk to understand issue(s) at hand

  • Follow all applicable Information Assurance (IA) protocols defined by the Department of Defense (DoD) and our customer

  • Maintain all applicable DoD security technical implementation guides (STIGs), operating system and application patches for all systems under their responsibility

  • Ensure that backups of critical data are done within the capabilities and specifications provided by the customer

  • Maintain user accounts on system(s) to which the employee administrates under DoD and / or system specific authority to operate documentation

  • Serve as shift lead over junior systems analysts during shift work periods

  • Provide subject matter expertise in the areas of systems administration, military simulations, and stimulation of military tactical systems by simulation systems

  • Must be proficient with current and future versions of simulation operating software and hardware fielded to the Mission Training Complex

Basic Qualifications:

  • One of the following:

    • High school diploma or GED with a minimum of 10 years of Computer Science (CS), Information Technology (IT), Cybersecurity & Information Assurance (IA), or related experience

    • Bachelors of Science degree with a minimum of 6 years of experience in CS, IT, Cybersecurity & IA, or related experience

    • Masters of Science degree with a minimum of 4 years of experience in CS, IT, Cybersecurity & IA, or related experience

  • Current DoD Information Assurance Technical (IAT) level II certification (e.g. CompTIA Security+ CE)

  • Must be able to successfully meet all training required of DoD computing environments for all supported systems within 6 months of hire

  • Must have an active U.S. DoD Secret security clearance (or higher); applicant will be subject to a government security investigation

  • Operational support experience with Joint Land Component Constructive Training Capability (JLCCTC) in support of Corps and below level events

  • Experience as a federation manager of High-Level Architecture (HLA) and Distributed Interactive Simulation (DIS) training federations

  • Experience in the configuration and operation of RedHat Enterprise Linux (versions 6 & above) workstation and server and Microsoft Windows (10 & higher) workstation and server operating systems (2012 & higher) in bare metal and virtualized configurations. Emphasis on Linux setup and operations

  • Have a working knowledge of the tools and techniques necessary to mass deploy over a network 1 – 400+ Microsoft Windows and Linux systems on unformatted / un-configured hardware utilizing government provided tools

  • Able to work independently and act as staff lead when section manager is unavailable

  • Must be willing to work on-site at Fort Hood in Texas

  • Must be able to travel as needed (up to 10% of the time)

  • Must be able to support shifts that occur outside normal working hours to support 24/7 operations during exercise events that may be in excess of 40 hours per week

  • Employee will frequently move computer workstations and other equipment weighing up to 50 pounds around the worksite

    • Selected candidate will also need to be able to position self into areas of the facility above and below desks / tables to facilitate hookup of computer workstations and peripheral equipment

Preferred Qualifications:

  • A minimum of 5 years of experience with JLCCTC in support of Corps and below level events

  • A minimum of 5 years of experience as a Federation Manager of High-Level Architecture training federations

  • Certification in Linux OS such as CompTIA Linux+, Linux Foundation Certified System Administrator or Redhat Certified Systems Administrator

  • Experience with VMWare installation, configuration, and maintenance

  • Microsoft Certified Solutions Associate certification in Windows 10 and / or Server 2012
