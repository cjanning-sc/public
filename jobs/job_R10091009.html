Essential Functions
 

•    Responsible for overall safety of operations and shift employees.
•    Supervise day-to-day work of a shift of production operators, serving as a link between operators and management. 
•    Responsible for productivity, policy and procedure compliance, operational safety, environmental protection, labor management, and cost management.  
•    Must determine staffing on each shift and adjust based upon priority and availability.
•    Responsible for employee training.  
•    Responsible for procedure review and assuring changes in process are done properly and in a timely fashion. 
•    Responsible for implementation of new systems and procedures.  
•    Required to interact with engineering and maintenance on a routine basis.  
•    Responsible for determining repair needs of the operations and assuring maintenance work is done. 
•    Responsible for assuring all tools and materials necessary are available to employees.
•    Responsible for record keeping and for keeping management aware of all problems, incidents or other information required to operate the area.
•    Be an advocate for, and strictly follow, all NRE safety, quality, environmental, and production rules and procedures.
•    Responsible for communicating company goals, objectives, policies, and procedures to shift employees and assuring they are understood.

Physical Requirements to Perform Essential Functions
 

•    Bend and stoop frequently.
•    Lift up to 50 lbs. occasionally.
•    Stand, walk, and climb steps frequently.
•    Ability to safely operate a motor vehicle.
•    Ability to operate a computer. 
 

Key Abilities Required to Perform Essential Functions
 

•    Excellent communication and leadership skills.  
•    Excellent written communication skills, including ability to write reports and summaries of events.
•    Mathematical ability to calculate production metrics, including percentages and efficiencies. 
•    Ability to solve complex problems.
•    Proficiency in Microsoft Office Suite. 
•    Detail oriented. 
•    Ability to effectively lead a diverse group of workers, and exemplify Northrop Grumman’s code of conduct. 
•    Ability to function in a team environment.
•    Ability to train employees. 
•    Ability to carry out job assignment without direct supervision.
•    Commitment to operating safely, with environmental awareness, and with a focus on producing a quality product.

Basic Qualifications:

•    A Bachelor’s Degree with 6 years of experience, A Masters Degree with 4 years of experience OR 10 years’ experience in a manufacturing supervisory role.
•    Must possess a valid driver’s license.
•    Must be able to obtain clearance from the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, and Firearms and Explosives as an Employee Possessor.
•    Must pass RFAAP badging requirements, including background check and drug screening.
