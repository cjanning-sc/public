Put your skills to the test by pushing the boundaries of what’s possible.  From global defense to sustainment and modernization to mission readiness, your experience and ability will make it a reality.  Our programs are built on equal parts of curiosity and collaboration.  Our combined effort means our customers can connect and defend millions of people around the world.  With ACME Engineering, you’ll have the opportunity to be an essential part of projects that will define your career, now and in the future.

ACME Engineering Defense Systems sector (NGDS) is seeking a Military Training Manager 2 to serve as the Site Deputy and Exercise Planner for the III Corps Mission Command Training program at Fort Hood in Killeen, Texas. The selected candidate will provide expertise to units in the development and evaluation of military training and doctrine.

Responsibilities:

  • Responsible for designing and tailoring large exercises to meet training and technical requirements.

  • Represents the Fort Hood Mission Training Complex (MTC) at all in progress reports (IPR) for each exercise. 

  • Coordinates information flow across the MTC with both the local Government Staff and the Contract Staff to support training units through the TC 7-101 and to ensure the facility, simulations, networks connections are properly planned and executed. 

  • Coordinate with outside agencies to support large scale training events.  

  • Must have specialized expertise in planning and executing some or all phases of military training exercises, including doctrinal development, scenario development, and after-action review and analysis.

  • Maintains a working knowledge of Army, Forces Command (FORSCOM) and United States Army Training and Doctrine Command (TRADOC) training development programs and policies to determine how these may impact home station training requirements. 

Basic Qualifications:

  • One of the following:

    • High school diploma or GED with a minimum of 14 years of professional and/or military leadership experience

    • Bachelor’s degree with a minimum of 10 years of professional and/or military leadership experience

    • Master’s degree with a minimum of 8 years of professional and/or military leadership experience

  • Must possess an active U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) Secret security clearance

  • Knowledge of Corps and Division tactics, use of military graphics, tactical terms and acronyms

  • Knowledge of process to prepare Combat Operations

  • Knowledge of currently used simulations

  • Must be able to work on-site at Fort Hood in Texas

  • Must be able to travel as needed (up to 10% of the time)

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Army FA57 – Simulations Officer experience
