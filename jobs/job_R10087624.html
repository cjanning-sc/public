Join the Engineering team at Northrop Grumman Xetron, part of the Mission Systems sector, focused on development of new capabilities for our military and intelligence customers around the globe.  Northrop Grumman engineers are motivated by a deep desire to blaze new trails.  We enable our engineering teams to be technically excellent, design systems on the cutting edge, and explore capabilities that seem like they couldn't be done.  We are defining what’s possible. 

At our Cincinnati location, you will work in an environment that has a true small company culture.  Our variety of smaller projects wrestle with the latest, most challenging problems.  Our small teams work closely together in an atmosphere of collaboration and cooperation, sharing their expertise while also learning from other.  We share the challenge and success of each project and ensure our engineers are supported and challenged across programs. Having a variety of projects ensures consistent work for our engineering organization. 

We are seeking a cleared or clearable Staff Systems Engineer with a background in Electronic RF Hardware Design for a critical role as a key contributor and leader on hardware development teams. If you are always looking for the next problem to solve, this is the role for you. 

Essential Duties:

  • Drive the successful technical completion of projects.

  • Understand each project mission and architect the systems our teams are creating.

  • Be directly aware of and contribute to technical work while also ensuring the team is meeting requirements and documenting designs to demonstrate success.

  • Help guide our engineers from requirements through design and build, and finally through delivery and deployment.

  • Create novel solutions for challenging missions supporting our Nation as second to none!

Technology:

  • Projects range from RF hardware and SIGINT sensor systems to Electronic Warfare.

  • Most emphasize requirements for low size, weight, and power (SWAP) while functioning within extreme environments.

  • Novel RF waveforms

  • RF domains covering VHF through microwave frequencies
