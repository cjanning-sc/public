ACME Engineering is seeking a Sr Principal Cyber Architect to join our team. The position is onsite and based at a facility in Fort Wayne, Indiana.

This role is for a Lead System Administrator who will provide technical solutions to difficult System Administration and hardware problems, including assisting development and maintenance of internal and operational infrastructure, on-site installations, system training support, and maintaining strong working relationships with mission partners. The right applicant will be a team player among a diverse group of employees and this opportunity will be a great learning and growing position in supporting and leading technical SA tasks, program sustainment, system training, and cyber security support.

Become a part of our enthusiastic teams where we have fun working together and take pride in our contributions to the nation’s safety.

Basic Qualifications

  • 9 years of relevant experience with a Bachelor’s degree; 7 years of relevant experience with a Master’s degree; 4 years of relevant experience with a Ph.D.; 13 or more years of experience may be considered in lieu of a degree
  • Must have current/active DoD Top Secret/SCI with SSBI clearance or higher to be considered (**US citizenship required)
  • Must be willing to submit and obtain a Counterintelligence Polygraph (CI-Poly) upon hire
  • Current DOD 8570 IAT Level 2 baseline certification (example: Security+ CE); OR must have the ability to obtain one within 6 months of start date
  • Experience with Linux server administration
  • Ability to lift equipment weighing up to 40 pounds
  • Ability to support after hours and on weekends as needed

Preferred Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s or higher degree in a STEM-related discipline
  • Experience with Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL) server administration in an SCI-classified environment
  • Experience operating under and managing systems within NISPOM Chapter 8, DCID 6/3-ICD 503, RMF, STIG, JAFAN, or JSIG information system environments
  • Experience with NAS storage device (preferably NetApp) administration (ONTAP 8.x or higher; CDOT)
  • Solid understanding of Enterprise-level storage concepts to include all commonly used RAID-levels
  • Solid understanding of remediating security vulnerabilities
  • Experience hardening Linux systems in accordance with RMF and STIGs
  • Experience Identity Management (Single Sign-On)
  • Experience VMware and ESXi (6.x or higher) configuration and management
  • Experience with host-based firewall implementations and configuration (Redhat “iptables”)
  • Experience with: Windows (7, 10) administration; Windows Server; Group Policies (GPOs); Active Directory management
  • Solid writing skills and use of Wiki pages and Sharepoint sites for documentation
  • DoD 8570 Certification in a primary OS (Windows, Linux, Cisco)
  • Currently valid Security+ CE, CASP, CISSP, or similar security certification

This position will need to support after-hours and weekends as needed.  The typical work schedule is flexible and can accommodate either a 9/80 schedule allowing employees to work a nine-hour day Monday through Thursday and take every other Friday off, or a standard work week consisting of 5 8-hour days making up 40-hour weeks.
