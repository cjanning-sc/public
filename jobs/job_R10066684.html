Strategic Space Systems Division (SSSD) is an industry-leading provider of prime satellite and payload capabilities and directed energy and electronics solutions for national security, military, and civil customers. We are built on a heritage of providing innovative, affordable, and reliable aerospace and defense products that our customers rely on to achieve mission success. Join the Space revolution and make the impossible, possible.
 

Northrop Grumman Strategic Space Systems Division (SSSD) has an opening for a Staff Cable & Harness Electrical Engineer to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals in the Vehicle & Software Engineering Department. This position will be located in Redondo Beach, California.
 

The Staff Cable & Harness Electrical Engineer will be responsible for the architecture, development, and delivery of multiple cable/harness engineering design solutions. This role provides the opportunity to be a technical expert, take ownership of the CHE statement of work from concept design to product delivery, and to be one of the recognized Subject Matter Experts (SME’s) within the department for Cable and Harness. Responsibilities include (but are not limited to) the following:

  • Serve as CHE Lead in the development of various CHE products required for the assigned program
  • Define and document electrical interconnects through coordination with system, subsystem, and unit engineers
  • Pro-actively communicate with multiple other subsystems to negotiate interface requirements
  • Incorporate project flow down requirements into the harness/cable design
  • Prepare Preliminary Design Audit (PDA) and Critical Design Audit (CDA) data packages and support design reviews
  • Prepare and present harness subsystem design, architecture and implementation plans at various design team meetings and reviews
  • Assist in establishing harness/cable routing and segmentation
  • Perform voltage drop analysis and capacitance studies
  • Experience in RF transmission line analysis and performance
  • Prepare and release equipment specifications, manufacturing assembly drawings, and interconnect diagrams
  • Assure the integrity of the harness from inception through delivery
  • Prepare fuse module, fusistor module, diode module, resistor module assembly drawings and test procedures
  • Provide support to Manufacturing and Spacecraft Assembly and Test
  • Support Program Proposals as the Cable & Harness Lead
  • Mentor entry-level engineers
     

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in Electrical Engineering or related STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering or Mathematics) discipline from an accredited university with 14 years of engineering experience OR Master’s Degree with 12 years of engineering experience OR Ph.D. with 9 years of engineering experience.
  • Candidate must have (and be able to maintain) an active DoD Top Secret OR SCI clearance within scope of 5 years.  
     

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Previous experience with the successful design and delivery of spacecraft and/or aircraft DC harnesses and RF cables
  • Experience with Earned Value Management System (EVMS)
  • Detail-oriented and have excellent verbal and written communications skills, leadership skills, and organization skills
  • Extensive knowledge of military standards of connectors, wires, and contacts for space applications such as MIL-C-38999, MIL-C-24308, MIL-DTL-83513, MIL-STD-1553, MIL-HDBK-83575, DOD-W-83575
  • Proficient using harness design software tools such as Mentor Graphics, Routed Systems Design (RSD), CATIA and with Microsoft Access and Visio
