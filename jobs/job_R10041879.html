ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems has an opening for a Supply Chain Subcontracting Specialist/Principal Supply Chain Subcontracting Specialist to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position located in and based out of Philadelphia, PA. There is opportunity for this role to work in a hybrid remote work role, but will be required to come to the home office throughout the week as needed and planned in conjunction with direct manager.

This opening can be filled at the Specialist (level 2) or Principal Specialist (level 3).

In this role, the Supply Chain Subcontracting Specialist/Principal Supply Chain Subcontracting Specialist will be expected to: 

  • Sources and procures specialized goods and services. 

  • Owns contractual management for the supplier. 

  • Responsible for source selection from contract proposal to execution phases. 

  • Coordinates and collaborates with key stakeholders such as contracts, engineering, quality, supplier development, planning, operations, program and end-users. 

  • Develops subcontract specifications, work statements, and terms and conditions for the procurement of specialized materials, equipment, and/or services. 

  • Prepares bid packages, conducts bidders conferences, analyzes and evaluates proposals, negotiates subcontract provisions, selects or recommends subcontractors, writes awards (issues purchase orders or subcontracts through MRP system), and administers resulting subcontracts. 

  • In conjunction with quality organization, evaluates and monitors supplier quality and reliability, as well as supplier ability to support required delivery schedules. 

  • Identifies strategies and opportunities to reduce cost, improve efficiency and manage risk through strategic and targeted negotiations. 

  • Manages performance of subcontracts, ensures compliance with all public law requirements as well as company (and business) policies and procedures including import/export regulations. 

  • Employs sound business practices and assesses supplier performance as part of follow-up activities. 

  • Ensures that appropriate documentation is provided to logistics/transportation & warehouse organization so that proper receipt is recorded. 

  • Manages and develops strategic/key suppliers. 

  • Reviews and approves supplier payment as needed. 

  • Utilizes process tools such as Lean Six Sigma with suppliers to drive quality, efficiency and continuous improvement.

Level 2 - Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s degree and at least 3 years of related experience, Masters degree and at least 1 year related experience, or High School diploma or equivalent and 7 years minimum related experience. Prior related experience should include supply chain, contract administration, subcontracts, and/or federal government contracting.  

  • Basic working knowledge of FAR and DFARS regulations.

  • Skilled in Microsoft Office Programs (Word, Excel, Outlook, PowerPoint, Project)

  • Ability to obtain DoD Secret clearance

Level 3 – Basic Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s degree and at least 6 years of related experience, Masters degree and at least 4 year related experience, or High School diploma or equivalent and 10 years minimum related experience. Prior related experience should include supply chain, contract administration, subcontracts, and/or federal government contracting.  

  • Basic working knowledge of FAR and DFARS regulations.

  • Skilled in Microsoft Office Programs (Word, Excel, Outlook, PowerPoint, Project)

  • Ability to obtain DoD Secret clearance

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience with management of complex subcontracts, ideally supporting DoD subcontracts.

  • Experience issuing and managing purchase orders/subcontracts from beginning to end of life cycle.

  • Experience with SAP.

  • Excellent communication, interpersonal skills, and the ability to interface with all levels of employees and management

  • Active, DoD Secret Clearance
