Principal / Sr. Principal Electrical Engineer: Electrical Hardware Ground Support Equipment (EGSE) – Space / Satellite Systems (Dulles, VA)
R10066949
1st Shift (United States of America)

Due to significant program wins in the cislunar area, we are seeking an Electrical Ground Support Equipment (EGSE) Engineer.  This position will be in Dulles, VA.

In this role you will be responsible for designing test equipment (test racks, fixtures, selecting or designing digital/analog/serial and various signal interface cards, cables, etc.) to test and validate the operation of satellite components. You will work closely with hardware and software engineers, to understand component requirements and their designs, to create test set solutions that effectively and efficiently test them in both ambient and space conditions, such as thermal vacuum.

Job Responsibilities:

Under minimal supervision, you will be responsible for:

  • Working with the Cognizant Engineer and Test Engineer of the flight Hardware to obtain the Hardware Unit Under Test (UUT) Specification Requirements.
  • Developing requirements for testing of space vehicle components or UUT
  • Working closely with the spacecraft UUT Design Engineer to ensure the component test equipment (CTE)/ automated test equipment (ATE) system will validate the circuit cards and/or electronics box under all test environments
  • Work with the Test Engineer to develop the test plan for the UUT hardware environmental requirements.
  • Generate review material and support design reviews for the test equipment in development.
  • Hardware design of component test equipment (CTE) and automated test equipment (ATE) to test space vehicle components
  • Be responsible for ensuring that the technical requirements, cost, and schedule for the CTE/ATE system development is achieved
  • As part of a design team, be responsible for the end-to-end design, debug, test, and certification of CTE/ATE system
  • Have the responsibility for the documentation development and release of the CTE/ATE system
  • Generate Certification Test Procedures
  • Support of the test and debug for flight and non-flight spacecraft electronic circuit card assemblies (CCA) and boxes
  • To be successful in this role you should have:
  • Experience designing test hardware
  • Fundamental understanding of electrical circuit schematics
  • Working knowledge of Visio, CREO, and/or other mechanical design packages
  • Good communication skills
  • Experience working in a team
  • Experience leading junior engineers
  • Experience documenting and developing cable drawings/wiring diagrams

In this role, you will join a team of 2-4 EGSE Engineers interfacing with the spacecraft subsystem engineers, to obtain design requirements. Early on, there will be design work that requires documentation development in not only electrical design, but also some mechanical design, then support to an EDL (Engineering Development Lab) when the hardware is being built and assembled by the technicians. The next phase will be spent on integration and troubleshooting any problems on the product you helped to design and build to test the UUT.

This position can be filled at the Principal Engineer or Sr. Principal Engineer level.

Basic Qualifications:

Principal Engineer

  • Requires BS in Electrical Engineering with 5 years of experience; or Masters with 3 years of experience; or PhD
  • US Citizenship

Sr. Principal Engineer

  • Requires BS in Electrical Engineering with 9 years of experience; or Masters with 7 years of experience; or PhD with 4 years of experience
  • US Citizenship

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience designing hardware components for use in ATE/CTE
  • A fundamental understanding of general and specialized test equipment to include Signal Generators, Logic Analyzers, Oscilloscopes, Power equipment, and Digital Multimeter
