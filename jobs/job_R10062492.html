ACME Engineering Space Systems is seeking a Principal / Senior Principal Electrical Engineer to join the System Engineering Team. This position can be filled in Madison, AL or Detroit, UT and supports the Ground Based Strategic Deterrent (GBSD) program. Final grade level offered will be based on candidate level of experience.

What You’ll Get To Do:

This position is for an electrical engineer experienced in design for EMI/EMC compliance. The selected candidate will be expected to contribute to the Sentinel (GBSD) Electromagnetic Environmental Effects (E3) team in the areas of TEMPEST, EMI/EMC design, and test. Technical areas include Space and Launch Vehicles and associated Ground Control Systems.

Job duties may include:

•Perform system level decomposition of EMI/EMC requirements

•Develop EMI/EMC design requirements at sub-system level

•Provide support to the Design review process 

•Support hardware design engineering to ensure compliance with EMI/EMC requirements

•Development of the Electromagnetic Interference and Compatibility (EMI/EMC) test plans and test procedures to meet the Data Item Descriptions, and witness associated testing

•Contribute to the development of the Electromagnetic Environmental Effects (E3) Integration and Analysis Report (E3IAR), E3 Verification Procedures and Reports.

Job Qualifications:

You’ll Bring These Qualifications:

•Sr. Principal Engineer: Bachelor of Science degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Math) discipline and 9 years of experience; 7 years with Master’s Degree; 4 years with PhD

•Principal Engineer: Bachelor of Science degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Math) discipline and 5 years of experience; 3 years with Master’s Degree; 0 years with PhD

•Experience with design for EMI/EMC compliance and system level integration (bonding, grounding, shielding, filtering, transient suppression)

•Knowledge of MIL-STD-464; MIL-STD-461

•Ability to solve problems and drive tasks to completion

•Ability to present engineering findings orally and in written technical reports and presentations

•Experience in fast-paced work environment and ability to work in a team environment

•US citizen with ability to obtain a DoD Secret clearance

These Qualifications Would be Nice to Have:

•Prior weapon system experience (GBSD, Minuteman III or similar military or industry experience)

•Experience with EMI/EMC design and simulation at both system and hardware levels

•Experience in showing compliance to EMI/EMC requirements processes for equipment and sub-systems

•Technical ability to support decomposition of system level EMI/EMC requirements to sub-assembly and equipment levels.

•Experience with equipment, sub-system, and system level EMI/EMC test (Expertise in MIL-STD 461, automotive or commercial EMI/EMC testing)

•Ability to develop, document, maintain tailored EMI/EMC requirements at equipment, sub-system and system levels

•Systems engineering experience 

•Experience with modeling and simulation using SPICE, MATLAB and other electrical design codes

•Experience with TEMPEST

•A current DoD Secret clearance or higher granted or re-investigated within last 6 years
