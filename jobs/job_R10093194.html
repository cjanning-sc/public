Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems sector is searching for Air Vehicle Engineers specializing in one of the following Flight Sciences disciplines: Aerodynamics, Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), Vehicle Performance, Propulsion Integration, Mass Properties, or Configuration Design. Positions are in Melbourne, FL and relocation assistance is available.

Job Summary: 

Flight Sciences Engineers are responsible for the design, analysis, and testing of aircraft in the assigned discipline.  Successful candidates will thrive in a fast-paced work environment with high expectations, significantly diverse assignments, and collaborative team settings across all levels. Candidates must be self-starters capable of flourishing in an environment with minimal direction.

Responsibilities Include:

  • Aerodynamics: design, analyze, and test the outer mold line of air vehicles to meet customer requirements

  • Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD): specializes in modeling a flow field virtually to provide information for aerodynamic design, compute forces and moments and surface pressures

  • Vehicle Performance & Sizing: responsible for analyzing vehicle performance characteristics (takeoff and landing, mission profiles, maneuver) and providing sizing requirements for aircraft design

  • Propulsion Integration: integrate engine(s), inlet, and exhaust onto aircraft to meet air vehicle performance and mission requirements

  • Mass Properties: estimate and verify weight / center of gravity (CG) / and inertias to ensure requirements are met and vehicle weight is optimized

  • Configuration Design & Integration: create vehicle concepts that meet performance, mission, and cost objectives and balance the requirements of each discipline

Requisition may be filled as a Principal Engineer (level 3) or Sr Principal Engineer (level 4) depending on the qualifications below.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Principal Engineer: Bachelor's degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering or Mathematics (STEM) discipline with 5+ years relevant experience; OR Master’s Degree in STEM discipline with 3+ years relevant experience

  • Sr Principal Engineer:  Bachelor's degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering or Mathematics (STEM) discipline with 9+ years of relevant experience, or a Master’s degree in a relevant STEM discipline and 7+ years of experience

  • Demonstrated knowledge of or experience in one of the flight sciences disciplines

  • Excellent oral and written communication skills

  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Secret clearance and Program Access prior to the commencement of employment

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Current, active DoD Secret or higher clearance (with a background investigation completed within the last 6 years or currently enrolled into Continuous Evaluation)

  • Current, active Special Program Access (PAR/SAP)

  • Master’s degree in Mechanical or Aerospace Engineering or related discipline

  • Experience using Flight Sciences discipline-specific toolsets

  • Aircraft design and analysis experience
