Role Description:

The NGI Program Business Operations and Execution Manager will support the NGI Program Director, NGI Finance Director, and Program Execution Manager in all aspects of program and business management.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Manage planning of program execution activities.
  • Manage IPTs and/or Program Managers to execute program milestones.
  • Develop design to cost models.
  • Manage all proposal development from receipt of RFP, through estimating strategy, proposal, development, proposal submission and fact finding.  Special emphasis will be placed on negotiating undefinitized contract actions.  Support the program’s new business capture strategy, proposal development, negotiations, and ensure sound business deals within delegated parameters are achieved.
  • Perform program planning & control oversight that may require full EVMS, and/or other innovative reporting tools and approaches.  Interface with customers to ensure CDRLs, and all other financial and programmatic deliverables are timely and accurate.  Manage baseline efforts, and lead efforts for all Initial Baseline Reviews (IBR’s) with customers.
  • Perform monthly review of schedule and other metrics essential to award fee scores.  Present ongoing status to internal and external management.
  • Drive a high level of accuracy into program cost and schedule variance analysis.  Present ongoing status to internal and external management.
  • Perform, review, and present monthly, quarterly, and annual, financial and risk and opportunity data and reviews to all levels of management.  Candidate will be expected to provide valuable review, insight, and positions to Business Management, and Program leadership above and beyond simple reporting.
  • Exhibit a high degree of flexibility on fast changing, fast growing, and high visibility program(s).  Create, develop, present, and defend complex What If / Excursion analysis to all levels of management.
  • Lead interface with applicable customers, subcontractors and auditors (internal and external)
  • Interface with diverse functional areas to include but not limited to the Program Office, Contracts, Material Controls, Supply Chain Management, Scheduling and Engineering to ensure risks are understood and financial performance is achieved.   
  • Other duties to include leading program efficiency initiatives and other special projects to improve profitability and/or compliance.
  • Basic Qualifications:
  • Bachelors degree with minimum of 9 years related experience.
  • DoD/active clearance required to start.
  • Only apply if you have an active clearance
