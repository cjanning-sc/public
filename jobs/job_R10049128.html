Northrop Grumman Space Systems is seeking a Model Based Systems Engineering (MBSE) Architecture Engineer. This position will be located in Roy, UT and will support the Ground Based Strategic Deterrent (GBSD) program. This role may offer a competitive relocation assistance package. This requisition may be filled at a higher grade based on qualifications listed below.

What you’ll get to do: 

The Mission Defense Team (MDT) is seeking a highly motivated and qualified system engineer to serve as a Model Based Systems Engineering (MBSE) Architecture Engineer, Level 2 or 3.  The role includes applying the MBSE approach for systems engineering within Systems Security Engineering (SSE).

Additional Responsibilities include:

  • Support SSE MBSE strategy and implementation in the Weapon System (WS) Architecture Model
  • Utilize knowledge of DOORS requirements management tool
  • Define and execute model integration processes, tools, and techniques
  • Execute DoDAF standards and methods & SysML
  • Implement and enforce model governance at all levels of the architecture
  • Interfacing with the Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE) team to integrate DOORS with requirements and verification architecture models
  • Coordinate with integrated product teams for establishing content within the WS Architecture Model
  • Implement requirements and verification plan changes in DOORS and drive changes through Change Management (CM) process
  • Participate in a variety of working groups and customer meetings
  • Execute completion Statement of Work requirements, Program Milestone Exit Criteria, and program maturity commitments
  • Self-starters compelled to take action in the workplace without requiring prompting from supervisors
  • Support MDT with other duties as assigned
  • Travel: 10%
  • In addition to technical skills, you will be a self-starter with strong time management skills. Your organizational skills and ability to anticipate future challenges will serve you well.

You’ll bring these qualifications:

This position may be filled at a Systems Engineer or Principal Systems Engineer level

  • Bachelor's degree in Engineering or a related technical discipline
  • Systems Engineer requires a minimum 2 years of related experience with a Bachelor’s degree; 0 years with a Master’s
  • Principal Systems Engineer requires a minimum of 5 years of related experience with a Bachelor’s degree; 3 years with a Master in Science or 0 years with a PHD
  • U.S. Citizenship and an Active Secret Security Clearance or higher awarded or reviewed in the last 6 years, ability to obtain Top Secret Clearance and special program access
  • Model Based Systems Engineering (MBSE) experience using Cameo Enterprise Architect, MagicDraw, SysML or other comparable tools
  • Experience with DoDAF standards and methods, DOORS requirements management, and SysML
  • Minimum 2 years of applying and understanding Systems Security Engineering principles applicable to US Government Defense Programs
  • Minimum 2 years in showing the ability to communicate effectively and clearly present technical approaches and findings

These qualifications would be nice to have:

  • Active Top Secret clearance
  • A solid understanding of Program Protection applicable to US Government Defense Programs and applied knowledge in the application of SSE principles across a broad spectrum of security measures (Cybersecurity, Counterfeit Awareness, Anti-Tamper, HW/SW Assurance, OPSEC, etc.) to protect critical program information (CPI)
  • Experience working in an Agile team
  • Requirements Management within MBSE, Functional Block Diagrams, and Specification Trees
  • Minimum 2 years of applying and understanding systems and end item requirements for validation and verification
  • Ability to show self as team player, able to multi-task, able to generate quality work products independently, able to make excellent judgement and show interpersonal skills
