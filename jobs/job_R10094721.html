R10094721
Days (United States of America)

We have an opening for Engineering Manager – RF/COMMS to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals in the Tactical Space organization. This position will be located in Dulles, VA.  This position can also be performed with a hybrid work schedule.

This role is for a leader in an engineering organization that employs multi-disciplined engineering resources, tools, and processes to support the specification, design, implementation, verification, validation, and delivery of spacecraft related products.  These products include RF components, antennas, radios, RF systems analyses and integration and test.

You will report to and work with the RF/Communications department managers and be a key member of the Engineering leadership team working side-by-side with the Operations, Manufacturing, Integration and Test, Supply Chain, and Program Managers from all Business Areas.

To be considered, you must have a proven track record that includes technical leadership as well as broad technical exposure to the following skill sets:

  • FPGA – Radio/RF specific uses; waveform development, error correction, channelization, etc.
  • RF component design/test
  • RF system integration
  • RF system analysis – cosite interference, link budgets, etc.
  • “Payload manager”
  • Antenna design/analysis

DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

In this role you will be responsible and accountable for technical, schedule, and cost performance in the delivery of RF components and RF systems products to programs. You will provide RF/Comms Technical oversight by engaging in:

  • Proposal Development (including review and approval of Technical, Cost, Schedule baselines)
  • Engineering Review Board and CCBs
  • Technical peer and readiness reviews
  • Lessons Learned, Corrective/Preventative Actions, Continuous Improvement
  • IRAD Management

You will also assist the Sr. Manager by providing direction and leadership to the RF/Comms team for:

  • Employee Allocation, Utilization Planning, and Assignments
  • Recruiting
  • Training, Career Development, and Professional Growth
  • Personnel Communications
  • Software Tools, Computers, and Facilities and Equipment Infrastructure Support
  • Performance Evaluation and Salary Management
  • Proposal Support, Review, and Approval
  • Strategic Planning and Risk Management
  • Technical Standards and Engineering Processes
  • Increase Product Line Competitiveness through innovative and paradigm changing solutions in technology, cost, schedule, and mission designs
  • Drive common RF product solutions across all target mission areas
  • Evolve RF product capabilities to minimize complexity, reduce cost/schedule/risk while leveraging state of the art technologies
  • Identify, assess, and improve upon core competencies across the organization
  • Improve work force competitiveness through culture integration, talent management, and infrastructure investment
  • Improve the State of Health of the organization resources through active and engaging management processes (Personnel, Capital, Facility, Safety)

PERSONAL ATTRIBUTES

To be considered for this role you should have/be:

  • Good attitude, strong work ethic, and the ability to motivate and inspire others
  • Strong leadership style and excellent verbal, written, and presentation skills
  • Demonstrated ability to be adaptable to changing conditions and new organizational requirements.  Experience working in a fast paced, dynamic environment
  • Ability to think creatively and encourage innovation
  • Highly skilled in communicating issues, impacts, and corrective actions
  • Respectful of the views of others and diplomatic in handling difficult situations
  • Demonstrated ability to think clearly and act proactively in stressful situations
  • Decisive with the ability to weigh the positives and negatives of each decision

Basic Qualifications

  • Minimum 7 years of relevant experience as a technical leader or manager of an electrical engineering effort
  • Bachelor of Science degree in Electrical Engineering, Physics, or other technical field
  • Demonstrated ability to understand and troubleshoot RF systems and process related issues. Ability to manage failure review boards to successful completion in a timely fashion
  • Ability to effectively lead, train, mentor, and develop engineering professionals in all career phases
  • A strong engineering background with excellent organizational and communications skills
  • A view towards continuous improvement with ability to build effective relationships with company senior management and internal stakeholders
  • Ability to oversee projects effectively and to take personal responsibility for their completion on schedule and within budget
  • Ability to gather data from multiple sources and summarize key issues

Preferred Qualifications

  • Master of Science
  • Broad and in-depth experience in Spacecraft Engineering Development
  • Top Secret Clearance

The Northrop Grumman Tactical Space Division is a strategic partner specializing in commercial and classified partnerships with the design, delivery, operation and sustainment of satellites and human spacecraft.  We support science and space exploration through our various partnerships, including NASA’s Artemis program with the goal to return humans to the Moon in 2024 and the TESS (Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite) program that has discovered more than twenty confirmed plants.  Recognized as an industry leader, we also develop highly specialized space and satellite components.
