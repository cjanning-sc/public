Northrop Grumman is seeking a Paralegal for an exciting opportunity to join our Northrop Grumman Defense Systems (DS) Sector and primarily support the Global Sustainment and Modernization Division (GSM) in the McLean, Virginia area.  The position will be filled with either a Paralegal Level 3 or 4, the requirements for which are listed below.  The Paralegal will report to a Corporate Legal Director within Defense Systems Sector and support 2-3 attorneys, as well as occasional assignments for other DS Divisions or at the Sector or corporate level. 

Location: The preferred candidate will be hired and located at Northrop Grumman’s McLean, Virginia office, where the GSM division is headquartered.    There is a potential for a hybrid work schedule (some days in office and some days working remotely) but this is not a 100% remote job as onsite support and interaction with team members is important for career success.

Specifically, this position supports a broad range of duties and responsibilities in the rapidly growing defense market involving both Government and commercial customers.  

                                                           

Keys to success include:

  • Ability to solve problems independently.
  • Completing requirements in a fast-paced, multifaceted environment.
  • Proactive mindset, self-motivated, well organized and detail oriented.
  • Excellent communication and interpersonal skills.
  • Ability to exercise a heightened sense of confidentiality, discretion, integrity and professionalism in supporting a variety of sensitive matters often involving national security, large contractual matters and corporate matters. 

Roles and responsibilities of the Paralegal will include (but not be limited to) the following:

  • Interfacing with various levels of management, other paralegals, employees, lawyers, and outside parties.  
  • Conducting legal research.
  • Supporting negotiation of NDAs and contract terms and conditions on both the buy (supply chain organization) and sell (contracts organization) side.
  • Identifying and supporting process improvements in corporate and sector policies and procedures.
  • Drafting letters.
  • Assisting with all aspects of internal investigations and litigation.
  • Preparing training materials, briefings, and guidance documents.
  • Preparing and managing firewall documents and training.
  • Navigating legal and corporate systems, tools, and databases.

    

Basic Qualifications:

This requisition may be filled at either a level 3 or a level 4.

Basic Qualifications for a Level 3:

  • High School Diploma with a minimum of 4 years’ experience as a paralegal or in a legal related position in a law firm or corporate environment. College degree preferred.
  • Must be able to sit onsite in Mclean, Virginia
  • US citizenship

Basic Qualifications for a Level 4:

  • High School Diploma with a minimum of 6 years’ experience as a paralegal or in a legal related position in a law firm or corporate environment. College degree preferred.
  • Must be able to sit onsite in McLean, Virginia
  • US citizenship

Additional Basic qualifications for both levels:

  • Computer skills required include advanced expertise in Microsoft Office software (Word, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Excel), SharePoint (or equivalent), and intranet/internet proficiency.
  • Ability to work in a fast-paced environment and adaptable to changes in processes.
  • Ability to balance multiple, competing priorities, individuals and geographies.
  • Quality writing ability with attention to detail and solid grammar and proofreading skills.
  • Demonstrated oral communication skills with an ability to interact effectively across a large complex organization and with outside individuals.
  • Ability to execute tasks and projects independently.
  • Demonstrated ability to build and foster effective, collaborative relationships with others.

               

Preferred Qualifications:

Candidates with a combination of these desired skills will be given preferential consideration:

  • Bachelor’s degree.
  • Paralegal certificate or studies.
  • Experience working in the law department of an aerospace or defense company, federal government contractor, other large companies, and/or law firm experience.
  • Experience conducting on-line legal research, e.g., researching statutes, regulations, case law, government reports, etc.
  • Experience using complex electronic databases, budget analysis and billing systems.
  • Experience assisting lawyers with internal investigations, including document collection, interviews, reviews, and organization.
