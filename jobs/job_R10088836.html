Superconducting Electronics IC Design Engineer
R10088836
1st Shift (United States of America)

Are you interested in exploring a career in the cutting edge of IC circuit design? You'll leverage your current experience against our training and your team's wealth of knowledge with the goal of designing Reciprocal Quantum Logic (RQL) and superconducting devices in a highly collaborative team environment.

Northrop Grumman Mission Systems (NGMS) Advanced Capabilities division is a leader in the development and manufacture of next generation high-performance computing. Superconducting electronics form the core of our technology, with development thrusts in both quantum and classical logic. Our quantum computing activities include the development of superconducting devices for both quantum annealing and gate-model quantum computation. Our classical computing activities focus on energy-efficient computation using our patented superconducting digital logic technology, RQL. With a well-established impressive performance this technology is appealing for a variety of immediate applications and future research possibilities at a time when Moore’s law is dramatically slowing. You’ll work in a fast-paced team environment alongside a broad array of scientists and engineers to make these design solutions a reality.

We are seeking motivated Analog/Digital/Mixed Signal Circuit Design Engineers to join our team in the design and layout of custom digital superconducting circuits. You will use your skills as a Circuit designer while learning about superconducting circuit design. Tasks will include schematic capture, circuit simulation, circuit layout, physical verification (Layout vs. Schematic and Design Rule Checking), and parasitic extraction for a variety of superconducting circuits. The right candidate will be comfortable in a dynamic environment with rapidly developing technologies and tools. The candidate must have some experience in custom circuit design, preferably with Cadence Virtuoso. Experience with modeling circuit performance using Spice tools or ADE/Spectre, and/or experience with EM software is highly desirable. Strong written and oral communication skills are required.

Required Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering or related field with 2 years of experience in either Analog, Digital or Mixed Signal Circuit design (0 years with technical MS or a PhD)
  • Solid understanding of circuit design theory and related coursework; hands-on custom circuit design experience
  • Strong problem solving and analytical skills
  • Experience with some of the following: custom IC design tools (e.g. Cadence or Tanner EDA) for schematic capture, circuit simulation, and custom layout; physical verification using Cadence Assura or Mentor Calibre
  • Strong communication skills, able to efficiently disseminate information to leadership and respective working group
  • Ability to obtain and maintain a Top-Secret clearance per business requirements. US Citizenship is a requirement for this.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Working knowledge of analog/mixed-signal IC design flow including schematic capture, simulation, layout and physical verification (DRC/LVS)
  • Microwave engineering design background; solid understanding of transmission line theory and matching circuitry
  • Electromagnetic modeling background employing commercial tools, such as HFSS, Clarity, or CST
  • Understanding of combinatorial and sequential logic; experience working with digital building blocks
  • Knowledge of Quantum Information or Quantum Mechanics
