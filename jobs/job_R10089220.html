Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems (NGAS) has an opening for a Manager Software Engineering 2 (S02) position. The selected candidate will be responsible for the functional management of the software engineering personnel under the NGAS West Coast Autonomy/Modeling Simulation (ModSim) Software Department.

The selected candidate will solve difficult programs while demonstrating professionalism and leading by example. Will build or maintain diverse/inclusive teams with engaged team members. Will have knowledge and understanding of standard NGAS software engineering processes. Will have time management and organizational skills to prioritize across concurrent program and functional responsibilities. Statement Of Work (SOW) will include managing a team for Integration & Test (I&T) activities or developing Artificial Intelligence/Machine Learning (AI/ML) applications/services. This position will be located at El Segundo, CA.

Duties Include:

  • Conducting performance assessments, staffing, employee development, communication meetings, disciplinary actions, compensation adjustments, training & educational oversight/ approval, and other routine personnel issues.

  • Responsible for establishing common processes and practices in cooperation with colleague managers across the sector.

  • Develop requirements and negotiate with the customer where needed; develop designs and software in a team environment; and be able to provide technical guidance for junior engineers, provide leadership integrating software in a laboratory environment, and able to develop the complete documentation set required for the software life cycle.

  • This role will work closely with program-assigned systems engineering, software development, and management professionals to ensure that the software lifecycle is accomplished with high quality and efficiency.

  • Ability to track both the quality and timeliness of the organization’s products, and identification and remediation of potential issues before they become problems.

  • Develop and manage detailed budgets and schedules and make work assignments for indirect tasking.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering or Mathematics (STEM) discipline from an accredited university and 9 years of related engineering experience OR Master’s Degree with 7 years of software engineering experience.

  • Candidate must have, and be able to maintain, at least ONE of the following active U.S. Government security clearances:

  • Department of Defense Secret level with a background investigation date within 10 years OR Department of Energy L clearance with a background investigation date within 10 years.

  • Solid understanding of software engineering best practices and the software development lifecycle: requirements gathering, design, development, documentation, testing, and deployment.

  • Experience in technical leadership or management.

  • Experience with leading or working in Agile teams.

  • Excellent communication skills and the ability to work effectively with engineers and stakeholders.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Prefer candidate to have and be able to maintain, at least ONE of the following active U.S. Government security clearances:

  • Department of Defense Top Secret level with a background investigation date within 6 years OR Department of Energy Q clearance with a Single Scope Background Investigation (SSBI) completed within 5 years.

  • Demonstrated recent experience on developing Autonomy and/or Modeling & Simulation software.

  • Prior experience in engineering functional management and demonstrated coaching skills.

  • Experience with software design for autonomous unmanned vehicles (either real time embedded software or command and control interface).

  • Experience in full-stack web development, including frontend and backend development using technologies/frameworks/languages such as React, Vue, Angular, Node, JavaScript, and Typescript.

  • Experience with noSQL databases (MongoDB, Neo4j) and building REST or GraphQL APIs.

  • An understanding of both software and hardware concepts to support requirements, architecture, design, development, integration, and test.

  • Familiarity with Model Based System Engineering (MBSE) concepts.

  • Familiarity with Dev*Ops concepts, technologies, and best practices.

  • Prior experience in engineering management or technical leadership or mentoring.

  • Experience in modeling and simulation in a laboratory environment.

  • Familiar with standards as UCI/OMS.
