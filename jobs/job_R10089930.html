Northrop Grumman Strategic Space Systems Division (SSSD) is an industry-leading provider for prime satellite and payload capabilities and directed energy and electronics solutions for national security, military, and civil customers. We are built on a heritage of providing innovative, affordable and reliable aerospace and defense products that our customers rely on to achieve mission success. Join the Space revolution and make the impossible, possible.

SSSD is seeking a qualified leader for a Sub-Project Manager (Electronics Engineering Manager 3) opportunity within Operations. The selected Individual is responsible for cost and schedule performance and overall execution for P093 RF hardware package within Ground and Electronics Manufacturing (GEM) and Space Manufacturing and Engineering (SM&E). This position is located at our Space Park Campus in Redondo Beach, CA.

The Sub-Project Manager (SPM) will be responsible for production execution and delivery of this program work package across all manufacturing areas within SSSD, including mechanical fabrication, assembly and test of modules, board assemblies, slices, and final configured items. 

In this role you:

  • Serve as primary interface to program leadership for all electronic and mechanical manufacturing on ~$120M RF P093 work package

    • Includes coordinating inputs from all manufacturing areas for baselining, IBR, cost and schedule reporting and updates, manufacturing plan, proposals, CEACs as required

    • Accountable for cost and schedule variance explanations and EV methodology

  • Manage risk, issues, and opportunities across work package, escalate to program and functional leadership as needed

  • Provide leadership for forward strategic manufacturing plan, incorporating inputs from each manufacturing area to result in the best plan for program work package

  • Manage critical path and overall work package priorities

  • Determine staffing plan for support organizations: such as MPS, OPM, MRP-make, PMI-make, PC.

  • Coordinate and escalate help needed from other organization: such as Engineering, Quality, GSC, PM&P, etc.

  • Provide inputs to coordinate procurement / receiving priorities

  • Demonstrate leadership qualities and ability to motivate and engage cross discipline teams to execute efficiently

  • Individual will be key stakeholder, and may be required to lead Tiger Teams and lead various proposal efforts within organization

  • A minimum five (5) years experience leading people, project, or programs with technical, cross-functional teams
