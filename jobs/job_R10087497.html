The Test Engineering Technician:

  • Sets up, runs and troubleshoots (down to the component level) test sets for a wide variety of programs

  • Supports technicians through troubleshooting Hardware and Test Sets
  • Mentors and trains new technicians and experienced technicians on newer test sets
  • Gathers and analyzes test data to identify early trends
  • Supports program meetings in developing root cause and corrective actions
  • Collaborates with Test Engineering to develop and implement hardware and test system improvements and solutions
  • Assists engineering in creating designs and/or developing circuits, processes and operations that assure proper function of product and test equipment per design specifications by working from general block diagrams, rough outlines of basic circuits, oral instructions, schematics, etc; makes necessary calculations to obtain or compare data as required
  • Develops, constructs and supports test of highly complex components, assemblies, devices, circuits and systems; develops and constructs experimental and prototype devices, models and systems working from very general information; performs repairs and calibrations as necessary
  • Performs initial procedural tests on new and/or changed equipment; makes modifications/calibrations as required to achieve desired results
  • Assists Test Engineering with the development of processes, test procedures, and revision notices
  • Performs integration testing of software and hardware
  • Adheres to established procedures and practices as required to ensure compliance

The ideal candidate will have the ability to effectively communicate and work with a variety of internal personnel from Test, Manufacturing, Quality, and Design engineering. He/she should also be self-directed, organized, and team oriented.

Basic Qualifications:

  • High school graduate or recognized equivalent (GED) and an additional 2 years of relevant education and/or experience
  • Experience using MS Office tools
  • Experience as an electronics technician or equivalent
  • Must be able to obtain and maintain a DoD clearance; US citizenship is required.


Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience with Power Control Systems and Hardware
  • Experience working on Naval systems.
  • Experience with ERP/MRP systems. Example: SAP
  • Experience with configuration control
  • Experience training and/or mentoring other personnel
  • Component level troubleshooting experience
  • System Level troubleshooting experience
  • Test Procedure development experience
  • Test Tool development experience

  • Experience with LabView software
