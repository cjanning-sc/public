ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems has openings for Manufacturing Engineers to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals.  The selected candidates will support process design and respond to production issues related to aircraft manufacturing (composite, metallic, and assembly).   These positions will be located at our Lansing, Michigan facility.  

Want to work on the cutting edge of aerospace?  Come join us!

Responsibilities:

  • Develops manufacturing process structure
  • Identifies process improvements, captures feedback from Operations, and incorporates into the manufacturing process
  • Reviews design changes and specification changes and ensures they are captured in the manufacturing process
  • Works with Process engineering to Create/Modify existing planning work instructions
  • Promptly responds to technical issues that arise on the production floor utilizing work instructions, design models, and specifications
  • Clarifies work instructions to Operations and crafts required modifications as needed
  • Documents issues and disseminates information to core manufacturing engineering for incorporation into future releases
  • Resolves issues by collaborating with other resource groups as needed
  • Gets involved in MRB repairs and special processes to assist Operations as needed
  • Performs PFMEA and RCCA investigations
  • Creates and maintains MBOMs as needed by the program
  • Travel is minimal, if at all
  • Plans sequence of fabrication, assembly, installation, and other manufacturing operations relating to specific portion of the product for guidance of production workers
  • Plans detail operations from blueprints, engineering orders, change notices, and other engineering releases

The ideal candidate thrives in a fast-paced work environment with diverse assignments.  Our team is highly collaborative, working in team settings across all levels and subject areas including operations, engineering, supply chain, and mission assurance.  We enjoy working in a production environment and being hands on to solve problems that influence company performance.

This role may be filled at a Level 1 or Level 2 based on the qualifications below.

Basic Qualifications

Associate Manufacturing Engineer (Level 1)

  • Bachelor of Science Degree in STEM and 0 years of relevant work experience. Bachelor’s Degree (non-STEM) with 2 years of experience.Please note an Associates Degree or High School Diploma/GED with 4 years of work experience in production assembly/fabrication including composites, metallic, coatings application, and/or production manufacturing operations may be considered in lieu of a degree
  • Ability to obtain a DoD Secret Security Clearance and Special Program Access within a reasonable period of time as determined by the needs of the business

Manufacturing Engineer (Level 2):

  • Bachelor of Science Degree in STEM and 2 years of relevant work experience, Bachelor’s Degree (non-STEM) with 4 years of experience OR Master’s Degree and 0 years of relevant work experience.  Please note an Associates Degree or High School Diploma/GED with an additional 4 years of work experience in production assembly/fabrication including composites, metallic, coatings application, and/or production manufacturing operations may be considered in lieu of a degree
  • Ability to obtain a DoD Secret Security Clearance and Special Program Access within a reasonable period of time as determined by the needs of the business
  • Prior exposure to Aerospace manufacturing

Preferred Qualifications

  • Active DoD Secret Security Clearance
  • Experience in manufacturing engineering or design engineering
  • Work instruction planning experience
  • Liaison / Floor production engineering experience
  • Experience with composite layup and curing processes
  • Experience with aircraft assembly of composite, aluminum, and titanium components
  • Experience with wiring and hydraulic installation
  • GD&D experience in aircraft manufacturing
  • Experience with formal root cause and corrective action processes (PFMEA, Fault Tree, etc.)
  • Fluent with PDM, MRP, and EWI business systems
  • Proficiency with NX11 or higher
  • Preference will be given to individuals already accessed to Special Access Programs
