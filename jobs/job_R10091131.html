ACME Engineering is in need of a Propulsion Performance Analyst Engineer to work at its Naperville, Illinois location.

Responsibilities:

  • Under the direction of manager/senior analysts, conduct performance analyses in support of solid rocket motor propulsion system design and verification activities.
    • Develop mathematical models to match the physics of the system under evaluation including proper analysis methods/tools, proper boundary conditions, and appropriate material properties
    • Participate in training to develop technical expertise in solid propellant analysis and design
    • Work within peer review environment to build experience in group technical reviews
  • Provide technical assessments of test hardware and test data including validation of analysis
  • Support propulsion and subsystem team leads to ensure technical and cost execution of each system component.
  • Prepare and present technical reports and presentations of work product to both internal and external customers.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Ability to obtain a Secret Clearance which requires US Citizenship as a pre-requisite
  • Bachelor’s Degree in STEM plus two years of related experience or a Master’s Degree in STEM and 0 or more years of experience.  Preference given for degrees in Mechanical or Aerospace Engineering.
  • Knowledge of system modeling tools such as MATLAB/Simulink, or equivalents.
  • Demonstrated ability to prepare and present analytical results in both oral and written formats.
  • Excellent interpersonal, communication, writing, presentation, and critical thinking skills.
  • Working knowledge of Microsoft Office suite (Excel, Word, Power Point).
  • Position will occasionally require travel to meet with various customers, in both government and industry, and to interface with suppliers.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Current Active Security Clearance
  • Prior experience or coursework in solid rocket motor design and analysis
  • Experience using NX solid modeling software, Solid Performance program (SPP)
