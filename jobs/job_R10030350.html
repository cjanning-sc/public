ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems has an opening for an Engineering Manager 2 to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals located in rural Minneapolis, MS.

The assignment includes responsibility for managing technical, schedule, cost, and resources across multiple programs and may include tooling development projects supporting manufacturing needs such as hand layup, fiber placement, assembly, machining, painting, and structural testing of composite parts. Minneapolis currently produces structures for Atlas, Delta, Antares, F-35 and other high-end related parts. Some of our work requires Secret Clearance. Our Minneapolis facility employs about 200 people across Engineering, Operations, Quality, and Support Services. The work we do affects our national security. Does this sound exciting to you? Come join us and be a part of a rich tradition of aerospace innovation!

Responsibilities include the following:

  • Provide technical leadership and oversight of engineering staff
  • Support staff with the appropriate resources to solve problems
  • Track department schedules, working to identify key parameters and metrics to increase efficiency and reduce program costs
  • Plan manpower requirements, performing quarterly and annual performance reviews of staff

Our engineering team works across multiple programs, which are broken out by program and product families. You will guide your team to meet our customer needs and deliver quality products in a timely manner. Ownership and pride in work are important to our employees. Are you ready to lead and empower a team to succeed? We trust and empower our folks to make decisions affecting our daily work.

This position is for a day shift (Monday to Friday), and may require off-hour support. ACME Engineering offers a full suite of benefits including health, retirement, and wellness initiatives. We are regularly ranked as a top company for work life balance!

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelors degree and 7+ years of engineering related work experience, or a Masters degree and 5+ years of engineering related work experience
  • Ability to obtain and maintain a Secret clearance and Special Program Access
  • Experience with cross-functional engagement and building relationships with internal and external customers

Preferred Qualifications:

  • A Bachelors in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering or Math) field, or a Masters in a STEM field
  • Active DoD security clearance
  • Demonstrated work experience leading diverse teams and building teams from the ground up
  • Working knowledge of composite manufacturing techniques
  • Knowledge of continuous improvement
  • Demonstrated broad skill set that is balanced between interpersonal, business and technical capabilities
