The Associate Industrial Security Analyst develops and administers security programs and procedures for classified or proprietary materials, documents, and equipment. Studies and implements federal security regulations that apply to company operations. Obtains rulings, interpretations, and acceptable deviations for compliance with regulations from government agencies. Prepares manuals outlining regulations, and establishes procedures for handling, storing, and keeping records, and for granting personnel and visitors access to restricted records and materials. Conducts security education classes and security audits. Investigates security violations and prepares reports specifying preventive action to be taken.

Basic Qualifications:

  • High School Diploma/GED
  • Candidate must have an be able to maintain an Active FINAL U.S. Government security clearance at the DoD Secret level with a background investigation date within the 6 years
  • Experience in file/data management or Personnel Security
  • Strong attention to detail
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills
  • Ability to work in a rapid and dynamic work environment
  • Ability to obtain and maintain Special Access Programs

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree
  • Minimum 1 year of Security experience
  • Ability to work independently with minimum supervision
  • Working knowledge of the NISPOM and DoD SAP Manuals
  • Working knowledge of JPAS/DISS and Northrop Grumman Security Business Systems        
  • Experience working on SAP programs
  • CDSE certifications
