Corporate Director 1, Independent Cost Evaluation
R10090102
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Corporation is currently seeking a Director 1 of Independent Cost Evaluation.   This individual will report to the corporate Director 2 of Independent Cost Evaluation out of our Falls Church, VA office. The Director 1 of ICE is responsible for supporting and leading, as assigned by direct management, independent cost & business management evaluation reviews.  This individual will also be responsible for leading various independent cost and business management evaluation reviews called for by the Corporate C101 process.  These efforts will be in support of all sectors and all business activities within those sectors.
 

Specific responsibilities include, but are not limited to:

  • Identify and evaluate proposal risk issues in cost estimates, terms and conditions, and all other business management related functions. 
  • Oversee the work contributions of a team of individuals.  Provide objective/results oriented direction to the team including planning, project performance management, and budgeting.
  • Support and assist the Corporate Director 2 ICE in executing the company-wide ICE process.
  • Coordinate, when necessary, Northrop Grumman Corporate Office teams including Legal, Audit, Treasury, Enterprise Shared Services (ESS), and IT on areas including logistics management, budget oversight, regulatory compliance, and corporate policy implementation.
  • Maintain Sector relationships and coordinate ICE resources.
  • Approve and present ICE analyses submitted in support of risk reviews.
  • Participate in the review and approval of Risk Reviews submitted for Executive Office approval.
     

Relocation, as well as hybrid work arrangement, may be available for the successful candidate.  If primary work environment is fully virtual, the role will require 10% to 20% travel throughout the year at a minimum for meetings at our Corporate Office in Falls Church, VA.
 

Basic Qualifications - to be considered for this position, you must minimally meet the knowledge, skills and abilities listed below:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Finance, Accounting, Business Management or a related field with minimum 10 years of relevant professional or military experience.  Appropriate equivalent combination of education and experience will also be considered.
  • Must possess at least a Secret clearance with the ability to obtain a Top Secret/SCI security clearance.
  • Ability to travel up to 40%+ of the time.
  • Demonstrated expertise in business management, cost/ risk modeling, and government contracts.
  • Excellent communication skills, both written and verbal, with the ability to use them in informing, presenting, negotiating and problem solving with a wide range of people.
  • Demonstrated ability to support complex operations while collaborating with multiple stakeholders.
  • Dynamic, self-starter with strong attention to detail and the ability to function independently.
  • A flexible approach to work, an understanding of working collaboratively on a team, and the willingness to be an active and supportive member of a cross-functional team.
  • Strong problem solving and analytical skills; ability to make recommendations and provide guidance to effectively bring projects to completion.

Preferred Qualifications – candidates with these desired skills will be given preferential consideration:

  • Master’s degree in Finance, Accounting, Business Management, or a related field from an accredited educational institution
  • Cross-sector experience
  • 15 years relevant experience including exposures in the cost/business management/ops research world, with a strong knowledge of quantitative risk techniques, cost and risk modeling and government contracts.
  • Experience in the Aerospace industry
  • Active/transferable Top Secret/SCI security clearance
  • Local to Falls Church, VA
