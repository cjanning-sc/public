Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems has an opening for an Engineer - Systems Modeling and Simulation OR Principal Engineer – Systems Modeling and Simulation to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located at our Palmdale, CA site, where technology and teamwork come together.

The selected candidate will use modeling and simulation tools and apply probability-based analytics to analyze current and projected spare parts inventories and optimize recommendations for key metrics. This roll requires frequent collaboration across functional disciplines, offering a highly unique opportunity to obtain a broad understanding of many areas. The Systems Modeling and Simulation Engineer should possess strong communication and technical writing skills, as well as the ability to handle multiple tasks.

Skills in the areas of statistics, simulation modeling, trend & sensitivity analysis, data sampling, and computing are essential.

Modeling & Simulation analysis tasks will include:

  • Spares Modeling
  • Readiness Modeling
  • Obsolescence forecasting
  • Level of Repair (LOR) analysis
  • Cost Analysis
  • Business Case Analysis

The selected candidate should thrive in a fast-paced work environment with high expectations, significantly diverse assignments, collaborative/team settings across all levels.

This position may be filled at either the Engineer Systems Modeling and Simulation OR Principal Engineer Systems Modeling and Simulation level.

Basic Qualifications for Engineer Systems Modeling and Simulation:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics (STEM) discipline from an accredited university and 2 years of experience OR a Master’s Degree in a STEM discipline and 0 years of experience
  • Must have advanced proficiency with Microsoft Excel (extensive formula calculations, pivot tables/charts, macros, etc.)
  • Must have an Active, In-Scope, DOD Secret Security Clearance or higher with periodic review or re-investigation within the last 5 years required to start.
  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain Special Access Program Access (PAR/SAP). This PAR/SAP must be obtained within a reasonable amount of time as determined by the company to meet its business needs.

Basic Qualifications for Principal Engineer Systems Modeling and Simulation:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics (STEM) discipline from an accredited university and 5 years of experience OR a Master’s Degree in a STEM discipline and 3 years of experience OR a PhD in a STEM discipline with 0 years of experience.
  • Must have advanced proficiency with Microsoft Excel (extensive formula calculations, pivot tables/charts, macros, etc.)
  • Must have an Active, In-Scope, DOD Secret Security Clearance or higher with periodic review or re-investigation within the last 5 years required to start.
  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain Special Access Program Access (PAR/SAP). This PAR/SAP must be obtained within a reasonable amount of time as determined by the company to meet its business needs.

Preferred Qualifications for both levels:

  • Active TS with Special Program Access
  • Experience in aviation and defense programs
  • Project management experience
  • Familiarity with Databases concepts, Microsoft Access, Microsoft SQL Server, or Oracle
  • Experience with spares forecasting, availability modeling, repair level strategy modeling, reliability, or maintainability
  • Experience with data mining techniques
