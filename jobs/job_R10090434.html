1st Shift (United States of America)

This position can be a remote position or hybrid position in Baltimore, MD or other NGMS locations to include Annapolis, MD, Apopka, Fl, Bethpage, NY, Charlottesville, VA, Huntsville, AL, Mclean, VA, Rolling Meadows, IL, Salt Lake City, UT, San Diego, CA, or Sykesville, MD, where the individual will be responsible for the management of hardware and services programs for the LMS (Land and Maritime Sensors) Business Unit of the MLSS (Maritime/Land Systems & Sensors) Division.  Manages as well as oversees and coordinates requirements assignments across the Business Unit. Supports and leads special projects that requires advanced knowledge and experience of Supply Chain planning and procurement processes.  Participates in proposal efforts including complex source selection, strategic supplier management and negotiations. Provides mentorship and training to junior team members with planning processes. Coordinates and collaborates with key stakeholders such as contracts, engineering, quality, supplier development, planning, operations, program office disciplines in program level Supply Chain strategies and plans. Ensure the timely placement and receipt of all material requirements to meet those strategies and plans in support of program requirements including complete material management / ownership (schedule, cost and Material EAC). This candidate may be responsible for multiple programs, which will have numerous variations in responsibilities from simple to complex. Utilizes process tools internally and with supplier(s) to drive quality, efficiency, and continuous improvement.

Manages Requests for Quotations and Proposals and ensures timely receipt, analyzing proposed pricing from potential supplier(s). In conjunction with manufacturing and quality organization, evaluates and monitors supplier quality and reliability, as well as supplier ability to support required delivery schedules. Works with internal customers to ensure specifications are appropriate for the services or products required. Identifies opportunities to reduce cost, improve efficiency and manage risk through targeted negotiations.  Manages performance of purchase orders, ensures compliance with all public law requirements as well as company (and business) policies and procedures including import/export regulations, if applicable. Employs sound business practices and assesses supplier performance as part of follow-up activities. Ensures that appropriate documentation is provided to logistics/transportation & warehouse organization so that proper receipt is recorded. Reviews and approves supplier(s) payment as needed.
