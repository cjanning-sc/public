Staff Digital ASIC Physical Design Engineer
R10092802
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Mission Systems (NGMS) Advanced Processing Solutions is working to develop next generation high performance computing systems to meet the mission demands of the NGMS Networked Information Solutions (NIS) business. Superconducting electronics forms the core of our technology, with a focus on novel means of energy-efficient computation. Qualified individuals will join our team in its mission to transform computing beyond Moore’s Law, advancing development of computer architectures, processing/memory subsystems, and large scale high performance computing systems. You’ll work in a fast-paced team environment alongside a broad array of scientists and engineers to make these processing solutions a reality.

This work will be done onsite at our Advanced Technologies Lab in Linthicum, MD.

Overview and Duties:

Responsible for complete design, working with the RTL designer creating the code and working the analog and RF designers to integrate the digital core with elements of the signal path. Utilize foundry approved verification tools such as Calibre for LVS/DRC.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree in Electrical or Computer Engineering, or other related disciplines
  • Minimum Education or Experience - 14 Years with Bachelors in Science; 12 Years with Masters; 9 Years with PhD.
  • Experience with synthesis of gate level logic and incorporating compiled macro blocks such as memories as required
  • Floor-planning, followed by place and route of the physical layout
  • US Citizen and able to obtain and maintain a Top-Secret security clearance

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Delivery of multiple verified digital IC design projects
  • Design of power and clock distribution networks
  • Experience following a rigorous design process with checkpoint reviews
  • Experience in CAD design network, tool configuration, and data management
  • Current TS/SCI security clearance
