ACME Engineering has an exciting opportunity for an Engineer for Energetics Manufacturing at our Elk River, Minnesota location.

Headquartered in Plymouth, Minnesota, Northrup Engineering is a leading innovator and producer of advanced weapons that provide the warfighter superior battlefield capabilities. The division specializes in creating low-cost, highly reliable and precise munitions for artillery and mortar systems and is a world leader in developing and producing tactical and training ammunition for medium and large caliber weapon platforms.  Armament Systems also creates systems that protect soldiers on the battlefield and reduce collateral damage.

The division is a leading supplier of integrated medium-caliber weapon systems for land, air and sea platforms through its world-renowned Bushmaster® line of automatic cannons produced at its Owensboro, Kentucky facility.

Through its management of America’s largest, privately owned proving grounds, Armament Systems allows Northrup Engineering and its customers the ability to rapidly test weapons and demilitarize them when necessary.

Position Description:

Provide overall technical leadership for our Load, Assemble, Pack (LAP) organization.  Act as a Liaison Engineer to coordinate between Northrup Engineering internal functional disciplines including Program Personnel, Engineering, Supply Chain, Quality and Test.  Ensures / reviews product design for compliance with engineering principles, company standards, and related specifications.  Create or update and publish all necessary documentation required to support program builds including but not limited to production procedures (SOP’s, DOP’s, PPD’s,), work authorization documents (TR’s, SWA’s) and support of Hazard Analysis.

Additional responsibilities include support and completion of engineering and capital projects within area of assigned responsibility.  Applies knowledge of engineering and scientific methods, theories and research techniques in the investigation and solution of technical problems and implementation of appropriate automation solutions.

Typical Duties:

Typical duties will include but not be limited to the following:

  • Coordinate and provide Engineering interface to support program build activity with Supply Chain, Test, Logistics, Maintenance and LAP operations.
  • Provide technical disposition on non-conforming product from internal and external sources
  • Provide Engineering support / design help on tooling and gages specific to Northrup Engineering product lines.
  • Inform and work with Program Engineering teams on design and/or manufacturability issues
  • Present Northrup Engineering technical image and act as a technical point of contact on all assigned areas
  • Provide and publish proper build documentation to support assigned programs

Essential Functions:

Responsibilities will include but not be limited to the following:

  • Technical understanding of mechanical mechanisms, manufacturing processes, metal coatings, heat treatments, processing technologies and standard assembly processes.
  • Accountable for the successful completion of engineering and capital projects.
  • Requires extensive regular contact with assigned project customers, both internal and external.
  • Assist in the preparation of engineering project proposals.
  • Work with programs and engineering peers to schedule assigned program work schedules.
  • Participate in customer meetings to resolve a variety of project issues.
  • Regular status updates on program progress and internal assigned projects.

Minimum Requirements:

  • BS degree in Mechanical, Industrial, Electrical or Manufacturing Engineering and 2 to 5 years of experience with manufacturing of mechanical devices and automated systems, or master’s degree plus 0 years of experience
  • Applicants selected may be subject to a government security investigation and must meet eligibility requirements for access to classified information. US CITIZENSHIP REQUIRED.
  • Demonstrated experience in manufacturing and production of mechanical components and/or systems.
  • Demonstrated experience in fixture design.
  • Demonstrated experience in product design verification, inspection, and non-conformance handling protocols.
  • Excellent written and verbal communications skills
  • Experience and proficiency in working with multiple projects.
  • Excellent computer skills in Microsoft Suite, including MS Project.
