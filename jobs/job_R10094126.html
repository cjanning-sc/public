ACME Engineering Space Systems is seeking a Staff Electrical Engineer to join the Flight Systems Team (FST). This position is located in Detroit, UT and supports the Ground Based Strategic Deterrent (GBSD) program. A competitive relocation package may be offered.

What You’ll Get To Do:

The selected candidate will report to the On Board Test Equipment (OBTE) Design Integration Manager and OBTE Chief Engineer and is responsible for electrical design and engineering analysis of RF and electrical Line Replaceable Units (LRUs). Specific roles and duties include:

  • Schematic analysis and design review

  • Manage electrical interface characteristics

  • Develop vehicle block diagrams, schematics, wiring diagrams, and cables

  • Support testing of OBTE systems test in a lab and on vehicle environment

  • Provide technical oversight and issue resolution for Onboard Test Equipment (OBTE) hardware during development and test

  • Provide technical direction and support to the hardware, software, and systems engineering teams in the development and documentation of hardware/software interfaces, including electrical harness design, routing, and RF system design

  • Assist OBTE IPT Lead and OBTE Chief Engineer in risk identification and tracking, especially risks associated with Flight Termination and Telemetry

  • Liaison between OBTE and AVE I&T for issues related to OBTE and integration/test

You’ll Bring These Qualifications:

  • Bachelor of Science degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Math) discipline and 14 years of experience; 12 years with Master’s Degree; 9 years with PhD

  • Experience developing and managing technical requirements for military/aerospace subcontractors

  • Strong CAD skills with experience developing wire diagrams or electrical schematics

  • Experience designing, analyzing, and developing a power budget for an electrical power system of a military / aerospace system

  • Experience developing multi discipline system architecture for a military / aerospace system

  • Active DoD Secret clearance, granted or re-investigated within last 6 years

  • Experience leading TEMPEST or EMI efforts in product development

  • Active DoD Top Secret clearance granted or re-investigated within last 6 years
