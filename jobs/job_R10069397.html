Northrop Grumman Space Systems is seeking an Electrical Computer Aided Design (ECAD) Librarian/Configuration Analyst. This position will be located in Roy, Utah and will support the Sentinel (GBSD) program. 

What You’ll Get To Do: 

Specific duties to include, but are not limited to the following:

  • Develop and maintain libraries of EEE parts within eCAD tools, specifically Mentor Xpedition and Siemens Capital.

  • Collaborate with electrical, components, manufacturing, and other engineering disciplines to support the design of weapon systems and equipment under an overarching Systems Engineering and full lifecycle management framework.

  • Conduct multidisciplinary research and collaborate with circuit designers and/or product line engineers in design, development, and utilization of software to create a representation of components for the electrical tool library.

  • Must be detail-oriented and have a background in CAD usage and knowledge of part specs.

  • Develop and maintain symbol and cell generation libraries.

  • Create and review library components, symbols, and cells in accordance with established NGC and IPC industry guidelines meeting high levels of accuracy.

  • Generate support documentation to meet NGC standards.

This position’s standard work schedule is a 9/80. The 9/80 schedule allows employees who work a nine-hour day Monday through Thursday to take every other Friday off.  

You’ll Bring These Qualifications:

  • 5 Years of experience with Bachelors’ degree and a minimum of 4 years of experience working with eCAD library maintenance or 3 Years with Masters; note 9 years of experience will be accepted in lieu of degree.

  • Must have U.S. Citizenship with the ability to obtain DOD Secret Clearance and the ability to obtain and retain Special Program access clearance.

  • Proficiency in Microsoft Office tools is essential. 

  • Experience using Mentor Xpedition and/or Capital eCAD tools.

  • Experience working with IPC standards for cell creation in support of PCB layout, land patterns, and acceptance criteria.

These Qualifications Would be Nice to Have:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in Electrical Engineering or other degree in Science, Technology, Engineering and Math.

  • Thorough understanding of wiring harness assemblies and design, printed circuit board design.

  • Background and understanding of geometric dimensioning and tolerancing (GD&T) for EEE parts.

  • Experience with Siemens NX, and Siemens Teamcenter.

  • An Active, In-Scope DOD Secret Security Clearance or higher that has been granted / renewed within the past 6 years.

  • Special Program Access.

  • Working knowledge of military and industry standards for EEE parts and assemblies.

  • Familiar with technical drawings and engineering data for EEE parts and assemblies.

  • Experience and familiarity with life cycle management.

  • Familiar with supply chain management as it relates to EEE parts management and obtaining technical specifications on components.

  • Excellent communication, interpersonal skills, and the ability to interface with all levels of employees and management.
