Senior Principal RF Microwave Design Engineer-OKC (AHT)
R10087491
1st Shift (United States of America)

Defense Systems Sector is seeking an Senior Principal RF Microwave Design Engineer to join our growing team in Oklahoma City, OK

You will become part of Northrop Grumman’s Lifecycle Sustainment and Modernization Solutions (LSMS). LSMS provides sustainment support for the US Air Force B-1 and B-2 Bombers, E-3 Sentry, Airborne Warning and Control System, and numerous other Government platforms. Our team members complete engineering, reverse engineering, and logistics solution efforts to solve complex obsolescence problems and ensure warfighter supportability.

As a Senior Principal RF Microwave Design Engineer you will support large projects within an Integrated Product Team (IPT), to include Field Service Representative engineering reach back support, technical planning, requirements development and tracking, system integration, systems integrations lab (SIL), ground and flight testing, verification and validation, airworthiness, system safety, risk, reliability/maintainability, and supportability and effectiveness analyses for total systems. Analyses are performed at all levels to include concept, requirements, design, fabrication, test, installation, operation, maintenance and disposal. Additional duties include:

Responsibilities:

  • Support the development of RF system architectures including block diagrams of hardware design concepts from system requirements, industry standards, and requirements flowed down by systems engineers

  • Simulate RF systems, subsystems, and circuits using industry standard simulation tools including Keysight Advanced Design System (ADS) and Cadence Microwave Office (MWO)

  • Modeling of system level functions using Matlab or other modeling tools for analysis prior to hardware implementation

  • Test and troubleshoot prototype and production hardware in the lab using RF, analog, and digital test equipment

  • Work closely with software and hardware team in developing and testing control software for RF circuits and subsystems

Basic Required Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics (STEM) discipline with a minimum of 9 years of relevant RF/microwave engineering experience OR a Master’s degree in a STEM discipline with a minimum of 7 years of relevant engineering experience with Masters

  • Candidate must have experience with RF systems

  • Experience testing antennas and antenna arrays

  • Proven knowledge of electrical engineering design software and equipment

  • Candidate must have solid written and verbal skills to effectively interact with Government counterparts, other defense contractor organizations, and other cross-functional organizations

  • Ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Security Clearance

  • A basic knowledge of the Microsoft Office Suite

  • Relocation assistance to OKC may be offered for the right candidate

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience in military, government, commercial aerospace industry (e.g. aviation/equipment maintenance, aircraft operations, or commercial industrial technology companies)

  • Experienced with the design, analysis and architecture of Passive Antenna Array systems

  • Experience with the use of RF test equipment like spectrum analyzers, network analyzers and waveform generators

  • Experience with cable management and cable assembly requirements for RF and fiber optic applications

  • Experienced developing technical data packages and documentation supporting HW baselines via engineering drawings

  • Familiarity with Pin Diode Phase Shifters

  • Familiarity with RF and mixed signal printed circuit board and circuit card assembly level design

  • Familiarity with RF budgeting, frequency planning, RF chain analysis and modeling, schematic capture, electromagnetic simulation, signal integrity analysis

  • Experience with Electronic troubleshooting from component to assembly levels
