Manufacturing Engineer/ Principal Manufacturing Engineer - Surface Mount Technology
R10092498
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman is looking for a Manufacturing Engineer to join our dynamic and diverse team environment in Lanham, Maryland. 

 

Job Duties:

- Responsible for all Manufacturing engineering aspects of high reliability programs to ensure mission success

- Develops, modifies, applies and maintains manufacturing systems and protocols for processing materials into partially finished or finished materials product

- Collaborates with all engineering disciplines and manufacturing functions to ensure efficient production of the highest quality product

- Devises and implements methods and procedures for manufacturing, inspecting, testing and evaluating products and production equipment

- Designs and analyzes manufacturing and testing processes, mechanisms and equipment

- Performs statistical analysis to assess the performance of equipment, processes and functions

- Determines area of possible improvement and documents all required actions to improve the product/process

- Employee will participate in program start up duties including production readiness, design, drawing and process reviews

- Additionally, employee will be responsible for the creation of process planning work orders to direct the production for each piece of hardware that is produced

- Once hardware is on the production floor the employee will work with the floor operators to answer any questions and solve technical issues in real time

- Coordinate with all supporting functional areas in order to determine the best solution for the product to maintain the highest levels of quality

- Conduct briefings and technical meetings for top management and customer representatives

 

The Manufacturing Engineer should have a demonstrated track record of working and communicating with upper management/customer representative as well as a self-starter and able to work independently with minimal supervisor oversight.

This positions may be filled at either level based on the qualifications below:

Basic Qualifications:

  • Manufacturing Engineer: Bachelor of Science degree or higher in Engineering or related STEM area + 2 years of relevant professional experience (or 0 years with an MS)
  • Principal Manufacturing Engineer: Bachelor of Science degree or higher in Engineering or related STEM area + 5 years of relevant professional experience (or 0 years with an MS)
  • Hands on manufacturing experience
  • Ability to obtain and maintain security clearance

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience with Lean Six Sigma
  • Knowledge of microelectronics technology and materials
  • Familiarity with J-STD and MIL-STD requirements and Space Class hardware requirements
  • Experience with automated equipment and robotics in a high volume manufacturing environment
