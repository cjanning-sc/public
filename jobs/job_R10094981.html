ACME Engineering Mission Systems (NGMS) Advanced Processing Solutions is working to develop next generation high performance computing systems to meet the mission demands of the NGMS Networked Information Solutions (NIS) business. Superconducting electronics forms the core of our technology, with a focus on energy-efficient computation including our patented superconducting digital logic technology, Reciprocal Quantum Logic (RQL). On a gate for gate basis, RQL consumes orders of magnitude less power than CMOS while running at significantly higher clock speeds. The Advanced Technology Lab of ACME Engineering Mission Systems is seeking Cyber Systems Engineers. The qualified candidate will become part of ACME Engineering’s Transformational Technology Business Area. Transformational Technology is focused on developing state-of-the-art next generation superconducting electronics (SCE) and advanced computing technologies.

Qualified Cyber Systems engineers will have experience with some of the following:

Responsibilities:

• Demonstrated project management skills
• Championing process improvement activities
• Establishing and analyzing test procedures
• Devises and implements methods and procedures for evaluating the accuracy of test stand installs and qualification
• Devises and implements methods and procedures for tracking test assets and develops a plan for periodic review of assets activity (e.g., in use, idle, at calibration, needs repair/upgrade, awaiting final disposition, etc) across multiple labs.
• Provide a monthly report on the status of all assets to the program offices as required

This position requires onsite work at our Chicago, MD and Peoria, MD locations.

This position may be filled at the Associate Cyber Systems Engineer or Cyber Systems Engineer Level. Qualifications for both levels can be found below.

Basic Qualifications for Associate Cyber Systems Engineer:

  • Bachelor's degree in a technical area (BSEE or other Engineering discipline preferred) with 0 years of relevant experience with cryogenic microwave test. 4 additional years of experience can be added in lieu of degree.
  • Ability to obtain and maintain a TS/SCI security clearance (US citizenship required for this)
  • Strong problem solving and analytical skills.
  • Ability to communicate effectively to team members both verbally and in writing.
  • Experience troubleshooting electronic test systems.
  • Cabling and Rack installation/cyber system integration experience

Basic Qualifications for Cyber Systems Engineer:

  • Bachelor's degree in a technical area (BSEE or other Engineering discipline preferred) with 2 years of relevant experience with cryogenic microwave test (0 years with technical MS). 4 additional years of experience can be added in lieu of degree.
  • Ability to obtain and maintain a TS/SCI security clearance (US citizenship required for this)
  • Strong problem solving and analytical skills.
  • Ability to communicate effectively to team members both verbally and in writing.
  • Experience troubleshooting electronic test systems.
  • Cabling and Rack installation/cyber system integration experience

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Active TS/SCI security clearance.
  • Experience with superconducting electronics test platforms
  • Experience with cryogenic test environments
  • Experience with microwave electronics
  • Ability to adapt to changing priorities and requirements.
