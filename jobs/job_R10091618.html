Job Summary

Northrop Grumman Space Systems is searching for a Staff Mechanical Engineer to be part of our Integration (Build) and Test team in Launch Vehicles at our Chandler, AZ facility.

Job Responsibilities

The candidate will serve as an Integration and Test Staff Mechanical Engineer for the Glide Phase Interceptor (GPI) Program, requiring the candidate to function in a dynamic multi-disciplinary environment.  Daily responsibilities will require direct contact with launch vehicle flight hardware and mechanical ground support equipment.  The candidate will develop and lead the concept of operation for all integration and test activities associated with preparation and execution of flight test operations at the launch range. Specific duties may include:

  • Establish Space Systems system tools to be used for program execution
  • Leading hands-on mechanical integration
  • Developing and maintaining procedures for the assembly and test of launch vehicle systems
  • Incorporating hardware design changes
  • Documenting & dispositioning hardware discrepancies, pursuing root cause & corrective actions
  • Coordinating and/or leading integration activities
  • Planning and scheduling day to day tasks
  • Leading, training, tasking other engineers and team members

Basic Qualifications 

  • Minimum Education / Experience: 14 Years with Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering or qualifying discipline; 12 Years with Master of Science in Mechanical Engineering or qualifying discipline, or 9 Years with PhD of Science in Mechanical Engineering or qualifying discipline.
  • Candidate must possess or have the ability to obtain a U.S. Passport for travel
  • Candidate must possess a DoD Secret level security clearance
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills; ability to present to large customer community
  • Ability to solve problems, support troubleshooting efforts, drive tasks to completion
  • Ability to work in a fast-paced environment and multi-task
  • Ability to work in a team environment, and lead small projects or teams
  • Ability to travel: Travel expectation 25%
  • Knowledge and experience with CAD software
  • Experience working with design, integration, or testing of launch vehicles
  • Mechanical integration and diagnostic experience
  • Experience leading/training other engineers and/or team members

Preferred Qualifications 

  • Experience performing formal requirements decomposition and/or verification
  • Electrical, Mechanical, Aerospace or Systems Engineering background
  • Experience with aerospace standards/guidelines
  • Experience with external customer interfacing including meetings, presentations & negotiations
  • Experience working with operational hazards such as propulsive devices, pressurized systems, overhead lifting, and high voltage systems
  • Extensive knowledge and experience with CAD software
  • Extensive experience working with design, integration, or testing of launch vehicles
  • Extensive mechanical integration and diagnostic experience
  • Extensive experience leading/training other engineers and/or team members
