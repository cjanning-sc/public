Cyber Systems Engineer 1/2
R10093410
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Corporate Cyber Security is seeking a level 1 or level 2 Cyber System Engineer. This position is remote, though will require significant amounts of in-person travel and will be directly supporting manufacturing sites/locations across the United States.

Roles and Responsibilities

Candidates will have the opportunity to visit and assess NGC production sites, applying a cyber security viewpoint to how risk can be reduced while improving productivity for site employees. This role has significant visibility and exposure across multiple sectors of NGC.

  • Perform risk assessments of manufacturing site IT and OT (operational technology) equipment for potential security concerns.
  • Assist manufacturing site staff and sector CISO teams with cyber uplift activities for identified cyber risks.
  • Assist in evaluation of new manufacturing IT/OT equipment for usage within manufacturing sites.
  • Provide input on potential new security solutions and designs in support of manufacturing environments.
  • Assist manufacturing site personnel with documentation of site security posture.

Role Expectations:

Candidate should be comfortable working across many different teams, sites, and with a variety of different computing environments. Most successful candidates will be willing to explore and be comfortable discussing network and systems architecture and various operating systems (Windows, and different Linux flavors). Being able to research different technologies and ‘get up to speed’ quickly is essential to success in this role. Candidate should also be comfortable working independently without supervision and direct daily tasking.

Basic Qualifications for a level 1:

  • Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science, Cyber Engineering, or other relevant degree or 4 years of experience in lieu of degree
  • Ability to obtain a security clearance.

Basic Qualifications for a level 2:

  • Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science, Cyber Engineering, or other relevant degree with 2 years of relevant experience OR 0 years of experience with a relevant master's degree OR 4 years of relevant experience with an associate degree OR 6 years of relevant experience with a High School Diploma/GED.
  • Ability to obtain a security clearance.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • DoD 8570 IAT/IAM Level II or III certification a plus (CompTIA Security+, CISSP Associate, etc.)
  • Knowledge of cyber security best practices
  • Basic system administration knowledge
  • Understanding of basic networking
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills
  • Comfortable explaining technical details to a non-technical audience.
  • Comfortable navigating a matrixed organization.
  • Comfortable collaborating with multiple stakeholders across functional and technical skillsets
  • Experience with NIST publications, (NIST 800-53, 800-171)
  • Familiar with Agile practices
  • Comfortable in an environment that requires strategic thinking and decision-making.
  • Familiarity with container-based applications also a plus.
