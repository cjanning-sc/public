Northrop Grumman Mission Systems is seeking a talented Employee Health Nurse to join our team in San Diego, CA. This is a first shift position.

The Employee Health Nurse provides nursing services and first aid to employees or persons who become ill or injured at work facilities. Delivers OSHA and job/site required surveillance program services such as hearing conservation or biological monitoring to employee populations in collaboration with appropriate functional teams. Maintains accurate recordkeeping in compliance with OSHA requirements; provides health promotion and disease prevention strategies; provides counseling, health education and training programs as applicable. Must be a Registered Nurse (RN). May work with industrial toxicology and/or hygienists to recognize environmental factors and/or stresses in or from the workplace that may cause sickness, impaired health, or discomfort among the workers.


Due to the current COVID-19 pandemic, the Employee Health Nurse will also manage COVID-19 situations at the site to include occasional on call hours after the work day and on weekends.

Basic Qualifications:

  • At least 7+ years of experience as a Registered Nurse with Associate of Science in Nursing (or 5+ years of experience as a Registered Nurse with Bachelor's of Science in Nursing).
  • Proficiency in Microsoft Office Suite, e-mail and internet navigation
  • Strong presentation skills
  • Ability to work independently or with a team on special projects
  • Excellent written/oral communication and interpersonal skills
  • Strong attention to detail
  • Ability to perform a variety of tasks simultaneously
  • Ability to maintain confidential documents and information
  • Ability to prioritize and handle multiple projects at one time under tight deadlines
  • Ability to function within all levels of organization and at various locations
  • Ability to work second shift.
  • CPR/BLS certification

Preferred Qualifications:

  • 2+ years in occupational health
  • Bachelor of Science Degree in Nursing
  • Council for Accreditation in Occupational Hearing Conservation (CAOHC)
  • Spirometry (NIOSH approved course optimal)
  • Certified Occupational Health Nurse (COHN)
