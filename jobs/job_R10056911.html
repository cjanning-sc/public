The Mechanical Engineering organization team at the Chandler, AZ facility, seeks an Engineer Aeronautical who enjoys problem solving, out-of-the-box thinking, and is interested in advancing technologies from cradle to wide-spread use. 

Current team applications include vibration attenuation, thermal protection, optimized strength to weight ratio, advancements in polymer properties, radiation shielding, and digital simulation.  The team is dedicated to being a resource for fleet advancement with an emphasis on Mechanical Engineering disciplines in a launch vehicle context.  However, advancements to the broader range of products offered by Northrop Grumman will merit attention.

Essential Functions:

This role will focus broadly on research, development, and business capture efforts under the supervision of a Lead Engineer.  Effort will span a wide range of design, integration and test responsibilities in support of experimental research.  Specific tasks can include but are not limited to:

  • Organize, review, and release prototype and production drawing packages to produce components and assemblies for test and production.
  • Size components for vehicle interfaces, available operating envelopes, and performance requirements.
  • Initiate overall design concepts, detailed component designs, material selection, and assembly approaches to fabricate vehicle components and systems.
  • Interpret, select, and apply company/commercial/military specifications and customer requirements to processes, components, and systems to accomplish specific design requirements.
  • Perform stress analysis and evaluate weight and mass properties to size components for available operating envelopes and performance requirements.
  • Supply hands-on engineering support to build, assemble, and test innovative components and hardware prototypes comprised of carbon composite or other materials to resolve design, manufacturing, assembly, quality, and performance issues.
  • Produce engineering documentation, technical reports, and drawings to support design, integration, and testing efforts.
  • Interaction with internal teams in structures, dynamics, instrumentation, and test will be somewhat common, and will include exposure to external customers or technology vendors.
