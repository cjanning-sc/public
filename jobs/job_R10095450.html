Associate Reliability Engineer
R10095450
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Mission Systems has an immediate opening for an Associate Reliability Engineer.  The selected candidate for this position will report to the Northrop Grumman Precision Targeting and Protection facility in Apopka, FL.

What you’ll get to do:

The Associate Reliability Engineer will support the design, integration, test, and production of laser/electro-optical, network and communication systems. Candidate will be responsible for monitoring and verifying system/subsystem R&M performance and ensuring that program requirements are achieved.

Roles & Responsibilities:

  • The Reliability Engineer we are seeking will be an individual who thrives in a diverse work environment, has outstanding communication skills, and possesses a strong sense of engagement and continuous process improvement. Occasional travel is required to support technical reviews, meetings, and working groups.
  • A fundamental knowledge of Reliability Engineering principles and System Safety Program conduct in accordance with MIL-HDBK-217 and MIL-STD-882 is preferred. 
  • Perform Root Cause and Corrective Action investigations in accordance with Failure Reporting, Analysis, and Corrective Action System (FRACAS) processes.
  • Fundamental principles of reliability engineering including the capability to perform: Fault Tree Analysis (FTA), Reliability Predictions, Built-In Test (BIT) Analysis, Failure Modes Effects and Criticality Analysis (FMECA), Safety Analysis Report (SAR) and fundamental knowledge of statistics are preferred. 
  • Supporting development and qualification of EO/IR systems by independently analyzing and recommending safety criteria in system design, equipment, and procedures to control or eliminate hazards.
  • Liaison to Integrated Product Teams (IPT) and suppliers for assigned systems.
  • Perform hazard analyses for systems and sub-systems, software system safety assessments, and hazardous material management planning and execution.
  • Develops safety documents as required to support customer and test/operational range requirements.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's Degree in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) and a minimum of 6 months professional experience
  • US Citizenship is required with the ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Secret Clearance.
  • Basic knowledge of engineering drawings, and Reliability engineering.
  • Experience in Problem Solving skills.
  • Demonstrated experience conducting RCCA’s (Root Cause Corrective Actions) principles for verified failures.
  • Experience in conducting or participating in FRB’s (Failure Review Boards) to review trends and corrective action status.  
  • Ability to effectively communicate and present complex technical concepts to project, program, and company management, other technical managers, and customers.
  • Basic use of MS Office applications (Word, Excel, and PowerPoint).
  • Ability to effectively communicate and present complex technical concepts to project, program, and company management, other technical managers, and customers.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Technical ability to develop safety analytical skills necessary to assess systems/subsystems, identify safety hazards and recommend safety requirements to system design and operations to mitigate safety risks.
  • Ability to develop safety documents as required to support customer requirements.
  • Capable of understanding incident investigations and root cause and corrective actions.
  • Develop reliability predictions using Windchill Risk and Reliability and/or approved prediction methodologies for mechanical and electrical designs.
  • Perform FMECA, FTA, BIT analysis, implementation, and validation.
  • Develop and/or maintain system models using SysML, UAFP and UML with MBSE tools such as Cameo.
  • Experience writing statements of work and estimating project scope.
  • Current active US DoD Secret Clearance.
