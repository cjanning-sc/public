Northrop Grumman Payload and Ground Systems (PGS) Division in Aurora, CO seeks a highly motivated individual to manage and develop early career engineers. This manager will have approximately 30 direct reports and will be part of a larger team of managers for the Aurora campus. Sound engineering experience as well as a passion for mentoring and developing employees is a must.

This person will report to the Department Manager for specific engineering discipline areas and will not have direct program assignments.

Responsibilities include:

  • Day-to-day communications, compensation reviews, career counseling and mentoring, professional development, employee rewards and recognition, and resolving personnel issues for assigned staff.
  • Lead team in completing all corporate requirements completely and on time including: goal setting, mid-year feedback, and end-of-year performance reviews.
  • Provide staffing support to programs and other initiatives across the Aurora campus.
  • Collaborate with other Section Managers and Department Managers to ensure a common approach to engineering leadership is established.
  • Provide status to or consult with Department Manager regarding assigned staff, including performance, engagement, compensation, recognition, training, staffing, employee relations, and other items.
  • Responsible for staffing, including the re-assignment of current staff, bringing in NG staff from outside of the Aurora area, and hiring new employees.
  • Collaborate with HRBP in support of assigned staff, including performance, compensation, engagement, employee relations, short-term disability, staffing requisitions and offers, employee mobility, and other activities.
  • Monthly 1:1s with individuals to ensure bi-lateral communications are established to focus on the needs and aspirations of the employee.
  • Monthly staff meetings to keep all informed of current events and to allow for networking and team building.

In addition, a strong focus on early career development to include:

  • Understanding and expertise on the Pathways program, partnering with PGSD Early Career Talent (ECT) Leadership
  • Professional and technical development training opportunities targeted for early career.
  • Coaching and mentoring on career development.
  • Organize 1:1s, skip levels, and other engagement opportunities with senior and executive leadership.
  • Learn and educate others on employee rotational programs by partnering with PGS Early Career Talent (ECT) Lead and other support organizations that offer targeted opportunities for rotations.
  • Identify site openings for Pathways rotations and work to closure.
  • Partnership with University Relations & Recruiting for attracting new talent for internship and full-time opportunities including participating in recruiting, interviews, and selection.
  • Help organize and implement summer intern engagement activities.

Salary Range for Manager Software Engineering 1: $117,600.00-$176,400.00

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering, or Math (STEM) discipline with 5+ years of engineering experience OR Master's degree in a STEM discipline with 3+ years of engineering experience.
  • Excellent communication skills (oral and written) are required, and the ideal candidate will have demonstrated success collaborating and leading technical teams.
  • Ability to provide career development guidance to new college graduates throughout the first 3 years of their career.
  • Demonstrated experience in Engineering.
  • Candidate must be eligible to obtain DoD Top Secret and SCI clearances.
  • Candidate will have a track record as an effective communicator and problem solver who is able to develop and maintain good working relationships with internal and external stakeholders. This candidate will demonstrate a commitment to ethics and integrity, and the capability to innovate while challenging traditional ways of doing business.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • 7 years experience in either Software Engineering, Network Administration, or Linux/Windows systems administration
  • Experience as a team lead or manager
  • Ability to effectively interface with other Engineering and Production disciplines across the Business Unit as well as Programs.
  • Experience with providing career development guidance to junior employees
  • ACTIVE TS/SCI Clearance.
