ACME Engineering Defense Systems is currently seeking an Electronic Technician Level 1 or 2 for our Naperville, Illinois location.

The level to be hired will depend upon exhibited education, experience, and competency.

Job Summary:

This position works closely with Engineering to configure and execute testing operations including troubleshooting equipment, setup, fixturing, testing, equipment maintenance, documenting setup, and test results. Assists Engineers and Technicians with projects through building or procuring test equipment, setup, performing testing and process validations. Primary role is an instrumentation installation technician.

  • Provide technical support to engineers on a variety of technical tasks
  • Installs instrumentation including but not limited to strain gages and thermocouples using a variety of techniques
  • Gathers, maintains, formats, compiles, and manipulates technical data
  • Calibrates, adjusts and maintains equipment, components, devices or systems.
  • Works from schematics, engineering drawings and technical instructions.
  • Sets up and configures test equipment, systems, test articles, and software
  • Perform tests/experiments and records data to assist with engineering evaluation or analysis
  • Utilizes various test equipment for data gathering
  • Reports data in prescribed format
  • Perform routine calculations using Excel or other related software and proficient in MS Office
  • Use a computer daily to perform testing operations. This includes using Excel and custom software.

Basic Qualifications Level 1:

  • High School Diploma.  related electronics experience a plus.
  • Work requires the ability to use soldering techniques for building and repairing various types of electrical components including but not limited to cables and assemblies.
  • Ability to follow strict safety protocols, elevate concerns, follow highly detailed instructions, document discrepancies and resolve conflicts in instructions, tasks or designs.
  • Detail oriented and an ability to remain highly focused during complex test processes.
  • Must be a US citizen and meet requirements to obtain a secret security clearance.
  • Ability to lift 50 lbs on a routine basis.
  • Strong fluency in Microsoft Office applications and computer use

Basic Qualifications Level 2:

  • High School Diploma and a minimum of two years related experience, or a technology degree / certificate, or military electronics experience.
  • Work requires the ability to use soldering techniques for building and repairing various types of electrical components including but not limited to cables and assemblies.
  • Ability to follow strict safety protocols, elevate concerns, follow highly detailed instructions, document discrepancies and resolve conflicts in instructions, tasks or designs.
  • Detail oriented and an ability to remain highly focused during complex test processes.
  • Must be a US citizen and meet requirements to obtain a secret security clearance.
  • Ability to lift 50 lbs on a routine basis.
  • Strong fluency in Microsoft Office applications and computer use

Preferred Qualifications: 

  • Experience with test equipment and systems’ interfaces, including installation, troubleshooting, diagnostics, repairs and maintenance of test instrumentation.
  • Experience reading and interpreting schematic diagrams, operating a variety of test equipment, duties such as testing, troubleshooting, modifying, calibrating, installing, maintaining, repairing, constructing, developing, on electronic equipment, or similar functions.
  • The ability to setup and install complex rocket motor instrumentation arrangements using engineering direction, drawings and instructions.

This position falls under a Federal Explosives License issued by the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives (ATF) for this location.  Candidates hired into this role will be required to complete and submit an Employee Possessor Questionnaire to ATF for approval to be a possessor.
