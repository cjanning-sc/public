Strategic Space Systems Division (SSSD) is an industry-leading provider for prime satellite and payload capabilities and directed energy and electronics solutions for national security, military, and civil customers. We are built on a heritage of providing innovative, affordable and reliable aerospace and defense products that our customers rely on to achieve mission success. Join the Space revolution and make the impossible, possible.

ACME Engineering Strategic Space Systems Division (SSSD) has an opening for an Operations Director 1 - Ruby to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Columbus, CA., and will report to the Ruby Program Manager.

Ruby is a high priority restricted program within the Responsive Innovative Systems Environment (RISE) Business Unit.  As a member of the Ruby Program Management Office (PMO), the Operations Director will:

  • Develop the Program Operations Strategy across IPTs for all Operations functions, including Manufacturing, Supply Chain, Integration & Test, Mission Assurance and Facilities.  Collaboration with other ACME Sectors, Divisions and Sites will be essential to developing a comprehensive Program Operations Strategy.

  • Assess risk and opportunities for Operations across the program, integrating into a comprehensive risk & opportunity management plan, including mitigation / realization activities.  Drive execution of mitigation / realization activities in collaboration with the program IPTs, functions and other stakeholders.

  • Work collaboratively with the IPTs to optimize Operations activities across the program, including the manufacturing and integration & test schedule, supply chain oversight and status, mission assurance requirements alignment and facilities optimization.

  • Oversee streamlining production process development in collaboration with the IPTs, and work to improve quality, productivity and efficiency of the program.

  • Work closely with Supply Chain, Suppliers, Assembly, Integration & Test and the Schedule Management teams to develop integrated production processes across the program. 

  • Develop metrics to track progress, identify issues and development corrective action strategies. 

  • Brief the Ruby Operations Strategy and status to the customer at program key milestone reviews, and provide status to the customer on program wide initiatives at program management reviews. 

  • Lead and support program special assignments to address critical areas as identified by the Program Manager.

  • Support  Program and Business Unit proposal activity for Ruby change proposals and RISE new business captures.

As a leader, you always act with integrity, and the highest ethical standards characterize everything you do. You enable your team to achieve predictable and balanced results, while satisfying the needs of internal and external partners. You treat all people with respect, and you model personal growth and continuous development. You know how to build an inclusive environment that attracts, retains, and inspires a diverse and engaged team. You develop trusted and valued customer relationships and communicate effectively, so that people throughout the organization feel engaged and connected to their work. You deliver excellence, strive for continuous improvement and respond vigorously to change.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s degree with ten (10) years of related professional/military experience with a background in manufacturing space or airborne hardware, and/or supply chain management

  • Five (5) years of experience leading people, projects, or programs

  • Proven ability to deliver effective communication verbally, written

  • Ability to build consensus and relationships among managers, partners and employee

  • Active Top Secret/SCI; or recent SSBI

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience with first time development programs and hardware builds.

  • Experience with other ACME Engineering Sectors and Divisions

  • Demonstrated ability to develop and implement affordability initiatives across all phases of the program and production lifecycle.

  • Supply Chain Management experience with major subcontractors and general procurement suppliers
