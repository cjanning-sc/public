Northrop Grumman’s Space Systems sector is looking for a Deputy Chief Engineer for Infrastructure, Deployment, & Integration supporting Command and Launch team for the Office of the Chief Engineer in our SDS Division. This leadership role is located in Roy, Utah. Learn more about the Sentinel (GBSD) program here. Northrop Grumman supports the Air Force’s sustainment, development, production and deployment of hardware and system modifications for Intercontinental Ballistic Missile (ICBM) Ground and Airborne Launch Control Systems, Launch Facilities and associated infrastructure.

This role may offer a competitive relocation assistance package.

 

What You’ll Get To Do: 

Be a Deputy Chief Engineer for the Command and Launch segment, reporting to the Office of the Chief Engineer. Responsibilities include technical leadership by:

  • Providing technical leadership in Infrastructure, Deployment, and Integration activities

  • Driving technical execution in support of major program events

  • Collaborating within the Office of the Chief Engineer to deliver exceptional customer value

  • Focusing horizontally on resolving high-level Systems challenges

  • Driving technical execution with an emphasis on infrastructure and deployment

  • Ensuring execution of overall technical plan within the Integrated Master Schedule

  • Supporting strategic SDS Division initiatives on tools, processes, and cross-segment technical reviews

  • Driving technical rigor into technical interchange meetings with Sentinel partners

  • Utilizing Model-Based Engineering to deliver a highly sustainable Weapon System

  • Advising program technical leadership on program, schedule, and technical matters

  • Active DoD Secret clearance, investigation must have been completed within last 5 years

  • ICBM ground system, command and control, communication, or complex aerospace systems experience

  • Demonstrated experience in making sound decisions in uncertain and difficult situations
