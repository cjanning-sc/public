Northrop Grumman Space Systems sector has an opening for a Intellectual Asset Manager 2 in the Strategic Development organization, which is part of the sector’s Strategy & Business Development function. This position will preferably be in one of the following locations:

  • Redondo Beach, CA
  • Dulles, VA
  • Baltimore, MD
  • Chandler, AZ

The Manager of Intellectual Property Strategy will be responsible for overseeing the sector’s invention disclosure and evaluation process, managing the sector’s patent portfolio, and leading technology licensing efforts.

The scope of responsibilities includes intellectual property (IP) education and outreach, organizing and chairing the Invention Evaluation Committee, and assisting and advising inventor teams and individual employee inventors on best practices for IP capture and protection.

We are seeking an individual with a demonstrated ability to lead distributed teams and manage complex processes, familiarity with aerospace and defense technology, and experience with intellectual property management.

The selected individual should thrive in a fast-paced work environment, with high expectations, diverse assignments, and collaborative team settings. This leader must be comfortable working closely with executive leadership, program managers, contracts, legal, and other key functions.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Lead the capture, protection, and management of the sector’s IP including patents, trade secrets, trademarks, licenses and other forms of intellectual property
  • Develop and deliver IP capture and protection process training to increase the awareness of the need for IP capture and encourage proper IP management across the sector
  • Work with business areas to understand strategic drivers of business success, work with subject matter experts to conduct IP landscape assessments, and formulate IP strategies to optimize the sector’s competitive position
  • Work with the Legal Department to manage the patent prosecution process, review incoming patent and trademark related correspondence from outside counsel and internal customers, and identify and develop resolution to IP issues

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree
  • 5 years of relevant experience working with intellectual property and technology
  • 2 years in a lead or project management role
  • Demonstrated ability to lead distributed teams and manage complex processes
  • Demonstrated experience in strategic planning, transaction structuring, and technology licensing
  • Able to obtain and maintain a security clearance

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience working in the aerospace/defense industry
  • Experience drafting and negotiating license agreements including licenses
  • Paralegal with patent background or Patent agent
  • Experience with IP rights in US Government Contracts (FAR/DFAR)
  • Juris Doctor or Master of Business Administration degree or equivalent
