This position is for a working manager, involving duties and responsibilities for team management and technical performance, as follows.

Manager Duties/Responsibilities

  • People leadership for a structural analysis team, to include performance management, career development, staffing, and merit planning
  • Provide project and technical oversight for activities performed by the group
  • Provide ownership of, and direction to, the processes used by the group
  • Provide proposal inputs and review proposal content for both internal and external customers
  • Support department initiatives, recruiting, technology roadmap creation, cross-company/sector involvement, and IRAD project leadership
  • Communicate effectively with both team members and internal/external customers and stakeholders
  • Uphold engineering principles of ethics and integrity, and ACME Engineering values

Technical Duties/Responsibilities:

  • Analyzes, researches, and develops structural engineering specifications for metallic, non-metallic, and composite structures in all phases of the product life cycle from preliminary design to sustainment
  • Performance of structural analysis includes loads and dynamics, stress, stiffness, stability, fatigue, and thermal
  • Analyses will be performed utilizing hand calculations and Finite Element Methods (FEM) software packages such as FEMAP (Nastran), Abaqus, and ANSYS
  • When FEM models are appropriate, critical thinking skills will be required to develop a FEM to capture appropriate behavior of complex linear and non-linear structures
  • Excellent communication and people skills required to work with key stakeholders within other engineering disciplines including but not limited to system engineering and mechanical design, to achieve program objectives in a timely manner 
  • Additional work responsibilities may include task planning, conducting technical peer reviews, and mentoring junior engineers

Basic Qualifications: 

Bachelor of Science degree in Mechanical or Aerospace Engineering

  • Minimum 7 years of related mechanical, structural, stress, or structural dynamics engineering experience with a Bachelor's degree; 5 years of experience with a related Master's degree
  • Minimum 1 year of experience providing technical leadership of an engineering team
  • Active DoD Secret security clearance, or higher

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Master of Science in Mechanical or Aerospace Engineering
  • 3+ years of experience with analysis of aircraft structures
  • 3+ years of experience using FEM software, including FEMAP (Nastran), Abaqus, ANSYS, or similar
  • 1+ years of analysis experience performing Durability and Damage Tolerance of aircraft structures
  • Implicit or explicit dynamic FEA experience analyzing dynamic environments: static, random vibration, sinusoidal vibration, and shock
  • Provide technical solutions to a wide range of complex difficult problems. Solutions are imaginative, thorough, practicable, and consistent with organization objectives.
  • Demonstrate strong leadership skills, effective communication, broad skillset spanning interpersonal, business, and technical capabilities
