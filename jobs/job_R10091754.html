Strategic Space Systems Division (SSSD) is an industry-leading provider for prime satellite and payload capabilities and directed energy and electronics solutions for national security, military, and civil customers. We are built on a heritage of providing innovative, affordable and reliable aerospace and defense products that our customers rely on to achieve mission success. Join the Space revolution and make the impossible, possible.

ACME Engineering Strategic Space Systems Division (SSSD) has an opening for a Director 2 - AMP Space Vehicle Program Manager to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Columbus, OH., and report directly to the Advanced Mission Programs Vice President & Program Manager.

The Space Vehicle Project Manager leads all AMP Space Vehicle (SV) activities, spanning from conceptual design, proposal preparation, detailed design, manufacturing and test, technical support of Vehicle integration and test, and technical support of operational systems. The Space Vehicle Project Manager must be able to drive execution, strive for operational efficiencies and affordability, develop talent and build strong teams, lead change, solve complex technical problems, be agile to evolving customer needs, and drive growth across the portfolio and enterprise. The Space Vehicle Project Manager will report directly to the Advanced Mission Programs Vice President & Program Manager.

Responsibilities will include but are not limited to the following:

  • Establishing and executing the SV cost, schedule and technical baselines to design, build, integrate and deliver flight hardware. SV is fully incorporated into the AMP program management, planning, control, and metrics processes

  • Cost, schedule, and technical management of all Space Vehicle elements, as well as ensuring technical execution and process compliance for all Vehicle Engineering disciplines supporting AMP.

  • Identifying and realizing opportunities to reduce cost and improve processes to realize current contract and future affordable systems

  • Securing RFPs, preparing proposals, negotiating and definitizing contracts

  • Leading and managing a project team of >500 people across multiple simultaneous program phases (conceptual design through support to operations)

  • Managing the production of a wide variety and large quantity of Space Vehicle mechanical and electrical hardware, firmware and software for multiple integrated diverse vehicle systems

  • Directing the management of a broad set of Space Vehicle component suppliers

  • Meeting all security, organizational conflict of interest, export control, protection of proprietary information, and risk and opportunity management processes

  • Closely coordinating and collaborating with stakeholders: AMP Program Office, peer AMP Project Managers, AMP support organizations (Business Development, Security, HR, IT Solutions, Legal), Space Systems business units, ACME Space functional area counterparts (Engineering, Global Supply Chain, etc.), senior level customers, technical advisors, and subject matter experts, suppliers, and Executives.

In order to be successful in this role, you will require the following key attributes:

  • Exceptional leadership and teambuilding skills

  • Excellent communication skills and ability to effectively interact with the customer, program management, business unit and supply base executives and functional management

  • Willing and able to travel as required

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s degree with 10 years of space hardware project and program management experience

  • 5 years experience in a management role

  • Experience with flight hardware/firmware/software integration

  • Experience with spacecraft subsystem and/or system level test definition and execution

  • Active SCI clearance; or Active Top Secret clearance with recent SSBI

Preferred Qualifications: 

  • Bachelor's in Science, Technology, Engineering or Math (STEM)

  • Master’s degree in an engineering or technical discipline

  • Proven experience in delivering complex space vehicle flight hardware

  • Proven ability to lead a high performing team with evidence of being able to create an inclusive work environment that attracts, retains, and inspires diverse and engaged teams

  • Exceptional communication skills (verbal, written, presentation) across all levels of the organization

  • Spacecraft activation and operations experience

  • Earned value management of complex multi-layer projects

  • Proficient with executive presentations and communication skills

  • Subcontract Management experience
