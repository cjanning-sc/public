Northrop Grumman Defense Systems is seeking a Sr. Principal Network Communications (level 4) to join our diverse team of professionals located at Chantilly, Va.

The Network Communication Subject Matter Expert (SME) will plan, evaluate, and coordinate installation and/or reconfiguration of hardware and software elements of data, satellite, RF, and/or voice communication network. Maintains technical expertise in all areas of networks and computer hardware and software interconnection and interfacing, such as servers, virtual hosts, databases, thin/thick clients, routers, firewalls, etc. May propose solutions to management to ensure all communications requirements based on future needs and current usage, configuring such solutions to optimize cost savings. Prepares or ensures that appropriate network documentation exists, including operational instructions, and actively participates in drafting and updating existing documentation. Provides regular monitoring and network analysis regarding short- and long-range planning for in-house systems, including reporting discrepancies or system anomalies. May coordinate third-party maintenance for network equipment. May develop network engineering solutions or portions of network engineering solutions that include selection of hardware and software packages.

The Network Communication SME will develop technical manual and training curriculum to be employed during procedures, simulation, and/or flight training. Writes, rewrites and/or edits technical documents such as technical procedure manuals, user manuals, operational specifications, and related technical publications to communicate clearly and effectively technical specifications and instructions to aviation & engineering related audiences. Evaluates curriculum materials based on trainee feedback and instructor input. Reviews and conducts simulator design and training effectiveness to provide maximum proficiency. Reviews mission planning processes and software for incorporation into training materials. Coordinates meetings and working groups with engineering support and SMEs to develop new, and expand already designed training curriculum. Conducts quality review of materials.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelors degree and at least 5 years of Network Communications experience; Masters and 3 years for relevant experience. In lieu of formal education, High School Diploma or equivalent (GED) and at least 9 years of relevant job experience may be considered.

  • Must be able to complete initial qualification training within 180 days from program access.

  • Must have an active CompTIA Security + ce or within 180 days of on-boarding

  • Must be willing to work a flexible work schedule that may include extended shifts, holidays, and weekends and support on call status for 24 hours-a-day, 7 days- a-week, and 365 days a year.

  • Must meet medical requirements for worldwide travel. Must have and maintain a valid U.S. passport, accept customer furnished quarters for a period up to 120 days away from primary work location and comply with mobility requirements to include required immunizations and vaccinations.

  • Must possess an active DoD Top Secret security clearance and be able to maintain that clearance

  • Ability to obtain and retain Special Program access clearance within 365 days from submission.
