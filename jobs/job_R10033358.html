The Specialty Test Team in Cincinnati Ca. is looking for a qualified Instrumentation Engineering Manager to lead a team of Instrumentation engineers for Full Scale Test execution using Loads Control and Data Acquisition Systems in Structural Testing applications. The selected candidate should thrive in a fast-paced work environment with elevated expectations, be result oriented and be capable of overseeing multiple unique Test Set-up and Test Execution. The STT performs all structural testing, both static and fatigue. Ranging from component level to full scale article on both R&D and flight hardware. 
  
The ideal candidate will apply their electrical, mechanical, and leadership skills while utilizing complex, state of the art, load control and data acquisition systems to control hundreds of load actuators while monitoring/recording data from thousands of instrumentation acquisition channels. Qualified individuals will collaborate with various disciplines and all levels of personnel throughout all phases of Structural Testing, IE: Test Planning, Hardware and Instrumentation design, procurement and or manufacturing, Test and network architecture set-up, Test Execution, Data Reduction and Test Report Preparation and presentation. Candidate must have Structural Test experience and an extensive understanding of the hardware and methodology employed during a large variety of different testing environments and scenarios. Candidate must have considerable knowledge/experience with operation, concepts, and components comprised within data acquisition and load control hardware as well as related instrumentation such as: LVDTs, Strain Gages, String Pots, Load Cells, pressure transducers, etc. 
  
The ideal candidate must be a motivated, driven individual with excellent communication skills and possess the ability to lead, mentor, and motivate their team of engineers. 

Responsibilities for the role include, but are not limited to:
  • General understanding of and experience with Control and Data Acquisition Systems
  • Understanding and implementation of effective budget and schedule roadmap planning and management in support of efficient project execution
  • Plan and arrange for the equipment and personnel required for testing. 
  • Interpret and fulfill Test Requirements and author/publish/review Test Procedures, requirements documents, etc.
  • Develop/Lead/Contribute to Readiness/Design Reviews, Customer Briefings, Scheduling & Budgeting Plans, etc.  
  • Test anomaly investigation
  • Participate in post-test data evaluation, problem identification, and resolutions 
  • Skilled with Microsoft Office tools, Excel, Power Point, Visio, and Visual Basic

  • A DoD Secret Clearance within 5 years scope and the ability to obtain and maintain a Special Program access within a reasonable period of time, as determined by the company to meet its business needs.

  • 5+ years direct hands-on experience with test, measurement & control engineering applications

  • Foundational knowledge of/experience with emerging measurement technologies such as Digital Image Correlation and Fiber Optic Strain Sensors
  • Hydraulic/pneumatic control system, set up, commissioning and checkout experience.

  • Experience with and understanding of complete cradle-to-grave structural testing cycle, interfaces, and integrations from hydraulic & pneumatic system design, structural analytics and design, and instrumentation system designs.
