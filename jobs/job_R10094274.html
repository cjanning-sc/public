Principal Optical Engineer or Sr. Principal Optical Engineer
R10094274
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Mission Systems has an immediate opening for a Principal Optical Engineer or Sr. Principal Optical Engineer.  The selected candidate for this position will report to the Northrop Grumman Precision Targeting and Protection facility in Apopka, FL. 

What You’ll Get To Do:

The new team member will support the design, integration, test, and production of laser/electro-optical, network and communication systems.

Roles & Responsibilities:

  • Designing optical instruments, assemblies and subsystems using optical and engineering design tool
  • Supporting the assembly, alignment, integration & test of optical hardware
  • Working with and managing external suppliers to enable delivery of critical optical hardware
  • Working both independently and as part of larger diverse teams, including the capacity to lead engineering teams which draw upon a wide range of scientific and engineering disciplines
  • Developing technical solutions to complex problems which require the regular use of ingenuity and creativity.
  • Perform an end-to-end optical lens design, analysis, tolerancing, and simulation using Zemax
  • Attend and prepares materials for presentation during System Requirement Review, System Functional Review, Preliminary Design Review, Critical Design Review and System Verification Review.
  • Support trade studies, system sensitivity studies, design optimization studies, system performance assessments, system design assessments, mission analysis and timelines and concept of operations development.

"This position may be offered at the Principal Optical Engineer, or a Sr. Principal Optical Engineer based on the qualifications listed below."

Basic Qualifications for a Principal Optical Engineer: 

  • BS Engineering degree in Optical Engineering, Optical Sciences, Opto-Mechanical Engineering, or related Engineering field with a concentration in Optics and a minimum of 5 years of related experience.
  • US Citizenship is required with the ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Secret Clearance
  • Proficiency in one of the optical design/analysis tools such as Zemax (preferred), Code V, OSLO, Trace Pro, FRED or equivalent.
  • Experience developing engineering solutions for EO/IR instruments, sensors and payloads, or high energy lasers & directed energy systems, or optical communications systems
  • Fundamental understanding of optical architectures from specifications and first principles (i.e. reflective, refractive, pupils, stops, or relays)
  • Understanding of contributors to radiometric performance in EO/IR payloads

Basic Qualifications for a Sr. Principal Optical Engineer:

  • BS Engineering degree in Optical Engineering, Optical Sciences, Opto-Mechanical Engineering, or related Engineering field with a concentration in Optics and a minimum of 9 years of related experience.
  • US Citizenship is required with the ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Secret Clearance
  • Proficiency in one of the optical design/analysis tools such as Zemax (preferred), Code V, OSLO, Trace Pro, FRED or equivalent.
  • Experience developing engineering solutions for EO/IR instruments, sensors and payloads, or high energy lasers & directed energy systems, or optical communications systems
  • Fundamental understanding of optical architectures from specifications and first principles (i.e. reflective, refractive, pupils, stops, or relays)
  • Understanding of contributors to radiometric performance in EO/IR payloads

Preferred Qualifications: 

  • Expertise in optical design of multi-element imaging systems.
  • Familiarity with MWIR/LWIR optical materials.
  • Direct experience in the specification and testing of optical systems including working knowledge of optical test and evaluation equipment, and understanding of precision alignment & metrology techniques
  • Experience working with and managing suppliers of optical components
  • Familiarity with alignment sensitivities associated with free-space beam paths, coating performance, and stray light and thermal management.
  • Ability to design optical systems and test equipment, including free-space optical beam trains, telescopes, adaptive optics assemblies, and focal planes, and diagnostics such as interferometers, wavefront sensors, and near-field, far-field and imaging sensors
  • Experience with technical writing and proposal efforts
  • Capable of preparing and presenting briefing packages to both internal and external customers, communicating technical approaches and findings
