ACME Engineering Mission Systems (NGMS) is looking for a Logistics Project Manager 2/3 to join our team in Groton, CT on the General Dynamics-Electric Boat shipyard.Â This role will be supporting our Marine Systems-Groton/Quonset Point locations which specializes in the shipboard installation and testing of the launcher systems for U.S. Navy Columbia ballistic missile submarine new construction program.

What You'll Get to Do:

  • Oversee and manage the logistical aspects of new construction submarine builds and serve as logistics liaison between the field engineers, the Strategic Systems Program, and prime contractor at the Groton, CT and Quonset Point, RI shipyards.
  • Support return of reusable support equipment and shipping equipment.
  • Coordinate tactical hardware and support equipment deliveries to the shipyard.
  • Ensure hardware provided to the shipyard complies with information assurance / cyber security.
  • Plan and coordinate with the shipyard for the receipt, inspection, and storage of launcher hardware, support equipment, and I&C Spares.
  • Plan and coordinate with the shipyard for the conduct and completion of scheduled recertification, maintenance, and re-calibration of Launcher Support Equipment, including shipment to /from the test /recertification locations.
  • Ensure limited life material is available, is received on time, and that it has not expired when it is needed. Dispose of expired material in accordance with shipyard and company policies.
  • Support provisioning conferences and formal provisioning.
  • Support Launcher Equipment Requirements Chart (LERC) changes and attend meetings. Complete actions items from the LERC as required.
  • Support cross decking plan to include the movement, stowage, and re-issue of designated components from the legacy submarines to new construction submarines.
  • Assesses project issues and develop resolutions to meet productivity, quality, and client-satisfaction goals and objectives.
  • Develop mechanisms for monitoring project progress and for intervention and problem solving with project managers, field engineers, the Strategic Systems Program office, and prime contractor.
  • Prepare data items in accordance with DoD requirements.
  • Compile data from a variety of sources to develop material requirements and spares quantities.
  • Participate in provisioning conferences and physical inventories.
  • Monitor supply support effectiveness through document reviews, identify issues and provide recommended solutions.
  • Prepare significant data analysis in accordance with DoD requirements.

This position can be filled at a Project Manager 2 level or a Principal Project Manager 3 level.
