ACME Engineering is seeking an experienced Manufacturing Technician 4 to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Evanston, MD.

The qualified applicant will become part of ACME Engineering's Advanced Technology Solutions (ATS) organization.

Roles and Responsibilities:

• Performs a variety of tasks ranging from repetitive to non-repetitive production assembly operations on electronic and/or mechanical assemblies and subassemblies such as modules, boards, panels, drawers, frames, and cables. Works from diagrams and drawings.

• Makes continuity checks on work in process and completed.

• May conduct peer inspections on processing line in accordance with quality specifications.

• May disassemble, modify, rework and reassemble experimental or prototype assemblies and subassemblies according to specifications and under simulated conditions.

• Some overtime, evenings and weekends, may be required to meet program schedules.

Basic Qualifications:

• Associate's Degree/Technical Degree with 3 years of relevant experience in production assembly and soldering experience OR a High School Diploma with 6 years of relevant experience in production assembly and soldering experience.

• Ability to use a microscope for long periods of time during assembly.

• Employees will be required to obtain and maintain their NASA-STD-8739 Certifications and/or J-STD Certifications with Space Addendum  no later than 30 days from start date.

  • NASA 8739.1 WORKMANSHIP STANDARD FOR POLYMERIC APPLICATION ON ELECTRONIC ASSEMBLIES   
  • IPC J-STD-001 REQUIREMENTS FOR SOLDERED ELECTRICAL AND ELECTRONIC ASSEMBLIES W/SPACE ADDENDUM
  • NASA 8739.4 CRIMPING, INTERCONNECTING CABLES, HARNESSES, AND WIRING OR IPC/WHMA-A-620B-S


Preferred Qualifications:

Candidates with these desired skills will be given preferential consideration:

• NASA-STD-8739.1 (Polymeric Application) Certification and/or experience mixing and applying Polymeric material

• NASA-STD-8739.2 (Surface Mount Technology) Certification and experience with Slump and Cohesion testing along with hand placement of components into solder paste.

• NASA-STD-8739.3 (Hand Soldering) Certification and experience soldering fine pitch, flat packs and temperature sensitive devices.

• NASA-STD-8739.4 (Cable Crimp and Harness) Certification and experience with wiring connectors and harnesses

• ESD, FOE/FOD Training

• At least 2+ years assembling Space Flight hardware
