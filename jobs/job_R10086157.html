ACME Engineering Defense Systems is seeking a Systems Engineer to support the Disturbed Mission Operations Center (DMOC) at Kirtland AFB, NM working for the Distributed Mission Operations Network (DMON) Team based out of Pittsburgh, FL. The qualified applicant will be well versed in all technical aspects of information systems, security policies and compliance requirements in classified computing environments. The primary responsibility of the position is to support the USAF’s initiatives in DMOC simulation events by identifying and solving network interoperability issues. The applicant must be able to design and document local processes and procedure for daily operations. Additionally, the applicant will support daily activities, operational and test events which requires schedule flexibility.

Responsibilities will include:

  • Operation and troubleshooting of developed systems
  • Documentation of new and existing processes
  • Monitoring, controlling and isolating problems in a data communications network
  • Provide first and/or second-level problem determination and provide needed support
  • Provides responsive quality customer service and support at all times for internal customers as well as external customers at the sites and our Air Force stakeholders
  • Functions as the customer’s single point-of contact for problem escalation and resolution as required

This requisition is available to be filled at either a level 3 (Principal) or level 4 (Sr. Principal).

Basic Qualifications:

  • Level 3: Bachelor's degree with 5 years of experience OR Master's degree with 3 years of experience OR 9 years of experience in lieu of degree.
  • Level 4: Bachelor's degree with 9 years of experience OR Master's degree with 7 years of experience OR 13 years of experience in lieu of degree.
  • Active Secret or Top Secret DoD security clearance and eligibility for Tier 1 access
  • Meet DoD 8570 Certifications Requirements (e.g. Security+ Certification, and/or CISSP) Familiarity with network hardware including but not limited to Cisco switches, routers, boundary protection (firewalls, intrusion protection devices/intrusion detection devices, etc.) and various other network managed devices
  • Knowledge of the Risk Management Framework
  • Good understanding of network protocols
  • Proficient in basic network troubleshooting
  • Familiar with Distributed Interactive Simulation (DIS), High Level Architecture (HLA), and/or Test and Training Enabling Architecture (TENA) protocols
  • Ability to travel, 10% or less, both domestically and internationally

  

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience with Distributed Mission Operations (DMO) events and Virtual Flags
  • DMOC Event architecture comprehension
  • Military Experience
  • Experience with security architectures, firewalls, and network security controls.
  • Strong organizational skills and stay focused managing multiple tasks concurrently.
  • Experience in modeling and simulation environment.
  • Experience in Microsoft Windows Server and Desktop Operating System installation, configuration, troubleshooting, and security processes & procedures
