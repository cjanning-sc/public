Sr. Principal Data & Instrumentation Wind Tunnel Engineer
R10086664
1st Shift (United States of America)

The Data Engineer will be supporting wind tunnel and ground test activities. Responsibilities will include the following tasks: developing data reduction and data analysis tools, source selection and implementation of data acquisition hardware and software, instrumentation sensor selection and specification, and maintaining/upgrading existing facility systems.  Duties include supporting fellow engineers with the preparation of system specifications, statements of work, drawings and schematics that satisfy test and system requirements.  Design and adapt data system interfaces to specific testing needs.  Design, develop and maintain databases that manage test instrumentation and data system requirements. Support engineering requests for data products and assist in analytical data processing.  Prospective candidates will also provide full-spectrum data engineering support for tests conducted at company facilities as well as company sponsored tests located at other facilities. 

Essential Functions:

  • Follow test methods, procedures, and standards during the performance of tests.
  • Write data reduction and processing code as needed to support special and unique testing efforts.
  • Program in engineering software environments (e.g. LabView, Python, C++, Fortran) to create PID control systems for unique aeromechanical hardware.
  • Utilize strain gages, pressure transducers, thermocouples, RTDs, accelerometers, …etc. to accurately capture the state and condition of test hardware.
  • Conduct tests and record data to assist with engineering evaluation and analysis.
  • Work with team leads to provide guidance and selection of instrumentation, sensors and tools to meet test objectives.
  • Setup of equipment and instrumentation in accordance with test procedures and specifications.
  • Produce engineering documentation, reports, drawings, and schematics.
  • Develop, update, and maintain wind tunnel test processes and procedures.
  • Prepare presentations that convey project status and challenges, and communicate to leadership as needed, along with contributing towards the department’s technical tools and resources.
  • Support offsite testing activities.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s degree in a STEM discipline (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math) from an accredited university with 8 years of relevant industry experience.
  • A current active in-scope U.S. Government (DoD) Department of Defense Secret security clearance and ability to obtain special program access is required.
  • Comprehend aerodynamic and propulsion principles and coefficients.
  • Applicants must have familiarity with coding in common engineering languages, e.g.  C/C++, Python, Matlab, and LabView.
  • Basic understanding of Wheatstone bridge sensors, strain gages, thermocouples, accelerometers, optical encoders.
  • Systems engineering experience with the ability to identify and interpret requirements and generate ICDs (Interface Control Document).
  • Ability to read, interpret and implement technical drawings, electrical schematics, requirements specifications and test procedures.
  • Familiarity with electronic equipment such as oscilloscopes, spectrum analyzers, signal generators, power supplies and multi-meters.
  • Highly motivated and willing to quickly learn aspects of wind tunnel testing and data acquisition.
  • Ability to travel up to 25% of the time.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Bachelors/Masters Degree in Aerospace Engineering with focus on controls or applications with instrumentation and data collection.
  • 10 years of engineering experience in an aerospace engineering environment.
  • Experience in electro-mechanical assembly (soldering irons, crimpers, etc.).
  • Experience with wind tunnel operations and testing methods.
  • Direct hands-on experience with data acquisition systems and test measurement.
  • Experience with electro-mechanical controls engineering applications.
  • Knowledge of specialized wind tunnel instrumentation types and their usage (force balances, electronically scanned pressure modules, hot wire anemometry).
  • Strain gage design, selection and application experience.
  • Understanding of metrology, uncertainty analysis, and sources of measurement error.
  • Knowledge with the Fortran programming language.
