Northrop Grumman is seeking a Hardware Engineer to join our team supporting PARCS.  The position is based at a facility in Colorado Springs.

This role conducts research, design, development, and testing of information processing hardware such as computer chips, circuit boards, computer systems, and electrical components. Designs new or modifies existing products, develops technical specifications for production, analyzes hardware configuration and processing solutions, and tests hardware conformance to specifications.

Job responsibilities will include, but not be limited to, the following:

Working in an Agile environment and serve as a hardware engineer supporting the Perimeter Acquisition Radar Attack Characterization System (PARCS). Conducts research, design, development, and testing of information processing hardware such as computer chips, circuit boards, computer systems, and electrical components. Designs new or modifies existing products, develops technical specifications for production, analyzes hardware configuration and processing solutions, and tests hardware conformance to specifications. Complete understanding and wide application of technical principle, theories, and concepts in the field. General knowledge of other related disciplines. Provide technical solutions to a wide range of complex difficult problems. Solutions are imaginative, thorough, practicable, and consistent with organization objectives. Works under only general direction. Independently determines and develops approach to solutions. Contributes to the completion of specific programs and projects. Frequent inter-organizational and outside customer contacts. Represents the organization in providing solutions to difficult technical issues associated with specific projects.

Basic Qualifications:

    • Bachelor's degree with 5 years of hardware engineering experience; 3 Years with Masters's; 0 Years with PhD; 9 or more years of experience may be considered in lieu of degree
    • Position requires U.S. Citizenship.
    • Active DOD Secret Security Clearance

    Preferred Qualifications:

    • In-depth understanding of PARCS Phased Array technology and projects
    • Knowledge of emerging phased array modernization techniques and common repair practices
    • Detailed understanding of Air and Space Force Depot Level Maintenance processes and procedures
    • Experience with Signal Processing and Receive functions in phased array radars
    • Ability to perform depot level maintenance in a high-pressure operational environment
    • Expertise in using the Technical Baseline Configuration manager ARAS for documentation control
    • Ability to read engineering drawings and legacy documentation in order to make sound engineering decisions
    • Experience in the use of Atlassian tool
    • Expertise in leading projects in a multi-disciplined engineering environment.
