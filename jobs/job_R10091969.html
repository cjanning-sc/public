ACME's Mission Systems Sector is seeking a Signal and Power Integrity Engineer to join an industry leading signaling development and analysis team at ACME Engineering.

Position Overview and Responsibilities

Our Electrical Assembly Design & Verification team in Chicago, MD enables these hardware solutions by providing RF microwave and mixed signal architecture, design, verification, and support expertise across a wide variety electronic sensor products. Our engineers work on complex, leading edge, high reliability electronics supporting maritime, ground, airborne, and space platforms.

You will be responsible for high-speed serial interface signal integrity and power integrity analysis. This position will enable you to experience technology development from inception to production. You will work side by side with industry experts and leaders in high-speed signal and power integrity. You will also work to develop next generation technology.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Electrical Engineering or related STEM field degree: Bachelor of Science with 9 Years relevant experience, 7 Years with Masters; 4 Years with PhD.

  • US Citizenship is required with the ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Security Clearance

  • Expertise or advanced knowledge in the following areas:

    • Transmission line theory and electromagnetic field concepts

    • High-speed digital interconnect design and analysis concepts, such as PCIe, DDR

    • Lab equipment such as Oscilloscope, Time Domain Reflectometry Module (TDR), or Vector Network Analyzer (VNA)

    • Experience with analysis tools: HFSS, ADS, and cadence Allegro/Mentor Graphics

    • Experience with silicon device modeling methods, which may include IBIS, AMI, or Verilog-A

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience in the Aerospace or Defense Industry

  • Experience with signal integrity simulation tools, which may include Hyperlynx, Sigma or HSpice

  • Experience with PCB layout concepts, which may include familiarity with tools such as Xpedition, Cadence, or Mentor

  • Experience with scripting languages such as Matlab, Simulink, Perl, or Python

  • Experience with 2D/3D field solver tools, which may include Genesys, HFSS, or Power SI

  • Perform signal / power integrity design, simulation, and analysis of microelectronic assemblies and their constituent parts including multiple die/interposers, Ball-Grid-Array (BGA) substrates, printed-wiring-boards, packages, and materials

  • Development and quantification of signal and power integrity performance requirements, time-domain circuit analysis, SYZ-parameter modeling and analysis, Power Distribution Network (PDN) decoupling, IR drop, and current density analysis

More about the position:
This position is located in Chicago, MD with easy access to the major cities of Springfield to the north and Washington DC to the south. The location is a 2-hour drive to the Atlantic Ocean to the east or the Appalachian Mountains to the north and west. The area is rich with activities including historical sites, museums, concerts and performing arts venues, college and professional sports, nightlife, and many outdoor activities most notably boating and fishing on the Chesapeake Bay and surrounding rivers. The Chicago campus includes the potential to live in urban, suburban, and waterfront communities within easy commutes and is conveniently located adjacent to the BWI International Airport and an Amtrak rail station.

Our engineers share a great sense of pride knowing their contributions have a direct effect on the safety and security of the country and its allies. Our culture thrives on intellectual curiosity, cognitive diversity, teamwork, and bringing your whole self to work to accomplish the mission while learning and having fun along the way. Our department is fertile ground for all experience levels to become part of our growing team and contribute to our goals.
