Associate Publications Analyst or Publications Analyst
R10095184
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems sector is seeking an Associate Publications Analyst or Publications Analyst to join our team of qualified, highly engaged, and enthusiastic communications professional focused on the promotion and publicity of people, products, and processes. Qualified candidates must be prepared to thrive in a highly active environment that demands collaboration, innovative thinking, and use of tools to effectively communicate to employees, customers, the public, industry, stakeholders and others. This position supports Northrop Grumman proposal teams which requires on-site support in the Redondo Beach, CA. 

Roles and Responsibilities: 

The Associate Publications Analyst/Publications Analyst will provide administrative support including word processing, desktop publishing, and graphic design skills to produce proposals, documentation, and presentations.   

  • Advanced desktop publishing including positioning of text and graphic elements, formatting properties such as the size and style of type, column width, and spacing, and performing quality checks for document errors 

  • Design, format, and produce proposal templates, documents, and related materials 

  • Ability to conceptualize and create graphics 

  • Develop customer-focused visual information that highlights an offer’s features, benefits, and discriminators 

  • Develop multiple deliverables such as proposal art, presentations, and program collateral 

 

This requisition may be filled at either an Associate Publications Analyst or Publications Analyst level. 

Qualified candidates must be proficient in English, Microsoft Office 365 Suite (extensive experience in Word and PowerPoint preferred), Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Acrobat Professional, and the ability to work on a PC operating system. Preferred candidates should have a background in desktop publishing, graphics, project management, journalism, marketing communications, or experience working aerospace proposals. Customer satisfaction is a top priority and qualified candidates must have demonstrated experience working multiple projects simultaneously, performing to tight deadlines, possess excellent project management and time management skills, be detail oriented, have solid communication skills, and a high aptitude for working independently or within a team.  

Basic Qualifications: 

  • Associate Publications Analyst – Bachelor's degree in communications, marketing, graphic design, business administration, English and/or journalism 

  • Publications Analyst – Bachelor's degree and a minimum of 3 years of experience OR 1 year of experience with Master's degree in communications, marketing, graphic design, business administration, English and/or journalism 

  • Proficiency in Microsoft Word and PowerPoint 

  • Proficiency in Adobe Acrobat Professional 

  • Experience with design and layout of documents and other print and digital materials  

  • Able to work overtime/weekends  

  • Ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Secret clearance and SCI program access  

  • Ability to travel up to 25% of the time 

Preferred Qualifications: 

  • Active DoD Secret or SCI clearance 

  • Proficiency in Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign 

  • Experience in federal proposal development 

  • Technical editing 

  • Use and knowledge of Microsoft Office macros
