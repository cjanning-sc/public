Northrop Grumman Space Systems is seeking a Staff Systems Engineer. This position will be located in Roy, Utah and will support the will support the business portfolio for the Strategic Deterrent Systems Division which includes the Sentinel program, Minuteman III programs and related business development.

What You’ll Get To Do: 

Northrop Grumman Space Systems is seeking process-oriented self starters to join our Engineering process and architecture modeling team who can help us change the game with regards to how Northrop Grumman develops, manages, and communicates process content.

As a Technical Leader, Architect, process engineer and process change facilitator, the qualified applicant will assist in the development of an integrated, multi-dimensional, and maintainable process model, focusing on Engineering processes but also supporting other functional areas related to program execution (Program Management, Quality, Supply Chain, Production, Logistics, Business Management, etc.).

Specific duties to include, but are not limited to the following:

  • Develop Process Architecture(s) and Process Flows
  • Create and release standard processes into the modeling tool in the form of process maps, architectures, diagrams, resources, storyboards, landing pages, etc.
  • Integrating cross-functional processes covering the entire program lifecycle
  • Decomposing & sequencing activities, de-conflicting process overlaps, and developing new content to fill process gaps
  • Identifying giver/receiver artifacts and deliverables
  • Clarifying roles and associating process aids
  • Communicating with Integrated Product Teams (IPTs), functional teams, and other stakeholder organizations to ensure process intent, best practices, and lessons learned are captured
  • Supporting process modeling tool administration
  • Supporting program deployments of the process model to ensure the process needs of programs are being met
  • Tailoring standard processes according to unique program requirements
  • Facilitating the application of controlled changes and configuration management on program-tailored process models
  • Applying program model updates resulting from standard process changes
