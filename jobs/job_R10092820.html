Manager Programs 2 - Small Form Factors Dept
R10092820
1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Mission Systems sector is looking for a Manager, Programs 2, to run critical software/firmware and hardware development programs as part of the Small Form Factors Department in San Diego, California.

Program Managers are responsible for leading all phases of the program life cycle from inception (proposal development) through startup, execution, and completion (contract closeout). The individual will lead a cross-functional organization aligned to common program performance goals. Primary responsibilities are the management of cost, schedule, and technical performance of company programs or subsystems and include, but are not limited to:

  • Establishing a program organization that effectively addresses customer requirements and incorporates the necessary internal and external sub-organizations
  • Leading and directing cross-functional Integrated Program Teams (IPT) to meet program cost, schedule and technical performance objectives
  • Cultivating customer relationships and intimacy to develop further opportunities within the customer community
  • Delivering presentations to customers, executive management and other program stakeholders
  • Leadership of employees including mentoring, problem solving for all employees and functions reporting to the program directly (Engineers, support staff, contractors)
  • Identification, distribution, tracking, and completion of program requirements
  • Measuring and reporting program performance
  • Conducting thorough risk & opportunity management practices including identification, mitigation and realization
  • Managing the creation of design concepts, criteria, and engineering efforts for product research, development, integration and test.
  • Ensuring program team understands and adheres to contract scope, and manages change through control board activities  
  • Creation, review and finalization of the program Statement of Work
  • Participating in the negotiation of contracts, contract changes, specifications, operating budgets, schedule milestones, and key terms and conditions.
  • Development and adherence to budget and schedule baselines utilizing Earned Value Management (EVM) or similar cost & schedule control methodologies and tools
  • Adherence to all internal processes, policies, and applicable industry standards
  • Identifying, allocating and managing program resources, including workforce planning
  • Managing Government/customer supplied property or information (GFE, CFE, etc.)
  • Managing suppliers to meet program objectives.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s Degree and 7 years, or Master’s and 5 years’ experience supporting U.S. Government contracts and customers and/or project management in other industries
  • Demonstrated success leading the performance of tasks on schedule, at cost and achieving requirements as either project lead, integrated program team or cost account manager
  • Earned Value Management Systems (EVMS) experience and/or training
  • Must possess strong organization and interpersonal skills to communicate effectively verbally and in writing with all levels of management and individual contributors.
  • Active, in-scope (most recent investigation within the last five years) DoD Secret Clearance and ability to obtain/maintain special program accesses within a reasonable period of time

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Active DOD Top Secret SCI Clearance
  • Technical Bachelor's degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering or Math) discipline
  • Experience with management of Agile Software/Firmware development programs
  • Experience with Space environments, Network Engineering, and/or communications systems development
  • Experience building strong customer relationships
  • Systems Engineering experience (technical scope of work, and work breakdown)
  • Technical Planning and resource management
  • Experience with managing and directing suppliers
  • Experience with Capture and Proposal management
