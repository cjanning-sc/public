Northrop Grumman Enterprise Services is seeking a well-qualified Linux Computer Systems Analyst to join our dynamic team of technical professionals in Chandler, AZ.

Roles and responsibilities will include but not be limited to the following:

  • Perform as Linux Systems Administrator for multiple networks in the classified environment.

  • Maintain compliant networks with up-to-date software, hardware, patching, updates, etc.

  • Excellent oral, written, email, and phone communications skills, and able to communicate effectively at all levels of the organization.

  • Maintain smooth operation of multi-user computer systems, including coordination with network, software, system engineers, PC desktop technicians, project managers, end users, and IT management. 

  • Build, configure, and set up new environments, and decommission old network environments.

  • Quickly learn to maintain specialize computer equipment.

  • Analyze customer requirements and determine equipment and software solutions.

  • Recommend and implement system enhancements that will improve the performance and reliability.

  • Make recommendations to purchase hardware, software, and system components.

  • Perform upgrading/patching, monitoring, problem resolution and configuration management.

  • Create and maintain user and computer accounts and modify file permissions and security access lists.

  • Test new releases of products to ensure compatibility and minimize user impact.

  • Develop and document technical processes and procedures.

  • Adhere to strict Information Systems security guidelines.

  • Maintain security audit and logging information on all classified networked and standalone computers as directed by the Information Systems Security Manager (ISSM).

  • Report project status as required for all recurring and non-recurring efforts.

  • Works under minimal direction and independently determines and develops approach to solutions.

This position can be filled at a higher level based on the qualifications listed below:

Basic Qualifications for Level 3:

  • Bachelor’s degree and 5 years of related experience or 9 years of related experience is lieu of degree.

  • Active DoD Secret security clearance is required on Day 1 of employment; maintaining the required clearance will be a condition of continued employment.

  • Must have current DoD 8570 IAT Level II (e.g., Security+ CE, CySA+, GICSP, CCNA-Security, GSEC, CND, SSCP)  as condition of continued employment.

  • Demonstrable experience with RedHat Enterprise Linux OS and Linux administration.

  • Experience troubleshooting, installing, and configuring desktops running Linux OS.

  • Must have the ability to obtain, and maintain, access to Special Programs as condition of continued employment.

Basic Qualifications for Level 4:

  • Bachelor’s degree and 9 years of related experience or 13 years of related experience is lieu of degree.

  • Active DoD Secret security clearance is required on Day 1 of employment; maintaining the required clearance will be a condition of continued employment.

  • Must have IAT Level II certification (Security+ CE) or equivalent as condition of continued employment.

  • Experience with RedHat Enterprise Linux OS and Linux administration.

  • Experience troubleshooting, installing, and configuring desktops running Linux OS.

  • Must have the ability to obtain, and maintain, access to Special Programs as condition of continued employment.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience with Nessus, Splunk, SCAP, NIST, system audits, vulnerability scanning, RMF.

  • Experience with High Performance Computing (HPC), Slurm, Bright Cluster Manager, PBS Professional

  • Experience with Certification & Accreditation process.

  • Basic networking, including subnets, routing, and VLANs; Cisco experience and/or certification.

  • Windows Operating system experience.

  • CompTIA A+, Network+, Dell, HP, or other certification(s).

  • Experience with troubleshooting and problem solving.

  • Experience in Delinea, Active Directory, configuring user and computer accounts.

  • Experience supporting VMware VDI (Virtual Desktop Infrastructure).

  • Background experience creating technical documentation, how-to's, and SOP’s.

  • Must have the ability to obtain, and maintain, access to Special Programs as condition of continued employment.

  • Must have excellent customer service skills and people skills, including excellent oral, written, email, and phone communications skills.

  • Must have a cybersecurity mindset and the ability to educate others on it.

  • Self-starter with personal initiative, able to follow direction, good organizational skills.

  • Works well in a team environment; supportive, collaborative, cooperative, service oriented.

  • The ability to lift 40 pounds.

  • Flexibility to work a variety of shifts and available to work overtime. Must be able to work occasional evenings and weekends for maintenance.
