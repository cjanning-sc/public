The Maintenance Manager is responsible for administering and managing the maintenance of company aircraft, ground support equipment, and hangar facilities. The Maintenance Manager will serve as the FAR 145 Repair Station Accountable Manager and manage a team of Shift Supervisors and Chief Inspectors who oversee and direct the activities of 18 maintenance staff members at three company locations.

The primary work location of this position is at the company hangar facility at the Washington-Dulles Airport (IAD) in Virginia.

Other job responsibilities include:

  • Directing, motivating, developing, and engaging a diverse workforce in a dynamic environment across multiple locations

  • Coordinating with department leadership on day-to-day operational decisions and long-range strategic plans and budgets

  • Coordinating maintenance department activities with other departments such as Operations, Safety, Standards, Training and Administration

  • Establishing operational procedures and policies to ensure safe, effective, and cost-efficient departmental practices

  • Providing technical expertise on maintenance procedures, certifications, and aircraft airworthiness to all company operating sectors

  • Interfacing with the FAA on all regulatory compliance issues

  • Directing the administration of relationships with all airframe and powerplant manufacturers and other vendor services

BASIC QUALIFICATIONS

  • Bachelor's Degree and 15 years of aviation maintenance experience with a background in FAR Part 145 repair station operations (will consider additional experience in lieu of education)

  • Experience on corporate-class aircraft, including turbojet and turboprop aircraft

  • Previous aircraft maintenance management experience

  • FAA Aircraft and Powerplant certificate

  • Current FAA Inspection Authorization

  • High school diploma or equivalent

  • Eligibility to secure and maintain an airport-issued security badge

  • Eligibility to secure and maintain a US-issued passport

  • Proficiency with CMP and CAMP Maintenance Tracking programs

  • Proficiency with Microsoft Office products

  • Outstanding written and verbal communication skills

  • US citizenship

PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS

  • Maintenance training and experience on Citation 680, Gulfstream 550, and King Air 350 aircraft
