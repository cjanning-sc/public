ACME Engineering, Strategic Space Systems Division has an opening for a Sr. Principal Hybrids Component Engineer to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Dayton, Ohio.

Essential Functions:

  • Creating and maintaining part & process specifications to design, procure, assemble and install high reliability hermetic and non-hermetic Hybrid Microcircuits primarily to MIL-PRF-38534 Class K, H, L and F.

  • Assist design engineering with proper part selection and application. 

  • Participate in resolving design, manufacturing or supplier technical issues.

  • Support anomaly investigations and failure analysis. 

  • Create test plans and procedures. 

  • Create and deliver briefings to update the team and management. 

  • Support Design Engineering, Procurement, Integration & Test, and Production.

  • Demonstrate understanding with analog and digital circuits.

  • Demonstrate understanding with the usage of discrete active/passive components and microcircuits (ICs).

  • Demonstrate understanding with DC-DC Converter design topologies, Oscillator design topologies.

  • Demonstrate understanding with hybrid thick film, thin film and assembly module designs, materials, layouts, design rules and active/passive trims.

  • Support Hybrid manufacturing processes (including wire-bonding, epoxy/eutectic attach) and manufacturing flows.

  • Support and participate in Hybrid Element Evaluation, hybrid in-line screening, Groups A, B, C and D testing, hybrid qualification, HCAs (Hybrid Construction Analysis), DPAs (Destructive Physical Analysis).

  • Support STEs, test setups auto and manual, fixturing, test programs and procedures, equipment calibration.

  • Support and participate with various hybrid design analysis such as Stress, Derating, Thermal, Worst Case Analysis (WCA), Radiation/RLAT.

  • Support and participate with reviews and acceptance of all supplier manufacturing drawing and PIDs (Product Identification Document).

Basic Qualifications: 

  • Bachelors of Science Degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, or Math) discipline plus 9 years of engineering experience ; OR Master’s Degree in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, or Math) discipline plus 7 years of engineering experience.

  • Experience in parts, materials and process selection, manufacturing support, failure analysis or processing of materials for space hardware

  • Knowledge in the following MIL standard documents: MIL-PRF-38534 for Hybrids, MIL-STD-1580 for DPA, MIL-PRF-38535 for Microcircuits (ICs), MIL-PRF-19500 for Semiconductor Devices or TOR-2006(8583)

  • Ability to obtain and maintain DOD Top Secret Clearance or SCI Access

  • Hands on design and manufacturing experience of Hybrid Microcircuits

  • Active, in-scope DoD Top Secret clearance or SCI access
