The BWI Factory Quality Final Assembly Team is looking for a Staff Quality Engineer to serve as a Lead QE for the West Building Final Assembly areas of Radar System Integration and Test, Flex Electronics, and the Shipping Cell.

The selected candidate will:

  • Establish and apply strong technical knowledge in quality engineering, specifications/standards, drawing interpretation, measurement and analysis methods, processes and products, and relevant technologies
  • Identify and resolve complex technical and quality problems, issues, and defects on the production floor through problem-solving methodology, root cause investigation and corrective action implementations
  • Design and analyze inspection and testing processes, mechanisms and equipment while ensuring that correct and effective metrics are utilized to improve product compliance, minimize risks, and increase quality performance across the factory. 
  • Monitor and verify quality in accordance with statistical process, and/or other control procedures
  • Ability to coordinate/collaborate with external quality teams in data gathering, problem-solving, and risk mitigation to ensure quality standards are in place and maintained
  • Act as advisor to management and customers on advanced technical research studies and applications
  • Communicate program execution status and quality metrics to a wide audience of professionals and leadership
  • Review and evaluate non-conforming material for compliance to drawings, specifications, and standards
  • Provide engineering and manufacturing support for the inspection and quality engineering organization, while assisting in the corrective action board (CAB) and data-gathering and analysis
  • Use LEAN concepts and Six Sigma tools as aides to problem resolution
  • Experienced facilitator of Root Cause and Corrective Action activities.
  • Mentor and provide guidance to team members and less experienced personnel
  • Apply advanced technical principles, theories/concepts, policies and industry standards
  • Perform floor assessments and audits while collaborating with the compliance team

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor of Science degree in STEM discipline.
  • 14+ years of relevant work experience (12+ years with a master’s degree or 9+ years with a PhD)
  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills and confidence to brief senior management
  • Previous experience working in Manufacturing Quality
  • Six Sigma Greenbelt Certification or Equivalent
  • Experience with a manufacturing execution system such as SAP
  • Experience leading projects and/or teams
  • Possess technical knowledge in quality specifications ISO 9001 and/or AS9100 Quality Management System and IPC Industry Standards (J-STD-001, IPC-A-610, and/or IPC-A-620)
  • Proficiency in MS Office Applications especially Excel, Word, Power Point
  • Must be able to obtain and maintain a Secret Security Clearance. US Citizenship required.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree in Mechanical, Materials, Aerospace, Electrical Engineering
  • Master’s degree in STEM discipline.
  • Experience in final assembly and integration and testing of electronic assemblies
  • Experience in selloff/shipping compliance
  • Demonstrated experience integrating with multiple departments
  • LEAN Six Sigma Black Belt or Master Black Belt certification Highly Preferred
  • Knowledge of ISO 9001/AS9100 and internal auditing
  • Experience in a DoD Manufacturing environment.
  • Active DOD Secret Clearance
