Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems has an opening for a remote and rotational Operations & Sustainment (O&S) Site Manager for a rotational OCONUS program. When deployed the O&S Site Manager will lead a group of 30+ employees and will direct all aspects of program operations and logistics.

In this role, the selected candidate will be responsible for:

  • Program management and leadership at various sites on a rotating basis
  • Direct customer interface at all operating sites
  • Senior Northrop Grumman representative at all operating sites
  • Quality and mission assurance
  • Standardized training of personnel
  • Asset readiness and analysis
  • Budgetary decisions
  • Communication with Program Management and IPT leadership
  • Complex and dynamic problem resolution
  • Strategic plans development
  • Contingency plans development
  • Mobilization, deployment, and retrograde of program assets and personnel

The selected candidate will be responsible for the cost, schedule, and technical performance of company programs and subsystems. The selected candidate will serve as primary customer contact for program activities on site. Monitor technical performance and provide IWO oversight. The selected candidate will establish milestones and monitor adherence to master plans and schedules, identify  program problems, and yields solutions. The selected candidate will direct the work of employees assigned to the program from technical, manufacturing, and administrative areas.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's Degree at least 9 years of experience in aviation operational planning, execution, and training.
  • Active DOD Secret clearance, within scope.
  • Ability to obtain and maintain Top Secret and Special Program Access (SAP).
  • Must be willing to work evenings, weekends and holidays as needed and up to 30% of travel.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Master of Science degree in the field of aviation.
  • At least 10 years of experience in aviation operational planning, execution, and training.
  • At least 5 years of experience managing large organizations (20+) with multi-million dollar budgets.
  • Experience managing highly technical projects which have delivered critical/complex hardware and/or capability to customers.
  • Military experience leading tactical and operational teams with increasing levels of responsibility,
  • FAA Pilot with Commercial Privileges and at least 2000 hours mishap free flight time.
  • Certified Flight Instructor or military equivalent.
  • Experience living and working in OCONUS locations.
  • Willing to work evenings, weekends and holidays as needed and up to 75% of travel.
  • Willing to work curtailed hours when not deployed.
  • Ability to understand new technologies and help expand new business opportunities.
