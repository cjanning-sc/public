Strategic Space Systems Division (SSSD) is an industry-leading provider for prime satellite and payload capabilities and directed energy and electronics solutions for national security, military, and civil customers. We are built on a heritage of providing innovative, affordable and reliable aerospace and defense products that our customers rely on to achieve mission success. Join the Space revolution and make the impossible, possible.

ACME Engineering, Strategic Space Systems Division, has an opening for a Staff Materials and Process Engineer to join our team of highly qualified and diverse individuals. This position will be located in Dayton, Ohio.

Essential Functions:

Have a high degree of understanding of materials and processes (M&P), EEE Parts, high reliability electronics manufacturing, subcontractor oversight, materials selection, assembly drawings, failure analysis of electronics, test procedures and qualification of new M&P for space applications.  Develop and implement PM&P processes and have a good understanding of prohibited materials, Printed Circuit Boards (PCBs) and IPC/NASA/Military workmanship requirements for space hardware.  Take ownership of tasks, multitask, oversee delegated tasks, check results for accuracy and document the results.  Participate in design reviews, program reviews, failure analysis, trade studies and qualification review boards in support of PM&P issues across the entire Spacecraft Design (subsystems, units, slices, CCAs).  Formulate detailed instructions/test plans and be the lead in their execution with a proven understanding of data analysis methodologies and knowledge of design and manufacturing processes.  Lead and coordinate internal and external Materials & Processes efforts.  Collaborate closely with Subject Matter Experts, suppliers, procurement, design, analysis, manufacturing, quality and integration engineers, as well as functional management.

  • Bachelor's Degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering or Mathematics (STEM) discipline plus 13 years of engineering experience OR Master's Degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering or Mathematics (STEM) discipline plus 11 years of engineering experience.

  • Proven leadership experience on a project and/or team.

  • Experience with qualification of parts, materials or processes for space applications. 

  • Knowledge of Electrical/Electronic/Electro-mechanical parts or technologies.
