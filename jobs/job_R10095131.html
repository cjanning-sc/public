1st Shift (United States of America)

  • The Paralegal reports directly to a senior attorney in the Corporate Secretary’s Office and works with the attorneys and paralegals in the Corporate Secretary’s Office to:

    • draft routine corporate documents, including organizational documents, board resolutions and other certificates to form and maintain the company’s U.S. and foreign subsidiaries;
    • assist with obtaining legalization of corporate documents and certificates;
    • assist in the preparation and filing of routine SEC filings, including Section 16 forms, and NYSE filings; 
    • assist in the preparation of documentation for amendments, withdrawals, mergers and dissolutions of corporations and other forms of business entities;
    • prepare and file annual state and foreign filings for the company’s subsidiaries and support the management of the company’s subsidiaries and related governance matters;
    • maintain and update the company’s entity management database;
    • assist with tasks or projects that support the company’s corporate governance activities, including the Board of Directors and the annual shareholders meeting; and
    • conduct research and resolve routine legal questions under the direction of an attorney.

    This individual engages in a variety of tasks ranging from repetitive to those calling for judgment and independent initiative. This position will interact with all levels of management at both the corporate and sector level.  This individual must possess the highest standards of ethics, integrity and maturity of judgment. 

    BASIC QUALIFICATIONS:

    • High School Diploma and a minimum of 7 years experience in a corporate legal-related position
    • Paralegal certificate or degree in paralegal studies (or in the process of obtaining a paralegal certificate) required


    This position requires a basic understanding of corporate entity structures and related documentation, such as charters, bylaws and board resolutions.  Strong organizational and time-management skills are required, including the ability to balance multiple, competing priorities and individuals and meet deadlines.  A strong commitment, collaborative approach and ability to work in a team environment is essential.  The individual should be a motivated self-starter and be able to work independently.  Excellent verbal and written communication skills, as well as project management, analytical, planning, presentation and human relations’ skills are required.  The demonstrated ability to build and foster effective relationships with others is critical.

    PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS:

    A bachelor’s degree and previous in-house or law firm experience with SEC filings, including 10-Ks, 10-Q and Section 16 filings is highly preferred.  In-house corporate paralegal experience managing subsidiaries and/or familiarity with the hCue system or similar entity management system  also preferred.  Experience with managing international subsidiaries is a plus.     

    This position will be located in Falls Church, VA . This position is not relocation eligible.
