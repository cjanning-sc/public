ACME Engineering Space Systems is looking for you to join our team as a Systems Engineer based out of Indianapolis, IN. As a Systems Engineer, you will be developing and sustaining systems using current technologies and others yet to come. We are seeking team members who have a thirst for knowledge, approach each day with optimism, and have a relentless drive to solve complex problems. Creativity and innovation are imperative for this role.

This position will serve on the Sustainment and Modernization of Radar Sensors (SMORS) program, focused on the Ballistic Missile Early Warning System (BMEWS) Precision Acquisition Vehicle Entry (PAVE) Phased Array Warning System (PAWS) (BPP) and specifically the F0022 Systems Integration Laboratory (SIL) at the Product Support Facility (PSF) located on Peterson Space Force Base (SFB).

What You’ll Get to Do

  • Ability to work in a dynamic, fast-paced, team-oriented environment and be a self-starter, efficient in achieving goals, and proactive in pursuing subsequent tasks.
  • Perform System Architecture Design, Development, and Delivery
  • Evaluate system functional and performance characteristics and requirements to define product and services needs
  • Work in a team performing day-to-day delivery order tasks. Such as and not limited to hardware, software, cybersecurity, test, and sustainability functions.
  • Participate in trade studies to support the selection of preferred design solutions, commercial hardware or software components, and preferred course of action alternatives
  • Participate in system design reviews and create and/or update specifications, designs, and technical baseline documentation
  • Support system requirements reviews and create and/or update requirement specifications and technical baseline documentation
  • Generate, review, and update technical baseline artifacts and collaborate with subject matter experts to ensure specifications meet system needs and are accurate, comprehensive, consistent, and complete
  • Review technical baseline documentation to ensure the design meets system needs and is accurate, comprehensive, consistent, and complete
  • Identify, coordinate and generate risk and opportunity conditions. Participate in risk analysis and mitigation formation
  • Collaborate with system modelers who use MBSE tools and techniques to define system interfaces, requirements, and behaviors  
  • Support the development of system test and/or integration plans, procedures, and scenarios used in the conduct of integration and testing activities

Basic Qualifications: 

  • Must have completed a minimum of a bachelor's degree in a STEM discipline
  • Active/Current Interim DoD Secret clearance or higher
  • Bachelor’s Degree in Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics (STEM) with a minimum of two (2) years of CFD, aerospace, defense, or other product development industry experience; OR Master’s Degree in STEM with zero (0) years of CFD, aerospace, defense, or other product development industry experience.
  • Experience writing, updating, and/or verification of system-level requirements
  • Experience writing, developing, and/or managing project technical risk
  • Experience working with others to solve technical system problems, such as represented in the V-Model for the systems development lifecycle
  • Familiarity with MS Office suite of tools (i.e., Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Project)

Preferred Qualifications: 

  • Active DoD Secret clearance or higher
  • Technical writing skills for generating analysis and reports
  • Familiarity with the Ballistic Missile Early Warning System (BMEWS) Precision Acquisition Vehicle Entry (PAVE) Phased Array Warning System (PAWS) (BPP) or other ground-based radars such as the Acquisition Radar Attack Characterization System (PARCS)
  • Familiarity or experience with Agile software development methodologies, i.e., Scrum, Kanban, SAFe
  • Experience in using a requirements management tool, such as DOORS, Jira, and Confluence.
