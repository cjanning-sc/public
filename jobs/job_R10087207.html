3rd Shift (United States of America)

The General Manufacturing Manager 1 is responsible for the direct supervision of employees and processes in a manufacturing environment. The Manager provides direction to employees utilizing management guidelines and general policies. Overall responsibilities include: driving employee efficiency, meeting product cost and schedule targets, managing the area to produce quality products, and engaging employees to achieve their highest potential.

The Manager is responsible for the direct supervision of approximately 28 represented (union) employees and processes in a Manufacturing and Test environment. This position is for 3rd shift.

The Manager is expected to work independently across organizations; manage the use of consumables to achieve budget targets; assign and manage Operator and/or Test technicians to maximize efficiency and achieve target productive yields; implement our process improvements to improve affordability, quality, and schedule; and perform required SAP/MES transactions to manage Operator and/or Test Technicians.

Responsibilities include:

  • Directs and coordinates the activities to plan, prepare, issue, and control production schedules, in conjunction with Material Requirements Planning, to meet production/delivery requirements
  • Oversees the status of work in process, material availability, and potential production problems to ensure that equipment, materials, and services are available, as needed
  • Assigns and manage staff to maximize efficiency and achieve schedule and target productive yields
  • Performs require SAP/MES transactions to manage personnel, planning and financials
  • Provides specific direction and training, as required
  • Ensures 5S practices within cell areas
  • Utilizes both internal and external resources, and coordinates cell and functional activities with quality assurance, manufacturing, supply chain, engineering, inventory control, transportation, etc

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s degree or higher with a minimum of 5 years of professional experience OR at least 7 years of work experience in a technical role may be considered in lieu of a degree
  • At least 3 years of experience in a manufacturing, production, engineering environment
  • At least 2 years of experience in management or leadership of people and/or projects
  • Must be able to obtain and maintain a US Secret Level Security Clearance. (US citizenship required)

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's Degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering or Math) area
  • Experience with SAP/MES
  • Experience with ISO, LEAN/WCM, and problem-solving methodologies
  • 5 or more years of manufacturing experience
  • Prior experience working in a represented (union) environment
