Embark on a career putting innovative, reliable, and agile products and ideas into orbit, and beyond. Northrop Grumman has opportunities waiting for you that play a vital role in human space exploration, national defense, and scientific discovery, supporting multiple programs across the universe. With us, you’ll discover a culture of curiosity and collaboration that will have you defining possible from the day you start. Our space systems connect and protect millions of people on earth every day, now and for the future. Explore your future and launch your career today.

Northrop Grumman Space Systems designs, builds and delivers space related systems to customers around the world. Our main products include launch vehicles and related propulsion systems; strategic deterrents; satellites and associated space components and services; payload and ground systems; and other strategic space-based initiatives. 

We are currently seeking a Principal Technical Accounting Analyst to join the Space Sector Reporting Team in our Dulles, VA offices.

This position will report to the Manager of Technical Accounting and perform technical GAAP accounting research and other financial analysis as required.  This position will work closely with Sector Financial Reporting and Planning, Program Finance, Shared Service Centers and Corporate Financial Reporting.

Position Responsibilities and duties include:

  • Provide on call technical accounting guidance.
  • Review revenue recognition checklists, conclude on treatment and provide guidance on revenue accounting under ASC606.
  • Assist the monthly financial statement close process, including preparing Costpoint, SAP & HFM journal entries on a recurring basis
  • Assist in researching and analyzing application of accounting standards to complex business transactions
  • Assist in drafting white papers summarizing technical accounting issues, application of accounting guidance to the company, alternatives available and recommendations.
  • Prepare presentations of accounting treatment with recommendations for management
  • Draft updates to sector accounting policies for new accounting pronouncements and other changes.
  • Prepare and present accounting and policy training on an as-needed basis.
  • Prepare Costpoint journal entries on a monthly basis to support the close process, including purchase accounting, environmental accounting and other general entries.  
  • Provide support to and respond to questions from external auditors.
  • Perform miscellaneous financial analysis as required.

Basic Qualifications – Level 3 Analyst

  • Bachelor’s degree in accounting or related field, and 6 years of relevant work experience
  • Master’s degree or CPA may be traded for 2 years of experience. Master’s degree and a CPA may be traded to a total of 3 years of experience.
  • Public Accounting IF directly relevant may count for up to an additional year of experience for each year of relevant experience.

 

Basic Qualifications – Level 2 Analyst

  • Bachelor’s degree in accounting or related field, and 3 years of relevant work experience
  • Master’s degree or CPA may be traded for 2 years of experience. Master’s degree and a CPA may be traded to a total of 3 years of experience.
  • Public Accounting IF directly relevant may count for up to an additional year of experience for each year of relevant experience.

Preferred Qualifications

  • Recent Big 4 or recognized public accounting firm audit experience
  • GAAP accounting proficiency and research experience
  • Basic computer and financial analysis skills, including use and understanding of spreadsheets
  • Excellent oral and written communication skills
  • Self-motivated, yet functions well in a team environment
  • Prior experience in the aerospace & defense industry, or experience with cost-to-cost accounting and estimates at completion (EACs)
  • Focused on continuous improvement and identifying efficiencies in recurring processes
