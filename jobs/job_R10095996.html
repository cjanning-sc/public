ACME Engineering Mission Systems is a trusted provider of mission-enabling solutions for global security.  Our organization is chartered with providing the skills, innovative technologies to develop, design, produce and sustain optimized product lines across the sector while providing a decisive advantage to the warfighter. 

We are looking for you to join our team as a Staff Mechanical Engineer based out of Cleveland, OH. As a Staff Mechanical Engineer at ACME Engineering you will have a challenging and rewarding opportunity to be a part of our Enterprise-wide digital transformation. Through the use of Model-based Engineering, DevSecOps and Agile practices we continue to evolve how we deliver critical national defense products and capabilities for the warfighter. Our success is grounded in our ability to embrace change, move quickly and continuously drive innovation. The successful candidate will be collaborative, open, transparent, and team-oriented with a focus on team empowerment & shared responsibility, flexibility, continuous learning, and a culture of automation.

What You’ll get to Do:

Responsible for the successful development and delivery of a hardware item or system within the constraints established by the Project Plan and the customer’s systems specification. Has ultimate responsibility for the delivery of technically compliant work products within the resource, cost and schedule constraints established in the project’s Hardware Development Plan.  Directs and statuses the work of engineering teams as well as that of support staff engaged in planning, project control, configuration management, and subcontract management activities.  Shall be the focal point for customer interface and shall be the design review coordinator and presenter. Shall have authority, delegated by the Program Manager, to make decisions and to deliver high quality products using the organization’s approved processes for the antenna, RF, digital, infrastructure, and test hardware products, including avionics modules, racks for airborne and ground mobile military applications. Perform mechanical design tasks and thermal/structural analyses. Support electrical-mechanical tradeoffs and physical verification and qualification testing for new designs. Mentor and lead junior engineers and interns. 

Qualifications:

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor of Science (BS) degree in Mechanical/Aerospace Engineering or closely related STEM field with a minimum of 14 years experience; OR 12 years experience with a Masters (MS) degree in STEM; OR 9 Years with PhD.
  • Shall have experience with electronics packaging design, including producibility constraints, solids modeling techniques, GD&T, corrosion control, EMI concerns, and thermal/structural analysis methods
  • Shall have effective verbal and written communication skills, the ability to manage a multi-disciplinary engineering team, and the demonstrated ability to manage a project throughout the engineering life cycle.
  • Shall be capable of developing a system in accordance with a system specification and developing the necessary hardware and software requirements specifications.
  • Shall have experience successfully leading a technical team to develop, qualify and deliver a specification compliant product or system within constraints of cost and schedule.
  • Shall have experience with Class 1 and 2 change control and management.
  • Shall have experience developing technical and cost proposals.
  • CLEARANCE: Ability to obtain DoD Secret clearance and SAP/SAR.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience with airborne and ground-mobile military electronics equipment.
  • Proficiency in the use of the SolidWorks, CATIA V5, NX, Patran/Nastran or Flotherm tools
  • Experience with aircraft integration and airborne environmental requirements analysis and verification, including qualification.
  • Functional or project management experience
  • Active DoD Secret Clearance
