ACME Engineering Space Systems is seeking a Mechanical Test Engineering Manager 2 on the Sentinel program, supporting the Post Boost Attitude Control Module (PBACM) Element. This position will be permanently located in Detroit, Michigan with some travel to Akron/Columbus, OH; Madison, WI; and VSFB, OH. Learn more about the Sentinel (GBSD) program here.  ACME Engineering supports the Air Force’s sustainment, development, production and deployment of hardware and system modifications for Intercontinental Ballistic Missile (ICBM) Ground and Airborne Launch Control Systems, Launch Facilities and associated infrastructure.

What you will get to do:

Essential Functions:

  • Lead a small team of Integration and Test (I&T) engineers to integrate, test and deploy the PBACM Element.
  • Perform the duties of a section manager for a small team of test engineers and test tech support within the Sentinel functional hierarchy. Tasks include the performance review process, merit reward process, goal-setting, and general personnel management tasks and mentoring along with further hiring for the PBACM I&T team.
  • Support the test design process along with the design, analysis and fabrication of specialty test equipment required for environmental testing and handling.
  • Help develop and execute the integrated test strategy of the program. It is particularly critical that this individual be an excellent communicator and effective leader.  Collaborate with other IPTs to define test objectives and requirements for test activities in the System Integration Labs.
  • Support the development of test requirements and detailed integration test plans, test information sheets (TIS), test procedures, test readiness reviews (TRRs), test data and test reports. Execute qualification and acceptance testing per those requirements, plans and procedures.
  • Interact with the customer and program leadership to define and maintain the technical baseline.
  • Able to effectively communicate and present to project and program management, other technical managers, technical peers and customers
  • Produce test CONOPS flows that maximizes the utility of test resources while meeting the stringent schedule and technical requirements.
  • Be the Responsible Test Engineer (RTE) on floor for assigned tests running floor ops and crew during the implementation of various tests while verifying and validating test flow down requirements.
  • Generate and maintain detailed test schedules to the program IMS

BASIC QUALIFICATIONS:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in STEM with 6 years of experience; 4 years with a Master’s Degree. Mechanical or similar engineering degree preferred.
  • Active Secret Clearance or higher investigated within the past 5 years
  • 2 years of experience with testing of integrated complex mission systems within spacecraft, ground systems, and/or aircraft along with their subsystem elements.
  • 1 year experience leading a project and/or driving performance against schedule
  • U.S. Citizenship required

PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS:

  • Able to read/generate, interpret and use layout and production drawings and CAD models. Direct experience using NX and/or CATIA V5. Knowledge of GD&T.
  • Excellent communication and organizational skills; strong interpersonal and analytic skills; works well in a team environment within all levels of the test integrated team structure.
  • Working knowledge of systems engineering and I&T principles and processes
  • Knowledge and use of instrumentation and DAS type systems in regards to environmental testing (static loads, dynamic, modal, etc)
  • Direct experience using the DOORS Requirements Management Database
  • Knowledge of CAMEO and/or MBSE principals and CPLM
  • Experience with environmental test cost estimating, EVMS, and working proposals
  • Direct utilization and implementation of test instrumentation, SDAS, DDAS, LCS and test S/W.
  • Knowledge and experience with EGSE, electrical functional testing of spacecraft and/or launch vehicle systems
  • Launch support processing experience at VSFB and/or CCAFS
