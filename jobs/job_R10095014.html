Our Flint, Michigan, Fast Reaction Shop has an immediate opening for a Technician 2 to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals.

The successful candidate will possess knowledge of and experience with the fabrication and troubleshooting of electronic, mechanical, electro-mechanical test equipment and cabling.  You will interface with numerous departments and will directly support cross-campus collaboration. Working at ACME Engineering means making a direct contribution to mission projects that keep our homeland safe, expanding the boundaries of possibility, and reshaping the world we live in.

 

Job Description and Qualifications:

Provides technical support to engineers on a variety of technical tasks. Performs a variety of duties in the electronic, mechanical, electro-mechanical, or optical areas. Constructs, troubleshoots, calibrates, adjusts, tests, and maintains equipment, components, devices, or systems. Works from engineering drawings and written or verbal instructions. Operates related equipment; conducts tests and reports data in prescribed format. Performs calibration and alignment checks; makes adjustments, modifications, and replacements as directed; prepares prescribed compounds and solutions.

May conduct tests and record data to assist with engineering evaluation or analysis.

 

Responsibilities:

  • Provides technical support to the factory floor as well as engineering teams.
  • Collaborate on manufacturability of equipment, providing feedback on capabilities and processes.
  • Build test equipment, electromechanical prototypes, cables, etc.
  • Provide tactile build support; operate soldering, wiring, testing, and all general build equipment.
  • Provides estimates and proposals for sub and final assemblies.
  • Champion first-time-builds of manufacturing test equipment.
  • Support experimental projects driving innovation and technological advancement.
  • Routinely maintain equipment to ensure cleanliness, accuracy, and reliability specific to division requirements.
  • Observes all company policies and procedures, including safety rules, and maintains a clean and orderly work area.
  • Performs other related responsibilities as requested.

 

Basic Qualifications:

  • High School diploma or equivalent and 2 years of additional education and/or related experience.
  • Reads and follows blueprints, specifications, and verbal instructions.
  • Proficient at soldering per IPC-J-STD-001.
  • Familiar with sub and final assembly build structures.
  • Proficient with full test equipment assembly builds from drawings.
  • Demonstrates effective verbal, written, and interpersonal communication skills, as well as the ability to work in a team environment.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Secret Clearance
  • Complex circuit board build/assembly
  • Broad knowledge of complex cable builds
