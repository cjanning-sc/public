ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems has an opening for a System Engineering (SE) Functional Homeroom Section Manager to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.

The selected candidate will lead a team of Combat Vulnerability and Nuclear Surety professionals assigned across MAD COE programs and will report to the Specialty Engineering Department Manager on a 15% allocation.  The selected candidate must currently have and continue to provide their own remaining 85% work scope.  

Among other responsibilities, the successful candidate will:

•Set operating objectives and provide guidance and mentoring to assigned employees 
•Provide assistance to programs on staffing and technical processes associated with Combat Vulnerability or Nuclear Surety
•Develop and maintain assigned talent within the section 
•Manage the performance management process for assigned employees
•Review and make recommendations for promotions and special assignments
•Ensure the assigned workforce is properly trained for successful achievement of program goals
•Provide first-level resolution of personnel issues according to company processes and procedures
•Interact with equivalent level managers and programs to maintain performance


The selected candidate should thrive in a fast-paced work environment with high expectations, significantly diverse assignments, collaborative/team settings across all levels.  


Basic Qualifications:

  • Must have a Bachelor of Science degree AND 9 years of related professional/military experience in Engineering OR a Master’s degree AND 7 years of related professional/military experience in Engineering.
  • Experience working in Combat Vulnerability or Nuclear Surety
  • Must have an active DoD Secret clearance (or higher) that is in-scope (granted or renewed within the last 6 years or is under continuous evaluation). 
  • Must be able to attain and maintain Special Access Program (SAP).


Preferred Qualifications:

  • Knowledge of Combat Vulnerability Standards (i.e. MIL-HDBK-268, MIL-HDBK-2069, DI-MISC-80564)
  • Proficiency with Combat Vulnerability Toolsets (i.e. COVART, AJEM, ESAMS, or BRAWLER )
  • Experience with Combat Vulnerability Analyses  (i.e.Statistical Analysis, Finite Element Analysis, Design of Experiments)
  • Knowledge of Nuclear Surety Standards (i.e. AFI 63-125, AFMAN 91-118/91-119, AFI 91-107)
  • Proficiency with Nuclear Surety Toolsets (i.e. DOORS, Erwin Evolve Suite)
  • Experience with Nuclear Surety Analyses (i.e. Fault Tree Analysis, Failure Modes and Effects Analysis, MBSE)
  • Experience in the military aerospace development environment
  • Active, In scope DoD Top Secret or SCI clearance
