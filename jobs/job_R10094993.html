The Payload and Ground Systems organization within the Northrop Grumman Space Systems pushes the boundaries of innovation, redefines engineering capabilities, and drives advances in various sciences. Our team is chartered with providing the skills, innovative technologies to develop, design, produce and sustain optimized product lines across the sector while providing a decisive advantage to the warfighter. Come be a part of our mission!

As a Systems Engineer, you will be developing and sustaining systems using current technologies and others yet to come. We are seeking team members who have a thirst for knowledge, approach each day with optimism, and have a relentless drive to solve complex problems. Creativity and innovation are imperative for this role.

We are seeking applicants for Systems Engineering to work the Sustainment and Modernization of Radar Sensors (SMORS) program. This position will serve as an Engineer focused on the Ballistic Missile Early Warning System (BMEWS) Precision Acquisition Vehicle Entry (PAVE) Phased Array Warning System (PAWS) (BPP). This position will be located in Colorado Springs, CO.

What You’ll Get to Do

  • Ability to work in a dynamic, fast paced, team-oriented environment and be a self-starter, efficient in achieving goals and proactive in pursuing subsequent tasks.

  • Perform System Architecture Design, Development, and Delivery

  • Evaluate system functional and performance characteristics and requirements to define product and services needs

  • Work in a team performing day-to-day delivery order tasks.  Such as and not limited to hardware, software, cybersecurity, test and sustainability functions.

  • Participate in trade studies to support the selection of preferred design solutions, commercial hardware or software components, and preferred course of action alternatives

  • Participate in system design reviews and creating and/or updating specifications, designs and technical baseline documentation

  • Support system requirements reviews and creating and/or updating requirement specifications and technical baseline documentation

  • Generate, review, and update technical baseline artifacts and collaborate with subject matter experts to ensure specifications meet system needs and are accurate, comprehensive, consistent and complete

  • Review technical baseline documentation changes to ensure the design meets system needs and is accurate, comprehensive, consistent and complete

  • Identify, coordinate and generate risk and opportunity conditions.  Participate in risk analysis and mitigation formation

  • Collaborate with system modelers who use MBSE tools and techniques to define system interfaces, requirements and behaviors

  • Support the development of system test and/or integration plans, procedures and scenarios used in the conduct of integration and testing activities

Basic Qualifications: 

  • Bachelor’s Degree in Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics (STEM) with 0 to 3 years’ experience in a STEM related field; OR Masters Degree in STEM with 0 years’ experience

  • Interim DoD Secret clearance

Preferred Qualifications: 

  • Active DoD Secret clearance

  • Familiarity with MS Office suite of tools (i.e. Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Project)

  • Technical writing skills for generating analysis and reports

  • Familiarity with the Ballistic Missile Early Warning System (BMEWS) Precision Acquisition Vehicle Entry (PAVE) Phased Array Warning System (PAWS) (BPP) or other ground-based radars such as the Acquisition Radar Attack Characterization System (PARCS)

  • Familiarity or experience with Agile software development methodologies, i.e. Scrum, Kanban, SAFe

  • Experience in using a requirements management tool, such as DOORS to update and write system level requirements
