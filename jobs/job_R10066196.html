Overview:

Northrop Grumman Mission Systems sector delivers a decisive advantage to its customers through software-defined, hardware-enabled solutions. The Electrical Assembly Design & Verification section in Linthicum, MD enables these hardware solutions by providing RF microwave and mixed signal architecture, design, verification, and support expertise across a wide variety electronic sensor products. Our engineers work on complex, leading edge, high reliability electronics supporting maritime, ground, airborne, and space platforms.

What You Will Do:

  • Using RF test equipment verify, troubleshoot and repair RF microwave and mixed signal subsystem assemblies
  • Troubleshooting digital circuits and finding root cause of module failure.
  • Deriving test scripts to aid engineering team in troubleshooting
  • Troubleshooting RF test sets
  • Collaborating with peer functions such as Mechanical, Manufacturing, Quality, Supply Chain, and Test
  • Supporting various shifts
  • Finding trends within multiple modules to identify where design updates can be implemented for higher manufacturing yields
  • RF and mixed signal product types include Reference Generators, Waveform Generators, Narrowband Waveform Generators, IF, RF and Multi-Function Receivers, Antenna Interface Units and Array Drivers
  • Collaborating, training and working directly with represented employees
  • RF and mixed signal product types include antenna radiators, circulators, device interposers, filters, manifolds, receiver / exciter assemblies, signal conditioning assemblies, signal generators, and transmit / receive assemblies

Basic Qualifications:

  • Electrical Engineering or related STEM field degree: Bachelor of Science with 2-3 years of RF/microwave experience; Master’s degree with 0-1 year of RF/microwave experience
  • Proven knowledge of RF Microwave test software and test equipment
  • Ability to design, implement, install and maintain electrical systems
  • Effective verbal and written communication skills
  • Productive in a team working environment
  • Applies technical principles, theories, and concepts
  • Contributes to the development of new principles and concepts
  • US Citizenship is required and ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Security Clearance

Industry experience in one or more of the following areas:

  • RF and mixed signal printed circuit board and circuit card assembly level design
  • RF budgeting, frequency planning, RF chain analysis and modeling, schematic capture, electromagnetic simulation, signal integrity analysis
  • Component selection and/or custom component requirements flow down and trades
  • RF and mixed signal printed circuit board level troubleshooting
  • RF and Digital schematic understanding
  • Strong Digital background using JTAG and FPGA Boundary scan testing
  • RF lab equipment familiarity such as network analyzers, spectrum analyzers, signal generators, power meters, etc.

  • AESA / phased array, Radar, SIGINT, or SATCOM experience
  • Technical team leadership
