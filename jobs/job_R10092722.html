Northrop Grumman, Propulsion System is current recruiting for an Automation Technician to support the team at the Promontory Utah facility.

Role description & Responsibilities:

An Automation Technician is responsible for the following:

  • Troubleshoot, repair, modify and maintain of a variety of Allen Bradley PLC’s, VFD’s and other control systems.

  • Perform tasks like software control, trouble shooting PLC’s, replacing components, programming PLC’s, Programming HMI’s.

  • Assist with development of TPM practices to minimize downtime and increase reliability on plant equipment including CNC machines, auto claves, overhead bridge cranes, various PLC controlled systems, etc.

  • Perform test readings using several different instruments, including Analog signal testers, Power monitors, oscilloscopes, signal generators and digital multimeters.

  • Perform automation activities to troubleshoot and repair equipment malfunctions to prevent equipment failures and reduce downtime.

Basic Requirements:

  • 4+ years working in industry as an electronic technician

  • Valid driver license

  • Working on equipment, including climbing ladders to access equipment on roofs in all weather, using man lifts and scissor lifts when necessary

Education/Certifications

  • High School plus 4+ years of related experience

  • Associates Degree or equivalent training in Electronics Technology (Preferred)

Position may be filled at higher level requiring High School plus 6+ years related experience.

Preferred skills:

  • Must be able to understand and follow electrical schematics proficiently without supervision

  • Familiar with machine control systems

  • Familiar with AC/DC drives and servo systems

  • Ability to troubleshoot (complex) machine control systems including PLCs 

  • Experience working with 480 Volt / 3 phase equipment

  • Retrofit obsolete components and systems including controls

  • Familiarity of NDT systems, CNC machines, process/instrumentation controls, camera systems and fire alarm systems

  • Knowledge and experience using oscilloscopes and various high voltage measurement test equipment preferred but not required.

  • Ability to interpret PLC logic as it relates to its application to process equipment for troubleshooting purposes.
