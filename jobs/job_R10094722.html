ACME Engineering Space Systems has an exciting new opportunity for an Information Systems Security Officer in Indianapolis, CO.  The position will be based at Peterson SFB 90% of the time.

Primary Responsibilities:

  • Perform Information System Security Officer (ISSO) duties in support of in-house and external customers.

  • Provide support for proposing, coordinating, implementing, and enforcing information systems security policies, standards, and methodologies.

  • Assist with the management of security aspects of the information system and perform day-to-day security operations of the system. (Auditing)

  • Support security authorization activities in compliance with the Risk Management Framework (RMF) USSTRATCOM package for the Authorization to Operate (ATO).

  • Providing escort and escort guidance to contract mission support personnel for various contract and non-contract partners, and US Government contracted partners within the SIL.

  • Perform assessment of systems and networks within the networking environment and identify where those systems and networks deviate from acceptable configurations, enclave policy, or local policy. This is achieved through passive evaluations such as compliance audits using STIG Viewer, SCAP, Xylok, etc. and active evaluations such as vulnerability assessments utilizing ACAS.

  • Prepare and import into eMASS artifacts such as Test Results (TR), Authorization Boundary Diagrams (ABD), Network Topologies, Flow-diagrams, Hardware and Software listings, Ports, Protocols, and Services Management documentation, supporting Assessment and Authorization activities and maintain the Plan of Actions and Milestones (POA&M).

This position can be staffed at a level 3 or 4, based on the basic qualifications below.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Active Secret clearance required to start

  • Current Security+ certification required to start

  • Level 3: 5 Years with BS degree or 3 Years with Masters or 0 Years with PhD. An additional 4 years of experience required in lieu of a degree.

  • Level 4: 9 Years with BS degree or 7 Years with Masters or 4 Years with PhD.  An additional 4 years of experience required in lieu of a degree.

  • Security engineering skills with a working knowledge of cybersecurity technology and DoD/Federal cybersecurity policy (i.e., DoDI 8500.01, NIST SP 800-53, etc.).

  • IT background to ensure hands on activities such as auditing for Windows and Unix

  • Thorough understanding and utilization of Enterprise Mission Assurance Support Service (eMASS)

  • Familiarity in the Risk Management Framework (RMF) Cybersecurity Lifecycle to include identifying controls and overlays, generating testable requirements, providing analysis of vulnerability, conducting verification testing for compliance assessment.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience with Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) solutions (e.g., LogRhythm) Experience with Endpoint Security (ESS)
