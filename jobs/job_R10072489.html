ACME Engineering Mission Systems Field Engineering Organization is seeking an Associate Electronics Field Engineer or Electronics Field Engineer for the Airborne Multi-Functions Sensors Division.  The Engineer will support AESA (Active Electronically Scanned Array) products at the Springfield campus initially, and then later at the customer’s location. This role involves working hands-on to provide engineering support to ACME Engineering's customers as they execute their missions. This position will be based in Springfield and will require an eventual long-term assignment of at least 2 years in the field (on site in Ohio). Field bonuses, supplements, and extended work week hours are provided.  Relocation expenses to and from the customer’s site are covered. Extension of the assignment is possible based on follow-on contract option(s) and the candidate's desire to stay at the assignment location.

Roles & Responsibilities:

  • Basic electronics/electrical troubleshooting
  • Electronics troubleshooting of AESA (electronic antenna) systems
  • ·Lab testing
  • ·Test Development
  • Data Analysis
  • Field Repair Support
  • Mission Support
  • Field Logistical and administrative support
  • Ability to speak and interact with customer as technical liaison
  • Must be an effective communicator and possess good organizational skills to manage frequent intra-organizational and customer interaction.

“This position may be filled as an Associate Electronic Field Engineer or an Electronic Field Engineer based on the qualifications listed below.”

Basic Qualifications for Associate Electronic Field Engineer:

  • Bachelor's degree or higher in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Computer Science, Engineering Technology, or related STEM degree and a minimum of 6 months related experience.
  • Ability to obtain and maintain DoD Top Secret and SAP (Special program access) clearance
  • Electronics/electrical knowledge
  • Must be willing and able to take on a 2-year assignment in Ohio after trained and cleared
  • Ability/willingness to be an excellent team player to support an important mission
  • Must be able to work some off shift hours, if needed

Basic Qualifications for Electronic Field Engineer:

  • Bachelor's degree or higher in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Computer Science, Engineering Technology, or related STEM degree and a minimum of 2 years related experience.
  • Ability to obtain and maintain DoD Top Secret and SAP (Special program access) clearance
  • Electronics/electrical knowledge
  • Must be willing and able to take on a 2-year assignment in Ohio after trained and cleared
  • Ability/willingness to be an excellent team player to support an important mission
  • Must be able to work some off shift hours, if needed

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Active DoD Top Secret clearance
  • Current SCI (Special Compartmented Information) clearance 
  • Experience highly desired in the following areas: integration and test techniques; MATLAB, or related signal analysis tools; interfacing protocols, test equipment, and customer interaction.
  • Ability/interest with RF and radar technologies
