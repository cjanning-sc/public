As an integral part of our Multi-Discipline Engineering team in Mission Systems you will be on the forefront of developing next generation solutions to protect technology for our nation’s warfighter.

Roles and Responsibilities: 

  • Define system architectures that are resistant to tamper and cyber-attacks
  • Support system requirements development by following standard processes for assessing, implementing, and verifying security requirements
  • Identify Anti-Tamper Critical Program Information (CPI) and writing of Anti-Tamper Plans
  • Support Anti-Tamper/Cybersecurity Integration & Test and executing Verification & Validation requirements
  • Provide analysis, development, implementation, and security assessments to ensure compliance with National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) Special Publication (SP) 800-53, CNSSI 1253, and DoD RMF Knowledge Service guidance
  • Support the continuous assessment of IA Control compliance for the program
  • Utilize reverse engineering to architect and validate AT solutions
  • Strategically plan AT technology development
  • Support security assessments and continuous monitoring
  • Ensure secure software architectures and applications
  • Participate in communities of practice to further domain knowledge

Basic Qualifications:

Bachelor’s degree with 2 years of experience, a Master’s degree with 0 years of experience in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Computer Science, Applied Physics, Applied Mathematics, or related technical fields.

  • U.S. Citizenship and active secret DoD clearance is required with the ability to obtain higher Special Access Program (SAP) clearance
  • Knowledge of DoD based Platforms and Embedded Systems, including but not limited to: RMF, Cryptography and Systems Engineering design and implementation
  • Familiarity with requirements development and derivation or validation and verification
  • Understanding of deliverable technical documents and reports
  • Ability to travel occasionally CONUS and OCONUS (10% or less)

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Advanced degrees in Engineering, Software, or Science
  • Possess a DOD 8570 certification: CompTIA Security+, CISSP, or similar
  • Experience with Reverse Engineering, Hardware Design, Embedded Software or Cryptography
  • Technical problem solving, to include decomposition, root cause analysis, solution development, implementation and monitoring.
  • Experience contributing to and/or making technical presentations to a lead engineer
