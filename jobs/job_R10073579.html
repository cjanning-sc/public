Join ACME Engineering on our continued mission to push the boundaries of possible across land, sea, air, space, and cyberspace. Enjoy a culture where your voice is valued and start contributing to our team of passionate professionals providing real-life solutions to our world’s biggest challenges. We take pride in creating purposeful work and allowing our employees to grow and achieve their goals every day by Defining Possible. With our competitive pay and comprehensive benefits, we have the right opportunities to fit your life and launch your career today.

The Navigation & Cockpit Business unit is seeking an experienced Senior Principal/Principal Contracts Administrator to become a member of the Contracts organization, supporting our Fiber Optic Gyro & Inertial Measurement Unit programs. The position will report directly to the Manager of Contracts and will serve as the Contracts representative and contact with our customer base, managing proposal submissions, negotiations, and contracts.

This position is responsible for all formal communication with the customer, ensuring the team holds NG’s favorable financial and programmatic baseline. In addition, this responsibility will also include advancing product capability.

Candidates interested in hybrid and onsite work are encouraged to apply. In a hybrid situation, the candidate would be expected to work on the Athens, CA site 2-3 days per week to support program reviews, customer meetings, and contracts team events.

Come join our team! You will be a key contributor and empowered to make a difference!

Responsibilities include the following:

  • Lead and coordinate the response to Requests for Proposal (RFPs)

  • Interpret and advise your program team of contract terms and conditions

  • Advise the program team on risks and mitigation strategies based on the contract requirements

  • Ensure proposals/basis of estimates are fully responsive to the SOW requirements

  • Prepare proposal submittal documents and coordinate internal review and approval

  • Coordinate and develop responses to fact-finding, evaluation notices, and RFIs

  • Develop and execute negotiation strategy to reach award

  • Administer, modify, negotiate and terminate standard and nonstandard contractual agreements

  • Identify, document and manage contract changes

  • Oversee contract performance including schedules, deliverables, customer revisions and changes.

  • Complete actions to ensure satisfactory program performance

  • Research contract issues and prepare analysis with citations

  • Maintain excellent working relationships with customers

Basic Qualifications:

  • U.S. citizenship.

  • Ability to obtain/maintain security clearance.

  • Principal Contract Administrator (Level A03): Bachelor’s with 6 years of experience or Master’s Degree with 4 years experience in contracts, subcontracts, finance, or other business management related function.

  • Be attentive to detail and able to handle complex contracts with minimum supervision.

  • Have excellent verbal and written communication skills.

  • Capable skills using MS Office Suite (Excel, Word, PowerPoint) preferred.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Recent experience as a Senior or Principal Contracts Administrator in the defense industry, particularly with development contracts (including but not limited to Non-recurring Engineering, Level of Effort, and Trade Study activities).

  • Excellent working knowledge of FAR/DFARS.
