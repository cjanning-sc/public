ACME Engineering Space Systems is seeking a Principal Electrical Engineer to join the Ground Electrical Team. This position is located in Detroit, UT and supports the Ground Subsystems Support Contract (GSSC) program.

What You’ll Get To Do:

The selected candidate will be a contributing team member with following responsibilities:

• Support multiple Air Force working groups, potentially covering different engineering disciplines, according to weapon system priorities.

• Find, review, and develop new ideas to test and maintain aging weapon system equipment. Present rationale for needed changes and the chosen engineering approach to internal specialty engineering boards and Air Force customers.

• Document those changes according to government and commercial standards and Air Force processes. Work and advocate those corrections throughout the review cycle.

• Support Air Force in troubleshooting and maintenance efforts on-site at missile wings and depots.

• Perform analysis of systems and historical maintenance data to provide inputs to Air Force priorities and risks.

• Research systems and subsystems to complete/supplement existing documentation. Prepare, review, and present materials for systems and subsystems to train individuals at or below your technical level.

Basic Qualifications:

•Bachelor of Science degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Math) discipline and 5 years of experience; 3 years with Master’s Degree; 0 years with PhD

•Demonstrated application of innovative solutions to complex problems that arise within the testing and sustainment of existing electromechanical equipment

•Experience and knowledge in working with Nuclear Hardness and Survivability discipline

•Experience writing technical papers on EMI/EMC effects on electrical components

•Strong written, verbal and interpersonal communication skills, self-motivated

•US Citizenship required, ability to obtain DoD Secret Clearance

Preferred Qualifications:

•Experience with government System Integrity Verification (SIV) process and Air Force Standards.

•Demonstrated competence managing software tools such as PSpice, ANSYS and driving test data compilation and processing.

•Technical EMI/EMC SME on applicable Nuclear Hardness and Survivability (NH&S) requirements supporting the MMIII Weapon System.

•Experience writing white papers on ESD/EMI/EMC/EMP concerns and provide recommended solution to meet program requirements

•Active DoD Secret Security Clearance or higher, ability to obtain Top Secret Clearance
