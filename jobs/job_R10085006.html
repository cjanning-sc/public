  • In addition to functional Section Manager duties, the candidate will perform IPT engineering tasks, which will comprise most of the statement of work. Note: Internal candidates aligned to Vehicle Engineering will typically continue to perform their existing IPT engineering tasks.
  • Product integrity, process adherence, staffing, training, and tools.
  • Process oversight, participation in the performance review process, management of personnel records and participation in corrective or disciplinary actions.
  • Staffing responsibilities may include hiring, recruiting of personnel, and working to ensure that engineers with the appropriate skills and experience are deployed.
  • Manage training opportunities and budget allocations, as well as participation in tool, process, and metric initiatives.
  • Team engagement is a key focus.
  • Mentor, provide career guidance and coaching, and leading others.
  • Travel to other Northrop Grumman and/or customer sites as required.
  • Use excellent verbal and written communication skills.

As a leader, you always act with integrity, and the highest ethical standards characterize everything you do. You enable your team to achieve predictable and balanced results, while satisfying the needs of internal and external partners. You treat all people with respect, and you model personal growth and continuous development. Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor of Science degree in a STEM discipline AND 9 years of professional/military engineering experience, OR a Master’s degree AND 7 years of professional/military engineering experience.
  • Must have an active DoD Secret clearance (or higher) that is in-scope (granted or renewed within the last 6 years or under continuous evaluation).
  • Must be able to obtain and maintain Special Access Program (SAP) clearance. This SAP must be obtained within a reasonable amount of time as determined by the company to meet its business needs.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Current/Active Special Program Access.
  • Leadership experience in a people leadership role or matrixed environment.
  • Bachelor or Master of Science degree in Aerospace Engineering or Mechanical Engineering.
  • 5 or more years of aircraft Mass Properties experience, preferably with fixed-wing military aircraft.
  • Proficiency with MS Office software (Excel, Word, PowerPoint).
  • Experience with CAD tools, such as Siemens NX.
