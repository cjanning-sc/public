ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems is looking for a Supply Chain Manager 2 – 1st Shift (4/10 Monday - Thursday) at our Cincinnati Site located in Cincinnati, CA.

At ACME Engineering, we are on the cutting edge of innovation-- building the next generation of sophisticated aircraft to protect our country. Our diverse portfolio of programs means there are endless paths to cultivating your career. We are well-known for our inclusive, family environment, as well as our excellent work/life balance. We also offer exceptional benefits/healthcare, a 4/10 schedule, and a great 401K matching program.

Responsibilities include:

  • Lead a team of exempt and non-exempt employees, who are responsible for the tracking, receiving, stocking, kitting, issuing, shipping, preservation, and issuing of parts supporting various production programs and including chemical management.
  • Coordinates interdepartmental activity with Manufacturing, Quality Assurance, Engineering, Warehousing, Traffic, Procurement, etc.
  • Responsible for meeting direct budget targets, writing/updating work instructions, providing performance metrics, and making recommendations to improve productivity, quality, and efficiency of operations.
  • As the Global Supply Chain focal point, develop and execute the strategy for Global Supply Chain Goods Movement and Logistics operations in support of the program, affordability targets, schedule performance, and first-time quality.
  • Leads a team that has the mission statement to support all production/test organizational goals and objectives through accurate tracking, receiving, stocking, kitting, issuing parts, shipping, preservation, chemical management, , with a strong focus on safety and cost efficiency.
  • Leads the supply chain team in executing best practices and measuring performance through agreed Key Performance Indicators (KPIs).  
  • Train and guide employees in keeping with all ACME Engineering Values.
  • Will collaborate with various programs on projects related to material receipt, storage, issue, and transportation.
  • Lead, coordinate, and collaborate with internal and external customers, including local, state, federal, and international agencies to ensure compliance with regulatory requirements in support of Aeronautics Sector programs.
  • Directs team activities associated with program transportation, storage, and material handling requirements, including major deliveries (classified and unclassified), and specialty movements as they relate to transportability.

Basic Qualifications:

  • 5+ years’ experience in a  management position with a bachelor’s degree; 3+ years with a master’s degree; 9 years of experience in place  of a degree
  • Prior experience successfully leading a complex team/process/project
  • Knowledge of a Material Requirements Planning (MRP)/ Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) Systems
  • Ability to perform desk work and production shop floor responsibilities with the ability to lift, push, pull up to 30 pounds
  • Ability to stand, walk and sit for prolonged periods of time
  • Valid driver’s license and ability to drive company-owned vehicles (potentially in the dark at times)
  • Ability to work a (4/10 Shift schedule - Monday- Thursday)
  • Ability to obtain and maintain a DoD security clearance with Special Program Access

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Master's degree in Business Administration, Supply Chain, or a related field
  • Ability to deal effectively with competing priorities and deadlines, and able to manage multiple tasks in a fast-paced environment while demonstrating a professional attitude
  • Working knowledge of SAP
