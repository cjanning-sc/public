ACME Engineering Space Systems is currently seeking a Principal/Sr. Principal Cyber Systems Engineer – Compliance Lead to join our Systems Security Engineering team within Launch Vehicles business unit at the Louisville, AZ facility for the Next Generation Interceptor (NGI) Program.
 

Role Description & Responsibilities:

The Compliance Lead will perform system security activities supporting all phases of systems development in the areas of Cybersecurity/ Information Assurance (IA)/ System Security Engineering, and Security Assessment and Authorization (A&A) as per the established Risk Management Framework (RMF) program in accordance with guidance from Systems Engineering integration and Test (SEIT), System Security Engineering (SSE) management supporting the statement of work requirements for Program, DCSA and MDA policies.

Meeting and conference attendance:

  • Attend and support all meetings and conference events to report/brief on Compliance Status tasking as assigned by the SSE Management

Development environment support:

  • Perform gap analysis on ATO packages and coordinate with stakeholders, develop, and author artifact documentation (Work Instructions, white papers, trade studies, operating procedures, plans, etc) as required to support the authorizations to operate

  • Represent NGI SEIT to the NGI Program Security Office, and Classified Network IA/IS authorities to resolve authorized user issues: user account request activities, procedural guidance, process assistance to users, change management efforts, Code Blue events, etc

  • Assess and provide potential risk mitigation plans in support of environment Configuration Management process and change requests; provide required references, artifacts, documentation, and impact assessments related to the assigned Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability

  • Support the authorized secure movement of data artifacts between the environments (Data transfers: high to high, low to high, high to low) in accordance with environment governance directives

  • Coordinate and document Compliance requirements between other SSE Leads

  • Orchestrate and perform data deliveries in support of intra-ACME Site, Sub-Contractor, and Customer data exchanges

eMASS activity support:

  • Support the execution and workflows associated required to fulfil guidance requirements for System Security Plan (SSP) creation, maintenance, and authorization award by the designated Authorization Authority

  • Artifact collection and upload from responsible entities (e.g., sub-contractor, Software IPT, Hardware IPT, etc) for system package/ record completeness

  • Retrieve assigned Security Control Traceability Matrix (SCTM)/SSP supporting details from responsible groups for control and system requirement implementation planning, artifacts (i.e. test results, process reports, compliance/ work/process completion evidences), and status

  • Evaluate and document the compliance of each assigned security control from the associated SCTM for implementation status and quality of evidence provided

  • POA&M updates and reporting

Deliverable Product (SOW All Up Round Components) support:

  • Author Test and Evaluation Plans for cybersecurity control compliance and reporting

  • Coordinate with other groups (e.g., sub-contractors, Software IPT, Hardware IPT etc) to ensure test and evaluation plans support the collection of necessary results and support artifacts for compliance reporting

  • SCTM generation and artifact support

  • System Security Requirement development and evidence support

CDRL authoring and SME support:

  • Support Peer Reviews on all SSE authored documentation

  • Author Program Protection Implementation Plan (PPIP) Appendices (e.g., SCTM and Cyber V&V Plan)

  • Coordinate with the NGI SSE Leads to develop requirements and generate Software Vulnerability Review reports

  • Assist in the requirements modeling for compliance objective evidence completion

  • Provide SME inputs to other SSE generated CDRLs (e.g. Information Management Control Plan, Cybersecurity Implementation Plan, Anti-Tamper Plan)

General responsibilities:

  • Perform work for SSE Management resource and schedule status reporting

  • Earned Value Management (EVM) support documentation for NGI Program Management and Contract efforts

  • Other compliance related tasks as assigned by SSE management

This position may be offered at the Principal Cyber Systems Engineer Level 3 or Sr. Principal Cyber Systems Engineer Level 4.

Basic Qualifications:

If offered at the Principal Cyber Systems Engineer Level 3 requires: 5 Years with Bachelors in Science; 3 Years with Masters; 0Years with PhD.

If offered at the Sr. Principal Cyber Cyber Systems Engineer Level 4 requires: 9 Years with Bachelors in Science; 7 Years with Masters; 4 Years with PhD.

  • Candidate must have a DoD Secret level security clearance which generally requires US Citizenship as a prerequisite.

  • Experience designing and developing new systems, applications, and solutions for cyber systems and networks.

  • Experience with RMF process

  • Currently possess or ability to obtain the DoD 8570-M Certifications (Security+, CISSP, etc.)

  • Experience ensuring system security needs are established and maintained for operations development, security requirements definition, security risk assessment, systems analysis, systems design, security test and evaluation, and certification and accreditation.

  • Experience writing Cybersecurity related plans, procedures, and artifacts

  • Ability to work cross-functionally with program teams

  • Experience leading projects or teams
