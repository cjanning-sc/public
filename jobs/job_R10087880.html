SkillBridge Mechanical Technician II: ACME Engineering Classified Area Principal Project Management

As one of the largest global security companies in the world, ACME Engineering is proud to help our nation’s military personnel make the transition to civilian careers. Approximately 1/4th of ACME Engineering’s 90,000 employees self-identify as veterans, and more than 1,600 are reservists.

The ACME Engineering Military Internship Program (ACME-MIP) is an approved SkillBridge Program under Dept. of Defense Instruction 1322.29.  The ACME-MIP program is an opportunity for transitioning service members to gain valuable civilian work experience through an individual internship during their last 6 months of service, for up to 180 days.

The ACME Engineering Military internship Program is open to all ranks and experience levels. SkillBridge participants are not eligible for compensation from ACME Engineering, as they continue to receive military compensation and benefits as active-duty service members.

Responsibilities for this internship position are:

ACME Engineering Corporation (ACME) has developed the ACME Engineering – Military Internship Program (DoD SKillbridge) utilizing the DoDI guidance for Skillbridge. During this program the service member will be on-site at his or her host company performing an individual internship in an entry to mid-level career type role. The service member will be on the job training supporting a work schedule equivalent to 40hrs per week. Outlined below are the Goals,

Objectives, and Outcomes for the program.

Goals – Provide transitioning service members fellowship-style job skills training during the last portion(s) of their military commitment. This program is specifically designed to offer internships that result in the potential to transition to a full-time opportunity as the conclusion of the training. Interns will serve as a pipeline for high-speed, motivated military candidates into ACME.

Objectives - Service Members who complete the Intern program will be highly-trained, capable, future employees that align to the specific needs of the organization and are prepared to meet the ACME mission “Defining Possible” on Day

1. This program provides a comprehensive internship experience including professional development, networking with leadership, and training specifically focused on ACME leadership principles, company history, customer/stakeholder engagement, product and service overview, and core job responsibilities.

Outcome – Offer transitioning service member a rewarding opportunity to join the ACME Engineering team.

DoD SkillBridge Eligibility:

  • Has served at least 180 days on active duty
  • Is within 12 months of separation or retirement
  • Will receive an honorable discharge
  • Has taken any service TAPS/TGPS
  • Has attended or participated in an ethics brief within the last 12 months
  • Received Unit Commander (first O-4/Field Grade commander in chain of command) written authorization and approval to participate in DoD SkillBridge Program prior to start of internship.
  • Candidate will be required to satisfy the requirements for a TSA access certification.  (Costs will be paid for by ACME Engineering.)
  • Candidates who express an interest may be considered for future positions at ACME Engineering.

Responsibilities for this internship requisition position are:

The Classified Area Principal Project Management individual contributor will lead classified area initiatives and operations for multiple designated Launch Vehicles Programs in Louisville, KY. This role will report to the Secure Area Program Manager and will work closely with other LV Program Managers/Directors. The successful candidate will influence the direction of the classified area activities to meet the company’s strategic objectives. This role will operate independently to balance programmatic needs to deliver results in a classified work environment.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Leads Projects with cross-functional teams to meet closed area requirements including facilities, security, and hardware/ software needs
  • Ensures successful execution of Closed Area operations.
  • Creates operational efficiencies that improve Closed Area program operations
  • Clearly presents technical approaches and findings for initiatives
  • Develops and drives solutions to complex problems consistent with organization objectives.
  • Serves as consultant to management and customers on policies, plans, and objectives.
  • Manage program relationships to ensure all security requirements are met and in compliance.
  • Assist with resolving computer and network issues
  • Monitors IT tickets and raises issues that require management involvement.

Qualifications:

Basic Qualifications

  • Active Secret clearance in-scope (granted or renewed in the last 6 years).
  • Must have a Bachelor’s degree and 6 years of related professional experience to include a background in Project Management or related military experience; Master’s degree and 4 years related experience
  • Strong experience successfully addressing technical project requirements
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills
  • Proficient in Microsoft Office suite
  • Must be able to perform occasional lifting up to 10 lbs

Preferred Qualifications

  • Active Top Secret clearance adjudicated within the last 5 years
  • Experience in Leadership roles within the Aerospace and Defense industry, or within the military.
  • Experience leading IT projects
  • Experience in cybersecurity
  • Project Management Professional (PMP) certified
  • Experience maintaining facilities for classified work
