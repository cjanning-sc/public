Northrop Grumman Defense Services sector is currently seeking a Manufacturing Analyst to join our growing Subsystems & Software Sustainment (S&SS) Operating Unit Systems Support Group (SSG) team in Warner Robins, Georgia. In this position, the selected candidate will take an active role in information security governance protecting the men and women that fly our nation’s aircraft in harm’s way.


Responsibilities:

- Supports production, business, and other processes in a team-based manufacturing environment.

- Performs activities in a variety of cross-functional areas including production planning and control, total quality management, systems, manufacturing, equipment and facilities engineering, material management, and process reengineering.

- Facilitate self-directed work teams, provide training in core skill areas to teams and peers, and interact with internal and external customers with respect to product planning, problem resolution, and process improvement.

- Applies advanced technical knowledge to solve complex problems that typically cannot be solved solely by referencing manufacturers' manuals or similar documents, such as:
- Determining the location and density of circuitry
- Evaluating electromagnetic radiation
- Isolating malfunctions to component level
- Incorporating engineering designs
- Exercises independent judgment in performing such tasks as making circuit analyses, calculating wave forms, and tracing relationships in signal flow
- Uses complex test instruments such as high frequency pulse generators, frequency synthesizers, distortion analyzers, and complex computer control equipment
- Work may be reviewed by supervisor for general compliance with accepted practices
- May provide technical guidance to lower level technicians

Basic Qualifications:
- Must have an Associate's degree or appropriate Military Occupational Specialty in electronics repair (or related field) with a minimum of 5 years of electronics / electrical repair experience; 2 additional years of related experience/education may be considered in lieu of degree.
- Must have work experience in manufacturing automated test equipment, troubleshooting, testing, building, and repair
- Must have an in-depth understanding of the interrelationships of circuits

- Must be able to demonstrate the ability to solve real world complex electronic problems

- Must have the understanding of how to operate measuring equipment and tools

- Must have an interim or active U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) Secret security clearance, or an inactive clearance (last active within the past 2 years)
- Must be able to work any shift
- Must be able to work on-site in Warner Robins, Georgia

Preferred Qualifications:

- Experience with Electro-Optical test equipment

- Experience with NI drivers and software, Labview, CVI, and Test Stand

- Experience working solo: self-prioritizing and tasking, consistent cadence

- Experience working within teams: projects, meeting schedules, and goals

- Experience working with customers: meetings, presentations, and tours
