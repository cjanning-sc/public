ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems (NGAS) has an opening for a Sr. Metrology Programming (Principal) Engineer to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals within our Green Bay (Dayton), OH facility.  We are a world-class manufacturer of composite structures, radomes, and antenna systems for military applications.  We design and manufacturer solutions for our war fighters to keep our country safe and maintain peace on a global scale.

Our mechanical engineering group works on state-of-the-art designs, primarily for classified applications, in both team and individual settings.  Our multi-faceted team consists of engineers and scientist with backgrounds in electrical engineering, LO technology, CEM, RF test, mechanical, structural, thermal, CFD, material science, process, metrology, and quality.  Our team enjoys both theoretical design and hands-on engineering tasks.

The Sr. Metrology Programming Engineer will be part of the CNC and CMM Programming team, reporting to the Manager of Mechanical Engineering.  We deliver our internal customers a great team experience, from first request to development code, then production released code.  So together, we exceed our customer’s expectations for product quality and performance.

The ideal candidate will be a self-sufficient, problem solver, with determination and a positive attitude.  He / She will have good interpersonal communication and people skills needed to work with key stakeholders within other engineering disciplines to achieve program objectives in a timely manner.  If you are comfortable in this high-energy environment and can collaborate with others – this is the opportunity for you!

*This is a first shift position working a 9/80 schedule with every other Friday off.

Essential Functions / Responsibilities:

  • Creates programs and report templates for CMM and Leica Trackers to inspect items ranging from small metallic and non-metallic products to room size aircraft composite structures and related hard tooling
  • Leads engineering efforts related CMM
  • Mentors’ other engineers and Quality Inspectors
  • Works with Design Engineers, Quality Engineers, Quality Inspectors, and CNC Machining to develop measurement systems and techniques
  • Helps troubleshoot equipment and product issues
  • Designs fixtures, gauges and tools for inspection
  • Works with the Quality Lab manager to select, procure, and commission inspection equipment and software
  • Prepares proposal estimates
  • Additional work responsibilities may include task planning and conducting technical peer reviews

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s degree in science with 9 Years of related experience; Master’s degree with 7 Years of related experience, PHD with 4 years related experience
  • Experience mentoring other engineers
  • Experience programming with CMMs
  • Experience in Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T) and drawing interpretation
  • Experience in 3D modeling for tooling set-up
  • Ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Secret Security Clearance, including Special Access Program within a reasonable period

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Active DoD Secret Security Clearance, or higher
  • Bachelor's degree in science in Mechanical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering Technology, Manufacturing Engineering Technology, Aerospace Engineering Technology, or Aeronautics
  • 5 years’ experience using PolyWorks
  • 10 years’ experience in programming with CMMs 
  • 5 years of experience designing fixturing and tooling for Inspection
  • Experience with Mitutoyo 122010 and 910 CMMs
  • Experience programming or operating Leica Trackers
  • Experience with 3D modeling with Siemens NX or CATIA
