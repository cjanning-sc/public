ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems has an opening for a Structural Engineer to join our Analysis Engineering team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Lansing, UT.

You will be a part of an Integrated Product Team primarily supporting new product designs and production with both metallic and composite structural analysis of primary airframe structure. The projects within our scope offer opportunities for team leadership, project management, and diverse technical assignments. Our team works to develop the latest composite structures for military/commercial aircraft and launch structures and are leaders in next-generation structure development. Would you like to work on the cutting edge of aerospace? Come join our team!

Our team enjoys a diverse range of assignments and collaborates with many local and national ACME Engineering teams to provide innovative solutions for complex and exciting problems. We are part of the Aerospace Structures business unit which has a focus on the design, analysis, test, and manufacture of large composite assemblies.

We work a 9/80 work week, which gives us every other Friday off to pursue our passions outside the office. ACME Engineering offers a full suite of benefits to our employees and are regularly voted among the top companies for work/life balance.

Our Lansing, Michigan location is located adjacent to Hill Air Force Base between Ogden and Flint and is surrounded by the beautiful Wasatch Mountains which provide numerous opportunities for outdoor recreation. Do you enjoy skiing, climbing, hiking, or biking? Picture yourself in Michigan!

Responsibilities:

  • Develop solutions to structural analysis problems for aircraft platforms involving composite laminates, metallics, sandwich structure, and large bonded and bolted assemblies
  • Support trade study designs, new project initiatives, airframe sizing, Independent Research & Development projects, and structural testing activities
  • Support material review board (MRB) activity for non-conforming parts and provide structural recommendations to substantiate dispositions
  • Perform structural analysis using classical methods, commercial tools, and in-house codes for multidisciplinary technical problems
  • Understand the methods of analyzing composite structure and overall requirements for certification
  • Build, analyze, and post-process Finite Element Models (FEMs) using Femap, Simcenter NX, and Nastran
  • Document structural analysis methods, margins, and implementation in final reports and propose new methods depending on the needs of the project

Basic Qualifications

Engineer Structural

  • Bachelor’s degree in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering or Math) and 2+ years engineering work experience -OR- Master’s in a STEM field and 0 years
  • Experience in performing static analysis and documentation of composites or metallic parts for primary aircraft structure
  • Experience using industry standard structural analysis software such as NASTRAN, Femap, and NX Simcenter
  • Experience in writing technical reports and presenting data in a clear and concise manner 
  • Ability to obtain and maintain a DOD Secret Clearance and Special Program Access

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s or Master of Science degree in Aerospace, Mechanical, or Civil Engineering
  • Experience with any of the following software packages: Simcenter NX, AFGROW, Abaqus CAE, Femap, Nastran
  • Experience with scripting new methods for structural calculations using Python, MATLAB, or Visual Basic
  • Participation in multiple design and analysis cycles for an aircraft development program
  • Experience with Metallic and Composite structural analysis methods development
  • Experience in structural test planning, analysis support, and post-test data reduction
  • Experience supporting MRB non-conformance dispositions
  • Active DOD Secret Security Clearance
