Mission Command Data Management Analyst

1st Shift

ACME Engineering Defense Systems sector is seeking a Mission Command Data Management Analyst to join our growing team of diverse professionals supporting our Mission Command Training Program (MCTP) at Fort Leavenworth in Kansas.  The Mission Command Data Management Analyst supports and guides the MCTP Operations Group (OG) military analysts, observer-coach/trainers (OC/Ts), and their leaders at U.S. Army and Joint - Combined headquarters exercises as they define the data requirements for their After Action Reviews (AARs) and establish a collection plan for that data.

Duties and Responsibilities:

  • Executes data collection plan based on established standard operating procedures (SOPs)

  • Provides requested training and subject matter expertise on Army Mission Command Systems (AMCSs) utilized

  • Meets with OG Senior Analysts and OC/T Warfighter team leads regularly to get their updated requirements for data collection and the organization and analysis of that data

  • Coordinates and provides training opportunities for OG military analysts, OC/Ts, workstation controllers and military augmentees in the proper operation and use of AMCSs

  • Builds digital data folder structures that facilitate sharing of desired collected information on collaborative shared drives and SharePoint portals

  • Performs data mining and collection from training audience tactical portals and AMCS equipment as required during exercises

  • Creates digital products at the request of OC/Ts and OG military analysts to facilitate the training environment

  • Sets up and maintains proper AMCS resources to display the Common Operational Picture (COP) for the OG analysts and OC/Ts and assists the Command Information Center operators in displaying the COP in their area

  • Reviews with OG Senior Analysts and OC/T Warfighter team leads whether the data collection meets their benchmarks for organization and quality

  • Makes adjustments and recommendations for best business practice guidelines as needed

  • Reviews military correspondence on current and projected military hardware, software and tactics to maintain current knowledge of Joint and Army doctrine related to Mission Command (MC)

  • Provides assistance and instruction to OG military analysts and OC/T's on how data can be accessed and how and where it is stored and filed

  • Provides "over the shoulder" training and instruction on their current AMCS specialty to military personnel augmentees and contractors fulfilling operator roles in work cells and response cells during exercises

  • Ensures that digital MC data collection is integrated into the MCTP AAR structure

  • Researches and analyzes AMCS problems found during testing and exercises and identifies solutions

Basic Qualifications:

  • One of the following:

    • High school diploma or GED with a minimum of 7 years of professional and/or military experience

    • Bachelor’s degree with a minimum of 3 years of professional and/or military experience

    • Master’s degree with a minimum of 1 year of professional and/or military experience

  • Must have or be able to obtain and maintain a valid U.S. Passport

  • Must have a U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) Secret security clearance

  • Ability to lift up to 50 lbs.

  • Must be able to travel in the United States and overseas to support simulation-based Army training exercises as needed (up to 50% of the time) and support extended shifts

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience with Microsoft Office applications such as PowerPoint, Excel, Word, and SharePoint

  • Prior Military Service

  • A minimum of 2 years’ experience on battalion staff or higher, or equivalent training organization

  • DoD certificate of training for an Army Mission Command System such as CPOF or CPCE
