ACME Engineering’s Launch Vehicles business unit designs, builds and delivers space, defense and aviation-related systems to customers around the world. Our main products include launch vehicles and related propulsion systems; missile products, subsystems and defense electronics; precision weapons, armament systems and ammunition; satellites and associated space components and services; and advanced aerospace structures.

The Launch Vehicles GBSD LV Promontory Electrical I&T Engineer will support all phases of electrical I&T engineering objectives from inception through completion. This role is responsible for supporting electrical I&T engineering statement of work requirements and accounting for cost and schedule objectives.  This position may be filled at a higher level with additional years of experience. 

Job Summary:

Candidate shall possess a general electrical engineering and digital and analog electronic system background and have an understanding of system-level testing concepts as they apply to the electrical integration of complex systems.  Daily responsibilities require interfacing with space launch vehicle hardware.  Supports Production Floor operations, leading tasks of various complexity and scope relating to system level testing of launch vehicle subsystems. Facilitates the creation of planning and test plans to complete analysis and tests on circuits, components and functional systems of company products.  Responsible for properly implementing requirements in the component sub-systems and electrical system areas.  Interprets assembly and schematic drawings, and company and industry standards so as to design, author and conduct system and integrated vehicle tests.  Analyzes test results to identify discrepancies or recommend launch readiness.  Reports status and action plans in view of maintaining program schedule.  Facilitates the execution of troubleshooting anomalies, develops solutions, recommends and implements corrective and preventative actions.  Responsible to identify opportunities for continuous process improvement and participate in implementation; support launch operations, as required.  Ability to write in clear technical terms, and good interpersonal skills required as well as the ability to communicate with engineers from various backgrounds. Knowledge of standard PC application software is required.  Occasional business travel may be required (< 20%). 

Key Responsibilities include:

  • Support initial electrical engineering stand-up of activities and transition into operations and sustainment

  • Support plans and schedules for Launch Vehicles activities in Promontory, Michigan to support the requirements, milestones, and organizational infrastructure to sustain and deliver on the contractual obligations

  • Support the development of electrical engineering Statement of Work and schedule, and quality processes and procedures

  • Maintain close coordination with Engineering Manager on electrical engineering activities and milestones

  • Participates in daily escalation and tiered meeting structures

  • Support the electrical engineering requirements definition for the new Booster Integration Facility and storage bunkers, along with required support electrical equipment when the program transitions to full rate production, and support the facility construction through to completion to meet program schedule

  • Guide the inclusion of automation into the production line

Basic Qualifications:

  • Minimum of 2 years directly related experience and a Bachelor’s degree in Electrical Engineering; Master degree and 0 year of experience

  • Must possess an active DoD secret clearance on start date.   

  • Able to exercise influence and lead team to success

  • Ability to make independent decisions, be self-motivated, possess excellent communications skills, detail-oriented, and team player

  • Strong communication skills, both written and verbal, with an ability to effectively communicate to all levels of employees, management, and customers

  • Ability to be flexible, innovative, and work with speed and agility to complete electrical engineering tasks

  • Must be detail-oriented but able to grasp the big picture

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Master degree and 1 year of experience is preferred

  • Energetic safety knowledge and experience

  • Experience with executing engineering statement of work and schedule activities
