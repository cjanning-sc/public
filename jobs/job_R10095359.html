Northrop Grumman Aeronautics Systems has an opening for a Quality Engineer to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Clearfield, UT.

Our teams ensure mission success for the F35 war fighter and multiple other projects which support the security of our nation.

Responsibilities:

  • Bring products to production from first article creation to compliance validation
  • Work with customers and suppliers through all MRB activities
  • Lead failure investigations in a team environment to determine root cause and implement robust corrective actions
  • Work with customers to understand and implement requirements on a variety of different parts and processes
  • Inspect parts, understand requirements, conduct first articles, validate compliance and perform other mission assurance tasks.

Our group enjoys solving complex cutting edge problems in a team environment.  We are encouraged and supported to innovate, brainstorm, and elevate the aerospace industry.  We work a 9/80 schedule which allows us to pursue other passions outside of work on our off Fridays. Northrop Grumman has won awards for our work life balance.  Our Clearfield site is surrounded by the beautiful Wasatch Mountains, perfect for the outdoors enthusiast.

This role may be filled at an Associate or Engineer level depending on the qualifications below. 

Basic Qualifications:

Associate:

  • Bachelor’s degree in a STEM discipline and no years of experience
  • Experience conveying technical information (both written and verbal)
  • Experience with MS Word, Excel, and PowerPoint
  • Ability to obtain DoD Clearance when required

Engineer:

  • Bachelor's Degree in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering or Math) field and a minimum of 2+ years of engineering experience or Master's Degree in STEM Field
  • The ability to obtain an active DoD Secret or Top Secret clearance

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in Aerospace, Mechanical, Manufacturing, or Chemical Engineering
  • Experience in composite materials, procedures, failure modes, environmental effects.
  • Composite fabrication experience and planning
  • Experience supporting composite primary and secondary structures, metallic, vendor supplied material certifications, approved materials list, and support forensic activities as required
