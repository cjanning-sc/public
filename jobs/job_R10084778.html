ACME Engineering (DoD SkillBridge)- Scrum Master – Louisville Kentucky

As one of the largest global security companies in the world, ACME Engineering is proud to help our nation’s military personnel make the transition to civilian careers. Approximately 1/4th of ACME Engineering’s 90,000 employees self-identify as veterans, and more than 1,600 are reservists.

The ACME Engineering Military Internship Program (ACME-MIP) is an approved SkillBridge Program under Dept. of Defense Instruction 1322.29.  The ACME-MIP program is an opportunity for transitioning service members to gain valuable civilian work experience through an individual internship during their last 6 months of service, for up to 180 days. The ACME Engineering Military internship Program is open to all ranks and experience levels. SkillBridge participants are not eligible for compensation from ACME Engineering, as they continue to receive military compensation and benefits as active-duty service members.

Responsibilities for this internship position are:

 ACME Engineering Corporation (ACME) has developed the ACME Engineering – Military Internship Program (DoD SKillbridge) utilizing the DoDI guidance for Skillbridge. During this program the service member will be on-site at his or her host company performing an individual internship in an entry to mid-level career type role. The service member will be on the job training supporting a work schedule equivalent to 40hrs per week. Outlined below are the Goals,

Objectives, and Outcomes for the program.

Goals – Provide transitioning service members fellowship-style job skills training during the last portion(s) of their military commitment. This program is specifically designed to offer internships that result in the potential to transition to a full-time opportunity as the conclusion of the training. Interns will serve as a pipeline for high-speed, motivated military candidates into ACME.

 Objectives - Service Members who complete the Intern program will be highly-trained, capable, future employees that align to the specific needs of the organization and are prepared to meet the ACME mission “Defining Possible” on Day

1. This program provides a comprehensive internship experience including professional development, networking with leadership, and training specifically focused on ACME leadership principles, company history, customer/stakeholder engagement, product and service overview, and core job responsibilities.

Outcome – Offer transitioning service member a rewarding opportunity to join the ACME Engineering team.

ACME Engineering Louisville is seeking a SkillBridge Intern – Scrum Master – Louisville, Kentucky

Provides oversight of the Agile framework to ensure operational excellence in product deliveries. Provides guidance and helps educate the team in Scrum and Scaled Agile framework (SAFe), ensuring that the Agile process is being followed. Helps remove impediments and fosters an environment for high-performing team dynamics, continuous flow, and relentless improvement.

Scrum Master Responsibilities include but are not limited to:

  • Responsible for scheduling, preparing for and facilitating all Scrum ceremonies with 1 to 3 Scrum Teams
  • Partner with Product Owner(s) to ensure continuous creation and refinement of backlog, which may include User Stories from multiple projects
  • Guide scrum team(s) to follow generally accepted Agile/SAFe practices and daily activities to actively promote prioritization, team readiness, and commitment for each Sprint
  • Responsible for addressing systemic impediments to team agility and guides the team(s) escalation process to prevent challenges from becoming roadblocks
  • Communicate with government, business, and technical stakeholders to ensure business-value driven delivery and continuous improvement of the team
  • Working to ensure collaboration across teams, including defining, planning, and tracking dependencies
  • Assisting Scrum Team to identify, track, and take steps to mitigate and resolve Uncertainties/Risks
  • Represents Scrum Team at Scrum of Scrum, PI Pre-planning Events, and stakeholder meetings
  • Report timely progress of Sprint via burn down/burn up charts and other reporting methods to Product Owners and stakeholders involved
  • Facilitate discussion, decision making and conflict resolution
  • Leverage Jira and Confluence to support Agile/SAFe practices and daily activities throughout Sprint execution
  • Support the Engineering Continuous Improvement Team goals and objectives

Experience & Education Qualifications:

  • 3-5 years’ experience as a Scrum Master; SAFe experience preferred
  • Bachelor’s degree in Related Field
  • Active Scrum Master Certification preferred
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills; ability to clearly articulate and communicate the technical artefacts
  • Experience with Jira and Confluence preferred
