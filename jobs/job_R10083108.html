Are you looking for a rewarding and challenging career as a Principal/Sr Principal Mechanical Digital Transformation Engineer in Northridge, CA?  If so, then Northrop Grumman Defense Systems may be the employer for you!

We are currently seeking Principal/Sr Principal Mechanical Digital Transformation Engineer who will research, plan, design, develop, and document mechanical and electromechanical components and assemblies and assist the department during a crucial digital transformation process as part of Northrop Grumman’s Stand-in Attack Weapon (SiAW).

In this role, you will work with the Lead Design Engineer and IPT Leads in developing innovative strategies and model-based applications. You will also coordinate with CM and other departments in implementing digital transformation initiatives.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Interface with SiAW program leadership.
  • Drive culture change to establish the capabilities of a digital enterprise in support of mechanical design efforts from concept development to vehicle build and testing.
  • Support site digital transformation initiatives
  • Work with IPT leads and tool development managers to evolve a level of process knowledge
  • Mentor and lead less experienced staff in developing and implementing digital engineering solutions in an Agile environment.

Basic Qualifications:

  • Principal Mechanical Digital Transformation Engineer requires a minimum of Bachelor of Science degree in a STEM discipline (Science, Technology, Engineering or Math) and 5 years of related experience to include a background in design, analysis and/or engineering tools development discipline OR a Master’s degree in a STEM discipline and 3 years of related experience to include a background in design, analysis and/or engineering tools development

  • Sr. Principal Mechanical Digital Transformation Engineer requires a minimum of a Bachelor of Science degree in a STEM discipline (Science, Technology, Engineering or Math) and 9 years of related experience to include a background in design, analysis and/or engineering tools development discipline OR a Master’s degree in a STEM discipline and 7 years of related experience to include a background in design, analysis and/or engineering tools development
  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Secret clearance.
  • Familiarity with ASME Y14.41.
  • Experience with digital integration of various engineering tools.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience with PTC Creo (7.0 is preferred) and Windchill.
  • Familiarity with NX.
  • Experience with Teamcenter.
  • PMI Certification as a Disciplined Agile Scrum Master (DASM) or Agile Certified Practitioner (PMI-ACP).
  • Experience with ASME Y14.5.
  • Experience with CCA design and/or fabrication.
  • Experience with Machining (Mill, Lathe, Router, etc...).
  • Experience with additive manufacturing (3D Printing, Rapid Prototyping, etc...).
  • Experience with structural and/or thermal analysis using both hand-calculations and FEA software (Nastran, Abacus, etc...).
  • Familiarity with Trade Studies.
  • Certification as an Engineer-In-Training (EIT).
