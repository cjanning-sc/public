The Quality Inspector uses predetermined methods, operations, setups and prescribed specifications to inspect visually in-process and completed products such as electronic units and subsystems, precision electromechanical assemblies or mechanical units, subassemblies, structural flaws, internal defects, and missing welds. Uses various measuring devices. Accepts, rejects, or reworks defective or malfunctioning units or systems. Works from blueprints, diagrams, dial indicators, preset micrometers, scales, fixtures, customer specifications, drawing or inspection instructions and checklists. May monitor and verify quality in accordance with statistical process or other control procedures. Performs line clearances after each lot to ensure all materials from the previous lot have been removed.

Performs required inspections using general mechanical inspection tools and equipment to ensure conformance with the quality requirements. This job reports to the Quality Manager and works under general supervision.  Visually inspects products that conform to standardized Company and industry policies, practices, procedures and specifications.

The Quality Inspector works from drawings, diagrams, statements of work, purchase orders, inspection instructions and checklists. May monitor and verify tests in accordance with quality processes and procedures.

This job has decision-making responsibilities with regard to the acceptance or rejection of parts or products such as manufactured parts, boards, chassis, assemblies, cables and materials in various configurations. Inspector should have experience with mechanical and machine/fabrication of parts and components with critical tolerances to in-process functional, final product review sell off and receiving inspection.

Basic Qualifications:

  • High School Diploma and 6+ years of relevant experience (or AA or 2 year technical degree plus 4+ years of relevant experience).
  • Working knowledge of general inspection tools; microscopes, cameras, dimensional tools and equipment; gages, calipers, coordinate measurement machines (CMM) and Optical Comparators.
  • Experience in a high energy /fast paced ESD/FOD controlled, cross-functional manufacturing environment with good computer and communication skills.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • AS9100, ESD/FOD, IPC-610, familiarity of IPC J-STD-001 standard, Solumina or other shop floor system, Windchill or other document control repository, SAP/ MRP, Coordinate Measuring Machine (CMM) programming and receiving inspection.
