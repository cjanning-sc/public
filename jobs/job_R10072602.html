The Defense Systems (DS) Human Resources organization is seeking the expertise and leadership of a Manager of Talent Management to shape and implement a talent management strategy to enable top tier business performance. The Manager of Talent Management will join the DS Talent organization, reporting to the Senior Director of Talent and will lead a team of specialists responsible for succession planning, talent management and enterprise process rollout.   

This position is based out of our Waterloo, IA office with flexibility for a hybrid work arrangement, with onsite presence required based on team and business needs. 

Key Responsibilities: 

  • Collaborate with Human Resources Business Leaders and Sector leadership to develop and implement talent management strategies and associated operational rhythms aligned to business strategy 
  • Oversee sector talent identification and assessment, succession management, leadership pipeline development and the identification of development needs to inform talent development strategies 
  • Lead execution of core talent processes, including performance management, engagement, onboarding, assimilation, talent reviews and integration of emerging talent-related Workday capability 
  • Manage nomination and selection for corporate leadership cohort development programs 
  • Partner across the enterprise to expand/evolve existing initiatives and influence future plans and roadmaps, including the digital transformation of succession planning & talent review processes 
  • Leverage talent management & OE subject matter expertise and internal/external best practices to think creatively and envision / develop new solutions that drive business outcomes 
  • Lead a team of talent management professionals through ambiguity & shifting priorities, proactively managing workloads to deliver high quality products on time while maintaining team’s work-life balance  

Basic Qualifications 

  • Must have a high school diploma or GED with a minimum of 12 years of relevant experience; OR Bachelor's degree with a minimum of 8 years of relevant experience ; OR Master’s degree with a minimum of 6 years of relevant experience 
  • Experience in Talent Management, including Organizational Effectiveness, Change Management, Performance Management, Succession Planning, or other business-focused talent management role 
  • Strong relationship-building skills; proven track record of building collaborative partnerships with senior-level leaders as the basis for being able to influence direction and drive change 
  • Outstanding organization and project management skills with the ability to manage the delivery of high-quality, on-time programs in a dynamic environment 
  • Leadership experience, including direct or indirect management/delegation 

 Preferred Qualifications 

  • Possess exceptional consultative skills that combine thought leadership, influential communication, and strategic problem solving to design and drive people-related change  
  • Demonstrated experience leading comprehensive organizational assessments that result in development and implementation of strategies that strengthen talent outcomes 
  • Ability to leverage data and analytics to inform decisions and actions aligned to business outcomes 
  • Degree in business, management, organizational development, human resources, or closely related field 
  • Experience in Aerospace & Defense or other technology/manufacturing organization
