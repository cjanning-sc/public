Controls the efficient flow of goods, services, and information between point of origin through customer placement in order to meet customer requirements. Ensures that customer service and time objectives are achieved within existing financial constraints in order to meet marketing and financial objectives. Ensures the execution and continuous improvement of standard logistics processes, such as the replenishment system, data interchange systems, demand management, electronic data systems administration and related functions. Builds relationships with strategic customers through logistics initiatives. Integrates learning from customers, competitors, operating entities, distribution, transportation, customer service, other industries, industry groups, and professional training to continuously improve competitive position.

ACME Engineering is seeking a logistics management analyst to join our team of qualified, diverse professionals. The qualified applicant will become part of ACME Engineering's Integrated Battle Command System (IBCS) Supply Support Team. IBCS provides Command/Control and Integrated Fire Control capabilities for a distributed Air and Missile Defense Enterprise. The primary responsibilities involve the efficient flow of goods, services, and information between points of origin through customer placement in order to meet customer requirements. In this role, the logistics management analyst will ensure the execution of standard logistics processes including the replenishment system, data interchange systems, demand management, electronic data systems administration and related functions. These activities will include the efficient flow of spares, tracking vendor repairs, performing warehouse support, work order processing, and reporting of material disposition. Ability to travel required.

Roles and Responsibilities:

- Serves as a consultant to management regarding logistics policies, plans, and objectives

- Conducts work order management for field repairs by opening work orders for Field Service Representatives (FSR), updating work orders during repair activities, and closing work orders at the completion of repair activities

- Maintains accountability of spares and facilitates material distribution in support of field repairs

- Collects and reports data, metrics, and status of operations

- Attends boards and meetings to track the disposition of materials

- Accounts for and maintains records of Government Furnished Equipment (GFE)

- Operates information management systems to maintain records and provide status and reporting

- Reports cost and schedule information for items dispositioned for vendor repair

- Represents the organization as a prime contact to customer counterpart

- Develops and delivers presentations to program and customer leadership

-Demonstrates ability to properly package and handle equipment during shipping and receiving

- Supports material fielding with inventories and handover documentation

Basic Qualifications:

  • Understanding of the material replenishment system
  • Experience in the performance of sparing operations and material control
  • Expert level of ability in estimating resource requirements
  • Demonstrated ability to work across multiple Global Supply Chain (GSC) and Logistics disciplines
  • Ability to obtain a Department of Defense Secret security clearance


Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience tracking material disposition in SAP
  • Experience in receiving, maintaining, and the accountability of GFE
  • HazMat certified
  • IUID marking and processes
  • Experience using the COLTS database
