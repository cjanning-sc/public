ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems has an opening for an Engineer Systems / Principal Engineer Systems – Subsystems to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals within our Vehicle Engineering Flight Systems organization. This position is located in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.

Vehicle Management Systems (VMS) Engineer requires coursework and/or experience in aircraft vehicle management systems.

In this role, you will be responsible for design and development of system architectures and defining key capabilities and performance requirements. Defining design and technology maturity constraints of the system in accordance with customer specifications. Developing thorough definition of system external interfaces. Defining system implementation approach and operational concept. Ensuring requirements are met and evaluating performance with customer. Additional responsibilities include managing requirements, design, testing, and presenting updates to internal and external customers.

Primary responsibilities may include, but are not limited to:

  • Development of flight critical system design for aircraft control
  • Development of architecture design with optimized redundancy levels and fault management
  • Communicating with multiple subsystems to define interfaces to the Vehicle Management Computer. Subsystems may include;
    • Brakes/BCU
    • Hydraulics
    • Electrical Power
    • Propulsion
    • Cockpit Displays
    • Navigation/GPS
    • Air Data
  • Documenting Vehicle Management System physical architecture including all data bus and discrete interconnections
  • Definition of interface and processing latency requirements based on subsystem functional requirements
  • Specification of test requirements for VMS system level performance
  • Specification and oversight of Vehicle Management System software
  • Development of product specifications to support acquisition and procurement of subsystems from vendors
  • Development of subsystem simulations as needed to support Vehicle Management System verification
  • Developing test cases and executing test procedures in lab and on-aircraft environments
  • Presenting technical updates to internal and external customers
  • Demonstrating a commitment to ethics and integrity, and the capability to innovate while challenging traditional ways of doing business

Additional duties may include:

  • Performing technical planning, system integration, verification and validation, cost and risk, and supportability and effectiveness analyses for total systems
  • Performing analysis at all levels of total system product to include: concept, design, fabrication, test, installation, operation, maintenance and disposal
  • Ensuring the logical and systematic conversion of customer or product requirements into total systems solutions that acknowledge technical, schedule, and cost constraints
  • Performing functional analysis, timeline analysis, detailed trade studies, requirements allocation and interface definition studies to translate customer requirements into hardware and software specifications

We seek candidates with strong written and verbal communication skills, the ability to work as part of a team, and the ability to organize work, manage time, plan, and prioritize.

Basic Qualifications

This requisition may be filled at either an Engineer Systems or a Principal Engineer Systems level.

The basic qualifications for an Engineer Systems are:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics (STEM) field with 2 years of system architecture definition and/or integration test engineering experience OR a Master’s Degree in STEM.
  • Must have an active DoD Secret or higher clearance (with a background investigation completed within the last 6 years or currently enrolled into Continuous Evaluation).
  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain Special Access Program (SAP) clearance prior to start of employment.

The basic qualifications for a Principal Engineer Systems are:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics (STEM) field with 5 years of system architecture definition and/or integration test engineering experience OR a Master’s Degree in STEM with 3 years of system architecture definition and/or integration test engineering experience OR a PhD Degree in STEM.
  • Must have an active DoD Secret or higher clearance (with a background investigation completed within the last 6 years or currently enrolled into Continuous Evaluation).
  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain Special Access Program (SAP) clearance prior to start of employment.

Preferred Qualifications

  • Previous experience with design, development, and/or integration of Air Vehicle systems and subsystems in the military aerospace environment
  • Experience with advanced systems and development, integration, and test of aircraft subsystems
  • Experience using the CAMEO design suite
  • Hands-on experience writing engineering modeling and simulation code
  • Prior airworthiness experience
  • Experience in electronics hardware and software certification
  • Integrated Hardware/Software (Subsystem/System Feature) validation experience
