The Item Manager will work directly with an Item Management team, supporting the Navigation, Targeting, and Survivability division. This position is responsible for supporting IM efforts in coordinating and managing assets at customer locations, forecasting, maintaining inventory levels, repair and upgrade coordination, and shipment facilitation in support of program requirements. The candidate must be able to effectively communicate with both internal and external customers.

Item Management responsibilities include:
- Responsible for asset planning, allocation, and coordinating the movement of program assets between supported global locations to satisfy operational requirements
- Calculate and present plans for overall inventory levels to support asset availability metrics and sparing
- Report on repair activities in collaboration with Vendors and ACME Teams
- Track, manage, and report on internal assets used at ACME
- Track performance against program metrics and provide status updates internally to the PMO and to the Customer
- Work with internal Coding Team to refine asset tracking database to track and manage program assets at global locations (Customer, Vendor, ACME)
- Collaborate with internal/external Customer to obtain near real time information concerning the physical location of assets in support of the master database
- Update database with current data, generate, and deliver output contractual reports
- Support regular review meetings with Customer and participate in program related meetings
- Proactively manage risks and opportunities such as allocation of resources, modification of schedule, or revision of contractual specifications and work with the Functions, PMO and Customer on resolution
- Ensure Item Management content meets EV (cost, schedule) and quality requirements
- Develop and continuously improve Item Management processes that align with contractual requirements
- Manage and understand program artifacts

As a Database Processor, you will support multiple product lines in the Navigation, Targeting, and Survivability division. Candidate will be responsible for processing failure records to include: induction of the failure records from multiple data sources into a centralized database, identifying and summarizing repair information from the repair system of record, and preparing database records for further processing by the Failure Review Board.

Data Processor responsibilities include:

- Inducting hardware into Reliability Database

- Creating, updating, and managing database entries to ensure data accuracy
