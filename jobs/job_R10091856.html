The Space Systems Sector Payload and Ground Systems Division is seeking a Contamination Control Engineer for spacecraft systems or subsystems. The contamination control engineer will perform a mix of contamination control and systems engineering work. This will include contamination analysis, development of a contamination control plan across the whole facet of programs from unit level up to integration and test, coordination of contamination control design across in-house team, subcontracts, and ACME sectors.   In addition, the contamination control engineer will support a contamination characterization laboratory, including sample collection and analysis from cleanrooms and hardware, writing reports, and maintaining equipment.  

Basic qualifications

  • Principal - Bachelor's degree 5 years of professional experience, 3 years with a Masters Degree.

  • Sr Principal - Bachelor's degree 9 years of professional experience, 7 years with a master's degree, PhD 4 years exp.

  • Prior experience with Systems Engineering in a Space Environment

  • Be able to obtain a U.S. Government TS/SCI security clearance.

  • Be able to operate within a multi-disciplined Integrated Product Team.

  • Hands-on experience in contamination control field and materials engineering

  • Understanding of principles and methods for contamination control in clean room environments.

  • Knowledge and experience with Class 100 to Class 100,000 Cleanrooms.

Preferred qualifications

  • Chemical Engineering, Physics or Materials Science Engineering Knowledge

  • Prior experience with Systems Engineering in a Space Environment

  • Familiarity with contamination control industry specifications/standards

  • Hands-on experience in contamination control field and materials engineering

  • Experience with requirement derivation and verification.

  • Existing TS/SCI security clearance

  • Experience operating in an Integrated Product Team environment

  • Experience with Non-Volatile Residue (NVR) and Particle count/characterization
