Principal Software Development Analyst--Secret Clearance Required

1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman Enterprise Services is seeking a Software Development Analyst with CAD design and administration experience. This position may be located at one of the following two Northrop Grumman sites: Roy, Utah or Rancho Bernardo, CA. There is no option for full time telecommute in this role.

Roles and responsibilities will include, but not be limited to, the following:

  • Administration of CAD and CAD Model Management Systems – utilizing NX
  • End-user technical support of CAD software in development and production environments.
  • Performing root cause analysis and debugging problems within the CAD environment, and working with the appropriate technical teams to develop, test, and implement fixes to prevent recurrence.
  • Monitoring, addressing, and resolving production CAD environment incidents, service requests, workflows, client install packages, etc., collaboratively with other support teams.
  • Documenting all aspects of system support so that knowledge is readily shared among all those tasked with supporting CAD instances.
  • Supporting business readiness and change management activities prior to go-live and in production.

Basic Qualifications for Level 3:

  • BS Degree and 5+ years CAD support experience with NX or other 3D CAD  applications with similar functions and capabilities (AA Degree with 7 years of experience in lieu of degree)
  • Proven track record supporting major CAD software in an enterprise environment
  • Experience with CAD support and incident resolution
  • US Citizenship is required for this role as well as an active or current DoD Secret or Top Secret clearance; maintaining clearance will be a condition of continued employment.
  • Security+CE certified

Preferred Qualifications:

 

  • Experience with AWS Cloud
  • Familiarity with the functions, processes, and capabilities in native CAD applications and Data Management.
  • Experience supporting CAD in Windows and Linux environments.
  • Fluency in at least one computer programming language.
  • Ability to apply engineering techniques and analytical methods to make recommendations for improvements and provide information and assistance to end-users.
