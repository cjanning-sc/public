Manager Supply Chain Programs 2

1st Shift (United States of America)

Northrop Grumman is seeking a dynamic, energetic individual for the position of Supply Chain Manager 2 to join our growing team of diverse professionals. This Manager will be responsible for the day to day management, training, mentoring and career development of a team of pricing professionals.
 
The Supply Chain Manager supports the Global Supply Chain (GSC) organization.  This key role is responsible for providing timely and effective pre-proposal and post award pricing and estimating support to proposal teams and GSC staff through a functional homeroom.  The selected candidate will be an excellent communicator with a strong business background and will be able to communicate and collaborate effectively with all levels of management.
 
Roles and Responsibilities:
  

  • Supporting Strategic Captures and Proposals

  • Establishes and manages priorities in a fast-paced, schedule-driven environment

  • Manages cost/price analyst professionals engaged pricing and estimating activities (both pre-proposal submission and post award during execution), spanning all contract types

  • Ensures all GSC proposal pricing is adequately reviewed and approved prior to submission and inclusion in sector proposal

  • Ensures Estimating and Purchasing System compliance with Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) / Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement (DFARS) criteria and advises leadership of and issues or risks

  • Supports all Defense Contract Audit Agency (DCAA) proposal audits in coordination and collaboration with the Sector Manager of Internal Reviews

  • Participates on major proposals and initiatives within Pricing and GSC to include Non-Advocate Review / Independent Cost Estimate (NAR/ICE) and Color Team reviews when necessary

  • Develops, communicates Risk Management Plans, and then ensures execution

  • Develops and effectivity communicates strategic plans to leadership

  • Makes decisions on operational issues within prescribed area and manages a staff of professional to include mentoring, training, staff development, and conducting performance reviews

  • Develops, plans, and implements agile processes that support established GSC goals and objectives

Basic Qualifications: 

  • Must have one of the following:

    • Bachelor’s degree in business, supply chain, finance, economics or other related discipline with a minimum of 10 years of directly related experience

    • Master’s degree in business, finance, supply chain, economics or other related discipline with a minimum of 8 years of directly related experience

  • Must have experience with defense proposals and working on key captures

  • Through knowledge of FAR/DFARS and other applicable government regulations

  • Ability to perform and apply appropriate cost accounting, pricing and estimating methods, concepts and principles including the development and application of cost improvement curves and factors

  • Ability to work as an integral member of the GSC team to achieve Global Supply Chain, Program and sector objectives, while maintaining full compliance during all stages of the proposal and acquisition processes

  • Ability to initiate and effectively implement process improvement across the breadth of pricing and estimating policies and procedure

  • Must be able to travel as needed (up to 10% of the time)


Preferred Qualifications: 

  • Working Knowledge of SAP

  • Past experience with DCAA/DCMA audits

  • Professional Certifications: CPSM, CFCM, CPCM.   

  • Understanding of the defense acquisition process
