Job Description

ACME Engineering Space Systems Division designs, builds and delivers space, defense and aviation-related systems to customers around the world.  Our main products include launch vehicles and related propulsion systems; missile products, subsystems and defense electronics; precision weapons, armament systems and ammunition; satellites and associated space components and services; and advanced aerospace structures.

ACME Engineering Space Systems Division is seeking an experienced RF (Radio Frequency) Payload Mechanical engineer to assist or lead a team of other RF Payload Mechanical engineers to perform layout of RF equipment required for communications spacecraft.
 

Responsibilities include the following:

  • Layout of RF equipment on spacecraft payload panels. 
  • History of working with RF equipment such as test couplers, filters, muliplexers, high power amplifiers (ex. TWTA, SSPAs), etc.
  • Design of waveguide and coaxial cable networks that connect RF equipment. 
  • Ensure RF equipment meets mechanical environments for which they will reside.  Work with Mechanical Analysis team to meet compliance.
  • Work with RF engineering on overall design and layout of RF equipment on spacecraft to ensure compliance at system level.
  • Write or contribute to performance specifications w.r.t mechanical requirements.
  • Perform detailed design tasks using Computer Aided Design (CAD) and other software tools to develop and document designs. 
  • Interface and communicate with customers, program, Integration and Test (I&T), functional management, other engineering disciplines and component suppliers. 
  • Perform cost and schedule planning using Earned Value Management System (EVMS).
  • Be self-motivated and provide technical solutions to a wide range of difficult problems under limited supervision.

Basic Qualifications: RF Payload Mechanical Engineer 2:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in Aerospace Engineering, Mechanical Engineering or related area and 2+ years of experience OR a Masters Defree.
  • US Citizenship required
  • Experience in performing engineering tasks associated with design, manufacturing and integration of RF payloads and equipment layouts.
  • Basic understanding of RF principles.
  • Basic understanding and interpretation of RF system block diagrams.
  • Experience with waveguide and coaxial cable layout design.
  • Experience with Computer Aided Design (CAD) software.
  • Experience with interpreting and reviewing vendor provided Interface Control Drawings (ICDs) and associated 3D CAD models to provide feedback against requirements.
  • Ability to write process guides to document processes. 
  • Experience with Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T) standards and preparation of manufacturing documentation.
  • Experience with cost and schedule management using Earned Value Management System (EVMS).
  • Basic understanding of mechanical analysis and simulation methods and results.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience with CREO Parametric software.
  • Experience with hands-on hardware fabrication.
  • Experience with computer programming.  Specifically MS Visual Basic. 
  • Demonstrated leadership experience.
  • Proficiency with mechanical analysis and simulation tools.
